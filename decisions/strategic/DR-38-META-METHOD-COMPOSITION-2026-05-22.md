## §4 Frankenstein metaphor analysis (Phase 5)

### §4.1 The 3 angles (acceptance A-5)

1. **Shelley original (1818):** Close reading of Frankenstein vol. 1 ch. 4-5 + vol. 2 reveals: Shelley did NOT depict composition-as-monstrous. She depicted **abandonment** as the monstrous act. The creature is articulate, intelligent, morally lucid. He becomes destructive *because* Victor abandons him.
2. **Composition-as-monster (modern):** Popular reading treats Frankenstein = composed-from-incompatible-parts. But biology IS composition-from-disparate-parts (mitochondrial endosymbiosis, brain layers). The difference between «monster» and «organism» is integration discipline.
