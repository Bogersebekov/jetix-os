## §11.2 Per-Gate Narrative

### Gate G1 — €50K, Q2 2026 (the only committed absolute date in the plan)

**What unlocks.** G1 is the Phase-0→Phase-1 completion gate per JETIX-PLAN §3.1 and JETIX-VISION §13. At €50K cumulative revenue confirmed, the D15 spend-lock releases the following: GmbH + Stripe fully operational, $1000 public experiment documented, Продюсерский центр pilot with 2-3 paying clients. Revenue decomposition per §3.1.1: ~€30K from 2 productized contracts × 2 quarters + ~€35K from 233 hourly consulting hours. The ICP expanded spectrum (intake §6.1) is the active qualification layer: payment ability ≥$5K/month recurring OR ≥$10K one-shot; income width includes millionaires, $100K-150K+/year earners, предприниматели, блогеры — all passing the D22 5-criteria filter (startupper + azart + stable + adequate + upward-direction).

**What L6 capacities come online.** Outreach is manual and warm: LinkedIn DMs with pre-researched personalized openers, warm network intros, voice-pitch notes, 1-2 podcast appearances per JETIX-PLAN §3.4 (D12 TOF on maxималках). The Secure Network is a simple Telegram invite-based chat of 5-20 personally known people — zero mechanics, zero anti-free-riding, purely "попутно." Alliance is concept-only: Ruslan holds 5-10 warm contacts in memory. Matchmaker runs entirely in Ruslan's head. No digital portraits exist. Revenue from community = €0 (community is a trust-building layer and lead-quality filter, not a revenue line at G1).

**Bottleneck identified.** The primary constraint at G1 is *Ruslan's personal time and attention bandwidth*. With solo operation and 233 hours of consulting booked across the gate period alongside productized-contract delivery, there is negligible time to also design Secure Network mechanics, build Alliance governance, or develop digital portrait templates. Everything community-related that is not strictly "invite the right 5-20 people and chat" must be deferred. The secondary bottleneck is *ICP verification* — the expanded spectrum is broader than the prior $1M+ floor, which increases the population of candidates but increases the risk of admitting people who pass the income filter but fail the D22 qualitative criteria (adequate, upward-direction, stable). Without a written qualification protocol, the filter lives only in Ruslan's judgment.

*(F: F4, operational convention grounded in JETIX-PLAN §3.6 D15 unlock mechanics; ClaimScope: holds for Phase-1 solo operation ≤€50K; R: refuted if Ruslan successfully delegates L6 community design work to a part-time contractor before €50K gate; accepted if the Telegram chat is the only community artefact active at G1 close.)*

**Mitigation / preparation needed in G1 (before G2 transition).** Two pre-investments can be made inside G1 without violating D15 heavy-spend lock: (1) write the D22 qualification protocol as a 1-page document — zero cost, high leverage, prevents low-quality entries from embedding in the early chat; (2) draft the digital portrait template (skills / availability / archetype / contributions structured fields) in markdown — again zero cost. **Migration trigger to G2.** Revenue threshold: €50K cumulative confirmed. Leading indicators: (a) Продюсерский центр has ≥2 paying clients generating recurring retainer revenue; (b) at least 1 person from the Telegram chat has naturally said "I want to contribute more" or asked to refer someone — indicating organic pull that justifies moving from ad hoc chat to designed Secure Network. Without signal (b), G2 Secure Network design starts on a zero-demand premise, which is fragile.

## §11.3 Cross-Gate Scalability View — Systems-Expert Lens

### Meadows Leverage Points — Where They Are Most Pliable per Gate

Per Meadows' 12-rung leverage ladder (Thinking in Systems, book-as-frame), the most pliable rungs shift across gates.

**G1 — L7 (Information flows — who has access to what).** The highest-leverage intervention at G1 is not changing any constant or parameter: it is ensuring that the right people have accurate information about what Jetix offers and who qualifies. The ICP expanded spectrum (intake §6.1) is a L7 leverage move — opening the information channel to a broader but still-filtered population increases deal flow without structural change. The 5-criteria D22 filter is the membrane that makes L7 opening safe.

**G2 — L6 (Structure of information flows — feedback loops).** At G2, Alliance governance choice (§5 sibling) is a L6 leverage point: it determines *who feeds information back into the methodology* (Alliance members, roys, fork-contributors). Choosing a governance structure that incentivises high-quality feedback loops (e.g., merit-based contribution weight) is more leveraged than any parameter-level change (e.g., tweaking membership dues amounts, which is L12).

**G3 — L4 (Structure of material flows — platform architecture).** The Secure Network platform launch is a structural change (L4): it changes how information and trust flow between community members. The highest-leverage design decision is the portrait-completion gate (identified as bottleneck above) — a structural rule that prevents the matchmaker's reinforcing loop from being undermined by portrait quality degradation. Meadows L4 is where the matchmaker feedback loop is either enabled or broken at structural level.

**G4 — L3 (Goals of the system — what the Alliance parliament is optimizing for).** At $100M scale, the Alliance methodology-parliament is a new agent in the system with its own goals. If its goal is drift toward what is most convenient for large roys (L3 goal), it will corrupt canonical Jetix methodology. The leverage point is explicitly encoding the identity grammar into the parliament's optimization criterion — making "preserve D22 5-criteria ICP + D13 closed core" a constitutional goal, not a preference.

**G5 — L1 (Power to transcend paradigms — civilizational identity).** At $1T scale, the leverage is at the paradigm level: is Jetix a company, a methodology, a standard, or a civilizational infrastructure? The frame chosen at G5 determines what kinds of roys are attracted, what kinds of regulatory relationships are formed, and what the USB-C positioning (D20) ultimately means. This is L1 — the most powerful and least frequently changed leverage point.

### Ashby Requisite Variety — Where the Variety Budget Breaks

*(Book-as-frame: Ashby Introduction to Cybernetics)*

The L6 community system faces three requisite-variety breaks across the gate sequence:

**G1→G2 variety break.** Ruslan's individual variety as controller (ICP judgments, matchmaking, community quality) is sufficient for 5-20 members. At 50-200, the controller variety must expand. The compensating mechanism is the D22 written protocol + digital portrait template: these tools amplify Ruslan's control variety by encoding his judgment into a replicable filter. Without this amplification, Ruslan becomes the bottleneck (AP-SYS-10 Ashby variety mismatch applies).

**G3 variety break.** At 200-1000 members across multiple archetype sub-networks and a matchmaker platform, no single human controller has sufficient variety to maintain quality. The compensating mechanism is the L4 agent layer (6 ROY + 14 legacy) acting as a distributed controller with far higher variety than any individual. This is the gate at which agent-assisted moderation, portrait-completion enforcement, and match-quality review become load-bearing rather than optional.

**G5 variety break.** At 10,000+ members across global multi-language sub-networks and dozens of roys, even the agent layer + core team lacks sufficient variety. The compensating mechanism is the constitutional-amendment process (Beer VSM Level-5 function) and the Alliance methodology-parliament (VSM Level-4): distributed governance mechanisms that have variety proportional to the ecosystem's variety.

### Beer VSM — System Level Shifts Across Gates

*(Book-as-frame: Beer Brain of the Firm)*

| Gate | VSM shift |
|---|---|
| G1 | L1 operations only: Ruslan is simultaneously L1/L2/L3/L4/L5 — functional but not scalable |
| G2 | First separation: Ruslan begins delegating L1 (execution) to first hires; L2 coordination emerges |
| G3 | L3 audit/optimisation function emerges explicitly: matchmaker KPI tracking, portrait quality review, subscription churn analysis |
| G4 | L4 intelligence / futures function explicitly instantiated: Alliance methodology-parliament processes future-facing methodology updates; Ruslan lifts from L3 to pure L4/L5 |
| G5 | L5 identity / policy function distributed to constitutional governance: no single person holds L5; it is embedded in founding documents + amendment process |

The critical transition is G3→G4: this is where System-3 (operational coordination) must hand off to System-4 (intelligence / futures) as the primary governing mechanism. Missing this transition is the dominant failure mode for communities that plateau at 200-1000 members: they have good operations (L1/L2/L3) but no futures-intelligence function (L4) to adapt the methodology as the environment shifts.

### Senge "Limits to Growth" Archetype — Predictable Plateaus

*(Book-as-frame: Senge Fifth Discipline)*

The Limits to Growth archetype predicts that every reinforcing loop eventually encounters a balancing constraint. The L6 community system has three predictable plateaus:

**Plateau 1 — G1→G2 transition (~€50K).** Reinforcing loop: word-of-mouth referrals from early satisfied community members → more members → more value → more referrals. Balancing constraint: Ruslan's time for quality curation reaches ceiling (~10 connections/week manually manageable). Removal: D22 written protocol + first hire or contractor.

**Plateau 2 — G3 (~€1M).** Reinforcing loop: digital portrait graph grows → matchmaker precision increases → members see value → more portraits filled → more precision. Balancing constraint: portrait quality degrades as onboarding speed exceeds quality-enforcement speed (identified as G3 bottleneck above). Removal: portrait-completion as structural gate to premium features (Meadows L4 leverage point).

**Plateau 3 — G4 ($100M).** Reinforcing loop: Alliance members grow → methodology improves → more roys attracted → more Alliance members grow. Balancing constraint: canonical methodology drift from N diverging-interest roys competing to influence upstream. Removal: Alliance methodology-parliament with identity-preserving constitutional goals (Meadows L3/L1 leverage).

The Senge law most relevant here: *"The harder you push the system, the harder it pushes back."* Attempts to accelerate membership growth (more outreach, more channels, lower ICP filter threshold) without simultaneously removing each balancing constraint will hit each plateau harder and faster. The correct intervention is always to attack the *balancing constraint*, not to strengthen the reinforcing loop. This is why the §11.2 per-gate mitigations consistently focus on governance and structural design rather than marketing amplification.
