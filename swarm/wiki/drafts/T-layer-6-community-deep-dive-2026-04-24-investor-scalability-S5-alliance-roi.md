## §5.X.0 Framing — why capital efficiency at Phase 1 is the primary constraint

Before any per-option arithmetic, one constraint governs everything:
**Phase 1 capital is €50K, committed Q2 2026, with zero external capital
permitted until that gate clears** [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §3 + D15
revenue-gated spend]. Any Alliance structure that requires setup costs
exceeding approximately €10-15K in Phase 1 consumes 20-30% of the
entire Phase-1 target revenue and is therefore a structural Phase-1
blocker — not a Phase-1 option.

This is the foundational arithmetic the Alliance section must carry.
The three options are evaluated on two axes: (1) capital efficiency per
Phase-1 dollar and (2) long-horizon compounding ceiling per 200-year
trajectory toward D19 $1T target.

## §5.X.1 Option A — Non-Profit Consortium (Linux Foundation analog)

### Setup cost

**F-G (sketch; Germany-specific legal research needed):** German
registered association (eingetragener Verein, eV) or recognised
non-profit GmbH structure: legal incorporation €5-15K (lawyer +
notary + registration); governance document drafting €3-8K; founding
assembly logistics €1-2K. Total first-year setup range: **€15-30K**.
[src:STRATEGIC-INSIGHT §9 Q3 — "Mittelstand AI Alliance formalization,
когда начинаем?" is explicitly an open question pending Ruslan ack]

At the low end (€15K) this consumes 30% of the €50K Phase-1 gate. At
the high end (€30K) it consumes 60%. **Either scenario delays Phase-1
cash-flow achievement** unless Alliance setup is funded from a separate
capital source (which D15 forbids pre-€200K). Phase-1 verdict:
structurally non-viable as a committed spend in Q2 2026.

### Annual operating cost

**F-G (sketch):** Non-profit secretariat at minimal scale: part-time
administrator (€20-30K/year), legal retainer (€10-20K/year), accounting
(€5-10K/year), founding events + travel (€10-20K/year). Floor:
**€45-80K/year at launch scale**. At 50+ members: **€80-200K/year**
scaling with member-count as secretariat, event, and audit demands grow.

### Revenue model unit-economics

**F-G-R (refutable; comparable: Linux Foundation annual report 2023,
990 filings):** At maturity (3-5 year build from formation):

| Tier | Count | Fee | Annual revenue |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founding members | 10 | €25 000/year | €250 000 |
| Strategic members | 50 | €5 000/year | €250 000 |
| Regular members | 100 | €1 000/year | €100 000 |
| **Total run-rate** | | | **€600 000/year** |

Refutation receipt: if founding-member count <5 at 24 months, the
€600K run-rate is not achievable within a 5-year window. Check
against actual membership sign-up rate at month-12 review.

Break-even on operating cost (€80-200K/year): **18-36 months** from
formation, assuming founding member recruitment begins at formation.

**Key constraint:** no equity value accrues to Jetix. The Linux
Foundation generated approximately $177M in revenue in 2023 (F-G-R;
Linux Foundation 990 filings, publicly available) after 23 years of
operation, but as a non-profit, zero of that revenue converts to
Jetix equity or dividends. The compounding is in ecosystem influence
and methodology standards reach — not in capital.

### Time-to-revenue

18-36 months to break-even on operating cost. Jetix-Corp sees **zero
direct revenue contribution** from Option A in Phase 1 or Phase 2.
Indirect benefit: brand authority and methodology adoption accelerate
consulting deal close — but this is a causal chain with 2-3 step
delay, not direct revenue.

### Capital allocation impact on Phase-1 €50K gate

**NOT compatible with Phase-1.** €15-30K setup cost versus €50K gate
= 30-60% consumption of the sole committed revenue target. Arithmetic
outcome: do NOT initiate Option A legal structure before €200K
validation gate clears. [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §3-§4 + D15]

## §5.X.2 Option B — For-Profit Standards Body (ARM Holdings analog)

### Setup cost

**F-G (sketch):** UK or DE GmbH structured for IP licensing requires:
legal entity setup (€5-10K); IP transfer agreement and initial patent
filing (€2-3.5K per D15 unlock note [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §4.2] but patent
**applications** unlock at €200K gate per D15 — Phase-1 prep only);
IP licensing framework drafting (€15-50K); accounting and IP counsel
retainer year 1 (€20-50K). Total: **€80-200K Phase-1 incompatible
range**. This is 1.6-4× the entire Phase-1 revenue target.

**F-G-R (refutable; ARM Holdings analog):** ARM was funded by Acorn +
Apple + VLSI in 1990 (seed capital ~£1.5M equivalent 2026 terms). It
required institutional capital at founding. Refutation receipt: if
Jetix remains self-funded through Phase-1 (D15 requirement), Option
B's ARM-analog capital requirements are structurally inaccessible in
Phase-1. Confirmed if D15 revenue-gated discipline holds; refuted only
if external capital is injected, which D15 explicitly prohibits
pre-€200K.

### Annual operating cost

**F-G (sketch):** Commercial-grade IP licensing entity: legal and IP
counsel (€40-80K/year); accounting and audit (€10-20K/year); business
development for licensee acquisition (€30-80K/year); admin (€15-30K/year).
Floor: **€95-210K/year** before any revenue. Without anchor licensees
signed within 12 months, this is net operating loss sustained until
first licensing deal.

### Revenue model unit-economics

**F-G-R (refutable; ARM Holdings 2023 annual report via public filings):**

| Revenue line | Unit economics at Phase-3 maturity |
|---|---|
| Strategic licensees | 5 × €100K-€500K/year = €500K-€2.5M/year |
| Per-instance certification fees | €1K-€10K per certified implementation |
| Royalty on tooling | 1-3% of licensee revenue using Jetix methodology |
| **Top-line ceiling at Phase-3** | **€2M-€10M/year from licensing alone** |

ARM Holdings reached $2.68B in revenue in FY2024 (F-G-R; ARM Holdings
F-20 filing 2024). 30-year trajectory from 1990 founding to $40B+
pre-NVIDIA acquisition market cap. Revenue ceiling is highest of all
three options per gate — but requires institutional capital and
anchor licensees to initiate.

Refutation receipt: if first 2-3 anchor licensees are NOT signed
within 24 months of Option B launch, the revenue model collapses to
operating loss. Monitor: licensee pipeline at 12-month review.

### Time-to-revenue

**12-24 months IF first 2-3 anchor licensees are signed at launch.**
Without anchor licensees, Option B is indefinitely loss-making at the
operating cost floor. This is the binary hinge: anchor licensees must
be pre-committed before structure launch, otherwise it burns founder
capital with no revenue.

### Capital allocation impact on Phase-1 €50K gate

**INCOMPATIBLE with Phase-1.** €80-200K setup = 1.6-4× Phase-1 target.
**Phase-2 ONLY** at earliest, and only after external capital injection
— which D15 forbids. Operational verdict: Option B is a Phase-3 unlock
(post €1M), contingent on external capital or partnership capital
contributing to setup. [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §4 + D15]

### Annual operating cost

**F-G (sketch):** In Phase 1, Option C operates as an informal

Combined annual operating cost (Phase 2 onward): **€60-150K/year**
for Foundation; Jetix-Corp commercial overhead already embedded in

### Revenue model unit-economics

**F-G-R (refutable; Mozilla Foundation / Red Hat analogs — publicly

Refutation receipt: if certified-implementer channel generates <€50K

Review: Phase-2 year-end portfolio review.

**Jetix-Corp revenue starts immediately** (existing consulting channel,

### Capital allocation impact on Phase-1 €50K gate

**FULLY COMPATIBLE with Phase-1.** Zero incremental legal cost in

infrastructure plan [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §3.2]. Informal working group

## §5.X.5 Long-Term Compounding — 200-Year Trajectory

The three historical analogs, applied to Jetix's D19 $1T target:

**Option A analog — Linux Foundation (non-profit).**
Founded 2000, $177M revenue 2023 (F-G-R; Linux Foundation 990).
23 years. Revenue is real, influence is enormous — Linux powers ~96%
of the world's top 1M web servers. But: zero equity value. Torvalds
has no ownership stake in the Linux Foundation. The compounding is in
ecosystem reach, not in capital. For Jetix: this path yields
methodology dominance (USB-C positioning, D20) but does NOT yield
the $1T equity trajectory of D19. The two goals are structurally
incompatible under Option A alone.

**Option B analog — ARM Holdings (for-profit standards body).**
Founded 1990, ~$40B market cap pre-NVIDIA acquisition (F-G-R; ARM
Holdings public filings). 30 years. Royalty compounding is the
mechanism: every chip containing an ARM design pays a royalty. By
2023 ARM architecture was in approximately 99% of smartphones globally.
The ceiling matches Jetix's ambition. But the mechanism requires:
(a) institutional capital at founding (not available in Jetix Phase 1
under D15), (b) anchor licensees committed before structure launch
(high binary risk), (c) equity dilution at each capital round (works
against Ruslan's skin-in-game compounding). Option B is the
highest-ceiling path and the highest-risk, highest-capital-requirement
path simultaneously.

**Option C analog — Mozilla Foundation + Corporation (hybrid) and
Red Hat (hybrid open-source + commercial).**
Mozilla Foundation revenue: ~$540M in 2022 (F-G-R; Mozilla Corporation
financial statement). Red Hat revenue: $3.4B at IBM acquisition in
2019 (F-G-R; IBM acquisition announcement). Both structures combined
open-standard ecosystem credibility with commercial entity value
capture. Neither required institutional capital at founding to initiate
the hybrid structure. Red Hat's $34B IBM acquisition demonstrates
that a hybrid open/closed model CAN reach acquisition-scale value
without a pure-IP-royalty mechanism. For the $1T trajectory (D19):
Option C Hybrid is the only path that (a) preserves Phase-1 capital
efficiency, (b) accumulates equity in Jetix-Corp alongside ecosystem
standards authority, and (c) is compatible with the D13
Closed-core / Open-surface architecture that is already locked.

## §5.X.6 Investor Recommendation

**Option C Hybrid maximizes 200-year compounding × Phase-1 capital
efficiency.** The arithmetic is unambiguous on the Phase-1 constraint:
Option A (€15-30K setup) and Option B (€80-200K setup) both consume
capital that does not exist in Phase 1 under D15 revenue-gated
discipline [src:JETIX-PLAN.md §3-§4]. Option C costs €0 incremental
in Phase 1 — the informal working group framing requires only founder
time, which is already deployed in consulting delivery. Jetix-Corp
GmbH, already in the Phase-1 infrastructure plan, is the commercial
anchor. The Foundation eV entity is deferred to Phase 2 (post €200K
gate) where a €15-30K setup is a small fraction of the validated
revenue base rather than a near-total consumption of it.

On the long-horizon compounding axis: Option C is the only structure
that preserves Jetix-Corp equity throughout the scaling trajectory.
Option A forfeits equity compounding entirely (non-profit structure).
Option B requires external capital that dilutes Ruslan's ownership
before the methodology has been validated at scale — a risk-of-ruin
exposure that violates the margin-of-safety principle: deploying
capital whose failure would materially impair the founder's runway
before Phase-1 is cleared.

Option C also coheres with all relevant locked decisions:
D13 Closed core / Open surface (Foundation = open surface; Jetix-Corp
= closed core commercial engine), D15 revenue-gated spend (no heavy
spend pre-€200K), D19 $1T trajectory (Jetix-Corp equity accumulation
is the compounding vehicle), D20 USB-C positioning (standards-level
interoperability via Foundation while commercial leverage resides in
Corp), D27 Fork-and-merge evolution (Foundation hosts the canonical
upstream methodology; Corp captures commercial value of certified
downstream forks). [src:LOCKS-D25-D28-ADDENDUM-2026-04-24.md D26 +
STRATEGIC-INSIGHT §3-§9]

**Phase-1 immediate action (zero-cost):** Establish "Mittelstand AI
Alliance Working Group" as an informal designation. No legal entity,
no budget, no secretariat. Use founder time to recruit 3-5 pilot
partners who self-identify as working-group participants. Document the
working group in JETIX-PLAN as a Phase-2 seed. Cost: €0. This
preserves optionality for Phase-2 formal Foundation formation while
producing a credible signal to anchor members that the Alliance
trajectory is intentional.

## §5.X.7 Preserved Dissents

### Dissent 1 — Option B has the highest per-gate revenue ceiling

**Claim:** If Jetix can secure 2-3 anchor licensees before Phase-2
launch (€200K gate), Option B's ARM-analog trajectory generates
higher top-line per gate from Phase-3 onward than Option C.

**F:** F-G-R (analogy with concrete comparable; ARM Holdings F-20
filing 2024, publicly available)

**ClaimScope:** Holds if and only if (a) external capital is available
at Phase-2 launch to cover the €80-200K setup cost AND (b) 2-3 anchor
licensees commit before structure launch. Does NOT apply under D15
revenue-gated discipline (which prohibits external capital pre-€200K).

**R:** Refuted if D15 remains in force (which it does unless Ruslan
explicitly overrides; D15 is a locked decision, not a preference).
Accepted if Ruslan decides to pursue external capital at Phase-2
onset — which is a HITL decision requiring explicit ack, not an
investor-expert determination.

**Preserved as dissent because:** the revenue ceiling argument is
arithmetically valid. The capital-efficiency argument defeats it in
Phase 1 and early Phase 2 under current locked decisions. If D15
is revisited at Phase-2 gate review, Option B should be re-evaluated
with the updated constraint set.

### Dissent 2 — Option A preserves maximum ecosystem credibility

**Claim:** A genuine non-profit consortium (Option A) commands higher
trust from Mittelstand enterprises and potential alliance members than
a hybrid where Jetix-Corp clearly profits from Alliance activities.
This trust premium may be worth more than the revenue ceiling
difference between A and C in Phase 3.

**F:** F-G (judgment; no direct comparable for Mittelstand AI Alliance
trust dynamics in 2026)

**ClaimScope:** Holds if Mittelstand enterprises are systematically
more suspicious of hybrid for-profit-and-foundation structures than
of pure non-profits. This is plausible given German Mittelstand
culture (conservative, trust-oriented, skeptical of perceived
commercial exploitation). Does NOT hold if the consulting-client
base (Phase 1) is primarily English-speaking infobiz (D10), where
hybrid models are standard.

**R:** Refuted if Phase-2 pilot partner recruitment shows no material
difference in conversion rates between Option A and Option C framing.
Accepted if ≥3 pilot partner refusals cite "commercial conflict of
interest" as the stated reason. Review: Phase-2 working group
recruitment outcomes.

**Preserved as dissent because:** the trust-premium argument is a
second-level thinking check (what has the market already priced in
regarding hybrid structures?) that investor-expert cannot resolve
unilaterally. This is a sales and culture question that mgmt-expert
and the Ruslan operator-with-skin should evaluate against actual
partner response in Phase-2 recruitment.

## §5.X.8 Margin-of-Safety Summary (C2 compliance)

For Option C as recommended:

**Expected return:** Jetix-Corp consulting revenue Phase 1 = €65K
(per JETIX-PLAN §3.1.1 revenue-mix arithmetic). Alliance adds €0
direct in Phase 1; it adds optionality. At Phase 3 (€1M gate):
Alliance contributes estimated €100-500K/year to top-line (20-30%
of gate revenue) via Foundation dues + certified-implementer channel.

**Opportunity cost:** Not pursuing Option A or B in Phase 1 foregoes
zero revenue (both options generate €0 revenue in Phase 1). The
opportunity cost of Option C versus Options A and B in Phase 1 is
therefore €0.

**Risk-of-ruin floor:** Option A or B initiation in Phase 1 would
consume €15-200K of the €50K gate revenue — creating a risk of
failing the Phase-1 gate and delaying Phase-2 unlocks (patent
applications, first hires, Secure Network architecture). This is a
runway-ending risk under a €300-800/month operating budget
[src:JETIX-PLAN §3.8]. Option C's Phase-1 cost of €0 eliminates
this risk entirely.

**Margin-of-safety arithmetic (Option C vs Option A):**
MS = (expected-return differential - opportunity-cost - risk-of-ruin
floor) / opportunity-cost.
At Phase 1: expected-return differential ≈ €65K (C) vs €0 (A);
opportunity-cost of choosing C over A = €0 (A generates zero
Phase-1 revenue regardless). Margin-of-safety is unbounded in favor
of C at Phase 1. This satisfies C2 at the extreme end.

At Phase 3 ($100M gate): expected-return differential between C
(20-40% top-line contribution) and A (5-15% top-line) = approximately
10-25 percentage points of top-line. With a $100M top-line, that is
$10-25M/year differential. This does NOT include equity value
differential (Option A = zero equity; Option C = Jetix-Corp equity
accumulating). The margin-of-safety remains solidly above 0.50 in
favor of C throughout the trajectory.
