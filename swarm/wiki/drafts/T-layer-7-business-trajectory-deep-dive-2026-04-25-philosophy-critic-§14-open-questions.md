## §14.0 Framing: epistemic-honesty register

This section consolidates open questions and preserved dissents that surfaced across §2-§13. Its function is **epistemic**, not strategic: it records the claims-that-remain-contested, their F-level (Formality), their ClaimScope (where they hold and where they do not), and their R (Refutation receipt — what observation would falsify them and where to verify).

Three categories are distinguished:

1. **Tier-1 BLOCKING** — must resolve before Phase 3 canonical documents are writable. These are not optional backlog items; they gate the validity of the canonical document synthesis. Four questions: Q-L7-01 through Q-L7-04.

2. **Tier-2 PHASE-2+ BACKLOG** — open but non-blocking. Decision can be deferred to Phase-2+ planning without harming Phase-1 operations. Four questions: Q-L7-05 through Q-L7-08.

3. **Preserved dissents** — real disagreements between cell outputs that must NOT be averaged or collapsed. They remain as (F, ClaimScope, R) tagged pairs in the canonical document until HITL resolves them.

**F-G-R rubric (applied here, reminder in §14.5):**
- F = Formality level of the claim (F0 rumour → F9 formal proof; Phase-A modal = F3-F4)
- ClaimScope = bounded context (holds_when / not_valid_when)
- R = refuted_if + receipt (where to verify) + accepted_if

Epistemic wall (FPF L1003-1006): this section records epistemic status only. What to DO with the questions is instrumental Рациональность — that belongs to Ruslan + investor-expert.
