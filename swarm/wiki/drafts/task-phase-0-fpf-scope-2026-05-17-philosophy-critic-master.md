## §0 TL;DR + Epistemic Posture

**The four Tasks produced an honest, granular inventory.** The epistemic discipline
is largely intact at the cell-draft level. The risk is in the master document — when
the narrative compresses Tasks 1-4 into ≤2000 words for L1 consumption, the five
patterns below re-emerge automatically unless pre-empted by explicit disclosure gates.

**Five patterns that must be pre-empted in the master:**

- **EP-1.** Artefact-system gap: «LOCKED» means document approval (A.16), not operational
  instantiation (A.4). 10 of 14 objects carry this dual face. Master prose must split
  artefact claims from operational claims per object.
- **EP-2.** Use-mention drift: Workshop / Clan / «meta-workshop» names float from
  mentioned-as-frame to used-as-structural-claim without declaration. Master must
  mark each metaphor USE or MENTION explicitly on first occurrence.
- **EP-3.** Role/executor conflation: «12 agents» in CLAUDE.md = 12 declared role-types
  (Phase 1-4 schedule), not 12 running processes. IP-1 applies. Master prose must
  use «12 role-type declarations» not «12 agents.»
- **EP-4.** Promise without commitment machinery: TRM, R12, Clan Charter all carry
  promise-shaped language without U.Commitment (A.2.8) adjudication hooks. Master
  should label these U.PromiseContent, not claims of operational commitments.
- **EP-5.** F-G-R applied to wrong subject: Jetix «F8» = approval-grade via 8 single-
  author RUSLAN-ACKs, not FPF B.3 F8 (independent verification). Master must
  carry a one-line disclosure note wherever F8 is cited. CR-07 from Task 4 confirms
  this as critical for L1 audience.

**Honest framing that must be maintained** (per 06-honest-self-audit §12):
~27 components are memory and automation; ~12 are FPF-derivative intelligence
amplification. This split must survive compression. «Jetix implements FPF» is
an overstatement. «Jetix tactically adopts FPF primitives in ~12 of ~27 components»
is defensible.
