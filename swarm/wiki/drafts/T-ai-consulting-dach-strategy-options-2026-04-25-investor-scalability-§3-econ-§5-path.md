### H1 Economics — DACH Manufacturing Mittelstand

**Target customer:** Mittelstand manufacturing owner-operator, €5M–€50M revenue, DACH region, 10-500 employees. GDPR-strict. Has IT infrastructure. Decision-maker = Geschäftsführer with P&L authority. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§5.6]

**Pricing tier deployed (per L7 §2.1):**
- Productized engagement floor: €5,000 (20-30% case-study discount on first 2-3 contracts → €3,500–€4,000)
- Productized standard: €7,500–€10,000 (full pricing after first 3 case-study clients)
- Path B default: €3K onboarding + €15K/yr license = €18K yr1 total
- Monthly retainer (Phase-1): €1,500–€2,000/mo
- Hourly: €150/hr (complex task execution, discovery phase)

**Estimated CAC (Phase-1 solo-Ruslan):**
- Outreach hours per closed deal:
  - Cold LinkedIn/email (DACH manufacturing, German language required for senior management): 15–20 hrs/lead × 5–8% response → 18 hrs avg × €150 = €2,700 per closed client at 7% conversion
  - Warm referral (Verband / IHK event contact): 5–8 hrs per close × €150 = €750–€1,200
  - Mittelstand sales cycle is 8–16 weeks (longer than Startupper); this extends outreach-to-close horizon and inflates time-CAC
- Direct € cost: LinkedIn Premium (~€55/mo), Apollo or equivalent DACH email finder (~€80/mo), IHK Verband event attendance €200–€500/event
- Imputed CAC at cold outreach (weighted, 70% cold / 30% warm):
  - Cold: €2,700 × 0.70 = €1,890
  - Warm: €1,000 avg × 0.30 = €300
  - **Weighted CAC: ≈€2,190**

**Estimated LTV (over 2–3 year engagement):**
- Initial engagement: €7,500 (productized, mid)
- Year-1 Path B license renewal probability: 60–70% at month 12 (manufacturing companies rarely replace working infrastructure if ROI demonstrated); renewal = €15K/yr
- Year-2 renewal: 50% probability (cumulative)
- Referral: 0.5 avg additional clients in same Verband / regional network × €7,500
- LTV calculation (2-year mid-case):
  - Yr1: €7,500 engagement + €3,750 renewal (partial yr)
  - Yr2: €15,000 license × 50% renewal = €7,500
  - Referral: 0.5 × €7,500 = €3,750
  - **LTV total mid-estimate: ≈€22,500**

**F-G-R on H1 LTV:**
- F: F3 (arithmetic; renewal rates = Phase-A estimate based on SaaS/methodology license analogical base rates; DACH Mittelstand loyalty is generally higher than EN infobiz, but zero empirical data)
- ClaimScope: manufacturing Mittelstand €5M-€50M, Path-B delivery; does NOT apply if Path-C trigger fires (enterprise override) — in that case LTV rises to €30K-€45K yr1 alone
- R: refuted if first manufacturing client does not renew at 12 months; accepted if ≥50% of manufacturing clients renew Path-B license after yr1

**Payback (months):** Initial engagement €7,500 with 50% prepaid at signing = €3,750 cash M0. Full payback ≈ 1.5 months from signing.

**LTV:CAC ratio:** €22,500 / €2,190 = **10.3:1** (cold-weighted). Warm referral only: €22,500 / €750 = **30:1**.

**GM% Phase-A:**
- Sub-A productized (30 hrs Ruslan at €150 COGS + €150 tooling = €4,650 COGS on €7,500 revenue): **38%** — Phase-A flagged below D18 70% floor per L7 §3.1. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§3.1]
- Path B methodology license yr1 (€18K total, ~€5,250 COGS): **70.7%** — above floor.
- The critical distinction: if H1 is selected and Path-B methodology-kit delivery is chosen over full-service consulting delivery, GM flips from 38% (Sub-A) to 70.7% (Path-B license). This is the D18 productization path argument in arithmetic form.

**Brigadier proposed rating (1-10):** 7 — PLACEHOLDER. Reasoning: if H1 selected, strong LTV:CAC (10:1 cold) + high renewal probability in loyal Mittelstand manufacturing culture + GDPR moat (local-first positioning is a direct compliance answer) + Path-B GM structurally sound. Constraints: longer sales cycle 8-16 weeks inflates time-to-first-revenue; German language outreach requirement raises cold-outreach friction; IHK/Verband events require physical Berlin-or-regional presence.

**Sensitivity flags:**
- If Mittelstand budget compression (R-5 AI-adoption window closing 2026-2028 per L7 §11 R-5): manufacturing AI-automation spend may shift toward specialized MES/ERP AI vendors by 2028, narrowing Jetix's consulting window; CAC could inflate 30-50% if competition for same decision-maker intensifies. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§11 R-5]
- If Path-C trigger fires earlier than expected (client >€10M revenue + GDPR-audit demand): GM drops from 70.7% Path-B to 47-50% Path-C but revenue per engagement rises to €30K-€45K/yr, improving LTV:CAC substantially. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.3.2]
- If manufacturing client is knowledge-worker-light (<20% of revenue in knowledge roles): the 30-90 day ROI demonstration required for deal renewal weakens; renewal probability drops to 40-50% from 60-70%.
- R-7 concentration risk: a single €18K/yr manufacturing client represents ~36% of CC-1 €50K gate target; losing that client after M3 cash-event = immediate R-1 materialization. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§11 R-7]

### H2 Economics — DACH Professional Services Boutiques

**Target customer:** Law firms, accounting firms (Steuerberater), management consultancies, medical practices, financial advisory boutiques with 5-50 employees, DACH region. Confidential-data-first. Personal secrecy obligations make cloud AI legally impossible without on-prem solution.

**Pricing tier deployed (per L7 §2.1):**
- Productized engagement floor: €5,000
- Productized standard: €7,500–€10,000
- Path B default: €3K onboarding + €15K/yr = €18K yr1
- Hourly: €150/hr for complex task execution (premium compliance advice)
- Retainer Phase-1: €1,500–€2,000/mo

**Estimated CAC (Phase-1 solo-Ruslan):**
- Professional services boutiques have higher warm-intro conversion than manufacturing; many partner-level professionals are on LinkedIn but respond better to referral from a mutual (lawyer → lawyer; accountant → accountant). Estimated distribution: 50% warm-intro, 50% cold.
- Cold outreach (email preferred for professional services, not DM): 12 hrs × €150 / 6% reply rate = €3,000 cold CAC (higher than manufacturing because email-first channel has lower conversion than LinkedIn DM for this archetype)
- Warm intro: 4 hrs × €150 = €600
- **Weighted CAC: 50/50 → ≈€1,800**
- Direct €: Apollo / Hunter.io €80/mo + potential referral partner rev-share (Steuerberater, lawyer) = 10-15% of first engagement; if rev-share = 10% × €7,500 = €750 → weighted-in CAC ≈€2,100 with partner-channel overhead

**Estimated LTV (over 2–3 year engagement):**
- Initial engagement: €7,500 mid
- Professional services firms have HIGH renewal probability (IT infrastructure decisions are stable; once a law firm installs client-private KB, replacing it is a major disruption; estimated renewal 70-80% yr1→yr2)
- Referral coefficient: professional services networks are tight (bar association networks, DATEV/Steuerberater Kammer); referral multiplier potentially 0.7–1.0× (higher than manufacturing)
- LTV calculation (2-year):
  - Yr1: €7,500 + renewal at €15K × 75% = €11,250
  - Yr2: €15K × 60% = €9,000
  - Referral: 0.75 × €7,500 = €5,625
  - **LTV total mid-estimate: ≈€25,875** (higher than H1 due to stronger referral network)

**F-G-R on H2 LTV:**
- F: F3 (arithmetic; professional services renewal rates analogical from legal tech/practice management software; DATEV ecosystem loyalty in DACH accounting = industry-known but not formally validated for Jetix)
- ClaimScope: boutique professional services 5-50 employees, DACH; does NOT apply to large law firms (BigLaw) who have procurement departments and require formal RFP
- R: refuted if first professional services client does not renew after yr1; accepted if ≥70% of professional services clients renew Path-B license

**Payback:** 1.5 months (same structure as H1; 50% prepaid).

**LTV:CAC ratio:** €25,875 / €1,800 = **14.4:1** (cold/warm blended). With partner-channel overhead: €25,875 / €2,100 = **12.3:1**.

**GM% Phase-A:** Same Sub-A structure: 38% at productized consulting delivery; 70.7% at Path-B license. Professional services boutique is high-fit for Path-B (they have IT staff or can manage setup with a single IT contact; they do NOT want Jetix-hosted access to their confidential client files, which structurally prevents Path-A and limits Path-C).

**Brigadier proposed rating (1-10):** 8 — PLACEHOLDER. Reasoning: if H2 selected, highest LTV:CAC of the 4-5 hypotheses (12-14:1 blended), high renewal probability (data sovereignty is structurally mandatory not optional), tight referral network (bar association / DATEV ecosystem multiplies reach), Path-B is the natural default (client-private is non-negotiable for professional secrecy obligations). Constraint: professional services sales cycle is 6-12 weeks; law-firm decision-makers are conservative and require trust-building; cold outreach hits lower response rates than Startupper segment.

**Sensitivity flags:**
- Partner-channel dependency: if Steuerberater / lawyer rev-share partners are secured, CAC drops to ~€600-€800 for warm referrals through that network; this could make H2 the highest-ROI acquisition channel per hour invested. If partner channel does not materialize in Phase-1, cold email CAC of €3,000 makes per-deal economics tighter.
- German language proficiency: law firm partners and Steuerberater communicate in German; cold email in English will substantially lower response rates. Native-review caveat per D10 applies.
- EU AI Act liability exposure: professional services firms face emerging liability for AI-assisted client advice. Path-B (client owns the AI) is a legal liability shield; this is a structural advantage for Jetix positioning that competitors hosting data for clients cannot replicate.

### H3 Economics — DACH B2B SaaS Startupper

**Target customer:** English-speaking digital business founder, $100K–$500K/yr revenue, already AI-literate but operating without a compounding knowledge system. Decision-maker = the founder themselves. Faster sales cycle than Mittelstand (days-weeks not months).

**Pricing tier deployed (per L7 §2.1 + §5.3 Startupper floor):**
- Entry: Starter package €500–€800 (lead-in; optional)
- Productized engagement: €2,500–€5,000 (Startupper tier below Mittelstand floor; per L7 §5.3 $100K company → $5K engagement = 5% of revenue = feasible)
- Path B license variant for Startupper: lighter deployment at €1,500–€3,000 onboarding + €6,000–€10,000/yr = €7,500–€13,000 yr1 (scaled down from Mittelstand Path-B pricing; rationale: Startupper company revenue 5-10× lower than Mittelstand)
- Retainer: €1,000–€2,000/mo
- Hourly: €150/hr

**F-G-R on Startupper Path-B pricing variant:**
- F: F4 (analogical scaling from L7 §2.1 Mittelstand pricing; Startupper revenue 5-10× lower → proportional reduction; no separate empirical anchor)
- ClaimScope: Startupper $100K-$500K/yr revenue; does NOT apply to Mittelstand or high-earner archetype
- R: refuted if first Startupper rejects €3K onboarding + €6K/yr as overpriced and closes only at <€5K total yr1; accepted if first Startupper closes at ≥€5K total yr1 commitment

**Estimated CAC:**
- English LinkedIn DM + after-comment: highest response rate of all segments (5-15% cold, 15-25% after prior engagement per L6 §7.1). [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§7.1]
- Outreach hrs: 8–10 hrs/lead at 12% avg response → close rate from response = 25% (motivated buyer, short sales cycle) → 8.5 hrs × €150 / 12% × 25% = **€1,417 per closed client (cold)**
- Warm intro: 3 hrs × €150 = €450
- **Weighted CAC (60% cold / 40% warm): ≈€1,030**
- Direct €: LinkedIn Premium €55/mo (shared across all segments)

**Estimated LTV:**
- Initial engagement: €3,750 mid (between €2,500 and €5,000 Startupper tier)
- Renewal probability: 50–60% (Startuppers are higher churn than Mittelstand; they are more price-sensitive and more likely to try to self-implement after initial engagement)
- Referral: strong (Startupper communities on Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Telegram; referred clients = 0.8–1.2× per closed Startupper) → referral value = 1.0 × €3,750 = €3,750
- LTV calculation (18-month typical retention):
  - Initial: €3,750
  - Renewal 12-month license: €6,000–€8,000 × 55% = €3,850
  - Referral: 1.0 × €3,750 = €3,750
  - **LTV total mid-estimate: ≈€11,350**

**F-G-R on H3 LTV:**
- F: F3 (arithmetic model; Startupper churn benchmarks from infobiz/digital coaching industry; referral coefficient from L6 community data; not Jetix empirical)
- ClaimScope: English-speaking Startupper $100K-$500K/yr revenue; fails for SaaS startups with VC funding where procurement cycles differ
- R: refuted if Startupper segment shows LTV < €5,000 avg after first 5 clients (single-engagement pattern, no renewals, no referrals); accepted if ≥40% of Startupper clients renew within 18 months

**Payback:** 1–2 months (faster close + prepaid typical for digital buyers).

**LTV:CAC ratio:** €11,350 / €1,030 = **11:1** (blended cold/warm). Warm referral only: €11,350 / €450 = **25:1**.

**GM% Phase-A:** 38% at Sub-A delivery; at Path-B Startupper variant (lighter onboarding, fewer delivery hours), estimated GM rises to 55-65% if onboarding requires 10 hrs Ruslan at €1,500 COGS on €3,000 revenue (50% GM). Still below D18 70% floor; G2 decoupling path applies.

**Brigadier proposed rating (1-10):** 7 — PLACEHOLDER. Reasoning: if H3 selected, fastest sales cycle (days-weeks vs months), English-language channel is Ruslan's strongest, high referral coefficient in Startupper communities, good LTV:CAC at 11:1. Constraints: lower ticket size (€3,750 avg vs €7,500 Mittelstand), higher churn risk, R-7 concentration risk lower (more clients at smaller ticket = natural diversification, which is a positive).

**Sensitivity flags:**
- If DACH B2B SaaS context means German-language operations (DACH startup doing German market): sales cycle lengthens, channel shifts from English LinkedIn DM to DE channels, CAC inflates toward H1/H2 territory. The H3 economics assume English-language outreach to EN-market Startupper regardless of DACH geography.
- Startupper segment has highest price sensitivity; if Jetix case studies are not available (first 3 clients), conversion rate from discovery to close may be 10-15% lower, inflating CAC toward €1,500-€1,800.
- Path-A Phase-1 light variant for Startupper ($50-500K founders): see §5 for feasibility analysis.

### H4 Economics — DACH Specialty Mittelstand Vertical (e.g., Medizintechnik / Sustainable Manufacturing)

**Target customer:** Mittelstand operator in a compliance-heavy specialty vertical — Medizintechnik (medical devices, €5M-€50M revenue, BfArM/MDR regulated), sustainable manufacturing (ESG reporting obligations, supply chain documentation), or precision engineering (DIN/ISO standards documentation-heavy). These verticals have compounding documentation burdens that AI-augmented KB directly solves.

**Pricing tier deployed:**
- Discovery: €300 + compliance assessment add-on: +€500–€1,000 (specialty vertical justifies premium discovery)
- Productized engagement: €7,500–€12,000 (compliance-documentation complexity warrants premium vs standard Mittelstand)
- Path B license: €3K onboarding + €15K–€20K/yr = €18K–€23K yr1 (MDR/compliance documentation add-on justifies higher license)
- Path C trigger probability: higher than general manufacturing (Medizintechnik frequently has ISO 13485 requirements that co-trigger GDPR-audit; Path-C becomes likely default for medical device companies >€10M revenue)
- Retainer: €2,000–€3,000/mo (premium for ongoing compliance documentation support)

**Estimated CAC:**
- Specialty verticals have dedicated Verbände (Spectaris for Medizintechnik; VDMA for mechanical engineering) with targeted events. Warm intro via Verband contact = CAC similar to H1 warm route.
- Cold outreach: 14 hrs × €150 / 6% conversion = €3,500 per closed client (higher than general Mittelstand due to specialized pre-qualification required)
- Warm / Verband intro: 6 hrs × €150 = €900
- **Weighted CAC (65% cold / 35% warm): ≈€2,590**
- Direct €: Spectaris/VDMA membership or event attendance €300–€800/event, twice annually = €600–€1,600/yr

**Estimated LTV:**
- Initial engagement: €9,750 mid (premium over general Mittelstand)
- Renewal: very high (70-80%); specialty documentation systems are deeply embedded; Medizintechnik companies live and die by their documentation; replacing Jetix KB = MDR audit disruption → strong switching cost moat
- Referral: specialty verticals have tight networks (Spectaris conferences, Munich Medizintechnik cluster) → referral coefficient 0.6–0.8×
- LTV calculation:
  - Yr1: €9,750 initial + renewal €20K × 75% = €15,000
  - Yr2: €20K × 65% = €13,000
  - Referral: 0.7 × €9,750 = €6,825
  - **LTV total mid-estimate: ≈€34,825**

**F-G-R on H4 LTV:**
- F: F3 (arithmetic; MDR documentation burden = structural anchor for renewal; specialty vertical switching cost = judgment-based; referral coefficient = estimate from industry conference dynamics)
- ClaimScope: Medizintechnik or compliance-heavy manufacturing, DACH, €5M-€50M; fails for verticals without mandatory documentation burden (e.g., retail)
- R: refuted if Medizintechnik client's compliance team decides to manage KB themselves without Jetix support post-onboarding; accepted if 70%+ of specialty-vertical clients renew yr2

**Payback:** 1.5 months (same structure; higher engagement size = faster absolute payback).

**LTV:CAC ratio:** €34,825 / €2,590 = **13.4:1** (blended). Warm: €34,825 / €900 = **38.7:1**.

**GM% Phase-A:** 38% Sub-A delivery; Path-B at 70.7%; premium engagement (€9,750 with same ~€4,650 COGS estimate) = **52% GM at productized level** — closer to D18 70% target than standard Mittelstand because revenue is higher with similar delivery hour structure.

**Brigadier proposed rating (1-10):** 8 — PLACEHOLDER. Reasoning: if H4 selected, highest LTV of the 4 primary hypotheses (€34K vs €22.5K H1), strongest switching-cost moat (MDR compliance documentation), premium pricing justifiable, good LTV:CAC. Constraints: highest CAC among hypotheses (€2,590 blended), specialized vocabulary required (Ruslan needs Medizintechnik-specific positioning content), smaller addressable market than general Mittelstand. Verband membership investment required.

**Sensitivity flags:**
- If Path-C trigger fires for Medizintechnik clients >€10M revenue: GM drops to 47-50% but revenue jumps to €30K-€45K/yr. If 50% of H4 clients trigger Path-C, weighted GM = 0.5 × 70.7% + 0.5 × 48.5% = 59.6% — still above 30% floor, and below 70% is acceptable with higher absolute profit.
- R-5 AI-adoption window applies: medical device companies are early movers in AI-augmented documentation; a 2-year advantage window (2026-2028) before specialized MDR-AI tools emerge from established vendors (SAP, IBM, Oracle compliance modules).
- Language: Medizintechnik leadership communicates in German; all outreach templates must pass native-review per D10.

### §5.4 Path-C Trigger Conditions — Empirical Phase-1

Per L7 §2.3.2, Path-C fires when: client revenue >€30K one-time budget AND requires GDPR-audit trail in contract. [src:decisions/LAYER-7-BUSINESS-TRAJECTORY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.3.2]

Empirical question for Phase-1: should Jetix proactively offer Path-C to qualifying enterprise prospects, or wait for explicit GDPR-audit demand?

**Option A — Reactive Path-C (wait for client to demand):** Lower CAC (no extra explanation overhead); risk = enterprise client who needs Path-C discovers Jetix offers only Path-B → disqualified at later stage (higher sunk cost on deal).

**Option B — Proactive Path-C qualifier in discovery call:** Add one pre-qualification question: "Do you have formal GDPR compliance or audit-trail requirements for your software vendors?" If yes + company revenue suggests >€30K budget → propose Path-C upfront. This reduces late-stage deal death but adds early conversation complexity.

**Investor-expert arithmetic:** If 30% of enterprise-tier Mittelstand prospects trigger Path-C proactively and the average Path-C deal is €37.5K vs €18K Path-B, the expected revenue per qualified lead rises by: 0.30 × (€37.5K - €18K) = +€5,850 expected revenue lift per qualified enterprise lead. This is a meaningful uplift that favors Option B. However, Option B requires Ruslan to deliver 2 different sales conversations Phase-1 — execution bandwidth cost.

**28 Locks check (D18 / D15):** Path-C proactive activation is consistent with D18 (productization-over-hours) only if the Path-C delivery template is established. Phase-1 has no productized Path-C kit — all Path-C engagements are manual-under-consulting per L5 §3.3. Therefore Option B (proactive Path-C offer) requires accepting manual delivery overhead at 47-50% GM until Phase-2 productization kit ships. This is a D18-consistent temporary state, not a D18 violation, if duration is bounded to first 2-3 Path-C contracts.

### Channel 1 — LinkedIn Pre-Researched DM (EN-speaking Startupper primary; Mittelstand secondary)

- **Hours/week Ruslan investment:** 4–6 hrs/week (connection requests + follow-up messages + comment-first warm-up)
- **Direct € cost:** €55/mo LinkedIn Premium (Sales Navigator at €79/mo if scaling Phase-2)
- **Conversion funnel:** 5-15% response rate cold → 30% of responders → discovery call → 40% of discovery calls → proposal → 60% of proposals → close. Composite: 5-15% × 30% × 40% × 60% = **0.36% - 1.1% cold DM to closed client**. After-comment rate: 15-25% response → composite ≈ **1.1% - 1.7%**.
- **CAC per closed deal (cold DM):** 15 DMs/hr × (1/1.1%) = **91 DMs per close**; at 15 DMs/hr = **6.1 hrs per close**. However, each DM involves research (5 min) → realistic throughput 6 researched DMs/hr. Revised: 91 DMs / 6 DMs/hr = **15.2 hrs per close** × €150 = **€2,275 CAC** + €55/mo LinkedIn ≈ **€2,330 CAC blended**
- **CAC per closed deal (after-comment warm):** 45 DMs per close / 6 DMs/hr = **7.5 hrs × €150 = €1,125 CAC**
- **Risk per Ruslan-hour:** High (opportunity cost of €150/hr dedicated outreach). LinkedIn cold DM is high-effort, medium-return. After-comment DM is more efficient but requires content presence (posting consistently) as pre-condition.
- **Vertical fit:** Strongest for H3 (Startupper EN), moderate for H2 (Professional Services EN-speaking), lower for H1/H4 (Mittelstand prefers email for senior decision-maker; LinkedIn DM can be entry for junior contact)

### Channel 2 — Email Cold Outreach (Mittelstand primary; Professional Services secondary)

- **Hours/week Ruslan investment:** 3–5 hrs/week (research + write + send + follow-up cadence at 3 touches)
- **Direct € cost:** Apollo.io ~€80/mo (email finder + sequencer for DACH SMB); or Hunter.io €49/mo
- **Conversion funnel:** 20-40% open rate; 3-8% reply rate → 25% of replies → discovery call → 40% → proposal → 55% → close. Composite: 5-8% × 25% × 40% × 55% = **0.28% - 0.44% cold email to close**
- **CAC per closed deal:** 10 personalized emails/hr × (1/0.36%) = **278 emails per close**; at 10/hr = **27.8 hrs × €150 = €4,167 CAC** + €80/mo Apollo ≈ **€4,247 CAC**
- Note: this is the least economical channel on a per-hour basis at Phase-1. Email works best when accompanied by content presence (published article, webinar, case study) that warm-primes the prospect. Cold email to completely unknown Mittelstand without a prior touch = lowest ROI outreach channel.
- **Mitigation:** Use email as second-touch after LinkedIn connection; not as cold-first channel. This reduces the funnel to 15-20% reply rate (post-LinkedIn) and lowers CAC toward €1,500-€2,000 range.
- **Risk per Ruslan-hour:** Highest of all channels when purely cold. Becomes efficient when layered with LinkedIn warm-up.
- **Vertical fit:** Best for H2 (Professional Services; email is "serious channel" in German professional services culture), moderate H1/H4

### Channel 3 — Referral / Warm Intros (highest ROI per hour; bandwidth-constrained)

- **Hours/week Ruslan investment:** 1–3 hrs/week (referral ask messages to 2-3 existing contacts/week; follow-up)
- **Direct € cost:** €0 direct
- **Conversion funnel:** 30-60% response rate on warm intro per L6 §7.1 → 70% discovery call rate → 60% proposal → 65% close. Composite: **0.30-0.60 × 0.70 × 0.60 × 0.65 = 8.2% - 16.4% intro to close**. At mid 12%, 8 intros per close.
- **CAC per closed deal:** 8 intros / (2-3 ask conversations / hr) = **3.2 hrs × €150 = €475 CAC**
- **Phase-1 constraint:** Ruslan's first-degree network of qualified referral sources is finite. Estimated 20-40 warm qualified connections (former colleagues, conference contacts) who could refer. This channel saturates at ~8-15 referral closes before network exhaustion. After that, replenishment requires having delivered case-study clients (reinforcing loop: delivery quality → referrals → more clients → more case studies).
- **Risk per Ruslan-hour:** Lowest of all channels (€475 CAC). Only risk: under-investing in referral asks due to perceived awkwardness; this is a discipline issue, not an economics issue.
- **Vertical fit:** All hypotheses (H1-H4) benefit; particularly strong for H2 (professional services networks are tight; one lawyer referral opens their entire firm's network)
