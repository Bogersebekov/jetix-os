## §3 Moat Analysis — €1M Gate

> Primary moat analysis at the most decision-relevant gate: €1M, where Tier 3 deepens and capital allocation decisions are non-trivial.

`[src: reports/jetix-vs-iwe-audit-2026-05-17.md §7: Jetix highest lever = R12 anti-extraction (Meadows L2 system-goals); IWE highest lever = FPF Fallback Chain (Meadows L3 structural constraint)]`

`[src: reports/jetix-vs-iwe-audit-2026-05-17.md §3 J-U2: R12 «AI/substrate cannot extract value from members beyond agreed share; members can fork-and-leave without penalty» — NONE in public IWE template]`

### §3.1 Two candidate moats at €1M gate

**Moat A — FPF-derivation moat.** Jetix uses FPF mechanisms (IP-1, F-G-R, AWAITING-APPROVAL, etc.) as structural depth. This moat is knowable to anyone who reads the FPF spec and implements it. Durability: **low-to-medium** (FPF is open; any actor can implement it; IWE already implements it). Kill conditions below.

**Moat B — R12 anti-extraction substrate moat.** Jetix constitutionally encodes R12 («members can fork-and-leave without penalty»; «AI/substrate cannot extract value from members beyond agreed share») as a Tier 2 constitutional rule. This is Jetix-unique — no equivalent in public IWE template per audit §3 J-U2. Durability: **high conditional** (constitutional rules are hard to remove; the anti-extraction commitment creates a different social contract than any FPF-derived competitor). Kill conditions below.

`F: F4 | G: moat-analysis | R: refuted_if_IWE-paid-platform-reveals-R12-equivalent_OR_R12-constitutional-elevation-reversed`

### §3.2 Kill conditions (≥2 per moat, per §3.2 Conformance Checklist C4)

**Moat A (FPF-derivation) kill conditions:**
- **Kill-A1:** Another well-resourced actor implements FPF more completely (higher FPF-grade than Jetix's ~25% current implementation per audit §8.4) AND reaches Jetix's target market first. FPF is open; implementation depth is the differentiator — if outpaced, moat collapses.
- **Kill-A2:** Levenchuk publishes a major FPF revision that deprecates mechanisms Jetix has deeply embedded (annual revision pace noted per profiles §3 «yearly-updated textbooks»). Without Tier 3/4 relationship, Jetix may miss the revision or misinterpret it — FPF-derivation moat becomes «FPF-outdated-derivation moat», which is fragile.

**Moat B (R12 anti-extraction) kill conditions:**
- **Kill-B1:** R12 constitutional rule is effectively watered down under internal or investor pressure (Risk R-D in H7 document: «Anti-extraction watered down under external/internal pressure»). If the commitment weakens, the social-contract moat evaporates faster than any technical moat.
- **Kill-B2:** IWE paid platform (Aisystant AI guide — currently B2 blocked per Phase A) turns out to already encode an equivalent anti-extraction rule. If so, Moat B is not Jetix-unique; it is merely Jetix-first-documented. Falsifier receipt: Phase C B2 unblock + empirical IWE session inspection.

### §3.3 Structural moat verdict at €1M gate

At €1M, cooperation strengthens Moat A (FPF relationship keeps derivation current) while leaving Moat B independent of FPF partnership (R12 is Jetix-constitutional, not FPF-derived). The structurally durable moat is Moat B. Cooperation's contribution to Moat B is indirect: Levenchuk's methodology legitimacy attached to Jetix's R12 articulation raises credibility of the social contract claim, but does not alter its constitutional force.
