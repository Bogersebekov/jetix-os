## §5 R12 Ethical Operationalization — Is It Mechanical or Rhetorical?

This is the most-charged question in the brief. Eng-integrator's self-dissent D-DOC03-ENG-1 correctly anticipated this.

### §5.1 What R12 actually provides

From Charter §4.1 and the R12 packet:
- Constitutional prohibition on extraction beyond agreed share
- Fork-and-leave right
- Constitutional amendment barrier

R12 is **constitutionally binding on the substrate** (Jetix-as-platform, the AI system). This is mechanically operational within the Jetix OS system — Part 6b §I.2 can enforce it via halt_log_alert on agent actions.

### §5.2 What R12 does NOT provide — the gap

R12 does NOT provide:
1. **Enforcement against human-to-human extraction.** If member A extracts value from member B beyond agreed share through social manipulation (not mediated by the AI substrate), R12 has no mechanical lever. Charter §9 escalation ladder is the intended mechanism — but §9 relies on: (a) bilateral conversation, (b) Clan-level mediation with a «senior member» who does not yet exist (0 signatories), (c) a «Federation council» that does not exist.
2. **Definition of «agreed share» in ambiguous cases.** Charter §5.5 lists 6 resource types. What constitutes «extraction beyond agreed share» in a knowledge-sharing context (who owns a synthesis produced jointly?) is not specified. The abuse predicate in §4.2 assumes agreed_share is a well-defined quantity — at L1 with 5-15 humans and no formal measurement, it is underspecified.
3. **Protection against power asymmetry below the R12 threshold.** Charter §6 retains Ruslan as founding-strategist constitutional authority with cascading veto. This is structurally an asymmetric power relationship. R12 does not constrain founder authority; it constrains substrate extraction. These are different domains.

### §5.3 Verdict on the cultural enforcement layer

Eng-integrator dissent D-DOC03-ENG-1 states: «cultural layer (trust + respect) = not formalizable, treated as non-engineering». This is correct as an engineering decision but creates an epistemic gap the artefact does not adequately surface.

**The document currently frames R12 as a «safeguard against abuse» when it is more precisely a «constitutional constraint on substrate extraction».** The gap between «constitutional constraint on the AI system» and «sufficient protection against human participant abuse» is non-trivial and is not flagged as an open question.

**New dissent introduced (see §9):** D-DOC03-PHIL-1.

## §8 Abuse-Mode vs Collaboration-Mode Boundary — Epistemic Validation

The framework's ability to distinguish abuse from cooperation rests on three mechanisms:

**Mechanism 1 — formal predicate (§4.2).** The abuse predicate is well-formed. Four conditions are individually clear. However «agreed_share» in `extract(A, B.capabilities) > agreed_share(A, B)` is undefined for resource types where measurement is contested (knowledge, attention, reputation). At L1 with no prior Quests, agreed_share is established by verbal commitment (Charter §4.5) not by formal measurement.

**Falsifiability check (CC-1 gap):** What observation would demonstrate that the abuse predicate fails to catch a real abuse case at L1? The document does not state this. It should.

**Mechanism 2 — fork-and-leave.** Mechanically correct as a structural impossibility of lock-in. However fork-and-leave protects against sustained extraction — it does not prevent a single high-value extraction event followed by exit. This is a known limitation not acknowledged.

**Mechanism 3 — cultural layer.** The document correctly notes in §3.3 «trust, respect, responsible approach» from text_004. But the document treats culture as non-mechanizable and therefore moves immediately to formal mechanisms. This is the right epistemic move, but the consequence is that the document's abuse prevention rests 80% on formal mechanism 1 (underspecified) and 20% on mechanism 2 (protects against sustained but not acute extraction). This is honest but requires explicit acknowledgment.

**Key epistemic finding:** the framework does NOT collapse to «trust Ruslan / FPF works». The Abuse predicate, R12, and fork-and-leave together provide a structural boundary that is meaningfully different from «hope for good behaviour». However the document's current framing overstates the boundary's completeness by using «гарантирует» (§3.4) and by not flagging the agreed_share measurement gap.
