## §5.0 Framing — the tension as an epistemic object

The apparent contradiction is not a contradiction. It is an instance of **AP-PHIL-2 (paradigm
conflation)** — three claims from different epistemic registers being read as if they occupy
the same unit-of-analysis space. They do not. [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]

Before any reconciliation, the three claims must be disaggregated as epistemic objects:

| Claim-ID | Source | Unit of measure | Level of analysis |
|---|---|---|---|
| audio_529 | Ruslan voice memo 24.04.2026 02:48 | Personal income of ICP ($1M+/yr) | Client wealth profile |
| audio_470 | Ruslan voice memo (17.04.2026 context) | Business revenue of client company ($20-50K/month = $240-600K/yr) | Company revenue bracket |
| audio_452 | Ruslan voice memo 15.04.2026 | Ruslan's operational hourly economics (€150/hr × 4 hrs = €600/day) | Ruslan delivery unit |

The critical move: audio_470's «$20-50K/month» is **company monthly revenue** of the client
business — it describes the Startupper ICP bracket (digital business generating $240-600K/yr),
not the client's personal income. This reading is supported by the task context
[src:swarm/wiki/tasks/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25/intake.md#§6] and
consistent with L6's archetype table where Startupper = «$50K-$500K/yr revenue»
[src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]. Personal income of the Startupper may be
50-70% of that revenue figure, not the revenue itself.

L6 qualitative resolution (acked 2026-04-25 01:00 CET): payment-ability filter ($5K/mo OR $10K
one-shot) and P1A/P1B split are binding constraints that already resolve the tension
qualitatively. L7 §5 adds the **quantitative income-floor layer** required for unit-econ
modelling in §3 (LTV assumptions) and §11 (concentration risk register). The L6 acked filter is
NOT overridden; this section adds resolution granularity inside the acked framework.
[src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]

**Acceptance predicate for this section:**
All income-floor claims carry explicit falsifiers AND unit-of-measure labels (personal income vs
business revenue vs deployment budget vs Ruslan hourly unit); the миллионер-tier classification
(Phase-1 P1B referral-only vs Phase-2+ aspirational) is resolved as a binary verdict with

## §5.3 Phase-1 P1A Active Cold Outreach — Income Floor per Archetype

P1A = active cold outreach. Target archetypes: **Operator-Founder-CEO (Mittelstand)** and
**Startupper (English-speaking infobiz)**. [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]

### Operator-Founder-CEO (Mittelstand)

The Mittelstand ICP is an owner-operator of a DACH-region company with:

- **Company revenue:** €1M–€50M (per L6 §2.1 Archetype C table)
  [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]
- **Owner personal annual draw (typical):** €100K–€500K/yr for owner-operators of €1M-€10M
  companies; €500K-€2M+ for €10M-€50M companies (Mittelstand ownership is typically 80-100%
  held by founder, so revenue surplus converts at 15-30% to personal compensation)
- **Deployable strategic budget (annual):** typically 1-3% of company revenue
  - €1M company: €10K-€30K/yr strategic budget
  - €5M company: €50K-€150K/yr strategic budget
  - €10M+ company: €100K-€300K/yr strategic budget
- **Feasible Jetix engagement (§2 pricing reference):** €5K-€18K per engagement (Direction 1
  Path B default: €3K onboarding + €15K/yr license = €18K yr1)
  [src:swarm/wiki/drafts/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25-investor-integrator-§2-pricing.md#§2.3.1]

**Income-floor claim for Mittelstand P1A:**

Company revenue ≥€1M/yr (=owner annual draw likely ≥€100K) with a deployable strategic budget
≥€10K/yr is the minimum viable floor. Below €1M company revenue, the €5K-€18K Jetix engagement
represents >1.5% of annual revenue — a non-trivial allocation that rarely clears internal
approvals without exceptional ROI proof first.

- F: F4 (arithmetic-grounded: 1-3% strategic budget of €1M company = €10-30K/yr; €5K
  engagement = 17-50% of annual budget floor — feasible at upper end only; €5M company
  budget = €50-150K/yr; €5K engagement = 3-10% — comfortable; €18K engagement = 12-36% —
  requires ROI justification but feasible)
- ClaimScope: DACH Mittelstand owner-operators; does NOT apply to corporate middle-managers
  (no P&L ownership) or sole traders below €500K revenue (payment filter fails)
- R: refuted if Mittelstand prospects below €1M company revenue pass payment filter and close
  at listed tier pricing in first 3 attempts; accepted if ≥80% of qualified Mittelstand leads
  show ≥€1M company revenue in discovery Q6 (monthly revenue/budget question)

### Startupper (English-speaking infobiz)

The Startupper ICP is an English-speaking digital business founder with:

- **Company revenue:** $50K–$500K/yr (audio_470 bracket $240-600K/yr = upper half of this
  range) [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]
- **Owner personal income:** typically 60-80% of company revenue for solo/micro-team operators;
  roughly $30K-$400K/yr personal
- **Deployable strategic budget:** more variable than Mittelstand; digital business operators
  often spend 5-15% of revenue on tools + coaching + strategic services (higher % than
  traditional Mittelstand due to lean cost structure)
  - $100K company: $5K-$15K/yr strategic budget — tight for recurring retainer
  - $250K company: $12.5K-$37.5K/yr — viable for €5K/mo retainer at upper range
  - $500K company: $25K-$75K/yr — comfortable for €5K/mo retainer
- **Feasible Jetix engagement (§2 pricing):** €500-€5K (Direction 1 Startupper package);
  Продюсерский центр Pilot €2K-€3K/mo or Standard €4K-€6K/mo
  [src:swarm/wiki/drafts/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25-investor-integrator-§2-pricing.md#§2.1]

**Income-floor claim for Startupper P1A:**

Company revenue ≥$100K/yr (personal income ≥$50K-$80K/yr) with clear signs of audience
monetization is the minimum entry signal. At $100K revenue, a $5K engagement is 5% of annual
revenue — viable for a motivated Startupper who sees ROI. For recurring €4K+/mo retainer,
company revenue ≥$250K/yr is a practical floor.

- F: F4 (analogical from digital business budget norms + L6 payment-filter arithmetic; $5K
  one-shot at $100K company revenue = 5% of revenue; standard B2B threshold for single-vendor
  spend without board approval is typically 3-10% of annual revenue for services)
- ClaimScope: English-speaking infobiz digital operators; does NOT apply to traditional SMBs
  with physical cost structures where 5% of revenue on a single service vendor is very high
- R: refuted if Startuppers below $100K/yr revenue consistently pass discovery Q6 and Q7
  (budget questions) and close; accepted if ≥80% of qualified Startupper leads show ≥$100K/yr
  company revenue in discovery qualification

## §5.4 Phase-1 P1B Opportunistic Referral — Ceiling and Channel Logic

P1B = opportunistic referral. Target archetypes: **High-earner ($100K-$150K+/yr personal)**
and **Миллионер ($1M+/yr personal)**. These arrive inbound — not via active outreach.
[src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]

### High-earner professional ($100K-$150K+/yr personal)

High-earner professionals (specialist consultants, senior attorneys, high-revenue freelancers,
successful bloggers with monetized audiences) have personal income as the primary signal because
their businesses are often solo or micro-team structures where revenue ≈ personal draw.

- **Payment-filter clearance:** $10K one-shot is 7-10% of personal annual income at $100-150K
  — significant but manageable for someone with stable high income and clear ROI framing
- **Feasible engagement:** Direction 1 productized engagement €5K-€10K; Продюсерский центр
  Standard tier €4K-€6K/mo for 3 months = €12K-€18K commitment
- **Channel:** referral-primary; warm intro from existing network; LinkedIn engagement
  (not cold DM to cold account)

**No explicit ceiling for P1B.** Any referral-converted High-earner who passes D22 and payment
filter is a Phase-1 client. The ceiling is Ruslan's bandwidth, not the segment's income:
with 20+ billable hours/week already committed to P1A (CC-1 math), P1B engagements must fit
within residual capacity.

- F: F4 (bandwidth-constraint arithmetic: CC-1 requires 233 hours across H1 2026 ≈ 18-19
  hrs/week; Ruslan's capacity ≈ 30-35 hrs/week total; residual for P1B ≈ 11-16 hrs/week;
  sufficient for 1-2 P1B clients simultaneously at €5K-€10K engagement each)
  [src:swarm/wiki/drafts/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25-investor-integrator-§2-pricing.md#§2.12]
- ClaimScope: Phase-1 bandwidth ceiling; relaxes at G2 when first contractor hired
- R: refuted if High-earner P1B clients consistently consume >20 hrs/engagement (eating into
  P1A CC-1 hours); accepted if P1B engagements remain at ≤15 hrs average

### Миллионер ($1M+/yr personal)

Миллионер-tier clients have annual personal income or capital draw ≥$1M. They trivially pass the
payment filter. Their buyer psychology differs from all other segments: they optimize for
asymmetric upside («насколько умножает мои активы»), not for cost savings or efficiency.
[src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1 Category A]

**Phase-1 channel constraint — critical finding:**

Cold conversion rate for $1M+ ICP via unsolicited outreach approaches zero. Multiple independent
reasons converge (Munger lollapalooza, P4):

1. **Trust barrier (naval specific-knowledge filter, P10):** Миллионеры hire consultants
   through networks of vetted referrals, not via LinkedIn DMs. Their decision process involves
   multiple touchpoints and proof of track record. Phase-1 Ruslan has zero published track record.
2. **Resource mismatch (Stoic dichotomy of control, P5 applicable):** Engaging a миллионер
   client properly requires €20K-€50K/mo retainer pricing (per D-price-1 in §2.13). At that
   pricing, the engagement scope, complexity, and stakeholder management volume exceeds
   Phase-1 solo-Ruslan capacity without degradation.
   [src:swarm/wiki/drafts/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25-investor-integrator-§2-pricing.md#§2.13]
3. **Opportunity cost (Koen sotam, P6):** Active outreach to $1M+ ICP consumes the same
   calendar time as outreach to Mittelstand/Startupper but with near-zero conversion probability.
   The state-of-the-art outreach method for this segment is warm intro, not cold DM.

**Verdict: Миллионер-tier = Phase-1 P1B referral-only segment, NOT active cold-outreach target.**

- F: F3 (substantive with evidence — L6 acked + resource-constraint reasoning + cold-conversion
  near-zero for $1M+ ICP without warm intro, consistent with L6 §2.1 channel guidance for
  миллионеры: «cold к $1M+ segment без warm intro = низкая конверсия»)
  [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]
- ClaimScope: Phase-1 solo-Ruslan bandwidth; cold-outreach channel; fails when warm referral
  arrives organically — then миллионер becomes viable P1B client
- R: refuted if Ruslan closes 2+ миллионер-tier contracts via active cold outreach in
  Q1-Q2 2026 (currently zero empirical basis for cold conversion); accepted if 100% of
  Phase-1 миллионер clients arrive via referral (predicted)

**Phase-1 миллионер P1B engagement ceiling:** €20K single engagement or €5K-€10K/mo
3-month retainer are feasible as P1B when a referral materializes. Not pre-sized in Phase-1
outreach templates. Treated as AWAITING-APPROVAL HITL packet per D-price-1 dissent when
first миллионер referral arrives.
[src:swarm/wiki/drafts/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25-investor-integrator-§2-pricing.md#§2.13]

  [src:decisions/LAYER-6-COMMUNITY-DEEP-DIVE.md#§2.1]

## §5.6 Mittelstand €5M Revenue Company — Budget Feasibility Quantified

The Mittelstand reality check requires explicit arithmetic, not handwaving.
[src:swarm/wiki/tasks/T-layer-7-business-trajectory-deep-dive-2026-04-25/intake.md#§6]

**Scenario: owner of €5M/yr revenue Mittelstand company.**

| Variable | Value | Derivation |
|---|---|---|
| Company annual revenue | €5M/yr | Input scenario |
| Typical owner personal draw | €200K-€500K/yr | 4-10% of revenue; Mittelstand owner compensation norms (German GmbH Gesellschafter-Geschäftsführer) |
| Deployable strategic budget | €50K-€150K/yr | 1-3% of revenue; conservative for DACH Mittelstand |
| Jetix AI Consulting retainer (§2 standard) | €5K/mo = €60K/yr | Direction 1 productized; within 1.2% of revenue |
| USB-C Path B (yr1 total) | €18K/yr | €3K onboarding + €15K annual license |
| Retainer as % of strategic budget | 40-120% | Depends on budget size and competing priorities |
| ROI threshold to justify allocation | 30-90 day productivity lift documented | Standard Mittelstand CFO requirement for non-capital expenditure |

**Feasibility verdict:**

€5K/mo retainer = €60K/yr = **1.2% of €5M revenue** — structurally feasible IF:
(a) ROI is demonstrable within 30-90 days, AND
(b) the €5K/mo retainer competes with ≤3 other strategic priorities in that budget year.

The 30-90 day ROI threshold is achievable via knowledge-work productivity lift. A Mittelstand
company with €5M revenue typically has €1M-€3M in knowledge-worker salary cost (20-60% of
revenue at 10-30 employees). A 10% productivity lift on knowledge work = €100K-€300K annual
value. Jetix retainer ROI at this scale = 167% to 500% within 12 months on the productivity
dimension alone.

- F: F4 (arithmetic grounded; knowledge-worker cost % of revenue from DACH SMB benchmarks;
  10% productivity lift is conservative for AI-augmented knowledge work — actual gains in
  early AI adopters reported at 20-40% on specific task types; ROI multiple deliberately
  conservative to survive CFO scrutiny)
- ClaimScope: €5M revenue Mittelstand with ≥10 knowledge workers; does NOT apply to
  manufacturing-heavy companies where knowledge-worker cost is <20% of revenue (ROI case weaker)
- R: refuted if first Mittelstand client at €5K/mo fails to demonstrate measurable
  productivity lift within 90 days of engagement; accepted if ≥2 of first 3 Mittelstand
  clients report positive ROI statement within 90 days

**Pragmatic pricing anchor for Mittelstand by revenue tier:**

| Company revenue | Feasible Jetix engagement | Strategic budget estimate | Feasibility gate |
|---|---|---|---|
| €500K-€1M | €2K-€5K one-shot only | €5K-€30K/yr | CONDITIONAL — one-shot only; no recurring |
| €1M-€5M | €5K-€18K engagement; €2K-€5K/mo retainer | €10K-€150K/yr | YES — with 30-day ROI demonstration |
| €5M-€10M | €5K-€10K/mo retainer; Path B €18K/yr | €50K-€300K/yr | YES — comfortable |
| €10M-€50M | Path C €30K-€45K/yr; €10K-€20K/mo | €100K-€1.5M/yr | YES — Path C enterprise override trigger |

## §X Cell C-06 Self-Improvement Notes

### Pattern 1 — Income floor is a three-variable function, not a single number

The operationally useful income floor for client qualification is not «income ≥ $X». It is a
function of three variables that must be evaluated jointly:

```
income_floor = f(
  business_revenue,           -- company annual revenue (or personal if solo operator)
  strategic_budget_rate,      -- % of revenue deployable for strategic services (1-15%)
  engagement_value            -- Jetix price point for the target tier
)

gate_pass = (business_revenue × strategic_budget_rate) ≥ (engagement_value × 1.2)
            -- 1.2 multiplier = 20% margin to account for competing budget priorities
```

A Startupper at $500K/yr with 8% strategic budget rate ($40K/yr deployable) passes the gate for
€5K/mo retainer (€60K/yr ≈ $64K — barely misses). At 10% ($50K/yr), it passes. This means
the same archetype at the same income level may or may not qualify depending on budget allocation
behavior. Discovery qualification must probe the budget-rate variable directly (Q6 monthly
revenue, Q7 «if it costs $10K»), not just income level.

**Compound rule candidate:** «income-floor qualification = 3-variable function (business revenue
+ strategic budget rate + engagement value); qualification gate passes only when product of
first two exceeds engagement value × 1.2; discovery Q6 and Q7 probe all three.»

### Pattern 2 — Unit-of-measure decomposition prevents false contradictions

The audio_529 / audio_470 / audio_452 apparent contradiction dissolves immediately once units
of measure are explicitly declared. Three numbers from three different epistemic registers
(client income archetype / client company revenue / Ruslan delivery unit) cannot contradict
each other because they do not occupy the same variable space. The critic role should
default to unit-of-measure decomposition before declaring any apparent inconsistency a real
contradiction — it may be an AP-PHIL-2 (paradigm conflation) instance rather than a genuine
logical tension.

**Compound rule candidate:** «before escalating an apparent multi-source contradiction, run
unit-of-measure decomposition: declare the unit for each claim; claims from different unit
spaces are not contradictory — they are complementary and must be labeled with their respective
scopes.»

### Pattern 3 — Aspirational target vs acquisition channel are orthogonal dimensions

«Targeting» a segment (audio_529 миллионер framing) and «using cold outreach to acquire» that
segment are two independent variables. A company can target $1M+ income clients exclusively as
its long-horizon ideal while using referral-only acquisition in Phase-1 — these are fully
compatible positions. Conflating «target segment» with «primary acquisition channel» produces
false strategic binaries («do we go after миллионеры or not?») when the real question is
«what channel reaches each segment in each phase?». Philosophy-expert should surface this
orthogonality whenever a claim about target audience is interpreted as a claim about outreach
channel.

**Compound rule candidate:** «target-segment identity (who we want) and acquisition-channel
selection (how we reach them) are orthogonal; a claim about target segment carries no implicit
claim about channel; separate both before evaluating consistency with outreach strategy.»
