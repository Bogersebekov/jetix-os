It should either be (a) attributed explicitly to eng-integrator as a structural opinion, not a synthesis finding, or (b) removed from §8 and presented as one of the four candidate frames in the table (which already exists and is balanced).

### F5 — OQ-1: surface vs auto-decide

**PARTIAL-FAIL → EP-F3 (already flagged in §3.1 check 5 and F1 analysis).**

OQ-1 is correctly surfaced in §8 with a four-candidate table (O-01 / O-04 / O-03 / O-02), each with "what it gives" and "what is lost." The table is balanced. "OQ-1 surfaced. Ruslan decides." is the terminal sentence.

The problem is the sentence immediately before: "Но если надо одно слово — O-01 = primary FPF anchor (наиболее формально grounded как U.System A.1)." This is labelled "Synthesis pass finding (F3)" — which elevates it from an opinion to a synthesized conclusion. The word "наиболее формально grounded" is a comparative epistemic claim that amounts to a recommendation.

Strict R1 discipline (CLAUDE.md §4.1 rule 1: "agents draft, surface, recall; owner authors strategic prose") requires that any framing that functions as a recommendation be attributed as agent-opinion, not as synthesis-finding. Specifically:
- "This is the synthesis view (F3): O-01 = most formally grounded" = strategic recommendation (blocked by R1)
- "Eng-integrator structural opinion: O-01 maps most cleanly to A.1 U.System as FPF formal anchor" = transparent attribution (allowed)

The fix is one sentence: replace "Synthesis pass finding (F3):" with "Eng-integrator structural note (F3, not Ruslan's declaration):" and flag the entire paragraph as non-binding on OQ-1.
