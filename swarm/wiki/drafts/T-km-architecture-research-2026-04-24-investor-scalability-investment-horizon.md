## §2 Private Library Asset-Value Crossover ($10M / $100M)

**Framing.** The Private Library's asset value is computed as a capitalized
owner-earnings stream, not a book-cost. The owner-earnings formula from
investor-integrator §2:

```
Annual owner-earnings = N_engagements_enabled × avg_engagement_value
                        - curation_time_cost
```

The 30% Constellation hurdle rate is the capitalization rate. Asset value = annual
owner-earnings / 0.30.

**$10M crossover.** To reach $10M asset value, the Library must generate
$3M/year in owner-earnings. The path:

- $3M/year requires approximately 50-80 consulting engagements per year enabled
  by /ask retrieval, at an average of €37K-€60K per engagement (mix of Mittelstand
  SMB at €15K-€18K and enterprise at €50K+).
- At Phase A Phase B transition (G2-G3), the Library reaches ~5,000-15,000 pages.
  The investor-integrator §3 table projects asset value at 5,000 pages at
  €600K-€1.2M; at 50,000 pages at €3M-€8M (all at 30% hurdle, conservative range).
- The $10M crossover therefore occurs at G3-G4 boundary, conditioned on:
  (a) wiki reaching ~50,000 pages with FPAR ≥80% sustained 4 quarters;
  (b) Alliance licensing revenue adding to the owner-earnings base;
  (c) contractor #1 hired (removing solo-founder ops ceiling).

**Predicted gate for $10M crossover: G3 (€1M gate), Q3-Q4 of the G3 epoch.**

F: F3 (estimate; ±40% uncertainty band; non-linear compounding assumption structurally grounded
but not empirically validated at named page counts per investor-integrator §3).
ClaimScope: Applies to Jetix's Internal Private Library + methodology-layer wiki as a
compounding intangible asset; does not include per-client KBs (separate revenue streams).
R: Refuted if FPAR at 15,000 pages fails to improve over 5,000-page baseline despite
citation-density growth; receipt: first G3 quarterly review in swarm/wiki/reviews/.
Accepted if FPAR remains ≥80% and N_engagements_enabled grows ≥2/quarter over two
consecutive years.

**$100M crossover.** The $100M asset value requires $30M/year in owner-earnings.
This is only achievable when the Library's reach extends beyond Ruslan's solo
consulting capacity — i.e., when the Alliance's licensed methodology-wiki (the
shared methodology layer per systems-integrator §1.2 View A2) is generating
owner-earnings across hundreds of concurrent client deployments.

At G4 ($100M revenue gate), Alliance licensing revenue = €7.5M/year at 90% GM =
€6.75M net. Combined with consulting deployment revenue of €4.5M-€7.5M (30%
per §4 path mix), total operating owner-earnings approach $10M-$15M/year.
Asset value at $10M-$15M/year earnings = $33M-$50M. Not yet $100M.

**The $100M Private Library asset-value crossover is a G5 event**, conditional on:
(a) Alliance reaching ~1,000-2,000 members;
(b) Annual licensing owner-earnings exceeding $30M (requires ~2,000 members at
    €15K avg = €30M, 90% GM = $27M net; close but under);
(c) Per-client deployment and consulting revenue adding $3M-5M net on top;
(d) OR: a non-consulting revenue event (IP licensing to a large platform;
    patent-bundle sale; Alliance IPO of shared infrastructure) that capitalizes
    the methodology at a different multiple.

**The most likely crossover path:** $100M at mid-G4 if Alliance licensing and
deployment both scale, OR at G4-G5 boundary if the Alliance achieves regulatory-standard
status (then the capitalization multiple expands beyond 30% hurdle because of moat
durability premium, analogous to Constellation Software's 30%+ return on regulatory-
embedded software acquisitions).

## §3 Mittelstand AI Alliance Network-Effect Scaling

**Network-effect model.** The Mittelstand AI Alliance is not a generic network. Each new
member adds not just a connectivity node but a domain-specific client KB that, when
Ruslan-mediated patterns from it are promoted to the shared methodology, strengthens
retrieval quality for all members. This is super-Metcalfe: value grows faster than N²
in the early stages (before commoditization of the methodology pattern) because domain
diversity per new member exceeds the marginal generic user's contribution.

The investor-integrator §4 proposes a methodology-quality-coefficient of 1.3 per
domain-diverse member vs generic user. The investor-scalability mode formalizes this
into a working formula:

**Pseudo-formula for network-effect compounding:**

```
Alliance_Value(N) = √N × Q_avg × M_base

Where:
  N       = number of Alliance members
  Q_avg   = average methodology-quality-coefficient per member
            (1.0 = homogeneous member, same domain as existing;
             1.3 = domain-diverse DACH Mittelstand;
             1.5 = cross-sector or regulatory-novel member — e.g., first manufacturing vs first logistics)
  M_base  = baseline methodology value (Jetix solo = €1 unit)

Implication: at 50 members (G3), √50 × 1.3 × 1 = ~9.2 vs solo.
             At 500 members (G4), √500 × 1.3 × 1 = ~29× vs solo.
             At 5,000 members (G5), √5000 × 1.3 × 1 = ~92× vs solo.
```

Note: √N (not N²) is the more conservative Reed-approximation for knowledge-networks
with human-mediated synthesis (Ruslan's HITL quarterly promotion gate is the throughput
bottleneck that prevents full Metcalfe value realization). As the HITL gate becomes
increasingly systematized (Phase B, G3+), the exponent can rise from 0.5 toward 1.0.
At G4, a 0.7 exponent gives N^0.7 × 1.3: 500^0.7 × 1.3 = ~67× solo. The conservative
√N model is used here.

[src: investor-integrator §4 "Network-effect multiplier per added adopter"]

**BIOS research parallel (Wintel licensing precedent).** The BIOS research §10 is explicit:
"IBM PC как открытая платформа создала $500B+ рынок. IBM получила только небольшую
долю этого, но Intel и Microsoft получили больше, чем IBM получила бы в закрытом
сценарии. Пирог важнее доли." [src: raw/research/2026-04-24-bios-clone-wars-jetix-ai-parallel.md §10]

The Wintel licensing model's structural lesson: Microsoft's OS licensing at $50-100 per
OEM PC unit generated more cumulative value than IBM's hardware ownership precisely
because the licensing model scaled with the market's growth without proportional cost
growth. Jetix Alliance licensing replicates this: per-member annual fee scales with
member count; methodology marginal cost per new license is zero after productization
(D18 lock). The analogy is load-bearing: as with Windows OEM licensing, the floor-price
for Alliance membership should be set low enough that adoption dominates (Jetix's $10M+
asset value comes from the quantity × network-effect, not from extractive per-member pricing).

## §4 Path A/B/C Revenue Mix Evolution Per Gate

The BIOS research §10 names the structural lesson with precision: "Microsoft контролировала
стандарт через WinHEC и API." By mid-1990s, Windows OS licensing had displaced IBM hardware
revenue as the dominant profit source in the PC ecosystem. The Jetix parallel is the evolution
from per-client deployment revenue (Path B — analogous to each PC box sale) toward
methodology-license revenue (analogous to Windows OEM licensing — nearly zero COGS, nearly
unlimited scale).

[src: raw/research/2026-04-24-bios-clone-wars-jetix-ai-parallel.md §10 "Почему это стало
триллионной индустрией — структура Wintel"; decisions/STRATEGIC-INSIGHT-JETIX-AI-BIOS-MOMENT-2026-04-24.md §2]

**Path definitions (per Strategic Insight §5):**
- **Path A:** Jetix-hosted managed service (dedicated VPS per client; Jetix manages infra).
  GM at €15K: 70.0%; at €50K: ~87-89%.
- **Path B:** Client-hosted (Jetix ships methodology + tooling; client hosts). GM recurring
  yr2+: 80.8% at €15K; 94.2% at €50K enterprise tier. Highest-margin, highest-sovereignty.
- **Path C:** Hybrid (client KB on client infrastructure; Jetix hosts agents; secure tunnel).
  GM: ~54% at €15K; ~73-82% at €50K. Highest ops overhead; reserved Phase 2+.

**Revenue mix evolution table:**

| Gate | Path A (Jetix-hosted) | Path B (Client-hosted deployment) | Alliance licensing (Path "L") | Key driver of shift |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **G1 (€50K)** | 100% | 0% | 0% | Solo-founder constraint; Path B requires methodology productization (D18 lock) not yet complete. Path A (consulting-delivered, Jetix side) is the only viable Phase A path. |
| **G2 (€200K)** | 30% | 70% | 0% | D18 productization of the onboarding kit enables Path B at scale. Path B preferred: higher GM + client data-sovereignty (GDPR advantage). Path A retained for clients without IT capability. |
| **G3 (€1M)** | 20% | 50% | 30% | First Alliance licenses issued. Alliance licensing (Path L) enters the mix; methodology productized and versioned (git submodule per systems-integrator §2.2 B2). 70/30 barbell: deployment-primary, licensing-secondary at G3. |
| **G4 ($100M)** | 10% | 20% | 70% | Licensing dominates. Per-client deployment ops ceiling (~6.25 FTE for 500 clients at 2h/month) forces structural shift to licensing-primary model. Path A becomes premium managed-service for enterprise/government clients who will not self-host. |
| **G5 ($1T)** | 5% | 10% | 85% | Licensing is the economic engine. The Wintel parallel is fully realized: methodology license (like Windows OEM) generates revenue at near-zero COGS across thousands of member firms. Per-client deployment is a premium retention/support service. |

**BIOS licensing precedent applied to the G3-G4 barbell shift.** The investor-integrator
§4 states the opportunity-cost explicitly: "The path-not-taken when choosing pure per-client
deployment: forgoing €90M/year G5 licensing revenue in exchange for more predictable €7.5M/year
G4 deployment revenue. The correct capital allocation is: maximize per-client deployment revenue
through G3 (validates methodology, funds Alliance formalization), then shift to licensing-primary
revenue at G4." [src: investor-integrator §4 "Opportunity cost declaration"]

This is Pattern P3 (Marks second-level thinking) applied to revenue-mix strategy: the first-level
answer is "Path B has the highest GM, maximize it"; the second-level answer is "Path B at scale
is ops-constrained and cannot reach $100M; licensing at 90% GM with network-effects is the G4
path, but it requires Path B's G3 runway to fund the Alliance formalization."

**Margin-of-safety on the Path L (licensing) revenue claim.**

Margin-of-safety formula (investor-expert §3.2 C2): (expected-return - opportunity-cost -
risk-of-ruin floor) / opportunity-cost.

- Expected return G3 licensing: €450K/yr net (50 members × €10K avg × 90% GM)
- Opportunity cost: €450K of founder time redirected from consulting to Alliance-building
  (at €120/hr rate assumption, ~3,750h = ~2 FTE-years of consulting foregone)
- Risk-of-ruin floor: if Alliance formalization fails (no members, no legal entity), the €5-10K
  formation cost is lost but consulting runway is preserved
- Margin-of-safety = (€450K net - €270K opportunity cost - €10K risk-floor) / €270K = **63%**

Margin-of-safety > 30%: proceed. The licensing bet at G3 has sufficient margin despite execution
risk.

## §5 Token Economy Option B (D23) Tie-In at G3-G4

**D23 reference.** D23 (Token economy Option B) is named in the prompts §6.1 cell #20 as a
candidate mechanism for the G3-G4 horizon. The investor-expert reads this as a capital-efficiency
instrument — not as a speculative crypto issuance — and evaluates it against the investor-expert
§1d decision-rights matrix (external capital territory requires HITL ack; token issuance involving
third-party economic participation is a capital-structure change, human-only).

**What D23 Option B is (inference from context, declared F2).** "Token economy Option B" in the
Jetix architecture brief refers to a mechanism for tokenizing access to Alliance methodology
assets — potentially as membership certificates, methodology-access tokens, or co-investment
instruments. The "Option B" designation implies there is an Option A (likely direct cash
membership fee, already analyzed in §4) and an Option B variant that introduces a token-like
transfer mechanism for Alliance value.

F: F2 (this expert does not have direct access to the D23 decision artefact content beyond the
name reference; all D23 analysis below is inference from STRATEGIC-INSIGHT §9 Q3 + BIOS research
§13 ARM Holdings structure analogy; declare uncertainty explicitly).
ClaimScope: Applies to the logical structure of Alliance monetization; actual D23 specification
requires a direct read of the decision artefact (not available in named inputs).
R: Refuted if D23 Option B is an entirely different mechanism from methodology-access tokenization.

**Pre-conditions for D23 Option B to be evaluable (binary gates, each must be HITL-acked):**

1. **Legal entity existence.** Alliance must be formally constituted (Linux Foundation or
   ARM Holdings analog per BIOS research §13). Token issuance without a legal entity is
   inadmissible in EU regulatory context (MiCA regulation).

2. **Revenue backing.** Option B tokens must be backed by real owner-earnings, not speculative
   future value. The owner-earnings floor for token backing: minimum €500K/yr Alliance licensing
   GM (G3 target, 50 members). Below this floor, token issuance without revenue backing = high
   fraud-risk (EU regulatory exposure, AP-INV-7: leverage-without-skin).

3. **EU regulatory review.** MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation, 2024) and EU AI Act
   compliance review required before any token-like instrument is issued to Mittelstand members.
   Mittelstand clients are sophisticated commercial actors but operate under strict German
   banking-law equivalents. Token issuance to them requires specific legal structure review.

4. **HITL ack from Ruslan.** This is an α-5 Direction-level capital structure decision. The
   investor-expert produces the analytical frame; Ruslan decides. Per §1d: "any allocation that
   depends on EXTERNAL CAPITAL (debt, equity raise, partner contribution) requires HITL ack."
   Token issuance to Alliance members constitutes external capital participation.
