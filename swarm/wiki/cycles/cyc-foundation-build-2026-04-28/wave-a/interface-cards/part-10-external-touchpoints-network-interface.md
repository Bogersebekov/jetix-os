# Interface Card — Part 10: External Touchpoints & Network Interface

**Scope sentence.** The typed interface layer for all external relationship and
integration surfaces — the CRM/contact network (relationship lifecycle tracking,
communication history, generic pipeline), the external integration adapter pattern
(read-only intelligence trackers, write-ack action coordinators), and multi-channel
output routing (voice, text, webhook delivery).

**U.System.** Manages relationship state transitions (contact lifecycle); routes
external inbound signals to Part 2 (Signal Ingestion); coordinates outbound actions
through write-ack gates.

**Fork-separation declaration (D27 + OQ-MERGED-3):**
- Foundation-invariant (in this Part): contact record schema structure (14 sections), pipeline status taxonomy (generic states), privacy principles (§6.4), read-only vs write-ack split, append-only history discipline
- RUSLAN-LAYER (NOT in Foundation): specific ICP content (DACH / Mittelstand archetypes / AI Alliance), the `crm/_schema/strategy-hooks.yaml` 6 offers + 6 asks (these reference `decisions/` and `directions/_active/` — Ruslan-specific), phase-specific pipeline targets, specific contact lists
- Extension point: per-instance ICP schema parameterised as a RUSLAN-LAYER overlay over the Foundation contact schema [D27; A-1-critic-gate.md §4 OQ-MERGED-3; candidate-parts-merged.md §2 Part 10]

## A. Inputs

- External signals (new contact, inbound message, meeting note, mention) :: external-signal events → routed immediately to Part 2 (Signal Ingestion) for triage
- `crm/people/<slug>.md` + `crm/orgs/<slug>.md` :: existing contact record reads
- `crm/_schema/` :: frontmatter schema (roles, statuses — generic tier); template structure
- Owner ack signals for any write-action to external services :: write-ack events (MANDATORY per FUNDAMENTAL §6.1; FUNDAMENTAL §4.5 anti-pattern "auto-create commitments external")
- Part 9 (Owner Interaction Scaffold) weekly review :: CRM "stuck contact" list consumed in weekly review (contacts with >14d no touch surfaced via `/crm-stuck`)
- `wiki/graph/edges.jsonl` :: 8 CRM-to-wiki typed edge types (for graph relationship maintenance)

## B. Outputs

- `crm/people/<slug>.md` and `crm/orgs/<slug>.md` :: updated contact records (append-only §11 history)
- `crm/index.md` :: auto-generated index (re-generated by `/crm-rebuild-index`)
- `crm/log.md` :: append-only activity log
- `crm/views/` :: saved filtered views (weekly reports)
- Inbound signal forwarding → Part 2 (Signal Ingestion) :: signal-ready events (external capture is Part 2's domain; Part 10 is the boundary layer, not the ingestion pipeline)
- Write-ack gate requests to Part 6 (Governance) for any outbound action to external services :: gate-request events
- CRM graph edges → `wiki/graph/edges.jsonl` :: relationship-map events (8 CRM edge types per CLAUDE.md CRM System section)
- L.1-L.3 integration stubs (Phase-A): read-tracker digests and write-coordinator drafts (Phase-B materialisation surface only — no operational output in Phase A)

## C. Side-effects

- git commit `[crm] touch: <slug> (interaction logged)` per D25 / D17 (filesystem = SoT for CRM records)
- git commit `[crm] add: <slug> (new contact created)` on new contact creation
- Append to `crm/log.md` on every contact interaction (append-only, CRM log discipline)
- Append CRM relationship edges to `wiki/graph/edges.jsonl` on relationship state change
- NO auto-write to external services without Part 6 gate ack (FUNDAMENTAL §4.5 hard rule; FUNDAMENTAL §6.1 anti-pattern "auto-respond external без ack")

## D. Dependencies (typed per FPF A.14)

- `PhaseOf` **Part 2** — inbound external signal capture is the pre-processing phase before Part 2's ingestion pipeline; Part 10 is the external boundary, Part 2 processes what enters [A-1-critic-gate.md §2 Part 10 A.14; candidate-parts-merged.md §2 Part 10]
- `creates` **Part 1** — CRM records are filesystem-first committed artefacts (D17; FPF IP-3; D25)
- `creates` **Part 3** — CRM graph edges written to `wiki/graph/edges.jsonl` (8 CRM edge types feed Part 3's typed graph)
- `methodologically-uses` **Part 6** — all outbound write-actions to external services route through Part 6's gate mechanism (write-ack gate = AWAITING-APPROVAL or equivalent at appropriate SLA tier)

## E. Boundary (FPF A.6.B L/A/D/E)

**Laws (invariants that MUST hold):**
- All write-actions to external services REQUIRE explicit human ack before execution — no exceptions (FUNDAMENTAL §6.1 last bullet "якщо action не categorized — default deny + escalate"; FUNDAMENTAL §4.5 anti-pattern "auto-create commitments external"; FUNDAMENTAL §1 UC-L.3 principles) [A-1-critic-gate.md §2 Part 10 A.6.B]
- FUNDAMENTAL §6.4 privacy principles apply structurally to ALL CRM data (Part 10 is the only Foundation part that explicitly stores information about third parties):
  - Consent-respected: CRM stores only information contacts have shared publicly or explicitly consented to
  - Forget-request compliance: verifiable hard delete (not soft-flag) when contact requests data removal
  - Sensitive information encrypted at rest: passwords, financial details, health status — never plain text
  - Cross-context awareness: business interactions ≠ personal interactions; data collected in one context not repurposed in another without re-ack
  - No inference of protected characteristics: religion, sexuality, health, political views — NEVER inferred from observed data
  - Data not retained after delete-me: with verifiable hard delete
  [FUNDAMENTAL §6.4; A-1-critic-gate.md §6 item 5; A-1-critic-gate.md §2 Part 10 §6 anti-scope check]
- Append-only history: `§11` of each contact record is append-only; no editing or deletion of past interaction records (D25; FUNDAMENTAL §2.1 cross-cutting substrate)
- D17 Filesystem = SoT: CRM records live in the filesystem as the authoritative source; Notion or external views are read-only projections

**Admissibility (acceptance criteria for inputs):**
- New contact records accepted only if they carry: name, context (generic role relationship type), source provenance (how contact was established), initial status
- External write-action requests accepted only if accompanied by a draft + owner ack signal; dry-run mode available for preview pre-execute (FUNDAMENTAL §1 UC-L.3)
- Voice-to-CRM pipeline items accepted only as DRAFT state (`draft_from_voice` status) — never auto-promoted to production records; owner promotes manually after review [CLAUDE.md CRM System: Voice integration discipline]

**Deontics (obligations toward consumers):**
- MUST surface stuck contacts (>14d no touch, active status) to Part 9 weekly review [CLAUDE.md CRM System: Stuck detection]
- MUST route ALL inbound external signals to Part 2 (no direct ingestion into KB from Part 10)
- MUST open a Part 6 gate request for any external service write-action before execution (J-Approve minimum; J-Strategic for destructive actions)
- MUST NOT embed RUSLAN-LAYER ICP content in Foundation contact schema; specific offers/asks/strategy-hooks live in RUSLAN-LAYER overlay [D27; OQ-MERGED-3]

**Effects (measurable outcomes):**
- CRM records committed with provenance within one session of contact interaction
- Stuck-contact surface rate: all contacts with >14d no touch and active status appear in weekly review output (target 100% coverage)
- Write-ack gate opened via Part 6 for every outbound external action (audit trail: 100% of external write-actions have a committed gate record)

## F. Anti-scope

**Generic anti-scope:**
- Part 10 does NOT make strategic decisions about relationship direction — it surfaces relationship state; the owner decides how to proceed (FUNDAMENTAL §6.1, §6.2)
- Part 10 does NOT substitute for founder agency in relationship management (FUNDAMENTAL §6.2)
- Part 10 does NOT use engagement-economy patterns (FUNDAMENTAL §6.3) — no notification spam, no FOMO mechanics, no auto-message without ack
- Part 10 does NOT auto-respond to external contacts without owner ack (FUNDAMENTAL §6.1 hard rule; FUNDAMENTAL §4.5 anti-pattern)

**Part-specific anti-scope (CRITICAL — FUNDAMENTAL §6.4 privacy hard rules):**
- Part 10 does NOT infer protected characteristics (religion, sexuality, health status, political views) from CRM interaction data — NEVER [FUNDAMENTAL §6.4]
- Part 10 does NOT retain data after a "delete me" request without verifiable hard delete [FUNDAMENTAL §6.4]
- Part 10 does NOT log sensitive contact information (financial details, health status, credentials) in plain text — encrypted at rest mandatory [FUNDAMENTAL §6.4]
- Part 10 does NOT share or sell contact data to third parties under any pretext [FUNDAMENTAL §6.4]
- Part 10 does NOT embed RUSLAN-LAYER content in Foundation CRM schema: the `crm/_schema/strategy-hooks.yaml` (6 offers + 6 asks referencing `decisions/` and `directions/_active/`) is RUSLAN-LAYER content — NOT embedded in Foundation [D27; A-1-critic-gate.md §2 Part 10 Ruslan-creep check; OQ-MERGED-3]
- L.1-L.3 external integrations (Integration Hub, read-only trackers, action coordinators) are Phase-A stubs ONLY — no operational implementation in Phase A; materialisation is Phase-B/C surface [candidate-parts-merged.md §2 Part 10; Kauffman Ch. 1 adjacent-possible — external API connectivity requires structural prerequisites not yet operational]
- Part 10 does NOT own the ingestion pipeline — inbound signals are forwarded to Part 2 (Signal Ingestion); Part 10 is the external boundary, not the processing layer
