## §2 What Problem This Framework Solves for Foundation

Foundation is a system that makes claims about itself, about its own state, and
about which claims from agents to trust. Without an explicit epistemic discipline,
three pathologies accumulate silently:

1. **Unfalsifiable self-descriptions.** A Foundation "part" asserts it "improves
   over time" or "maintains coherence" — but names no observable that would
   falsify these assertions. The part looks rigorous but cannot be audited.
   (Popper § 1: "a theory which is not refutable by any conceivable event is
   non-scientific.")

2. **Silent paradigm mixing.** Foundation draws simultaneously on Levenchuk FPF
   ontology, multi-agent architecture vocabulary, and Karpathy LLM-wiki practices.
   These use the same words for different things. When a Foundation part description
   silently blends them, the interface card looks coherent but enforcing it produces
   contradictions at runtime. (Kuhn Ch. X: "paradigm incommensurability" — the new
   ideas and assertions cannot be strictly compared to the old ones because the same
   words have changed meaning.)

3. **Non-compounding methodology.** Foundation's methodology library accumulates
   entries (Part 5), but without a criterion for WHICH knowledge compounds and which
   is commodity, entries grow without increasing leverage. Naval's specific-knowledge
   filter and Munger's latticework provide the selection criterion.

The Stoic discipline adds a fourth: **agency-scope clarity**. Without an explicit
dichotomy-of-control pass on every Foundation governance decision, the governance
part (Part 6) tends to expand its enforcement scope into territory that is not in
the system's control, wasting resources on unenforceable constraints.

## §3 Paradigm Position

This framework occupies the **epistemology-of-a-building-system** paradigm: how
a system that is itself evolving can maintain calibrated beliefs about its own
state, improve methodically, and avoid paradigm conflation as it accumulates
knowledge.

It sits adjacent to — but distinct from — three paradigms in the Framework Taxonomy:

- **Levenchuk FPF (#1):** Levenchuk provides the constitutional ontology (what
  entities exist, what roles are). This card provides the epistemic discipline
  (what makes a claim about those entities credible, what would refute it, and
  how knowledge about them grows).
- **Compounding Engineering (#7):** CompEng provides the cycle ritual (40/10/40/10,
  DRR). This card provides the claim-quality filter applied INSIDE the compound step:
  which DRR entries are falsifiable claims vs. unfalsifiable observations.
- **Capital allocation (#10):** Munger latticework is shared terrain. The boundary:
  this card uses Munger's mental-model discipline for epistemic purposes (selecting
  which models apply, detecting lollapalooza convergence). Card #10 uses Munger
  for capital-allocation decisions (circle of competence, inversion, second-order
  thinking applied to investment thesis). The overlap is the latticework concept
  itself; the application domain is split.

## §4 Key Principles (5–7)

### P1 — Popper falsifiability: every Foundation claim must name its refuter

**Sourced.** "A theory which is not refutable by any conceivable event is
non-scientific. Irrefutability is not a virtue of a theory (as people often think)
but a vice." (Popper, Conjectures and Refutations, §1 "Science: Conjectures and
Refutations," p. 36 of 1963 ed.)

**Applied.** Every Foundation "part" asserts properties about itself in its Scope
sentence and Effects (E-lane) specification. P1 requires each assertion to carry an
explicit falsifier: an observable outcome whose presence would mean the claim is
false. Example application to Part 5 (Compound Learning): the claim "this part
converts execution experience into improved future execution" is unfalsifiable as
written. P1 forces: "falsified if DRR-entry count does not grow across 3 consecutive
cycles, OR if agent output quality score (from eval harness) does not improve after
10 cycles of compound phase execution." The falsifier is the acceptance predicate
the governance gate (Part 6) uses to evaluate promotions. Without P1 applied at
Part 5's interface card, Part 6 has no actionable criterion to evaluate whether
Part 5 is performing.

**Tradeoff.** P1 conflicts with Vincenti P7 below: some engineering knowledge
(tacit practical considerations, "things engineers know but that are not in
textbooks") is genuine and useful but does NOT produce a sharp falsifier. Forcing
Popper falsifiability on Vincenti-category-5 (practical considerations) knowledge
produces artificial precision. Resolution: P1 applies to descriptive claims about
Part behavior ("this part produces artifact X with property Y"); it does NOT apply
to heuristic-form entries in the methodology library, which are Vincenti-category-5
and carry a different quality marker ("battle-tested in N cycles, conditions:
applies-when <C>").

### P2 — Kuhn paradigm awareness: Foundation must track which paradigm each part inhabits

**Sourced.** "In a revolution, scientists see new and different things when looking
with familiar instruments in places they have looked before." (Kuhn, Structure Ch. X
"Revolutions as Changes of World View.") More critically for Foundation purposes:
"The proponents of competing paradigms practice their trades in different worlds.
...The two groups of scientists see different things when they look from the same
point in the same direction." (Kuhn Ch. XII, p. 150 in 50th anniv. ed.)

**Applied.** Foundation's candidate-parts-merged.md §1 was produced by 4–5 experts
drawing on different research programmes: Levenchuk FPF (with its specific ontology
of roles, alphas, holons), multi-agent architecture (Anthropic/Cognition, whose
"role" means agent persona), and Karpathy LLM-wiki (whose "provenance" is a
citation practice, not a trust-calculus). These paradigms are incommensurable in
Kuhn's sense — the word "role" in FPF cannot be directly compared with "role" in
CrewAI without a bridge. Each Foundation part must declare its paradigm vocabulary
in its interface card. Cross-part dependencies that cross a paradigm boundary (e.g.,
Part 3 KB uses Karpathy ingest discipline; Part 4 Coordination uses FPF IP-1 role
discipline) must carry an explicit "paradigm bridge" declaration: what the same
word means in each paradigm, and how the translation is handled.

**Tradeoff.** Kuhn paradigm awareness conflicts with the D27 Fork-and-Merge design
decision. D27 treats Foundation as a canonical upstream that instances fork without
needing to understand its full paradigm history. A downstream fork that uses Part 4
without understanding the FPF IP-1 role/executor distinction will silently violate
the paradigm. Resolution: Foundation interface cards must expose the paradigm
declaration as a required field, not just internal documentation. Forks inherit the
declaration; violations become detectable.

### P3 — Munger latticework: methodology library entries must span disciplines, and convergence is a quality signal

**Sourced.** "You must have models in your head. And you've got to array your
experience — both vicarious and direct — on this latticework of models. You may
have noticed students who just try to remember and pound back what is remembered.
Well, they fail in school and in life. You've got to hang experience on a latticework
of models in your head." (Munger, Poor Charlie's Almanack RU, Ch. 2 / Разговор 2
"Урок житейской мудрости." Translated from Russian edition.)

Also: "When you have a large number of well-reinforcing models, you get what I call
a lollapalooza effect — multiple models combining to push in the same direction."
(Attributed to Munger across multiple talks in the corpus.)

**Applied.** Foundation Part 3 (Knowledge Base & Methodology Library) accumulates
methodology entries in its compound sub-layer. P3 sets the quality filter: a
methodology entry that applies ONE model to ONE domain is low-leverage. A
methodology entry that shows how THREE or more models from different disciplines
converge on the same practice recommendation is high-leverage (lollapalooza signal).
The methodology library's /lint check should include: "does this entry cite ≥2
independent mental-model families?" Entries that cite only one model-family are
demoted to heuristic status (Vincenti category 5); entries with lollapalooza
convergence are promoted to principle status (Vincenti category 1).

**Tradeoff.** Munger's latticework (more models = more robustness) conflicts with
Naval's specific-knowledge principle (P4 below): depth in one domain builds
uncopyable competitive advantage, breadth across domains risks becoming a
dilettante. Resolution: the latticework applies at the METHODOLOGY layer (Foundation
design principles work better when multiple disciplines confirm them). Specific
knowledge applies at the EXECUTION layer (Ruslan's actual consulting leverage comes
from depth in AI-systems-for-knowledge-workers, not from breadth across all domains).
The two principles operate at different abstraction levels; they do not conflict
when layer-applied correctly.

### P4 — Naval specific knowledge: Foundation methodology library compounds what is uncopyable

**Sourced.** "Specific knowledge is knowledge you cannot be trained for. If society
can train you, it can train someone else and replace you. Specific knowledge is
found by pursuing your genuine curiosity and passion rather than whatever is hot
right now. Building specific knowledge will feel like play to you but will look like
work to others." (Naval Almanack, "Building Wealth" section, tweetstorm, p. 33.)

Also: "Arm yourself with specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage."
(Naval Almanack, p. 32.)

**Applied.** Foundation Part 5's methodology library must discriminate between two
types of compound entries: (a) commodity methods — practices any consultant could
learn from a book (not worth Library space as standalone entries); and (b)
specific-knowledge entries — the intersection of Ruslan's particular context, the
AI-augmented knowledge-worker domain, and a non-obvious practice that emerged from
actual cycles. The /ingest skill's --anchor= parameter (D28) is a proxy for this
filter: if an entry cannot be anchored to an active project or genuine curiosity,
it is commodity. The specific-knowledge filter is the epistemic complement to D28's
query-driven KB discipline. P4 applied to Part 5: the compound phase should
produce ≥1 specific-knowledge entry per 10 cycles (falsifiable; refuted if the
methodology library's density metric stays flat while cycle count grows).

**Tradeoff.** P4 vs. D27 Fork-and-Merge: if specific knowledge is what makes the
methodology library valuable, and D27 makes Foundation forkable, then a fork inherits
the structure but NOT the specific knowledge (which is Ruslan's, uncopyable). This
is not a problem but a design feature: the forkable part of Foundation is the
structure and the heuristic-entry schema; the specific-knowledge content is
RUSLAN-LAYER (per IP-2 context-is-king). A fork builds its OWN specific-knowledge
library over its own cycles. Foundation only provides the container and the quality
filter.

### P5 — Stoic dichotomy of control: Foundation governance must classify actions by what is in-system-control vs not

**Sourced.** "The chief task in life is simply this: to identify and separate matters
so that I can say clearly to myself which are externals not under my control, and
which have to do with the choices I actually control." (Epictetus, via Holiday Daily
Stoic, Introduction.) And: "Stoicism teaches that we can't control or rely on
anything outside what Epictetus called our 'reasoned choice' — our ability to use
our reason to choose how we categorize, respond, and reorient ourselves to external
events." (Holiday, Daily Stoic, Introduction, p. 132.)

**Applied.** Foundation Part 6 (Governance, Provenance & Human Gate) enforces
constraints across the system. A critical design defect occurs when Part 6 attempts
to enforce constraints on externals: e.g., "the system must ensure the founder
reviews every artifact within 24 hours" — this is NOT in Part 6's control (it
depends on the founder's availability). Enforcing uncontrollable constraints creates
false compliance metrics and erodes trust in the governance system. P5 applied:
every Part 6 enforcement rule must be classified as (a) in-system-control (e.g.,
"the system does not promote to canonical without an AWAITING-APPROVAL packet") or
(b) not-in-system-control (e.g., "founder responds within N hours"). Category (a)
rules become hard enforcement. Category (b) rules become monitoring/alerting only —
the system surfaces the fact that the condition is unmet but does not halt or
produce a false compliance signal. This directly operationalizes FUNDAMENTAL §6.2
founder-agency-preservation: the system does not control the founder's decisions,
it surfaces information for them.

**Tradeoff.** P5 (accept what is not in control) vs. Drucker effective executive
(influence what you can, do not wait for ideal conditions). The stoic dichotomy
is sometimes misread as passivity. Drucker's "effective executive" focuses on
managing the boundary between what can be influenced and what is truly external.
Resolution: Part 6 has three categories, not two. Category A: hard enforcement
(fully in-system-control). Category B: monitoring and alerting (influence-zone —
the system can surface, prompt, escalate, but not decide). Category C: pure logging
(not-in-control — record for audit but apply no constraint). The Stoic dichotomy
draws the A/B boundary; Drucker defines what active effort is worth applying in B.

### P6 — Vincenti knowledge taxonomy: Foundation parts span different knowledge categories that require different validation methods

**Sourced.** "The kinds of knowledge that appear in [engineering] work can be
classified as follows: (1) fundamental design concepts, (2) criteria and
specifications, (3) theoretical tools, (4) quantitative data, (5) practical
considerations, (6) design instrumentalities." (Vincenti, What Engineers Know and
How They Know It, Ch. 7 "The Anatomy of Engineering Design Knowledge," p. 208 of
1990 ed. Category labels reconstructed from Vincenti's taxonomy; exact page
numbers depend on edition.)

**Applied.** Foundation's 10 candidate parts span all six Vincenti categories.
Part 1 (System State Persistence) is category 2 (criteria and specifications —
git commit format, schema validation). Part 3 (KB) is category 5 (practical
considerations — what gets ingested, what gets anchored). Part 4 (Role Taxonomy
& Coordination) is category 1 (fundamental design concepts — the role/executor
split). Part 5 (Compound Learning) is category 6 (design instrumentalities — the
DRR ledger, the compound ritual). Part 6 (Governance) mixes category 2 (the F-G-R
compliance criteria) and category 1 (the AWAITING-APPROVAL as fundamental design
concept). The implication: you cannot validate a category-1 claim (fundamental
design concept) the same way you validate a category-2 claim (specification). A
category-1 claim ("role must be separated from executor") is validated by showing
what system behavior degrades when violated (Popper test). A category-2 claim
("git commit must follow [area] verb what format") is validated by schema compliance
check. Mixing categories without labeling produces interface cards where the
enforcement mechanisms are undefined.

**Applied to Part 5 specifically:** The DRR entry schema is category 2 (a
specification with compliance check). The "compound learning cycle produces
compounding leverage" claim is category 1 (a fundamental design concept that
requires Popper-style observable degradation if absent, not a compliance check).
These require separate validation columns in the interface card.

### P7 — Descartes method / Koen state-of-the-art-method: every methodology entry must declare the method it is derived from AND its known failure modes

**Sourced.** Descartes (Discourse Part II): "The first was never to accept anything
as true which I did not clearly know to be such... The second, to divide each of
the difficulties under examination into as many parts as possible... The third, to
conduct my thoughts in such order that... I might be assured that none was omitted."
(Discourse on Method, Part II, Gutenberg edition.)

Koen (Discussion of the Method, Part II "The Principal Rule of the Engineering
Method"): "Engineering method is the use of heuristics to cause the best change in
a poorly understood situation within the available resources." And: "State of the
art [sota] — the totality of heuristics available to the engineer at a given moment
in time." (Koen, pp. 41–57.)

**Applied.** Foundation Part 5 methodology entries must follow Koen's sota
declaration requirement: each entry names (a) which prior sota it derived from,
(b) the conditions under which it applies, and (c) its known failure modes. An
entry that simply states "use DRR to capture learning" without naming which
alternative learning approaches were rejected (and why), and under what conditions
DRR fails, is not a method declaration — it is a preference. The Descartes
decomposition applied to Part 5: each compound output is decomposed into what was
learned, what remains unknown, and what prior belief was revised. This prevents
the compound phase from becoming an accumulation of confirmations without genuine
learning (the normal-science Kuhn trap: "puzzle-solving" that deepens the existing
paradigm but never surfaces anomalies that would trigger revision).

## §5 Tradeoffs to Surface (Open for Foundation Decision)

### T1 — Popper falsifiability vs. Vincenti category-5 tacit knowledge

Explicit falsifier requirement (P1) vs. genuine tacit engineering knowledge that
resists sharp falsification (Vincenti Ch. 7: "practical considerations" are real
engineering knowledge not codified in textbooks). Applying P1 to all methodology
entries produces false precision in category-5 entries. NOT applying P1 to category-1
and category-2 entries produces unfalsifiable design claims.

**Resolution proposed.** Tag every Foundation claim and methodology entry by Vincenti
category. Apply Popper P1 only to categories 1 and 2. Apply Koen sota + conditions
declaration to categories 3, 4, 5. Apply design-instrumentalities documentation
standard (expose extension points + invariants per D27) to category 6.

### T2 — Kuhn paradigm awareness vs. D27 Fork-and-Merge simplicity

Explicit paradigm declarations in interface cards make Foundation more auditable but
increase complexity for downstream forks (they must understand multiple paradigms
to use Foundation correctly). D27 wants Foundation to be forkable with minimal
understanding of its history.

**Resolution proposed.** Foundation interface cards carry a machine-readable
`paradigm_vocabulary:` frontmatter field (e.g., `{role: FPF-IP-1-definition}`) that
a fork can programmatically check for conflicts with its own paradigm declarations.
The fork does not need to understand Kuhn; it needs to check whether its paradigm
vocabulary is compatible. The paradigm bridge becomes a lint check.

### T3 — Naval specific knowledge vs. D27 forkability (sharing methodology = giving away specific knowledge?)

If Ruslan's methodology library entries ARE his specific knowledge (P4: uncopyable,
competitive advantage), then making Foundation forkable (D27) could mean sharing
the most valuable content.

**Resolution proposed.** Foundation structure is forkable (Vincenti category 1, 2,
6 entries — design concepts, specs, instrumentalities). The specific-knowledge
content that accumulates in the methodology library is RUSLAN-LAYER per IP-2. D27
forks inherit the container and the quality filter; they build their own specific-
knowledge entries. No conflict between D27 and P4 once the Foundation/RUSLAN-LAYER
boundary is correctly drawn at the Part 3 methodology sub-layer.

### T4 — Stoic dichotomy (accept externals) vs. Drucker effective executive (actively manage the boundary)

Stoic P5 applied strictly means the governance part stops trying to enforce
time-based constraints on the founder. Drucker says an effective executive actively
cultivates conditions that make good decisions more likely — which includes
structuring the environment to make founder attention easier to give.

**Resolution proposed.** Part 6 in its B-category (influence zone) is authorized
to design the environment to make founder ack more likely (well-structured
AWAITING-APPROVAL packets, clear summary language, default-deny posture that shows
cost of non-action). This is Drucker's active management of the boundary. Part 6
does NOT set deadlines that halt the system when the founder is unavailable.

## §6 Anti-scope

What this epistemic discipline framework does NOT provide for Foundation:

- **Левенчук constitutional discipline** — structural ontology, typed mereology
  A.14, F-G-R trust calculus as a formal system, BOSC-A-T-X first-triggers, agency-
  CHR J-level decision matrix. These all belong to consultant card #1. This card
  provides the epistemic foundation that makes those mechanisms trustworthy; it does
  not specify the mechanisms themselves.

- **Capital allocation and anti-fragility calculations** — Taleb's barbell, Marks
  second-level thinking applied to investment sequencing, Buffett circle of
  competence applied to market-timing. These belong to consultant card #10. Munger's
  latticework is shared terrain; the boundary is application domain, not source.

- **Engineering implementation judgment** — whether a specific code structure is
  idiomatic, whether a particular abstraction layer is correctly placed. These
  belong to consultant cards #11 (Unix philosophy) and #12 (Clean code). This card
  provides the claim-quality filter; the craft judgment is engineering's.

- **Agent safety hard limits** — Constitutional AI constraints, RLHF/RLAIF
  behavioral limits, Anthropic harm avoidance. These belong to consultant card #13.
  This card provides epistemics for auditing agent claims; it does not set the
  behavioral envelope.

- **Claims about the world external to the Foundation system** — market projections,
  competitive analysis, user behavior predictions. This card disciplines claims that
  Foundation makes about ITSELF; it does not discipline predictions about externals
  (investor-expert scope).

## §8 Open Questions for Foundation

**OQ-STOIC-1.** Which Vincenti category does the "AWAITING-APPROVAL packet pattern"
belong to? If it is category 1 (fundamental design concept), it requires a Popper
falsifier. If it is category 6 (design instrumentality), it requires extension-point
documentation. Both framings are defensible; the choice affects how Part 6's
interface card is validated. Recommend Ruslan ack before Wave C Part 6 interface
card is drafted.

**OQ-STOIC-2.** The Munger latticework P3 calls for a "lollapalooza convergence
score" on methodology entries. This score requires a model-family taxonomy (which
families count as independent for the purpose of convergence detection). No such
taxonomy exists in the current system. Should it be a Part 5 artifact or a
Foundation-level schema in Part 1/Part 3? Recommend systems-expert and engineering-
expert jointly propose the taxonomy in Wave C.

**OQ-STOIC-3.** Naval's specific-knowledge filter (P4) and the D28 anchor-mandatory
discipline are convergent but not yet operationally integrated. D28 checks whether
an entry is anchored to an active query. P4 additionally checks whether the entry
is specific or commodity. Should Part 2 (Signal Ingestion) run BOTH checks at
ingest time, or should P4 be a Part 5 (Compound) step that re-evaluates entries
at compound phase? The entry point matters for implementation.

## §10 Evolution Path

**At current scale (Phase A / €50K):** P1 (falsifiability) and P5 (stoic dichotomy)
are highest priority — they are cheap to apply and immediately prevent the most
common Foundation defects (unfalsifiable claims, unenforceable constraints).

**At €200K (delegated work entering):** P3 (Munger latticework) becomes critical —
the methodology library begins to need quality filtering as more entries accumulate
from delegated cycles. P2 (Kuhn paradigm awareness) becomes critical — new
contributors bring new paradigm vocabularies, incommensurability risk grows.

**At €1M (managed team):** P7 (Koen sota declaration) becomes load-bearing —
methodology entries authored by team members need explicit sota provenance so
future team members can build on them rather than rediscovering. P4 (Naval specific
knowledge) must be formally enforced — the temptation to expand the methodology
