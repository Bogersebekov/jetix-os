## Topic group C — Analogy mapping AI ↔ electricity (3 topics)

**11. One-to-one mapping table (analogy core)**

Proposed correspondence pairs:
- Compute (GPU/TPU) ↔ Electricity (raw kWh)
- Model providers (OpenAI/Anthropic/etc.) ↔ Utility generators (power plants)
- Inference API ↔ Grid distribution
- Methods/agents (Jetix layer) ↔ Appliances + lighting + motors
- AI-using professionals ↔ Electricians + electrical engineers
- Foundation models ↔ Standardized voltage/frequency (60Hz US / 50Hz EU)
- Token economy ↔ Rate structures + load-factor economics (Insull pattern)
- Compute cost compression ↔ kWh price compression 1880s-1930s
- Open-source models ↔ Open standards (NEC code; UL certification)

**12. Where mapping breaks down (disanalogies)**

Hypothesized caveats (to test in Stage 2):
- Electricity is fungible commodity; AI models differ in capability (gpt-4 ≠ claude-4)
- Electricity has near-zero marginal cost of distribution; AI inference has positive marginal cost
- Electricity standards are physical (voltage/frequency); AI standards are emergent + multi-modal
- Workforce parallel partial: electricians trained 4-year apprenticeship; AI-users trained weeks-months
- Geopolitical: electricity grids national; AI models global by default
- Capital intensity asymmetric: electricity = physical infrastructure decades; AI = data center + training months
- Regulatory: electricity heavily regulated 100+ years; AI regulation nascent

**13. Predictive value of analogy**

If analogy holds, electricity precedent predicts:
- AI commoditization curve (kWh price compressed 95% 1880-1930; similar for inference?)
- Standardization emergence (NEC code analog для AI safety + interop)
- Cooperative governance opportunity (REA precedent для AI cooperatives — direct R12 + Mondragón + Jetix parallel)
- Workforce class emergence (electricians as middle-class profession 1900-1950; AI-using professionals?)
- Infrastructure consolidation (Insull holding companies 1920s; AI lab consolidation 2026+?)
