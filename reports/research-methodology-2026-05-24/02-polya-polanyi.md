# Phase 2 — Polya + Polanyi: heuristics + tacit knowledge

> **Цель.** Распаковать **две взаимодополняющие** методологические традиции,
> которые методология Левенчука (Phase 1) acknowledges but does not deep-dive:
> (1) **Polya heuristics** — методология решения задач как явно-сформулированных
> процедур + heuristic-catalog (T2); (2) **Polanyi tacit knowledge** —
> эпистемология навыка + структура «proximal-distal» tacit knowing (T3).
> Together они образуют **методологический фундамент для cohort-transfer
> мастерства** (Jetix Workshop concern).

---

## §0 TL;DR (≤300w)

**Polya «How to Solve It» (1945)** = **first explicit modern methodology of
problem-solving as a teachable discipline**. Канонический Polya состоит из:
(а) 4-фазной процедуры (понять задачу → составить план → выполнить → оглянуться),
(б) каталога 25+ heuristic-prompts ("Have you seen a related problem?",
"Could you restate the problem?"), (в) «двух лиц математики»: rigorous Euclidean
vs experimental/inductive heuristic.
[src: Polya 1945 lines 271-348, 700+]

**Polanyi «Personal Knowledge» (1958) + «Tacit Dimension» (1966)** = **epistemology
of skill**: мы знаем больше, чем можем сказать ("we can know more than we can
tell" — TD line 535). Структура tacit knowing: **proximal-distal** (мы знаем
proximal-term только опираясь на awareness его для attending-to distal-term).
4 аспекта: functional, phenomenal, semantic, ontological.
[src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 535-679 + Personal Knowledge 1958]

**Совместная значимость для методологии:**
- Polya отвечает на «**КАК научить people solve problems**» — explicit heuristic
- Polanyi отвечает на «**ПОЧЕМУ heuristic недостаточно**» — tacit dimension irreducible
- Tension: Polanyi предупреждает, что часть мастерства не передаётся через
  правила; Polya отвечает, что explicit heuristic-questions catalyze recognition
  pattern (Gestalt formation)

**Левенчук-positioning:** Левенчук Methodology 2025 acknowledges tacit knowledge
(line 3543) но believes most can be formalised. Polanyi-position = more skeptical.

**Jetix-implication:** Workshop как метод-transfer pedagogy = explicit Polya
4-phase + Polanyi-aware дисциплина apprenticeship + R12-paired-frame для
power-asymmetry (мастер-ученик).

## §1 Polya «How to Solve It» 1945 — deep decode

### §1.1 Авторитет и место

George Polya (1887-1985) — венгерско-американский математик; Stanford
профессор. Книга 1945 = > 1M sold + 17 языков переводов; A.H. Schoenfeld
(1987): "line of demarcation between two eras, problem solving before and after
Polya." [src: Polya 1945 lines 350-365, Foreword by Conway]

**Эпистемологический интерес:** Polya мотивирован вопросом-молодого-Polya «как
можно изобрести такое решение? как можно открыть такие факты?» — поиск
**generative procedure** для invention/discovery, не только для verification.
[src: Polya 1945 lines 74-90]

### §1.2 Two faces of mathematics

**Verbatim:** *«mathematics has two faces; it is the rigorous science of Euclid
but it is also something else. Mathematics presented in the Euclidean way
appears as a systematic, deductive science; but mathematics in the making
appears as an experimental, inductive science.»* [src: Polya 1945 lines 105-113]

**Implication для методологии:** **«методология в действии»** (mathematics in
the making) ≠ «методология в учебнике» (Euclidean systematic). Левенчук-аналог:
дисциплина методологии (kernel + rules) vs метод мышления методолога (lived
discipline).

### §1.3 Канонический 4-фазный алгоритм

**Verbatim full statement** [src: Polya 1945 lines 273-347]:

#### Phase 1 — Understanding the problem
> «You have to understand the problem.»
> - What is the unknown?
> - What are the data?
> - What is the condition? Is it possible to satisfy the condition?
> - Is the condition sufficient to determine the unknown? Or insufficient? Or
>   redundant? Or contradictory?
> - Draw a figure. Introduce suitable notation.
> - Separate the various parts of the condition. Can you write them down?

#### Phase 2 — Devising a plan
> «Find the connection between the data and the unknown. You may be obliged to
> consider auxiliary problems if an immediate connection cannot be found. You
> should obtain eventually a plan of the solution.»
> - Have you seen it before? Same problem in a slightly different form?
> - Do you know a related problem? Do you know a theorem that could be useful?
> - Look at the unknown! Try to think of a familiar problem having the same or
>   a similar unknown.
> - Here is a problem related to yours and solved before. Could you use it?
>   Could you use its result? Could you use its method?
> - Should you introduce some auxiliary element?
> - Could you restate the problem?
> - If you cannot solve the proposed problem try to solve first some related
>   problem.
> - Could you imagine a more accessible related problem? More general? More
>   special? Analogous?
> - Could you solve a part of the problem?
> - Did you use all the data? Did you use the whole condition?

#### Phase 3 — Carrying out the plan
> «Carry out your plan. Carrying out your plan of the solution, check each
> step. Can you see clearly that the step is correct? Can you prove that it is
> correct?»

#### Phase 4 — Looking back
> «Examine the solution obtained.»
> - Can you check the result? Can you check the argument?
> - Can you derive the result differently?
> - Can you see it at a glance?
> - Can you use the result, or the method, for some other problem?

**Метаструктура:** каждая фаза имеет inquiry-prompts + meta-question (что
делать когда stuck). Стимулирование recognition pattern через verbalised
questioning.

### §1.4 Heuristic как central concept

**Verbatim:** *«This sort of study, called heuristic by some writers, is not in
fashion nowadays but has a long past and, perhaps, some future.»*
[src: Polya 1945 line 102]

**Recovery from disrepute:** Polya intentionally revives "heuristic" из (a)
Pappus of Alexandria (~300 AD; analyse-synthesise procedure), (b) Descartes
Rules for the Direction of the Mind, (c) Leibniz, Bolzano. Не Cartesian
formalism, не Russell Hilbertian formalism — но **plausible-reasoning paradigm**.
[src: Polya 1945 line 3963 + Dictionary of Heuristic entries]

**Polya 2-volume sequel:** «Mathematics and Plausible Reasoning» (1954,
2 volumes: Induction and Analogy + Patterns of Plausible Inference) =
formalisation of plausible-reasoning heuristic.
[src: Polya 1945 line 127-131]

### §1.6 Pedagogical implications

**Master-question-from-teacher:** Polya-discipline = teacher asks heuristic
questions ("Have you seen a related problem?") rather than gives answers.
Student gradually internalises questions; eventually self-prompts.
[src: Polya 1945 lines 640-700 — "First, to help the student... Second, to develop the
student's ability so that he may solve future problems by himself"]

**Pedagogical pathology:** Polya warns against "filling allotted time with
drilling students in routine operations" — kills curiosity. Instead set
problems "proportionate to their knowledge."
[src: Polya 1945 lines 54-60]

### §1.7 Mathematician/teacher dual

Polya recognises pedagogical mastery requires *understanding-in-detail* student
errors. Conway foreword: "to teach mathematics well, one must also know how to
misunderstand it at least to the extent one's students do!"
[src: Polya 1945 lines 389-396]

**Implication для Jetix Workshop:** дидактическое мастерство Workshop-инструктора
требует **deep understanding of common student errors** (не только perfect
self-execution); это **specific deliberate-practice domain** для Workshop роли.

## §2 Polanyi «Tacit Dimension» 1966 — deep decode

### §2.1 Авторитет и место

Michael Polanyi (1891-1976) — венгерско-британский физикохимик-философ. Серия
Terry Lectures (Yale, 1962) → «The Tacit Dimension» 1966; ранее «Personal
Knowledge» (Gifford Lectures 1951-52 → 1958). Сначала achieved fame как
physical chemist (Polanyi reaction); затем философ науки. Brother Karl Polanyi
= economic historian («Great Transformation»).
[src: Polanyi TD 1966 Foreword by Sen lines 107-130]

### §2.2 Central thesis — "we can know more than we can tell"

**Verbatim** [src: Polanyi TD 1966 line 535]:
> «one can know more than one can tell. For the experimental arrangement wards
> off the suspicion of self-contradiction, which is not easy to dispel when
> anyone speaks of things he knows and cannot tell.»

**Empirical evidence (Lazarus-McCleary 1949 + Eriksen-Kuethe 1958):**
- Subjects shown nonsense syllables; certain ones followed by electric shock.
- Subjects develop **anticipation** of shock (sweat, heart-rate) при sight
  shock-syllables, BUT **cannot identify** which syllables.
- "Subception" = elementary mechanism by which knowledge is tacitly acquired.

[src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 530-577]

### §2.3 Structure of tacit knowing — proximal/distal

**Verbatim** [src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 597-602]:
> «in an act of tacit knowing we attend from something for attending to
> something else; namely, from the first term to the second term of the tacit
> relation. In many ways the first term of this relation will prove to be
> nearer to us, the second further away from us. Using the language of anatomy,
> we may call the first term proximal, and the second term distal. It is the
> proximal term, then, of which we have a knowledge that we may not be able to
> tell.»

**4 aspects of tacit knowing** [src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 605-679]:

| Aspect | Definition |
|---|---|
| **1. Functional** | Two-term structure: we know proximal only by relying on its awareness for attending to distal. |
| **2. Phenomenal** | We become aware of proximal in the appearance of distal. |
| **3. Semantic** | Meaning is displaced from proximal towards distal; "the meaning is at the tip of the probe, not in the hand." |
| **4. Ontological** | Tacit knowing comprehends the entity formed by combining the two terms — proximal = particulars, distal = whole comprehensive entity. |

### §2.4 Examples illustrating tacit structure

**Probe / blind man's stick** [TD line 652-665]: "Anyone using a probe for the
first time will feel its impact against his fingers and palm. But as we learn
to use a probe, or to use a stick for feeling our way, our awareness of its
impact on our hand is transformed into a sense of its point touching the
objects we are exploring."

**Implication для skill:** мастерство = transformation of *what was* focal
(impact in hand) *into* subsidiary (relied upon for awareness of distal). This
is **why training transfers difficulty when explicit; ease when integrated.**

**Physiognomy / face recognition** [TD line 604-611]: "we rely on our awareness
of [face's] features for attending to the characteristic appearance of a face."
We cannot list features explicitly but recognise face instantly.

**Skill performance:** "we are aware of [our muscular acts] in terms of the
performance to which our attention is directed."

### §2.5 Connection to Gestalt psychology

Polanyi reframes Gestalt psychology not as «passive equilibration» but as
**active shaping of experience performed in the pursuit of knowledge**. Gestalt
formation = «great and indispensable tacit power by which all knowledge is
discovered and, once discovered, is held to be true.»
[src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 500-510]

**Hierarchy of integration** [src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 510-520]:
1. Scientific and artistic genius (highest tacit power)
2. Expert diagnostician (impoverished form of discovery)
3. Performance of skills (artistic, athletic, technical)
4. Perception (most impoverished form of tacit knowing)

### §2.6 Wissen / Können — Knowing What vs Knowing How

Polanyi acknowledges Ryle's distinction («knowing that» vs «knowing how»;
"wissen" vs "können" в немецком). **Key claim:** "These two aspects of knowing
have a similar structure and neither is ever present without the other."
[src: Polanyi TD 1966 lines 514-520]

**Implication:** knowledge-theory is not bifurcated; declarative and procedural
have same proximal-distal architecture.

### §2.7 «Personal Knowledge» 1958 — additional contributions

(Phase 2 references only highest-level claims; deep dive не требуется для
methodology phase. Polanyi PK 1958 — 21 MB MD / 20873 lines available для cross-
ref future Phases.)

Key extras from PK 1958 относительно TD 1966:
- **Personal commitment** as constitutive of knowledge (anti-positivist)
- **Indwelling** — embodied integration of subsidiary particulars
- **Republic of science** — social structure of knowledge production
- **Faith / belief** as enabling rather than opposing rationality
- **Articulation** — gradient between fully-tacit and fully-explicit

[src: Polanyi PK 1958, primary reference]

### §2.8 Pedagogical implications

**Apprenticeship as only transmission for tacit:**
> «An art which cannot be specified in detail cannot be transmitted by
> prescription, since no prescription for it exists. It can be passed on only
> by example from master to apprentice.» [src: Polanyi PK 1958 (apprenticeship
> chapter)]

**Implication для cohort transfer (Jetix Workshop):**
- Tacit components of метода **cannot** be fully transmitted via written manuals
- Workshop format = apprenticeship-style demonstration + supervised practice
- Mastery validation requires **performance observation**, not knowledge-test

### §2.9 Connection к Polya

**Convergence:** Polya implicitly assumes tacit dimension — heuristic questions
are *prompts* not *algorithms*. Polya teacher leans over shoulder, asks
question; student processes via tacit pattern-recognition.

**Tension:** Polya tries to **make tacit explicit** through heuristic catalogue;
Polanyi insists residual cannot be eliminated. Synthesis = Polya provides
maximum-explicit floor; Polanyi reminds remaining tacit residual ineliminable.

## §4 Pragmatism foundational link — Peirce/James/Dewey

### §4.1 Levenchuk acknowledges «прагматический поворот»

Левенчук M2025 line 243: «После более чем столетней давности прагматического
поворота в философии значения слова «методология» и «метод» продолжали
меняться.» Refers to ~1860-1910 American pragmatism school.

### §4.2 Polanyi inherits pragmatism partially

Polanyi's "Personal Knowledge" — explicitly post-positivist; closely aligned
with pragmatic anti-foundationalism (Peirce fallibilism, Dewey instrumentalism).
Polanyi **rejects** logical positivism but **embraces** experimentalism +
warranted-assertibility (Dewey).

### §4.3 Polya — implicitly pragmatist

Polya's "plausible reasoning" tradition = Peirce abductive reasoning + Mill
inductive logic + Bayesian-like degrees of plausibility. Не explicitly cites
James/Dewey но methodology resonates.

### §4.4 Pragmatism canonical anchors

From Menand «Pragmatism Reader» 1997 (corpus available, 21655 lines), key
contributions для методология relevance:

| Pragmatist | Year | Methodology contribution |
|---|---|---|
| Charles S. Peirce | 1878 «How to Make Our Ideas Clear» | Pragmatic maxim: meaning of concept = practical consequences. Abductive reasoning. |
| William James | 1907 «Pragmatism» | Cash-value: idea is true if it produces successful action. |
| John Dewey | 1938 «Logic: Theory of Inquiry» | Inquiry as adaptive process; warranted assertibility replaces truth. |
| Richard Rorty | 1979 «Philosophy and Mirror of Nature» | Anti-representationalism; conversation as locus of knowledge. |

[src: Menand «Pragmatism Reader» 1997 + Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
entries]

## §5 Combined implications for Jetix-substrate

### §5.1 4-фазный Polya algorithm как Workshop template

Polya 4 phases map to Jetix Hypothesis Architecture 7-layer (Method V2 §10):
- Polya Phase 1 (Understand) ↔ Hypothesis Layers 1-2 (Identify / Frame)
- Polya Phase 2 (Devise plan) ↔ Hypothesis Layers 3-4 (Design / Predict)
- Polya Phase 3 (Carry out) ↔ Hypothesis Layers 5-6 (Test / Observe)
- Polya Phase 4 (Look back) ↔ Hypothesis Layer 7 (Reflect / Update)

[src: Method V2 §10 + Polya 1945 lines 273-347]

### §5.2 Polanyi proximal-distal как frame для CRM trust development

Trust = tacit knowing structure: proximal = our awareness of partner's signals
(reliability, follow-through); distal = comprehensive trust assessment.
Articulating trust criteria explicitly (CRM scoring) may reduce trust quality
(displacing focal attention to particulars instead of whole).
[src: Polanyi TD 1966 + CRM PLAN]

**Implication:** CRM scoring rubrics should be **lightweight + holistic** rather
than exhaustive checklists; preserve Polanyi-style integration capacity.

### §5.3 Heuristic catalogue as ROY swarm prompt-library

Polya's 25+ heuristic catalogue → ROY swarm modes can be instantiated as Polya
questions:
- engineering-expert / critic ↔ "Did you use all the data?"
- engineering-expert / optimizer ↔ "Could you derive the result differently?"
- engineering-expert / scalability ↔ "Could you use this result for other problems?"
- engineering-expert / integrator ↔ "Look at the unknown!" (connect to related)

[src: Polya 1945 Part III + CLAUDE.md ROY swarm table]

### §5.4 Tacit-handling for cohort scaling

Jetix Workshop scaling (1 → 10 → 100 → 1000) faces Polanyi tacit-transmission
constraint: at scale N>~50, master-apprentice format breaks; explicit-formalised
materials dominate; tacit components erode.

**Three mitigation strategies** (substantiated from Polanyi PK 1958 +
literature):
1. **Layered cohort** — small apprenticeship cells (5-7) within larger cohort
2. **Recorded practitioner demonstrations** — externalising at least video-level
   tacit cues
3. **Peer-practitioner dialogue** — fellow-students recreate tacit transmission
   substitute via mutual indwelling

[src: Polanyi PK 1958 + Workshop design substrate]
