## §0 TL;DR (≤300w)

**Schön «Educating the Reflective Practitioner» 1987** + предшественник
«Reflective Practitioner» 1983 = **anti-thesis к technical-rationality model
of professional knowledge**. Central concepts:

- **Knowing-in-action** — embedded/implicit knowing manifest в competent
  performance
- **Reflection-in-action** — mid-action thinking about what you are doing
- **Reflection-on-reflection-in-action** — meta-reflection (after-action)
- **Indeterminate zones of practice** — uncertainty / uniqueness / value conflict;
  где technical rationality breaks down
- **Reflective practicum** — coaching-by-doing pedagogy (architectural design
  studio как prototype); supplements applied-science teaching

[src: Schön «Educating Reflective Practitioner» 1987 lines 398-440, 556-740]

**Ericsson «Deliberate Practice» 1993** = quantitative learning theory:
performance growth requires (1) well-defined task at appropriate difficulty,
(2) feedback, (3) repetition with refinement, (4) coaching, (5) 10000+ hours.
Not «just practice» — needs critical features.

**Merleau-Ponty «Structure of Behaviour» 1942** + «Phenomenology of Perception»
1945 = phenomenology of embodied skill; body-schema as pre-reflective knowing.

**Dreyfus 5-stage Skill Model** (1980): Novice → Advanced Beginner → Competent
→ Proficient → Expert. Phenomenology of stage-transitions.

**Synthesis для Jetix:** Workshop как pedagogy combines (a) Schön reflective
practicum format + (b) Ericsson deliberate-practice discipline + (c) Polanyi-
Polya complementary stack from Phase 2 + (d) Dreyfus stage-awareness для
mastery curriculum.

## §1 Schön «Educating the Reflective Practitioner» 1987

### §1.1 Authority и lineage

Donald Schön (1930-1997) — MIT philosopher / urban planner / OD consultant.
Предыдущая книга: «Beyond the Stable State» (1971), «Theory in Practice» (1974
co-author Argyris), «The Reflective Practitioner» (1983). «Educating the
Reflective Practitioner» (1987) — pedagogical sequel.

Tradition: pragmatism (explicit Dewey citation) + phenomenology + critical
theory.

### §1.2 Anti-technical-rationality stance

**Verbatim** [src: Schön 1987 lines 596-611]:
> «But, as we have come to see with increasing clarity over the last twenty or
> so years, the problems of real-world practice do not present themselves to
> practitioners as well-formed structures. Indeed, they tend not to present
> themselves as problems at all but as messy, indeterminate situations.»

**Civil engineering example:** they know how to build roads suited to particular
sites... «When they must decide *what* road to build, however, or whether to
build it at all, their problem is not solvable by the application of technical
knowledge.» [src: Schön 1987 line 596]

**Implication:** канон professional school = first basic science → applied
science → practicum (apply). Schön: this gets it backwards для indeterminate
zones.

### §1.3 Knowing-in-action

**Verbatim** [src: Schön 1987 line 560]:
> «Knowing-in-action [is] embedded in skillful performance — the embedded
> knowing manifest in the practitioner's intelligent response.»

**Examples:**
- Bike rider doesn't explicitly compute steering angles
- Skilled diagnostician sees pattern before articulating
- Expert chess player recognises board configurations holistically

**Schön: tacit knowing-in-action is the BULK of competent practice.**

### §1.4 Reflection-in-action

When knowing-in-action breaks down (surprise / failure / new situation),
practitioner shifts to **reflection-in-action** — mid-action thinking that
restructures understanding.

> «Reflection-in-action is "thinking what they are doing while they are doing
> it."» [src: Schön 1987 line 398]

**3 features:**
1. Occurs in real-time (mid-action)
2. Restructures situation understanding
3. Generates new strategy on the fly

### §1.5 Reflection-on-reflection-in-action

Meta-level after-action review of one's own reflection-in-action processes.
This is what coaching primarily develops.

### §1.6 Indeterminate zones of practice

**Three types** [src: Schön 1987 line 627]:

| Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Uncertainty** | No clear well-formed problem | Civil engineer "what road to build?" |
| **Uniqueness** | Case outside known categories | Doctor with constellation of symptoms unmatched |
| **Value conflict** | Multiple legitimate ends in conflict | Lawyer with competing client interests |

**Critique of technical rationality:** all three types **escape canons of
technical rationality** — they require **artistry** (Schön's term).

### §1.7 «Rigor or relevance» dilemma

Professional schools face dilemma:
- **Rigor** = technical-rational systematic knowledge (privileged academically)
- **Relevance** = competence in indeterminate zones (what practitioners actually
  face)

Standard curriculum heavily skews towards rigor, sacrifices relevance. Schön's
proposal: **integrate them through reflective practicum.**

[src: Schön 1987 lines 582-700]

### §1.8 Reflective practicum (Schön's positive proposal)

**Definition** [src: Schön 1987 line 413]:
> «The generalized educational setting, derived from the design studio, is a
> reflective practicum. Here, students mainly learn by doing, with the help of
> coaching. Their practicum is "reflective" in two senses: it is intended to
> help students become proficient in a kind of reflection-in-action; and, when
> it works well, it involves a dialogue of coach and student that takes the form
> of reciprocal reflection-in-action.»

**Architectural design studio = prototype.** Variations: master classes in
musical performance, psychoanalytic supervision, counselling-skills seminar
(Argyris/Schön co-taught).

### §1.9 3 coaching models (Schön catalogue)

| Model | Description | Demands on coach | Demands on student |
|---|---|---|---|
| **«Follow me!»** | Demonstration + imitation | High demonstration skill; explicit show-tell | Pattern recognition + reproduction |
| **«Joint experimentation»** | Coach + student jointly invent + test | Critical-question skill; suspending judgement | Active hypothesis generation |
| **«Hall of mirrors»** | Coach mirrors student's process at meta-level | Self-awareness articulation | Reflexive openness |

[src: Schön 1987 lines 415-440]

### §1.10 Jetix-relevance

**Workshop format design = Schön reflective practicum applied.** Cohort sessions
should:
- Privilege **doing** over lecturing (Schön would reject "Workshop as seminar")
- Coach via 3 models contextually (Follow-me для simple SoTA / Joint-exp для
  novel methodology / Hall-of-mirrors для deep meta-reflection)
- Build **reflection-in-action** capability through guided indeterminate-zone
  practice
- Distinguish from systematic-knowledge teaching (= different pedagogical
  format)

**ROY swarm as «hall of mirrors»:** brigadier-dispatch ↔ owner = reciprocal
reflection-in-action between agentic critique + owner reflection.

## §2 Ericsson — Deliberate Practice

### §2.1 Canonical paper

Ericsson K.A., Krampe R., Tesch-Römer C. 1993 «The Role of Deliberate Practice
in the Acquisition of Expert Performance» Psychological Review 100(3):363-406.
Most-cited paper in expertise research.

### §2.2 Definition of deliberate practice

NOT all practice equal. **Deliberate practice** = specific structured activity
with:

1. **Well-defined task** at appropriate difficulty (just beyond current skill)
2. **Immediate feedback** on performance
3. **Repetition** with focused refinement
4. **Coach** или structured curriculum guiding refinement
5. **Effortful** (not automatic); requires concentration

### §2.3 «10000-hour rule» (Gladwell popularization)

Ericsson finding: top performers in many domains (music, chess, sports, science)
accumulated ~10000 hours of deliberate practice by age 20.

**Important:** NOT raw experience; deliberate-practice hours specifically.
Bartender working 30 years may have 0 deliberate-practice hours; pianist
practicing scales mindlessly = also 0. Critical features required.

[src: Ericsson 1993 + Ericsson 2016 «Peak»]

### §2.4 Relation to Schön / Polanyi / Polya

| Tradition | Mechanism of mastery acquisition |
|---|---|
| Polya 1945 | Heuristic-prompting question internalisation |
| Polanyi 1958/66 | Indwelling + apprenticeship; tacit-via-master |
| Schön 1983/87 | Reflective practicum; coaching artistry |
| Ericsson 1993 | Deliberate practice structured curriculum |

**Convergence:** все 4 emphasise (a) structured engagement, (b) coach/mentor
involvement, (c) feedback loop, (d) progressive difficulty.

**Differentiation:** Ericsson — most quantitative; Polanyi — most epistemological;
Schön — most pedagogical; Polya — most domain-specific (math).

### §2.5 Critique Ericsson (literature)

- Hambrick et al 2014: meta-analysis suggests deliberate practice explains
  smaller proportion of expertise variance than original Ericsson estimate
  (~25-35% in many domains)
- Individual differences (working memory, talent) play significant role
- Domain-dependent: deliberate practice strongest predictor in chess + music;
  weaker in sports + sciences

[src: Hambrick D.Z. et al 2014 «Deliberate practice: Is that all it takes to
become an expert?» Intelligence 45:34-45]

**Implication:** Ericsson's framework remains methodologically central but
not monopolistic.

## §3 Dreyfus 5-stage Skill Acquisition Model

### §3.1 Canonical (Dreyfus & Dreyfus 1980, 1986)

| Stage | Characteristics |
|---|---|
| **1. Novice** | Context-free rules; rigid adherence; no situational awareness |
| **2. Advanced Beginner** | Context-dependent recognition begins; aspects of situations noticed |
| **3. Competent** | Choose goals; deliberately plan; emotional involvement in outcomes |
| **4. Proficient** | Holistic recognition; analytical decision-making after pattern-recognition |
| **5. Expert** | Intuitive grasp; situations seen whole; rule-based reasoning falls away |

[src: Dreyfus H., Dreyfus S. 1980 «A Five-Stage Model of the Mental Activities
Involved in Directed Skill Acquisition»; Dreyfus 1986 «Mind Over Machine»]

### §3.2 Key claim — experts don't follow rules

**Verbatim (paraphrased):** "When situations are dealt with in their full
particularity, the expert's choice of action is not governed by rules. ... The
expert proficient performer does not solve problems and does not make decisions;
he does what normally works."

**Implication для methodology:** at expert level, explicit method-rules **fade
into background**; pattern-recognition dominates. This is **convergent with
Polanyi's tacit knowing**.

### §3.3 Implications for cohort scaling

- **Novice cohort** (stages 1-2): explicit rules + checklists + Polya-heuristic
  catalogue = appropriate methodology pedagogy
- **Competent cohort** (stage 3): deliberate-practice cycles + reflective
  practicum + ROY-swarm-style critique = appropriate
- **Proficient/Expert cohort** (stages 4-5): collaboration + dialogue + peer-
  to-peer = appropriate; over-rule-following becomes pathological

**Jetix implication для Workshop:** cohort should be **stage-aware**;
methodology pedagogy varies по stage. One-size-fits-all curriculum will fail
some.

## §4 Merleau-Ponty — phenomenology of embodied skill

### §4.1 «Structure of Behaviour» 1942

Maurice Merleau-Ponty (1908-1961) — French phenomenologist. Two key books:
«Structure of Behaviour» (1942) + «Phenomenology of Perception» (1945). 743 KB
MD available для cross-pulled extracts.

### §4.2 Body schema (canonical concept)

**Body schema** = pre-reflective bodily awareness that organises action.
NOT explicit knowledge of body position; rather, **integrated readiness to
act**. Body schema enables: posture, locomotion, tool-use, skilled performance
без conscious calculation.

### §4.3 Skill as embodied knowing

Mастер performs skill not by **calculating** muscle movements но by **enacting**
body schema. Skill **is** body schema for that domain.

**Convergence с Polanyi:** Merleau-Ponty body schema ≈ Polanyi proximal-distal
structure ≈ Schön knowing-in-action ≈ Dreyfus expert intuition. All point to
the **non-representational dimension of mastery**.

### §4.4 Methodological implication

Sk lessoning что privileges propositional knowledge ("explain the rule before
performing") fights body schema acquisition. Effective pedagogy = **enact-then-
articulate** rather than "articulate-then-enact."

[src: Dreyfus H. «What Computers Still Can't Do» 1992 cites Merleau-Ponty
extensively + cross-pull pmp-1942 corpus]

## §5 Cross-tradition synthesis для applied methodology

### §5.1 4-pillar applied-methodology stack

| Pillar | Lead source | Methodology gain |
|---|---|---|
| **A. Reflective practice discipline** | Schön 1987 | Reflection-in-action capability via reflective practicum |
| **B. Deliberate practice discipline** | Ericsson 1993 | Structured skill-building cycles |
| **C. Embodied skill phenomenology** | Merleau-Ponty 1942/45 | Body-schema-aware pedagogy |
| **D. Stage-aware curriculum** | Dreyfus 1980/86 | Different pedagogy per skill-stage |

**Together:** they answer the question "**how does methodology become lived
mastery**?" Phase 1-3 answered "**what is methodology theoretically**?"

### §5.2 Common error if missing any one pillar

| Missing pillar | Pathology |
|---|---|
| A (no reflective practicum) | Theoretical knowledge + zero artistry in indeterminate zones |
| B (no deliberate practice) | Years of experience but no skill growth ("10 years of 1-year experience") |
| C (no body-schema discipline) | Verbal/cognitive mastery without performance capability |
| D (no stage awareness) | One-size-fits-all curriculum demoralising both novices + experts |

## §7 Pragmatism re-entry

### §7.1 Schön explicitly cites Dewey

Schön 1987 line 596 cites John Dewey 1938 «Logic: Theory of Inquiry» —
"problematic situation" framework. Schön = explicit Dewey continuator.

### §7.2 Pragmatism as methodology — synopsis

| Pragmatist | Methodology contribution |
|---|---|
| Peirce (1878+) | Pragmatic maxim: meaning = practical consequences |
| James (1907) | Cash-value of ideas; truth = workability |
| Dewey (1938) | Inquiry as adaptive cycle (problematic situation → hypothesis → test → reconstruction) |
| Schön (1983+) | Reflection-in-action as Dewey inquiry mid-action |

**Implication:** Schön = pragmatism turned into pedagogical/skill methodology;
Polanyi = pragmatism turned into epistemology of skill.

pragmatic-fallibilist methodology grounded in Peirce/James/Dewey/Schön/Polanyi

## §8 Key takeaways (EP-5 summary)

1. **Schön reflective practicum** = pedagogical antithesis к technical-
   rationality curriculum; design studio model.
2. **Indeterminate zones** (uncertainty / uniqueness / value conflict) = where
   technical rationality breaks; where artistry/reflection required.
3. **Ericsson deliberate practice** = quantitative discipline для skill
   building; 5 critical features (task / feedback / repetition / coach /
   effortful).
4. **Dreyfus 5-stage Skill Model** = critical для **stage-aware curriculum
   design**; one-size-fits-all fails extremes.
5. **Merleau-Ponty body schema + Polanyi tacit + Schön knowing-in-action +
   Dreyfus expert intuition** = convergent thesis on non-representational
   mastery dimension.
6. **4-pillar applied methodology stack** (reflective / deliberate / embodied /
   stage-aware) — missing any one creates pathology.
7. **Jetix Workshop pillar C+D currently weakest** — embodied + stage-aware
   curriculum dimensions are R1 open questions для Phase 8 positioning.
