## §6.1 Henrich — The WEIRDest People in the World (2020)

Joseph Henrich's *The WEIRDest People in the World: How the West Became
Psychologically Peculiar and Particularly Prosperous* (FSG 2020) is the
**anthropology of why some societies trust strangers + cooperate
impersonally + form durable institutions** — and why most don't.

### WD1 — WEIRD = Western, Educated, Industrialized, Rich, Democratic

**Henrich:** *"They were psychologically weird... we wondered if WEIRD
researchers—who entirely dominate the field—..."* [src: henrich-weirdest-
people-2020 line 112-139]

WEIRD societies (post-Reformation Western + their descendants) have a
**distinct psychological profile**:
- Individualism + independence
- Analytical thinking (vs holistic)
- Impersonal trust + impersonal cooperation
- Universal moral rules (vs in-group special rules)
- Patience + future-orientation
- Lower in-group conformity

Most of human history + most of the world's population is **non-WEIRD**.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **CRITICAL DIAGNOSTIC.** R12 substrate (voluntary
opt-in + impersonal trust + universal exit rights) is **structurally
WEIRD**. Jetix-Clan can only function with WEIRD-pattern members.

**Jetix application:** Cohort target ontology O-161/O-162 implicitly
selects for WEIRD-pattern: builder identity, individual commitment,
impersonal cooperation around substrate (not personal loyalty), exit
rights as natural assumption.

---

### WD2 — Kinship-Based Institutions vs Impersonal Institutions

**Henrich:** Most societies organize around **kinship + clan + tribal
loyalty**; trust is *parochial* (in-group only). Western post-Reformation
shift came from **the Church's Marriage and Family Program (MFP)** — a
1000-year campaign that dissolved kinship systems (banned cousin
marriage to 6th degree, banned polygamy, banned arranged marriage, etc.)
[src: henrich-weirdest-people-2020 line 205-206, 675-]

This kinship dissolution forced people to form **non-kin associations**
(guilds, monasteries, universities, voluntary corporations). The
psychological adaptation to non-kin cooperation produced WEIRD
psychology.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **STRUCTURAL OBSERVATION.** Jetix-Clan is **explicitly
a non-kin association** built on impersonal substrate trust. It operates
in the WEIRD substrate and recruits from it.

**Jetix application:** Recognize that R12 is a *WEIRD-substrate construct*.
Members coming from less-WEIRD cultural backgrounds may have
**different default expectations** (more kinship-loyalty patterns, more
personal-leader binding). Welcome-frame must **make WEIRD assumptions
explicit** so non-WEIRD members can choose to adopt them or self-select out.

---

### WD3 — Cumulative Cultural Evolution

**Henrich:** Communities don't just transmit culture; they **accumulate**
it via faithful imitation + selective retention. Each generation can
build on the prior. Innovation is **cultural**, not just individual.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE substrate.** Jetix substrate
(Foundation architecture, F-G-R schema, Method V2, R12 itself) is
**cumulative culture** in Henrich's sense — engineered to accumulate,
not just transmit.

**Jetix application:** Foundation architecture + wiki + methodology are
the **cumulative cultural artefacts**. Every cycle adds; every member
contributes. The substrate compounds.

---

### WD4 — Norm Psychology + Impartial Rules

**Henrich:** WEIRD societies internalize **impartial rules** (apply
equally to all members + outsiders) vs **partial loyalties** (different
rules for in-group vs out-group).

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **DIRECT R12 ALIGNMENT.** R12 itself is an impartial
rule — applied equally to all members and all interactions. The
**impartiality** is what makes fork-and-leave structurally possible.

**Jetix application:** R12 + Foundation rules are impartial. Founder
gets no special treatment under R12. No in-group exemptions. This is
the **structural difference** between Jetix-Clan and a cult (which
operates on partial rules favoring in-group + leader).

## §6.2 Raymond — The Cathedral and the Bazaar (1999)

Eric S. Raymond's *The Cathedral and the Bazaar* (O'Reilly 1999) is
**the empirical decoding of how open-source communities work** — and
implicitly, how any voluntary contribution-based community can scale.
Raymond observed Linux + early open-source and extracted operational
laws.

### R1 — Cathedral vs Bazaar

**Raymond:** Two software development models:
- **Cathedral**: small group of architects builds in private; releases
  finished work; rest are users
- **Bazaar**: open to all contributors; release-early-release-often;
  user = co-developer; bugs surface fast

[src: raymond-cathedral-and-bazaar-1999 — Title essay; introduction
line 32, 86]

Linux succeeded as bazaar; previous open-source-but-cathedral models
(GNU, BSD) had structural limits.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE; BAZAAR = R12 STRUCTURALLY.**

Cathedral structure = small in-group decides; bazaar = open
participation + impersonal cooperation. Bazaar maps to R12 (voluntary
opt-in + impersonal trust). Cathedral maps to extractive default (small
group extracts the credit + control).

**Jetix application:** Jetix substrate must be **bazaar-mode** —
Foundation architecture publicly auditable, contribution open to all
qualified members, no insider-only knowledge tiers, no architect-class
extracting credit from contributor-class.

---

### R2 — Release Early, Release Often

**Raymond:** Linux's velocity came from **aggressive release cadence** —
treating users as co-developers + bug-finders, releasing imperfect
versions to surface problems.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE.** Honesty about substrate state
(imperfect → released anyway) → members can engage with real substrate,
not polished marketing version.

**Jetix application:** Foundation architecture released in F2-F8 grades
(not waiting for "final"); cycle cadence as release rhythm; member
feedback loops as bug-finding.

---

### R3 — Given Enough Eyeballs, All Bugs Are Shallow (Linus's Law)

**Raymond:** *"Given enough eyeballs, all bugs are shallow."*
Crowd-sourced inspection scales better than expert-only inspection.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE; DIRECT R12 SUPPORT.** Public
inspection prevents extraction-hiding. The R12 substrate *requires*
public inspection.

**Jetix application:** Foundation publicly auditable. Decisions DB
public. Cycle outputs public. Anyone can inspect for R12 violations.

---

### R4 — Gift Culture + Reputation Economy

**Raymond:** Hacker community economy is **gift-based** — status comes
from *giving* (good code, good documentation, good help) not from
*receiving*. Reputation is the durable currency.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE; ANTI-EXTRACTION SUBSTRATE.**
Gift culture + reputation economy structurally resists extraction:
extracting beyond share *destroys* reputation, which is the actual
currency.

**Jetix application:** Members get reputation for **contribution** (work
shipped, problems solved, others helped). Reputation is portable
(GitHub-like, public, member-keeps-it-on-exit). Extraction beyond
share would damage reputation more than it could gain — economic
incentive aligns with R12.

---

### R5 — Smart Maintainers + Good Lieutenants

**Raymond:** Bazaar projects scale via **delegation patterns** — core
maintainer + trusted lieutenants for subsystems. Maintainer doesn't
control content but **stewards process + integration**.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE leadership pattern.** Leader =
steward, not extractor. Foundation maintainers (brigadier role,
Ruslan as articulator) operate as stewards.

**Jetix application:** Brigadier role = process steward, not content
extractor. Ruslan articulates substrate; doesn't extract from
contributors. Future cohort leadership = bazaar-style lieutenants.

---

### R6 — Project Forking as Healthy

**Raymond:** Open-source acknowledges **forking as legitimate**. When
disagreements are deep, fork; both versions can succeed; member
identity persists across forks.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **DIRECT R12 ALIGNMENT.** Forking = fork-and-leave
preserved per R12.

**Jetix application:** Jetix-Clan structurally accommodates Clan-fork:
disagreement → fork → both continue → no penalty → reunion possible
later. This is the **structural opposite** of cult shunning (Phase 4
L8).

## §6.3 Srinivasan — The Network State (2022)

Balaji Srinivasan's *The Network State* (self-published 2022) proposes
**online-first parallel institutions** — communities that start in cloud,
acquire shared values + economy + governance + diplomatic standing, and
eventually obtain physical territory + recognition.

### NS1 — The Network State definition

**Srinivasan:** *"A network state is a highly aligned online community
with a capacity for collective action that crowdfunds territory around
the world and eventually gains diplomatic recognition from pre-existing
states."* (Srinivasan's one-sentence formula) [src: srinivasan-network-
state-2022 — TOC line 28-34]

**Seven-step process** Srinivasan outlines [src: srinivasan-network-state-
2022 — TOC line 56+]:
1. Form a startup society
2. Organize into a network union
3. Build trust + cryptoeconomy
4. Crowdfund territory
5. Build digital census
6. Achieve diplomatic recognition
7. Become a network state

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED PATTERN.** The model has strong R12-compatible
elements (voluntary opt-in, online-first, fork-and-leave preserved via
exit-tokens). It also has high-risk elements (high in-group cohesion,
moral-realignment framing, potential for cult dynamics if leadership
extracts).

**Jetix-Clan relevance:** Per memory [Balaji outreach target] —
Workshop Phase 3 ≈ Network State substrate pattern (5 of 7 NS steps
map). Jetix-Clan is a *partial* Network-State substrate, deliberately
**bounded** to avoid the 6th/7th steps (territory + recognition) that
introduce additional R12 risks.

---

### NS2 — Moral Innovation as Founding Move

**Srinivasan:** *"Moral Progress is Moral Innovation is Moral Inversion"*
[src: srinivasan-network-state-2022 — TOC line 250]

Network States found themselves on **a specific moral innovation** — a
value or practice the founders believe the future will look back on as
correct. *"Reunifying Technological and Moral Progress"* [TOC line 245]

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **HIGH-RISK STRUCTURALLY.** Moral-innovation framing
can produce Hoffer-style True Believer dynamics (Phase 4 H1-H4) if not
disciplined. R12 line: is the moral innovation **falsifiable**, or
**sacred**?

**Jetix application:** R12 itself is Jetix's moral innovation — "no
extraction beyond agreed share; fork-and-leave preserved." But R12
must remain **falsifiable** (could be wrong; we'd update) rather than
**sacred** (cannot be questioned = Lifton L5 sacred science violation).

---

### NS3 — Cryptohistory + Cryptographically Verifiable

**Srinivasan:** *"Cryptohistory is Cryptographically Verifiable
Macrohistory"* [src: srinivasan-network-state-2022 — TOC line 109]

Network State persistence requires **verifiable history** — events
recorded immutably + cryptographically signed + checkable by anyone.
Blockchains + immutable archives serve this function.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE substrate.** Verifiable history
makes extraction-hiding harder. The R12 Ethereum substrate (per
RUSLAN-LAYER 2026-05-18 ack) operates this principle.

**Jetix application:** R12 substrate on Ethereum with QF revenue
distribution + Mondragón ratio cap + fork-and-leave exit tokens =
cryptographically verifiable enforcement of R12. Per memory: this is
the Phase 2+ Ethereum overlay binding.

---

### NS4 — Cloud-First Then Land

**Srinivasan:** Build **online-first** — the network exists in cloud
before any physical territory. Physical territory crowdfunded by an
already-coherent online community.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Cloud-first is fine; the territory step
introduces R12 complications (exit becomes harder once one has built
a life in a physical Clan-territory). Jetix-Clan should **stay cloud-
first indefinitely** unless R12 substrate is provably preserved at
territorial scale.

**Jetix application:** Jetix-Clan = cloud-first (Berlin + distributed).
No territorial ambition in v1. Physical events possible (hackathons,
retreats) but participation strictly voluntary + bounded.

---

### NS5 — One Commandment per Network State

**Srinivasan:** Each network state founds itself on **one core moral
commandment** — single-issue parallelism. Too many → coalition fails;
exactly one → people can self-select cleanly.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE design heuristic.** Single
articulated principle = clear opt-in/opt-out signal.

**Jetix application:** Jetix-Clan's single commandment = R12 itself
("no extraction beyond agreed share; fork-and-leave preserved"). All
other principles derivative. Newcomers know in 1 sentence what they're
opting into.
