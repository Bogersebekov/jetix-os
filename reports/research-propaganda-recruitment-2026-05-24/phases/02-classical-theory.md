# Phase 2 — Classical Propaganda Theory

> 5 foundational theorists: Lippmann + Le Bon + Freud + Ellul + Chomsky-Herman.
> R12 paired-frame STRICT — every mechanism surfaced gets explicit
> R12-compatibility verdict + abuse-mode flag + reform path.

## §2.0 Why classical theory matters

Bernays gave Jetix the **practitioner methodology** (Phase 1); these 5
authors give Jetix the **causal mechanism layer** — *why* propaganda works,
under what social conditions, with what cognitive/emotional substrates, and
where the structural escape hatches are. Without this layer, the R12
discipline becomes hand-wavy: you cannot reliably SKIP techniques you don't
understand.

Five distinct lenses:

| Author | Year | Lens | Key contribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lippmann | 1922 | Cognitive | Pseudo-environment + stereotypes + manufacture of consent (term origin) |
| Le Bon | 1895 | Sociological | Crowd as new psychological entity with 5 properties |
| Freud | 1921 | Psychoanalytic | Leader as common ego-ideal; libidinal binding to group |
| Ellul | 1965 | Structural-sociological | Sociological propaganda; pre-propaganda; propagandee-collaborates |
| Chomsky-Herman | 1988 | Political-economy | 5-filter propaganda model of mass media |

## §2.1 Lippmann — Public Opinion (1922)

Walter Lippmann's *Public Opinion* (Harcourt Brace 1922) is the **prior
intellectual scaffold** Bernays explicitly cites (Phase 1 B5). It is the
first systematic treatment of how mass-democracy citizens form opinion under
conditions where they cannot directly observe most of what they have
opinions about.

### L1 — Pseudo-environment

**Lippmann:** Citizens do not respond to the world directly. They respond to
**a picture of the world in their heads** — a *pseudo-environment* —
which is a simplified, mediated, partial reconstruction. *"The world outside
and the pictures in our heads"* (Chapter I title).
[src: lippmann-public-opinion-1922.pdf — Chapter I title at TOC line 106]

**Mechanism:** between citizen and world stands a layer of journalists,
intermediaries, secondhand reports, and the citizen's own prior categories.
Action is taken on the pseudo-environment, but consequences land on the
real environment.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **STRUCTURAL OBSERVATION, NOT TECHNIQUE.** Lippmann is
describing an unavoidable cognitive condition, not prescribing a method.
The R12 question is what mediators are honest about the gap between
pseudo-environment and reality.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix Welcome-frame should **explicitly
acknowledge the pseudo-environment problem**: "here is what you can
verify; here is what you have to take on intermediated word; here are our
sources." Honesty about epistemic distance.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Pretending the pseudo-environment is the real environment
(authority laundering, hidden sourcing, deepfake / synthetic content
presented as primary). Skip.

---

### L2 — Stereotypes (Lippmann's coinage in modern sense)

**Lippmann:** Cognition relies on **stereotypes** — pre-formed mental
patterns that *precede observation*. *"For the most part we do not first
see, and then define, we define first and then see."* (paraphrase of
Lippmann's central thesis in Part III)
[src: lippmann-public-opinion-1922.pdf — Part III "Stereotypes" Chapters
VI-X; TOC lines 119-132]

The stereotype is functionally necessary (without it, cognition is
overloaded). It is also distortive: it filters new information through old
categories, which makes minds **conservative by default**.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **STRUCTURAL CONDITION, NOT TECHNIQUE.** Stereotypes
exist whether or not propagandists exploit them. The R12 question is who
shapes them and whether the shaping is disclosed.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix should **work with the listener's
existing stereotypes honestly** — frame Jetix-Clan as the kind of thing the
listener already has a positive stereotype for ("a cohort of disciplined
hungry builders, like the early YC batches you've heard about") **only when
the match is real.** Never frame the substrate as something it isn't.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Importing stereotype credit from substrates the system
doesn't actually match (false-flag branding). Skip.

---

### L3 — The "Manufacture of Consent" (Lippmann's coinage)

**Lippmann:** *"The manufacture of consent... has improved enormously in
technique, because it is now based on analysis rather than on rule of
thumb. And so... it is no longer possible... to believe in the original
dogma of democracy."*
[src: lippmann-public-opinion-1922.pdf — Part VIII "The Organization of
Intelligence" Chapter XV / per standard pagination ~p 247-248; Chomsky
borrows the phrase in 1988]

Lippmann's stance is **technocratic-elitist** — he concludes that since
public opinion is malleable and citizens cannot directly evaluate complex
policy, expert classes must mediate. This is the **structural antecedent**
of Bernays' "invisible government" claim (Phase 1 B1).

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **POSITION INCOMPATIBLE WITH R12.** Lippmann's
conclusion — expert classes manage public consent — is precisely the
"invisible government" structure that R12 forbids without disclosure +
opt-out. The **observation** (public opinion is malleable + intermediated)
is empirical; the **prescription** (therefore experts manage) is the
R12 violation.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Accept the observation; reject the
prescription. Jetix builds *transparent intermediation* (curators
disclose, members can verify, fork-and-leave is preserved) instead of
*technocratic intermediation* (curators decide, members consume).

**Abuse-mode flag:** Any "expert class manages opinion for the masses'
own good" framing. Skip.

---

### L4 — News vs Truth Distinction

**Lippmann:** *News* is what is reported (signal-of-event); *truth* is the
underlying reality (state-of-world). They are systematically distinct.
News selects events salient to journalism's incentive structure (novelty,
conflict, episode-shape), missing slow truths. [src: lippmann-public-
opinion-1922.pdf — Part VII "Newspapers" Chapter XXII-XXIII]

**Mechanism:** mass-media episodic logic creates predictable distortions.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **DIAGNOSTIC, NOT PRESCRIPTIVE.** Useful for designing
honest communication that resists the distortion (e.g. write durable
documents, not news-cycle bait).

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix prefers **substrate over news** —
canonical documents, signed Lock Entries, durable wikis. News-cycle
posting only as referrers to substrate, never as the substrate itself.

## §2.2 Le Bon — The Crowd (1895)

Gustave Le Bon's *Psychologie des foules* (1895; English trans. 1896
McClure) is the **first systematic treatment of crowd psychology**. Freud
1921 + Bernays 1928 + Hitler's *Mein Kampf* + nearly every 20th-century
propagandist read it. It is short (~200 pp), influential, and **structurally
problematic** (racialist, reactionary). Surface the mechanisms; SKIP the
prescriptions.

### LB1 — The Mental Unity of the Crowd

**Le Bon:** When individuals gather as a crowd, they form a **provisional
new psychological entity** with properties not present in any individual.
The crowd has *mental unity* — convergent thought, convergent action — not
because individuals agree, but because individual reason is **submerged**
in the collective. [src: le-bon-the-crowd-1895 — Book I "The Mind of
Crowds" Chapter I; per Le Bon's standard structure]

**Mechanism:** anonymity (loss of personal accountability) + contagion
(emotion / behaviour spreads rapidly) + suggestibility (heightened
hypnoid state) = a regression to a more primitive cognitive layer.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **EMPIRICAL OBSERVATION, R12-RELEVANT IN DESIGN.** The
mechanism is real (modern social psychology confirms group polarization,
deindividuation, contagion). The R12 question: are we **designing for**
crowd-mode or **against** it?

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix should design *against* crowd-mode
because crowd-mode reliably degrades individual reasoning, which violates
the "voluntary opt-in" leg of R12 (a person in crowd-mode is not exercising
voluntary opt-in, they are exercising contagion). Hence: small cohort
size (Dunbar-bounded), individual-reflection rituals (Part 9 monthly
reflection), explicit decision points outside group-emotional moments.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Events designed to *induce* crowd-mode for the
purpose of extracting commitment (rally / revival / "intensive" with
sleep deprivation). Skip.

---

### LB2 — Contagion / Suggestibility / Anonymity (the 3 mechanisms)

**Le Bon's 3 mechanisms of crowd transformation:**

1. **Anonymity** — individual feels exempt from accountability ("the
   crowd did it, not I")
2. **Contagion** — emotion + behaviour propagates between crowd members
   with limited resistance
3. **Suggestibility** — crowd members are in a "hypnoid" state, easily
   accepting suggestion from a leader or strong stimulus

[src: le-bon-the-crowd-1895 — Book I Chapter II "Sentiments and Morality
of Crowds" per standard structure]

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **ABUSE-FORM IS CORE R12 VIOLATION.** Designing to
maximize all 3 mechanisms = designing to remove voluntary opt-in. Cult
indoctrination camps (Phase 4) operate precisely on this triad.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix design *minimizes* all 3:
- **Against anonymity**: members are named, accountable, durable identity
- **Against contagion**: ritualized individual-reflection moments,
  written-not-shouted commitments
- **Against suggestibility**: 24h cooldown before high-commitment actions,
  documented reasons, opt-out preserved

---

### LB3 — Leaders of Crowds — Prestige + Assertion + Repetition

**Le Bon:** Crowds follow leaders who possess **prestige** (acquired by
position, wealth, victory, or pure assertion); leaders persuade by three
techniques — **affirmation** (simple, repeated, undefended assertion),
**repetition** (same affirmation again and again), and **contagion** (the
affirmation propagates through the crowd). [src: le-bon-the-crowd-1895 —
Book II "The Opinions and Beliefs of Crowds" Chapter III "The Leaders of
Crowds and Their Means of Persuasion"]

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **CLASSIC R12 VIOLATION** in pure form. The triad
explicitly bypasses argument and consent. This is what cult leaders +
demagogues + advertising before consumer-protection eras used.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Replace prestige-assertion with **earned
credibility + transparent reasoning + invitation to scrutinize**. Replace
repetition with **durable documentation** (you can re-read; doesn't depend
on hearing it again). Replace contagion with **individual evaluation
rituals**.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Pure-assertion-by-repetition messaging (motivational-
speaker patterns, MLM hype, cult-leader cadence). Skip.

---

### LB4 — The Universal Properties of Crowd Belief

**Le Bon:** Crowd beliefs are:
- **Simple** (binary, not nuanced)
- **Exaggerated** (intensity > content justifies)
- **Authoritative** (admit no doubt)
- **Intolerant** (no compromise with alternatives)

[src: le-bon-the-crowd-1895 — Book I Chapter III "The Ideas, Reasoning
Power, and Imagination of Crowds"]

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **DEFAULT-INCOMPATIBLE** as design target. Designing
for these 4 properties = designing for cognitive collapse = anti-R12.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix designs **against** all 4:
- **Nuanced** (gradient pricing, gradient trust, gradient commitment)
- **Calibrated** (intensity matches evidence)
- **Falsifiable** (R-low / R-medium / R-high explicit per F-G-R schema)
- **Pluralist** (multiple Clans tolerated; fork-and-leave preserved)

## §2.3 Freud — Group Psychology and the Analysis of the Ego (1921)

Sigmund Freud's *Massenpsychologie und Ich-Analyse* (1921) is the
**psychoanalytic decoding of Le Bon's crowd**. It identifies the *mechanism*
behind crowd cohesion: **libidinal binding to the leader** + **mutual
identification among members** through the shared object.

### F1 — The Group as Libidinally Bound

**Freud:** Groups are not held together by reason or interest alone. They
are held together by **libidinal ties** — emotional bonds of the same kind
(structurally) as those binding child to parent, lover to lover, follower
to leader. [src: freud-group-psychology-1921 — Chapter IV "Suggestion and
Libido" + Chapter V "Two Artificial Groups: the Church and the Army"]

**Mechanism:** the group member loves the leader (and via the leader, the
other members). The bond is pre-rational; it survives evidence against
the leader because evidence is not the substrate.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **STRUCTURAL, R12-RELEVANT IN DESIGN.** All durable
groups have some libidinal substrate; the R12 question is whether the
binding is **transparent + voluntary + reversible** or **engineered for
extraction.**

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix-Clan binding through **shared work,
shared values, shared substrate** (not through founder-charisma). Founder /
Ruslan role MUST be replaceable; the substrate must persist after any
particular leader. **Founder-cult is the R12-violating form.**

**Abuse-mode flag:** Movements where the founder is structurally
irreplaceable; where members' identity is bound to the founder rather than
to the work. Skip.

---

### F2 — The Leader as Common Ego-Ideal

**Freud:** *"A primary group is a number of individuals who have put one
and the same object in the place of their ego ideal and have consequently
identified themselves with one another in their ego."* [src: freud-group-
psychology-1921 — Chapter VIII "Being in Love and Hypnosis", canonical
formula]

**Mechanism:** members substitute the leader for their own super-ego;
mutual identification follows. The group is then a network of *me-like-her*
links anchored through the leader.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **HIGH-LEVERAGE, HIGH-RISK.** Powerful binding;
correspondingly dangerous if leader extracts. Mandatory R12 mitigation:
make the *substrate*, not the *leader*, the common ego-ideal.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Members identify with **the work / the
values / the Clan's mission** as their shared ego-ideal. The founder is
*custodian* of the substrate, not *substitute for it*. Jetix Pillar A
direction-card serves this function: direction is a document, not a person.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Movements where leaving the leader = leaving the
identity (typical cult structure). Skip.

---

### F3 — Identification (mutual + with leader)

**Freud:** Identification is the earliest form of emotional tie; it is
**ambivalent** (love + envy) and **partial** (we identify with one trait
of the object). In groups, members identify with each other *via* shared
identification with the leader/object. [src: freud-group-psychology-1921 —
Chapter VII "Identification"]

**Mechanism:** social cohesion through partial-trait identification creates
in-group/out-group structure: those who share the identification vs those
who don't.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **STRUCTURAL.** All cohesion uses identification. R12
question: does the in-group/out-group boundary serve **honest fit-matching**
(some people genuinely belong, some don't, the boundary is honest) or
**extraction enforcement** (the boundary creates exit cost)?

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix boundary is honest **fit-matching**
(cohort target ontology O-161/O-162). The boundary is between people for
whom the substrate works and people for whom it doesn't. **No exit cost.**
Members who leave remain people; identification is partial, not totalizing.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Movements where leaving = becoming an enemy (the
totalizing identification form). Skip.

## §2.4 Ellul — Propaganda (1965)

Jacques Ellul's *Propagandes* (1962; English 1965 Knopf) is the
**most R12-relevant** of the classical theorists because Ellul makes
explicit the *structural sociology* of propaganda — propaganda is not
something *done to* passive recipients, it is something the recipients
*collaborate in* and *want*.

### E1 — Sociological Propaganda (Ellul's distinction)

**Ellul:** Two propaganda types:

1. **Political propaganda** — direct, intentional, by a state/party for a
   specific outcome
2. **Sociological propaganda** — diffuse, ambient, structural; produced by
   a society about itself (Hollywood films exporting American lifestyle;
   advertising exporting consumer ideology; education exporting national
   self-image)

[src: ellul-propaganda-1965 — Chapter I "Political Propaganda and
Sociological Propaganda" section heading at line 591]

**Mechanism:** sociological propaganda is far more powerful because it is
**not perceived as propaganda**; it is perceived as "the way things are."

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **CRITICAL R12 INSIGHT.** Sociological propaganda is
*the* default R12 violation form — non-consensual distribution at scale
because the recipient doesn't know they're a recipient of propaganda.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix discloses its **sociological footprint**
explicitly: "we are exporting values X, Y, Z; here they are; here is the
substrate; choose in or out." Cannot prevent having a sociological footprint;
can prevent it from being **invisible**.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Cultural-export campaigns presented as "neutral
content" (e.g. branded entertainment without disclosure, "lifestyle"
marketing pretending not to be marketing). Skip.

---

### E2 — Pre-propaganda

**Ellul:** *"Ellul calls 'pre-propaganda'—the conditioning of minds with
vast amounts of incoherent information already disseminated for ostensibly
informative ends... and then providing pseudo-satisfactions for needs they
themselves have helped to create."* [src: ellul-propaganda-1965 — Editor
introduction line 85; Ellul's Chapter II expands]

**Mechanism:** before any specific propaganda message can land, the mind
must be **conditioned** — saturated with prior information that makes the
target receptive. Pre-propaganda creates the need; propaganda answers it.

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **R12 VIOLATION** in the manipulative form (creating
artificial need). Pre-propaganda as **education + context** is fine; as
**need-manufacture** it is anti-R12.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix Welcome-frame O-144 is **pre-onboarding
education** (context, substrate description, expectation-setting) — this is
honest preparation, not need-manufacture. The R12 violation form would be
flooding prospects with content designed to manufacture demand for Jetix.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Content-marketing-as-need-manufacture (vs.
content-marketing-as-honest-attraction). Skip.

---

### E3 — Integration Propaganda vs Agitation Propaganda

**Ellul:** *"Integration propaganda... aims to make the individual
participate in his society in every way..."* vs *"agitation propaganda
[which] leads men from mere resentment to rebellion..."* [src: ellul-
propaganda-1965 — line 71-78 editor summary; Chapter I §"Propaganda of
Agitation and Propaganda of Integration" line 593]

**Integration** = continuous, lifelong, identity-shaping (the dominant
form in stable societies — what most of Western media does).
**Agitation** = episodic, mobilization-oriented (typical of revolutionary
movements).

**R12 verdict:** Both are R12-relevant; integration is more dangerous
because it's continuous and invisible.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix is structurally an **integration**
substrate (continuous cohort identity formation), so the R12 discipline
must be *especially* strict — every integration moment has higher cumulative
weight than a single agitation moment. Hence: disclosure at every onboarding
step, opt-out preserved at every level, fork-and-leave structurally
embedded.

---

### E4 — The Propagandee Wants Propaganda

**Ellul's most counterintuitive claim:** In mass society, individuals
*want* propaganda. They want their information pre-digested, their opinions
pre-formed, their identity pre-shaped — because forming them from raw is
exhausting. Propaganda fills a real psychological need.
[src: ellul-propaganda-1965 — Chapter III "The Conditions for the Existence
of Propaganda" subsection "The Needs Propaganda Satisfies", per Ellul's
standard structure]

**Mechanism:** the cognitive load of forming independent opinion on
hundreds of topics in mass democracy exceeds individual capacity. People
gratefully accept pre-formed packages.

**R12 verdict:** **DIAGNOSTIC, NOT PRESCRIPTIVE.** This explains *why*
propaganda works — but does not justify it. R12 question: do we satisfy the
need *honestly* (giving users transparent pre-digestion they can audit) or
*dishonestly* (giving them pre-digestion that serves the digester)?

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix admits members **want** the convenience
of pre-formed opinion — and provides it **transparently** ("here is our
take; here is our reasoning; here are the alternative takes; here is how
to verify yourself"). Reduces cognitive load *while preserving
verifiability*.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Pre-digested opinion presented as the only truth, or
without disclosure of source. Skip.

## §2.5 Chomsky-Herman — Manufacturing Consent (1988)

Edward Herman + Noam Chomsky's *Manufacturing Consent: The Political
Economy of the Mass Media* (Pantheon 1988) extends Lippmann's term in the
opposite political direction — Lippmann saw "manufacture of consent" as
necessary; Chomsky-Herman frame it as a systematic distortion of mass
democracy. The book proposes a **5-filter propaganda model** explaining
how mass-media outputs converge on elite-favorable narratives without
overt censorship.

### CH1 — The 5 Filters of the Propaganda Model

**Chomsky-Herman:** Mass media output passes through 5 filters that
select for elite-compatible content even when no individual decision-maker
intends propaganda outcomes:

1. **Filter 1 — Ownership** — media owned by large corporations with
   their own interests
2. **Filter 2 — Advertising** — primary revenue source = advertisers, not
   readers; content must not antagonize advertisers
3. **Filter 3 — Sourcing** — journalists depend on official sources
   (govt, corp PR) for content; cannot bite the hand
4. **Filter 4 — Flak** — coordinated negative response to off-consensus
   content; produces self-censorship
5. **Filter 5 — Anti-communism / "the enemy"** — ideological frame that
   delegitimates dissent (in 1988 = anti-communism; later updated to
   anti-terrorism, etc.)

[src: chomsky-herman-manufacturing-consent-1988.pdf — Chapter 1 "A
Propaganda Model" line 156-208; updates at line 218+; filter 1 line 2081;
filters summary line 2047-2050]

**Mechanism:** no individual filter requires conscious intent; their
*combined structural effect* produces propaganda-shaped output. The
output looks like neutral journalism to insiders; looks like systematic
distortion to outsiders.

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **CRITICAL DIAGNOSTIC FOR R12.** Even *unintentional*
structural propaganda is an R12 problem — extraction beyond share happens
even without anyone deciding to extract. Hence structural design must
**pre-empt** these filters, not assume good intentions.

**R12-compatible re-coding for Jetix:**

| Filter | Jetix mitigation |
|---|---|
| **Ownership** | No single owner of Jetix substrate; LOCK-A documents publicly auditable; member-cooperative-owned per R12 substrate |
| **Advertising** | No advertiser revenue model; revenue from members directly per agreed share (R12 substrate) — eliminates the filter |
| **Sourcing** | No "official PR sources" dependency; primary sources are member-generated work artifacts |
| **Flak** | Critic-mode preserved structurally (debate as norm, dissent protected, fork-and-leave preserved) |
| **Enemy frame** | No external-enemy frame in identity construction (Phase 4 will deepen this re: Hoffer's "common devil") |

Each of the 5 filters has a structural R12-compatible counter. Building
without these counters reproduces the propaganda model **even with good
intent.**

---

### CH2 — Flak Production

**Chomsky-Herman:** *"...to provide 'experts' to confirm the official
slant on the news, and to fix the premises of discourse..."* [src:
chomsky-herman-manufacturing-consent-1988.pdf line 174-179]

Flak = coordinated negative pressure on outlets that deviate; produces
self-censorship even without overt censorship.

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **R12 VIOLATION FORM** in the offensive use
(weaponizing flak to suppress critics). **R12-compatible** in defensive
use (preparing for, transparently responding to, flak attempts against
the substrate).

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix expects flak from existing extractive
substrates that R12 makes obsolete (e.g. MLM industry, opaque-share
platforms). Prepare: document substrate; preserve fork-and-leave;
transparent response protocols.

---

### CH3 — Advertising as Primary Revenue (Structural Filter)

**Chomsky-Herman:** When ads are primary revenue, the audience is the
*product*, not the customer. Content optimizes to deliver audience to
advertisers, not value to audience. [src: chomsky-herman-manufacturing-
consent-1988.pdf — Chapter 1 line 232+ on advertising trends; line 2047
filter list]

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **STRUCTURAL R12 VIOLATION** built into the business
model. Audience-as-product = extraction beyond share by definition.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix structural choice: **no advertiser
revenue**. Member-direct revenue per agreed share preserves R12.

---

### CH4 — Updating the Propaganda Model (Chomsky-Herman 2002 introduction)

The 2002 introduction notes the model **strengthens** under deregulation
+ concentration + algorithmic amplification. Filter mechanisms apply to
social media, just with different surface forms. [src: chomsky-herman-
manufacturing-consent-1988.pdf line 218+ "Updating the Propaganda Model"]

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **MORE RELEVANT NOT LESS** in algorithmic media age.
Algorithmic feeds operate the 5 filters at far higher throughput than
1988 mass media.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix avoids algorithmic feed substrate
where members are the product. Direct member relationship (Welcome-frame
+ cohort + work) maintained as the substrate.

## §2.6 Cross-author synthesis — the converged classical theory

Five lenses converge on a single structural picture:

```
LIPPMANN  → cognition uses pseudo-environment + stereotypes
           → opinion is mediated, not direct
LE BON    → in crowds, individual reason regresses to contagion + suggestion
FREUD     → groups bind libidinally through identification with leader
ELLUL     → propaganda is sociological + invited by propagandee + integrative
CHOMSKY   → mass media structurally propagandizes even without intent
```

**Converged claim:** Mass-democracy opinion formation is **structurally
non-direct**, **structurally mediated**, **structurally susceptible** to
manipulation by anyone who controls intermediation. This is **not a bug**
of mass democracy, it is a **feature** of cognition + scale.

The R12 implication is sharp: **you cannot eliminate intermediation**; you
can only choose **whether intermediation is transparent + opt-out-preserving
or opaque + extractive.** R12 is the discipline of the former.
