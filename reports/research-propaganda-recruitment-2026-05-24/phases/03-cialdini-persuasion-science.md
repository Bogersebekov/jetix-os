# Phase 3 — Cialdini Persuasion Science

> Modern empirical persuasion. 6 (now 7) principles. R12-prescreened.
> Where Bernays and the classical theorists gave us *mechanisms*, Cialdini
> gives us *experiments + replicated principles* — the only large-scale
> empirical decoding of compliance in the literature.

---

## §3.0 Why Cialdini

Robert Cialdini's *Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion* (1st ed.
Quill / William Morrow 1984; standard 5th ed. Allyn & Bacon 2009) is
**the most replicated, most teachable, most operational** persuasion
framework. Cialdini spent ~3 years undercover in sales, fundraising,
recruiting, and "compliance professional" trainings before writing it.
Six principles. Each principle has 10+ experimental confirmations. Each
principle has 10+ field examples of misuse.

*Pre-Suasion* (Simon & Schuster 2016) is the **temporal precursor decoding**
— the persuasion that happens *before* the persuasive message arrives, by
shaping which mental categories are activated. Adds **Unity** as the 7th
principle (groupness as a persuasion lever).

Cialdini is **doubly R12-relevant**:

1. Every principle has a "compliance professional misuse mode" Cialdini
   *himself* documents — making R12 SKIP-paths well-marked
2. Every principle has an "ethical use mode" Cialdini also documents —
   making R12-compatible imports well-marked

R12 discipline here is sharper than for Bernays: Cialdini already gives us
the dual framing.

## §3.1 The 6 Principles (Cialdini 1984)

### C1 — Reciprocation

**Cialdini:** *"...the rule for reciprocation... says that we should try
to repay, in kind, what another person has provided us."* [src: cialdini-
influence-1984 — Chapter 2 "Reciprocation" line 668-678]

The reciprocation rule is **universal across human cultures** (per
anthropological survey Cialdini cites). It is so structurally embedded
that **even an unsolicited, unwanted gift produces obligation to
reciprocate.** Classic field: Hare Krishna flower → solicit donation;
free sample → purchase; favor done → favor returned.

**3 sub-rules Cialdini documents:**
- Reciprocation works **even when the initial favor is unwanted**
- Reciprocation triggers **disproportionate return** (small gift → larger
  return)
- Reciprocation enables **rejection-then-retreat** (start with big ask;
  retreat to actual ask; the retreat is perceived as a concession that
  demands reciprocation)

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Genuine reciprocity is the social fabric;
**manufactured obligation** is the R12 violation.

- Genuine gift → genuine reciprocity → ✅ R12-compatible
- Unsolicited gift designed to produce obligation → ❌ R12 violation
  (manufactured fork-prevention via guilt)

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix Welcome-frame gives value (Method V2
§J meta-method, public wiki, free events) **without ledger**. The R12
violation form would be: "we gave you the wiki, now you owe us." The
R12-compatible form: "we gave you the wiki, take it, contribute back if
and only if it fits."

**Abuse-mode flag:** "Free consultation that creates obligation",
unsolicited gifts with implicit ask attached, rejection-then-retreat
sales sequences. Skip.

### C2 — Commitment and Consistency

**Cialdini:** *"It is, quite simply, our nearly obsessive desire to be (and
to appear) consistent with what we have already done."* [src: cialdini-
influence-1984 — Chapter 3 "Commitment and Consistency: Hobgoblins of the
Mind" opening]

Once we commit to a position — particularly **in writing, publicly,
voluntarily, effortfully** — we feel pressure to act consistently with that
commitment. This is the engine of:
- **Foot-in-the-door** technique (small ask → larger ask)
- **Lowball** technique (commit to a deal, then change terms; few back out)
- **Public commitment** at rallies / pledges / "I am a customer" signs
- **Initiation rituals** (sunk-cost binding — Phase 4 will show how cults
  weaponize this)

**4 commitment-strengthening conditions Cialdini documents** [src:
cialdini-influence-1984 — Chapter 3 §"How They Cash In"]:
1. **Active** (writing > saying)
2. **Public** (witnessed > private)
3. **Effortful** (high-cost > low-cost)
4. **Self-attributed** (chosen-feeling > coerced-feeling)

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **HIGH-RISK; CULTS LIVE HERE.** Every cult initiation
(Lifton 1961 / Hassan 1988 — Phase 4) is engineered to satisfy all 4
conditions because the resulting commitment is **structurally
exit-resistant**. This is the canonical fork_prevention_attempt R12
violation class.

**R12-compatible re-coding:**
- **Commitments must be reversible** — Jetix Clan membership: written
  yes, public yes, effortful yes — **but** structurally reversible without
  shaming or penalty. Quarterly opt-out check-in. Fork-and-leave preserved
  per R12.
- **Effort must produce real value** — initiation isn't sunk-cost-for-its-
  own-sake; effort goes into work artifacts the member keeps even if
  they leave (their writing, their code, their network).
- **Commitment scaled to evidence** — small commitments → small evidence;
  larger commitments → larger evidence already gathered. No foot-in-the-
  door pressure ladders.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Engineering all 4 conditions to maximize exit-
resistance. Foot-in-the-door sales sequences. Lowball pricing. Public
commitment without reversibility. Skip — these are core cult patterns.

### C3 — Social Proof

**Cialdini:** *"We view a behavior as more correct in a given situation to
the degree that we see others performing it."* [src: cialdini-influence-1984
— Chapter 4 "Social Proof: Truths Are Us" opening]

In uncertainty, we follow the crowd. Strongest under 2 conditions Cialdini
documents:
- **Uncertainty** (unclear what to do)
- **Similarity** (the others are like us)

Classic field cases: laugh tracks (manufactured laughter → more laughs);
"bestseller" labels (sales → more sales); long lines (long line → more
joiners); testimonials from similar-demographic users.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED — manufactured form is core R12 violation.**

- **Honest social proof** (members really do build, here is what they
  built, here are their names + work artifacts) → ✅ R12-compatible
- **Manufactured social proof** (fake reviews, paid testimonials,
  cherry-picked cases, fake follower counts) → ❌ R12 violation
  (non_consensual_distribution if testimonials weren't consented; pure
  fraud if fabricated)

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix social proof = **verifiable work
artifacts** (member-built projects, published research, demos with names
attached). Anyone can audit the proof. No paid testimonials.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Fake reviews, paid testimonials, cherry-picked cases
presented as representative, fabricated NPS, bought followers. Skip.

### C4 — Liking

**Cialdini:** *"We most prefer to say yes to the requests of someone we
know and like."* [src: cialdini-influence-1984 — Chapter 5 "Liking: The
Friendly Thief" opening]

**5 components of liking that compliance professionals exploit:**

1. **Physical attractiveness** (halo effect — pretty = persuasive)
2. **Similarity** (we like people like us → comply with them more)
3. **Compliments** (praise increases liking even when transparent)
4. **Contact and cooperation** (familiarity → liking; shared-goal work →
   bonding)
5. **Association** (positive things linked to the messenger transfer to
   the message)

[src: cialdini-influence-1984 — Chapter 5 sub-sections]

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **HIGH-LEVERAGE; ABUSE-FORM IS CORE R12 VIOLATION.**

Liking is real and the social fabric runs on it. The R12 line:
- Genuine warmth, real similarity, honest cooperation → ✅ compatible
- Engineered similarity (matched salesperson, manufactured commonality,
  "we share so much!" scripts), strategic compliments, paid attractive
  models presenting product → ❌ R12 violation (engineered consent)

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix cohort fit-matching is **honest
similarity** — actual values + actual stage + actual disciplinarity
overlap. Welcome-frame uses real shared substrate, not manufactured.

**Abuse-mode flag:** "Mirror and match" sales tactics, scripted
compliments, salesperson assigned by demographic match to target. Skip.

### C5 — Authority

**Cialdini:** *"...the strong pressure in our society for compliance with
the requests of authority..."* [src: cialdini-influence-1984 — Chapter 6
"Authority: Directed Deference" — references Milgram experiments
extensively]

Authority operates **even when the authority's domain is irrelevant** to
the ask. Three authority cues Cialdini documents:
- **Titles** (Dr., Prof., CEO)
- **Clothing** (uniforms, suits, lab coats)
- **Trappings** (luxury cars, large desks, prestige addresses)

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Genuine authority is functional (we should
defer to expertise in expertise's domain). Manufactured authority is R12
violation.

- Real domain authority, transparently sourced → ✅ R12-compatible
- Costume authority, transferred authority (sports star endorsing
  unrelated product), borrowed prestige → ❌ R12 violation

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix Pillar A direction-cards cite F-G-R
graded sources (Formality + Group-scope + Reliability per `shared/schemas/
f-g-r.json`). Authority is **claimed only in the domain it actually
covers** and **graded for reliability**. No costume authority.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Sports-star endorsements outside sport, celebrity
testimonials outside celebrity's domain, fake credentials, borrowed
prestige (logos of clients on websites that never used the product).
Skip.

### C6 — Scarcity

**Cialdini:** *"Opportunities seem more valuable to us when their
availability is limited."* [src: cialdini-influence-1984 — Chapter 7
"Scarcity: The Rule of the Few" opening]

**Two scarcity sub-mechanisms Cialdini documents:**

1. **Loss aversion** (avoiding loss feels stronger than obtaining gain;
   limited supply = potential loss)
2. **Psychological reactance** (when freedom-to-choose is restricted,
   desire intensifies)

Classic field: "limited time offer", "only 3 left in stock",
"invitation-only", "deadline at midnight", "members-only".

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **DEFAULT-INCOMPATIBLE** in manufactured form.
**Manufactured scarcity** = engineered cognitive pressure = R12 violation
(extraction via urgency manipulation).

- Real scarcity (genuinely limited cohort size, genuine deadlines tied
  to substrate, genuine capacity limits) → ✅ R12-compatible (but use
  sparingly; default to abundance framing)
- Manufactured scarcity (fake countdown timers, "limited spots" when
  spots aren't limited, fake "members-only" when membership is open) →
  ❌ R12 violation

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix cohort caps are **real** (Dunbar-
bounded per cohort target ontology O-161/O-162). Communication uses
**abundance framing** by default ("come build with us; the substrate is
permanent; here's how to join when you're ready") not scarcity pressure.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Manufactured deadlines, fake countdown timers, fake
"last spots", false-invitation scarcity, MLM "next batch closes Friday".
Skip — this is the most-abused Cialdini principle in 2020s marketing.

## §3.2 Cialdini's "Click-Whirr" — Automatic Compliance Patterns

**Cialdini:** Most compliance happens via **fixed-action patterns** —
when a trigger is present, the response runs automatically without
deliberative thought. The 6 principles are the most common triggers.
*"Click, whirr"* — trigger fires, response runs. [src: cialdini-influence-
1984 — Chapter 1 "Weapons of Influence" lines 35-50, 443-]

The compliance professional's craft: identify trigger that fires the
target's desired response; deliver trigger. This is **structurally
adversarial** to the target's deliberation.

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **THE CLICK-WHIRR EXPLOITATION ITSELF IS R12 VIOLATION.**
Deliberately triggering automatic patterns to bypass deliberation = bypass
of voluntary opt-in.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix designs **against** click-whirr
exploitation:
- 24h cooldown before high-commitment decisions
- Written reasoning required before joining
- Multi-stage Welcome-frame with explicit pause points
- Members may withdraw from any decision within reflection window

**Abuse-mode flag:** High-pressure same-day decisions, "act now" framing,
sales sequences that bypass reflection. Skip.

## §3.3 Pre-Suasion (Cialdini 2016)

*Pre-Suasion: A Revolutionary Way to Influence and Persuade* extends the
1984 work backwards in time — what happens **before** the persuasive
message arrives.

### PS1 — Attention as Cause

**Cialdini:** Whatever a person is attending to **at the moment of
decision** is perceived as more important than it is. This is a robust
priming effect — *"the importance excessive given to an idea once
attention is oriented toward it"*. [src: cialdini-pre-suasion-2016 —
Chapter 3 "L'importance de l'attention" line 643-648 (FR translation)]

**Mechanism:** the persuasion **before** the persuasion: shape what the
target is attending to in the moment leading up to your message; their
decision then weights your message disproportionately.

Field examples Cialdini documents:
- Asking "are you adventurous?" before pitching → more yes-responses
  than pitching without primer
- Showing French flag-imagery before wine-tasting → more selection of
  French wine
- Asking about safety concerns before pitching insurance → higher
  conversion

**R12 verdict:** ❌ **HIGH-RISK PRIMING; R12 VIOLATION IN OPAQUE FORM.**

The priming itself is invisible to the target — they think they made a
spontaneous choice. This is **engineered consent that the target cannot
even know about** (worse than Bernays' "engineering of consent" because
the engineering happens *inside the target's pre-decisional attention
state*).

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Disclose the framing. Welcome-frame says:
"we're going to ask you about X, Y, Z; we orient attention that way
because A, B, C; we know this shapes your decision; here's the reasoning."
Explicit pre-suasion = ethical Cialdini-Pre-Suasion = honest framing.
Hidden pre-suasion = R12 violation.

**Abuse-mode flag:** Hidden priming surveys, ambient cue manipulation,
"casual" opener questions designed to prime specific responses. Skip.

---

### PS2 — Privileged Moments

**Cialdini:** *Pre-suasion* is most effective in **privileged moments** —
when the target's attention is naturally focused (after a question,
during a transition, during high-emotion moments).

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **AMPLIFICATION OF PS1.** Same R12 grade — opaque
exploitation of privileged moments = violation.

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Use privileged moments **honestly** —
explicit "now is the moment to reflect on whether this fits you" framing
during onboarding's natural attention peaks. The privileged moment becomes
a structured reflection point, not a hidden compliance window.

### PS3 — Unity (7th Principle)

**Cialdini 2016:** Adds **Unity** as 7th principle: "we" is more powerful
than "like-me". When persuader establishes **shared identity** with
target (kinship, locality, common-ingroup), compliance increases beyond
mere similarity. [src: cialdini-pre-suasion-2016 — Chapter 8 "Unity"
section, per book structure]

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **HIGH-LEVERAGE; HIGH-RISK.** Identity-binding is
both the substrate of Jetix-Clan cohesion AND the substrate of cult
recruitment. R12 line:

- Genuine shared identity (Jetix-Clan members really do share
  hungry+disciplined+ready profile per O-161/O-162) → ✅ compatible
- Manufactured we-frame ("we, the chosen few"; "we against the world";
  in-group-with-no-real-substrate) → ❌ R12 violation

**R12-compatible re-coding:** Jetix Unity is **substrate-grounded** — the
"we" refers to a real cohort with verifiable shared work, not a
manufactured community of consumers.

**Abuse-mode flag:** "We the special few" cult framing, "us vs them"
identity construction without real shared work, manufactured in-group
identity (typical MLM, typical cult, typical political identity
weaponization). Skip.

## §3.6 The Cialdini Discipline Test (operational check)

Before any Jetix communication, run the **Cialdini Discipline Test**:

1. **Reciprocation test**: Is anything in this communication structured
   to create unrequested obligation? If yes → revise.
2. **Commitment test**: Are we asking for commitment scaled to evidence
   provided? Is the commitment reversible? If no → revise.
3. **Social proof test**: Is every testimonial / claim about other members'
   experience verifiable by the reader? If no → revise.
4. **Liking test**: Are we manufacturing similarity / scripting warmth?
   If yes → revise.
5. **Authority test**: Is every authority claim in its domain, F-G-R
   graded, reliability-stated? If no → revise.
6. **Scarcity test**: Is scarcity real? If manufactured → revise.
7. **Unity test**: Is the "we" substrate-grounded? If manufactured → revise.
8. **Pre-Suasion test**: Is any attention-priming disclosed? If hidden →
   revise.

8-point discipline check. Any failure = revise or skip.
