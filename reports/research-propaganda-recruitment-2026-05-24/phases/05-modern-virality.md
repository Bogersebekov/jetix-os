## §5.1 Greene — 48 Laws of Power (1998)

Robert Greene's *48 Laws of Power* (Viking 1998) compresses Machiavelli +
Sun Tzu + court-strategist + con-artist + military tactician traditions
into 48 named laws. **The book is intentionally amoral** — Greene
explicitly frames it as descriptive ("this is how power operates") not
prescriptive. But the laws-as-techniques are overwhelmingly **extractive +
opaque + manipulative** by design — they SKIP heavily under R12.

## §5.2 Heath — Made to Stick (2007) — SUCCESs Framework

Chip + Dan Heath's *Made to Stick: Why Some Ideas Survive and Others Die*
(Random House 2007) decodes message stickiness via **6 properties** —
SUCCESs (one letter dropped from acronym):

### SU1 — Simple

**Heath:** *"Simple = core + compact"* [src: heath-made-to-stick-2007 —
Chapter 1 "Simple" lines 32-35]

**Mechanism:** strip message to its **core**; deliver in **compact form**
(commander's intent, proverb, one-sentence summary).

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible.** Simplification is service to the
receiver. (Note: oversimplification that distorts substance is R12
problematic — but that's an integrity test, not a SUCCESs problem.)

**Jetix application:** Welcome-frame compresses to commander's intent.
Direction cards distill to one core line. R12 itself: "no extraction
beyond agreed share; members can fork-and-leave without penalty" —
compact compact.

---

### SU2 — Unexpected

**Heath:** *"The successful flight safety announcement. The surprise
brow."* [src: heath-made-to-stick-2007 — Chapter 2 "Unexpected" lines 40-43]

**Mechanism:** break the expected pattern → attention. Then maintain
interest by creating **knowledge gap** (the listener wants resolution).

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible** when honest. Unexpected truths are
fine; manufactured shock is SKIP (Cialdini-Greene tactic, not Heath).

**Jetix application:** Welcome-frame's unexpected element — "this is not
a course / not a cohort program / not a network in the standard senses;
it's an X" — surprise gap → reader engages.

---

### SU3 — Concrete

**Heath:** Make ideas **sensory + specific** — "stop the bleeding" beats
"improve outcomes"; "a high school dropout" beats "a person with limited
educational attainment".

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible + R12-supportive.** Concreteness
prevents the abstract-language fog that lets extraction hide.

**Jetix application:** F-G-R schema is concretizing discipline. Every
claim has stated Formality + Group-scope + Reliability. No abstract
"trust me" claims.

---

### SU4 — Credible

**Heath:** *"Testable credentials. The Emotional Tank. Clinic."* [src:
heath-made-to-stick-2007 — Chapter 4 "Credible" lines 57-64]

**Mechanism:** ideas stick when listener can **test** them. Various
testable forms: external authority + anti-authority (independent voice
matching the message) + statistics (concrete) + Sinatra test (if it can
make it there...) + testable claim ("try it yourself: ...").

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible.** Verifiability is R12 itself in
discipline form.

**Jetix application:** Foundation architecture publicly auditable; F-G-R
graded claims; member-portable work artifacts that prove the substrate
works.

---

### SU5 — Emotional

**Heath:** *"People care about people, not abstractions."* Connect to
identity, not just utility.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Emotional connection is fine when **honest
emotion** is involved; manufactured emotion (Cialdini liking abuse,
Hassan BITE-E love-bombing) is SKIP.

**Jetix application:** Connect to builder-identity ("you, who ships,
who builds, who finishes") not to manufactured aspirational identity
("become the chosen few"). Honest stake in real outcomes.

---

### SU6 — Stories

**Heath:** Stories are the **simulation substrate** for human cognition;
listeners experience the story as if it happened to them; behavioral
priming results.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible** when stories are accurate. The
R12 line: case study representing what actually happened vs.
hero-narrative fabricated for marketing.

**Jetix application:** Member case studies (with member consent, factual,
specific). Founder origin story (factual). No fabricated testimonials.

## §5.3 Berger — Contagious (2013) — STEPPS Framework

Jonah Berger's *Contagious: Why Things Catch On* (Simon & Schuster 2013)
isolates **6 drivers of word-of-mouth sharing**:

### ST1 — Social Currency

**Berger:** people share what makes them **look good** to others.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Engineering sharing-for-social-currency
is fine if substrate is real (people share because the work is
genuinely impressive); manipulative if substrate is fake.

**Jetix application:** Members share Jetix work because it makes them
look like serious builders. Real substrate → honest social currency.

---

### ST2 — Triggers

**Berger:** ideas spread when **environmental cues trigger recall**.
Kit Kat + coffee (recall trigger); Friday → "Rebecca Black Friday" song.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible** (engineering frequency-of-recall
is fine if substrate genuinely supports recall).

**Jetix application:** Cycle cadence (weekly digest, monthly reflection)
creates natural recall triggers. Members think "what's the substrate
saying this week?" because the cadence creates the trigger.

---

### ST3 — Emotion

**Berger:** high-arousal emotions spread (awe, anger, anxiety); low-
arousal emotions don't (sadness, contentment).

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **HIGH-RISK.** Manufacturing high-arousal emotion
for spread = engineered virality at cognitive cost. R12 line: emotion
must be substrate-justified.

**Jetix application:** Awe at real builder accomplishments. Genuine
excitement at substrate working. No manufactured outrage / anxiety /
anger.

---

### ST4 — Public

**Berger:** behaviors that are **observable** spread more than private
ones. "Built to show" wins.

**R12 verdict:** ⚠️ **MIXED.** Public-visibility design is fine if
substrate-honest; can SKIP into vanity-signaling otherwise.

**Jetix application:** Public work artifacts (demos, wikis, code) are
visible substrate. Visible builder identity. Not manufactured
"performances of building".

---

### ST5 — Practical Value

**Berger:** useful information spreads (life-hack, how-to, deal). The
sharer signals helpfulness; the recipient gets utility.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible** straightforwardly.

**Jetix application:** Methodology, Method V2 §J 8-component meta-method,
Welcome-frame patterns shared because they have practical value to
recipients. R12-aligned by definition.

---

### ST6 — Stories

**Berger:** narrative wrapper for information increases sharing 22×.
Story is the durable container.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-compatible** (same as Heath SU6 — honest stories
fine; fabricated SKIP).

## §5.4 Godin — Tribes (2008) + Permission Marketing (1999)

Seth Godin is **the most R12-aligned modern marketing thinker** — his
core thesis is that **marketing should be voluntary opt-in**, not
interruption. This is structurally R12-compatible.

### GD1 — Tribes

**Godin:** *"Tribes need leadership. Sometimes one person leads, sometimes
more."* [src: godin-tribes-2008 — Introduction line 126]

A **tribe** = a group of people connected to (1) one another, (2) a
leader, and (3) an idea. Tribes pre-exist; they just need leadership +
infrastructure to coordinate.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE.** Godin's tribe = self-selected
+ voluntary + leadership-by-pull-not-push. Maps directly to Jetix-Clan
substrate intent.

**Jetix application:** Jetix-Clan as Godinian tribe — connect existing
hungry+disciplined builders (1) to each other, (2) to the Pillar A
direction substrate, (3) to the methodology + R12 substrate idea. Pull
not push.

---

### GD2 — Heretics + Leaders

**Godin:** Tribal leaders are **heretics** against the prior consensus.
Leadership = articulating the heresy + giving infrastructure to those
who already half-believed.

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **R12-COMPATIBLE.** Articulating substrate fact +
giving infrastructure = honest leadership.

**Jetix application:** Ruslan as articulator of R12 + FPF + Jetix
substrate. Heresy against extractive default. Infrastructure (wiki,
methodology, cycle) for those who already half-believe extraction is
broken.

---

### GD3 — Permission Marketing

**Godin (1999):** marketing should be **anticipated, personal, relevant**
— sent to people who explicitly **gave permission** to receive it.
Opposite: interruption marketing (TV ads, popups, cold calls).

**R12 verdict:** ✅ **DIRECT R12-ALIGNMENT.** Permission = voluntary
opt-in = R12 substrate.

**Jetix application:** Jetix recruitment = permission-only. Members
opt in to Welcome-frame; opt in to deeper conversations; opt in to
cohort participation. Cold outreach minimized; when used, low-pressure
+ opt-out trivially easy.

## §5.5 Kahneman — Thinking Fast and Slow (2011)

Daniel Kahneman's *Thinking, Fast and Slow* (FSG 2011) synthesizes 30+
years of cognitive psychology into the **System 1 / System 2** framework
+ extensive catalogue of cognitive biases. Kahneman is **diagnostic
substrate** for R12 design — understanding cognitive biases is the
prerequisite for designing both (a) R12-compatible interfaces that don't
exploit them and (b) defenses against extractive substrates that do.

### K1 — System 1 vs System 2

**Kahneman:** Two cognitive systems:
- **System 1**: fast, automatic, intuitive, effortless, always-on
- **System 2**: slow, deliberate, effortful, attention-demanding, lazy

System 1 produces most decisions; System 2 endorses or corrects, but is
expensive and frequently delegates to System 1.

**R12 implication:** **Most persuasion targets System 1.** This is what
makes Cialdini's click-whirr (Phase 3) work, makes Bernays' stereotypes
(Phase 1) effective, makes cult techniques (Phase 4) successful. R12
discipline = **forcing System 2 engagement** at decision points where
extraction risk is high.

### K2 — Anchoring, Availability, Representativeness

**Kahneman:** [src: kahneman-thinking-fast-and-slow-2011 — Chapter 12
"The Science of Availability" line 38, 255-306]

Key biases relevant to propaganda + R12:
- **Anchoring** — first number / first frame disproportionately weights
  decision (Cialdini pre-suasion at scale)
- **Availability** — judging probability by ease-of-recall (Le Bon's
  "what's vivid is true")
- **Representativeness** — judging similarity by stereotype-match (Lippmann
  + Bernays leverage)
- **Loss aversion** — losing $X feels ~2.5× as bad as gaining $X
  (Cialdini scarcity foundation)

**R12 implication:** Every bias is a potential extraction vector.
R12-compatible design **de-biases** decisions (writing them down,
sleeping on them, structured comparison, F-G-R grading).

### K3 — The Lazy Controller

**Kahneman:** System 2 is **lazy** — endorses System 1 unless explicitly
challenged. Most "deliberate decisions" are actually System 1 with
System 2 rationalization.

**R12 implication:** Voluntary opt-in is only meaningful when System 2
is actually engaged. Welcome-frame design must engage System 2
(structured reading, written response, time delay).

### K4 — WYSIATI (What You See Is All There Is)

**Kahneman:** System 1 treats currently-available information as the
complete picture. Doesn't account for unknown unknowns.

**R12 implication:** Recruitment messaging must surface what the member
*can't* see (substrate limits, failure modes, what didn't work) — to
counteract WYSIATI bias and make consent meaningful.
