## §7.0 The synthesis principle

Across 6 prior phases, the **R12-compatible recruitment substrate** has
a singular structural identity:

> **Pull, don't push. Disclose, don't conceal. Substrate-real, never
> manufactured. Reversible, never sunk-cost-bound. Member-portable, never
> Clan-totalizing. Glossaried, never loaded-language. Falsifiable, never
> sacred-science. Impartial, never partial-loyalty. Fork-and-leave preserved.**

Every technique that meets this 9-clause test = R12-compatible = IMPORT.
Every technique that violates any clause = R12-incompatible = SKIP.

**The 9-clause R12 test is the operational core of Phase 7.**

## §7.5 Q4 — Cohort target ontology O-161/O-162 (hungry+disciplined+ready)

**Substrate:** O-161/O-162 cohort target ontology (Tier A wiki).

### Recruiting the hungry+disciplined+ready profile (NOT general public)

**Why precise targeting matters for R12:**

Hoffer H1/H2 (Phase 4) showed: mass movements draw from **frustrated**
profiles, who structurally need *movement-form* not *substrate-fit*.
Recruiting frustrated profiles into Jetix-Clan would **create cult-shape
from below** regardless of leader intent.

**The 3-trait target profile (per O-161/O-162):**

| Trait | Behavioral marker | Discrimination test |
|---|---|---|
| **Hungry** | Already shipping, already learning, restless with current ceiling | "What did you ship in the last 90 days?" |
| **Disciplined** | Has sustained 6+ month project / habit / practice | "What have you sustained for ≥6 months?" |
| **Ready** | Available bandwidth + life-stage compatible with cohort cadence | "What's your sustainable hours/week + 6-month commitment capacity?" |

**Anti-targeting (explicit exclude):**

| Profile | Why exclude | Risk |
|---|---|---|
| Frustrated, identity-seeking, cause-needing | Hoffer H2 True Believer profile | Cult formation from below |
| New poor / recent status loss | Hoffer H2 | Movement as compensation, not substrate-fit |
| Chronic seekers (jumping between cohorts / coaches / movements) | Hoffer H1 interchangeability | Same substrate need, will leave when novelty fades |
| Single-issue zealots seeking ammunition | Greene Law 27 cultlike following | Will weaponize Clan rhetoric |
| External-validation seekers | Cialdini Unity abuse | Will need manufactured "we" |
| Non-WEIRD-pattern without disclosure | Henrich diagnostic abuse | R12 substrate assumes WEIRD priors |

### IMPORT-driven recruitment design (R12-compatible)

**Channels:**

1. **Permission marketing only** (IMPORT I1 Godin 1999) — opt-in to
   Welcome-frame
2. **Tribes mode** (IMPORT C5 Godin Tribes) — connect existing builders to
   each other + substrate idea
3. **Bazaar mode visibility** (IMPORT F4 Raymond) — public artifacts as
   substrate proof
4. **Reputation economy** (IMPORT F6 Raymond) — members' reputation grows
   in Jetix orbit; attracts compatible
5. **Heretic-leader articulation** (IMPORT C6 Godin) — Ruslan articulates
   R12 heresy against extractive default

**Anti-channels (do NOT recruit through):**

- Paid advertising at scale (Chomsky CH3 audience-as-product)
- Manufactured scarcity / urgency campaigns (SKIP-54)
- Email blast / cold DM at scale (anti-Godin permission marketing)
- Influencer marketing without disclosure (Bernays B4 abuse)
- Astroturf "movement" framing (SKIP-13)

**Cohort selection process (R12-compatible):**

1. Welcome-frame published (permission-marketed channel)
2. Self-select-in (reader chooses to apply)
3. Diagnostic interview (IMPORT A1 cohort interviewing) — 30-60 min
   structured assessment of 3 traits
4. 24h cooldown for both sides (IMPORT E5)
5. Written commitment — reversible (IMPORT E1)
6. Cohort kickoff producing first member-portable artifact (IMPORT E2)
7. Quarterly opt-out check-in (IMPORT E1 ongoing)

**This is the R12-compatible recruitment funnel.**
