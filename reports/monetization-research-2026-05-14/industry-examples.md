## §1. YouTube creator economy

| Creator pattern | Mechanics | Revenue scale |
|----------------|-----------|---------------|
| **Mr. Beast** | Multi-revenue: AdSense + sponsorships ($1-3M/video) + Feastables snack brand + Beast Burger + IRL game shows ($5M Amazon prime series) | $80M-100M+ annual (2024 est) |
| **Veritasium** | Funnel: educational content → sponsorship integration (Brilliant.org / Wondrium / Squarespace) — long-form trust as conversion mechanism | $5-15M annual est |
| **Lex Fridman** | Sponsorships + Patreon (capped at 50K supporters, ~$5/mo avg) + podcast distribution licensing | $5-10M annual est |
| **MKBHD** | Sponsorships + studio production + Waveform podcast + WVFRM | $5-10M annual est |

**Mechanics:**
- **Sponsorship CPM** for top creators: $30-80 per 1K views (vs. $1-5 AdSense baseline)
- **Top 1% creators** earn 50%+ of total YouTube revenue (whaling pattern at creator level)
- **Patreon membership ceiling** typically 2-5% of total audience converts to paying tier

**Transferable to Jetix Realm:**
- Multi-revenue stream pattern → H-M-001-003 (partner revenue share + audience direct + third-party sponsorship)
- Conversion ceiling ~5% audience → paying → reality check for H-A revenue projections
- Creator-as-business model (vs creator-as-employee) → manifest pattern precedent

## §2. Telegram channel monetization

| Channel pattern | Mechanics | Scale |

| **Дудь (Yury Dud, RU)** | YouTube primary + Telegram secondary (audience capture + private channel); ad reads ($20-50K per integration) | ~10M YT subs / ~500K TG |
| **Варламов** | Sponsored posts on TG ($5-15K each) + paid ads booking platform; donations on Patreon-equivalents | ~700K TG subs; >$1M annual est |
| **Кашин (Олег)** | Subscription tier (Patreon-equivalent) + direct paid TG posts + lecture circuits | ~$200K annual est |
| **Russian-language tech channels** | Affiliate links + paid posts ($1K-5K each per 100K subs) + Discord-paid community | Variable |

**Mechanics:**

- Direct-to-channel sale (no algorithm dependency) → higher LTV than YouTube

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Multi-platform stack (YT + TG + Discord) — Realm could federate across all → H-F-005 shared infrastructure
- Direct-creator-to-audience (no platform tax beyond network fees) → reference for H-M-001 partner-direct

## §3. Substack / Patreon tiering

| Pattern | Mechanics | Top earners |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **Stratechery (Ben Thompson)** | Paywalled analysis $14/mo or $140/yr; podcast + lite-membership | ~$3-5M+ annual est (>20K subs) |
| **Pivot (Kara Swisher + Scott Galloway)** | Free podcast + premium "Prof G+" tier $7/mo + book sales | ~$5M+ annual est |
| **The Free Press (Bari Weiss)** | Substack hosting + paywalled content $5/mo + events | ~$8-10M annual est (60K+ paid subs) |
| **Patreon top creators** | Direct fan support tiered $1-100+/mo + creator goals | Top 1% earn $5K-100K/mo |

**Mechanics:**
- **Substack take rate:** 10% + Stripe fees
- **Patreon take rate:** 8-12% depending on plan
- **Conversion rate**: free → paid typically 1-5%, premium tiers <0.5%
- **Tiered offering**: free / $5 / $25 / $100 — top tier often delivers <10% revenue but high engagement

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Tier-progression model → H-O-001 Free → Paid funnel
- Top-tier exclusivity drives loyalty (vs revenue) → H-C-006 identity formation
- Platform take rate 8-12% benchmark for Phase 3 marketplace fees → H-M-013/H-M-014

## §4. Twitch streaming economy

| Pattern | Mechanics | Top earners |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| **xQc** | Subs (~$5/mo × tier) + bits + sponsorships + ad revenue + crypto sponsorships (controversial) | $15-30M+ annual est |
| **Pokimane (retired Jan 2024)** | Subs + sponsorships + own brand (Myna Snacks) | $5-10M annual peak |

**Mechanics:**
- **Twitch standard sub split:** 50/50 small / 60/40 mid / 70/30 top partners

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Per-creator revenue split at scale tiers → H-M-001 partner ladder (50/50 → 70/30)
- Cooperative streaming (raid traditions) → H-C-004 cooperation rewards
- High churn risk if engagement drops → H-C-007 friendship + life-improvement layer mitigates

## §5. OnlyFans creator economy

- **Platform take rate:** 20%
- **Top 1% creators:** earn 33% of revenue (less extreme than other platforms)
- **Top 10% creators:** ~75% revenue concentration
- **Mid-tier earner:** $500-2K/month typical
- **Top earner**: Bryce Adams ($30M+ annual reported); Mia Khalifa, Bella Thorne ($1M+ per month transition events)
- **Annual platform GMV:** ~$5-7B (2023)
- **Cooperation:** Cross-creator promotion ("F4F" Follow-for-Follow); collaborative content shoots

**Transferable to Realm:**
- 20% platform take rate benchmark (vs Patreon 8-12%) → tier of Phase 3 marketplace
- Top-1% revenue concentration similar to whaling but less extreme → safer pattern than Candy Crush
- Cross-creator promotion → H-F-006 cross-Clan partnerships

## §6. Reddit r/wallstreetbets as cooperation cluster

- **2021 GameStop short squeeze:** ~$28B short squeeze; collective action by ~10M subscribers
- **Cooperation mechanic:** Shared identity (apes / diamond hands / "this is the way") + visible portfolio screenshots (reputation) + ritual phrases (focal points per Schelling)
- **No formal coordination** — emergent through shared symbols & memes
- **Defection penalty:** Social shame (paper hands accusations); no economic enforcement
- **Failure mode:** Pump-and-dump dynamics; whale-coordinator advantage; SEC scrutiny

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Shared-identity coordination at scale (10M users) → §6 H-C-003 brand identity, §3.3 Schelling focal points
- Visible portfolio = reputation system → H-C-001 visible reputation
- Failure mode: identity-without-rules → defection through opportunism → Realm needs rule layer beyond identity

## §7. Discord servers & DAO governance

| Pattern | Mechanics | Scale |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| **Bankless DAO** | Token-gated participation + Discord ranks + bounty system | ~$10M treasury at peak; declining |
| **Bored Ape Yacht Club** | NFT membership + Discord exclusive + IRL events; ApeCoin token | $4B peak market cap; significant decline 2023-2024 |
| **Gitcoin** | Quadratic funding for public goods; Discord coordination + on-chain voting | $50M+ raised cumulative |
| **Optimism Collective** | Retroactive Public Goods Funding (RPGF) — pay after impact verified | $80M+ distributed |
| **Nouns DAO** | Daily NFT auction → treasury; CC0 IP rights; absolute on-chain governance | $50M+ treasury; small but active |

**Mechanics:**
- **Discord rank system** = Realm rank precedent (E1)
- **Quadratic funding (Gitcoin):** more contributors per project > larger contributors → coop preference signal
- **Retroactive funding (Optimism):** pay after impact → reduces speculation; rewards builders
- **Token gating:** participation tied to ownership → risk of plutocracy vs meritocracy

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Discord ranks → Realm rank visibility (E1)
- Quadratic funding → H-M-014 cooperative pool funding mechanism
- Retroactive funding → H-O-006 phased onboarding payment
- Token gating → CAUTION: Tier 2 RUSLAN-LAYER §4.2 "token economy must be safe + adequate, not crypto-pump"

## §8. Mondragón Corporation (Spain) — cooperative gold standard

- **Founded:** 1956 (José María Arizmendiarrieta, Basque country)
- **Current scale (2024):** 81,000+ employee-members; €11.5B revenue; ~3% Basque GDP
- **Governance:** 1-member-1-vote (not 1-share-1-vote)
- **Pay ratio cap:** Manager : worker = 6:1 historically (vs 350:1 US S&P 500 average)
- **Surplus distribution:** Internal accounts; partial dividend; reinvestment
- **Federation structure:** 96 cooperatives federated; Mondragón Group oversees
- **Failure case:** Fagor Electrodomésticos bankruptcy (2013) — federation absorbed shock without member layoffs

**Transferable to Realm:**
- 1-member-1-vote governance → H-C-002 group accountability precedent
- Pay ratio cap → R12 anti-extraction enforcement mechanism reference
- Federation of 96 cooperatives → §12 Phase 3-4 federation governance pattern
- Failure absorption → Phase 4+ risk pooling mechanism

## §9. CrossFit affiliate model

- **Affiliates globally:** ~13,000 boxes (peak ~15,000 in 2018)
- **License fee:** $3,000/year per affiliate (was lower; cost has scaled)
- **Brand revenue:** ~$120M+ annual (License + Games + apparel)
- **CrossFit Games:** Annual competition; $300K+ prize for top finishers
- **Cooperation mechanic:** Cult-of-fitness; communal WOD (Workout of the Day); leaderboards per box + global
- **Defection mechanism:** None — affiliates can leave anytime; no IP lock

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Light brand licensing model (low fee + community) → H-M-006 standards body / certification revenue stream
- Annual flagship event → §11 Phase 1-2 in-person Jetix events
- Cult-of-practice → H-C-006 identity formation
- No IP lock → fork-and-leave (R12 compliant) → reference for Charter R12 anchor

## §12. Estonia e-Residency

- **e-Residents (2024):** ~100,000+ from 170+ countries (program launched 2014)
- **Revenue:** Estonian state €30-50M annual indirect (tax + fees + ecosystem growth)
- **Mechanics:** Digital signature + EU company formation + banking access for non-residents
- **Cooperation:** Limited — primarily individual benefit; no community layer
- **Failure mode:** Banking de-risking; AML compliance friction

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Digital identity + EU substrate → Phase 4 (Charter §12 Phase 4 precedent)
- Solo benefit, no community layer → cautionary: digital citizenship ≠ cooperation
- Scale 100K in 10y from cold start → realistic Phase 3 trajectory for Realm

## §13. Próspera ZEDE (Honduras)

- **Established:** 2017 (Honduran ZEDE charter city framework)
- **Population:** ~1,500 residents (2024 estimate)
- **Mechanics:** Special Economic Zone with own regulatory framework; low tax (5-10% income); pay-as-you-use governance
- **Investors:** Pronomos Capital; backed by Peter Thiel, Patri Friedman
- **Cooperation:** Largely commercial; emerging community
- **Failure mode:** Political opposition (Xiomara Castro government 2022+ moves to dissolve); legal uncertainty

**Transferable to Realm:**
- Direct charter city precedent (§12 Phase 4-5)
- Pay-per-service governance → Standards Body H8 candidate (R12 risk)
- Political fragility → Realm Phase 4+ must have multi-jurisdiction redundancy
- Small scale 1,500 after 7y → ambitious "10M users" target requires different model than physical territory
