## H-C-009 — Realm 6 archetypes — member self-identifies

**Hypothesis statement:** 6 archetypes (Hunter / Guardian / Scholar / Creator / Architect / Merchant) per Charter + H6. Member picks 1-2; identity-action congruence drives engagement.
**Why test:** Archetype self-ID creates Schelling coordination shortcut + identity formation.
**Minimum viable test:** Onboarding assessment → archetype selection for 30 members; track engagement vs no-archetype control cohort.
**Success metric:** Archetype-selected cohort shows higher engagement (≥20% vs control); members self-cite archetype in bios.
**Failure metric:** Archetypes seen as gimmick; selection rate <50%; behavior unchanged.
**Confounders:** Self-selection bias; archetype names cultural fit varies.
**Precedent test:** Myers-Briggs (cult sustained 80+ years, debated validity); D&D character classes (50 years); Pokémon teams; Harry Potter Houses; Discord roles.
**Phase fit:** Phase 1+ (Charter LOCKED archetypes).
**Required L1 fit:** Any.
**R12 retest:** Verify archetype change allowed (no lock-in); archetype doesn't gate access to opportunities (no role-based discrimination).
**Cross-pollination:** Combines with H-C-007 (charter); combines with H-C-015 (self-image); amplifies L4 identity layer.
**Bayesian prior:** **High** — MBTI / D&D / Pokémon proven archetype models.
