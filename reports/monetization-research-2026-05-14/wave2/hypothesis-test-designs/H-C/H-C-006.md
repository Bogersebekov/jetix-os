## H-C-006 — Mutual no-poach 12mo (Charter §4 clause)

**Hypothesis statement:** Signatories mutually agree no-poach within 12 months of any member's exit. Reduces fear of being raided → enables vulnerability + cooperation.
**Why test:** No-poach reduces information asymmetry attack vector; creates Schelling commitment.
**Minimum viable test:** Charter signing includes clause; track 12-month period for any poach attempts + sentiment.
**Success metric:** Zero poach attempts; signatories cite clause as trust-builder; no antitrust concerns.
**Failure metric:** Poach attempt occurs (clause violated); legal challenge to clause (antitrust); signatories drop out citing restriction.
**Confounders:** Small sample (9-10 L1); 12-month window short; voluntary nature limits enforceability.
**Precedent test:** Silicon Valley illegal no-poach (Apple/Google 2010, $415M settlement — anti-pattern); legitimate version WME/CAA industry norm; DOJ v Adobe/Apple/Google 2014 settlement (cautionary).
**Phase fit:** Phase 1 (Charter LOCKED).
**Required L1 fit:** Charter signatories.
**R12 retest:** Verify clause is voluntary (signatory can opt-out); 12mo window not extended unilaterally; no consequential damages for breach (only brand damage); legal counsel approves antitrust safety.
**Cross-pollination:** Combines with H-C-007 (signing identity); blocks with H-A-011 (placement commission may overlap if cross-signatory placement); combines with H-C-014 (exclusion last resort).
**Bayesian prior:** **Medium** — WME/CAA industry norm sustained but antitrust risk persistent.
