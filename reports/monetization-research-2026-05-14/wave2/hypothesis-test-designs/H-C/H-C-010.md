## H-C-010 — Joint Quest revenue split — organized-crimes model

**Hypothesis statement:** Multi-member Quest pays bonus to participants (80/20 split per Torn precedent). Cooperative completion > solo.
**Why test:** Direct material reward for cooperation; mechanism design.
**Minimum viable test:** Launch 5 joint Quests vs 5 solo Quests; compare completion rate + member NPS.
**Success metric:** Joint Quest completion rate ≥ solo; member NPS positive; split rate disputed <10%.
**Failure metric:** Free-riding on joint (one member does all work); split disputed >50%; solo preferred by all.
**Confounders:** Quest type composition; group dynamics; rank-weighting weight choice.
**Precedent test:** Torn organized crimes 80/20 (sustained 20+ years, online crime sim with $X revenue); WoW raid loot distribution (DKP / Suicide Kings systems); EVE corp fleet ops; Mondragón surplus distribution.
**Phase fit:** Phase 1+.
**Required L1 fit:** Any.
**R12 retest:** Verify split rules transparent; rank-weighting fair (algorithm public per H-C-001 retest); dispute resolution at Quest level (H-F-007 Tribunal).
**Cross-pollination:** Combines with H-A-001 (Quest bounties); combines with H-C-011 (cross-Clan); amplifies M-A + M-B layers.
**Bayesian prior:** **Medium-High** — Torn / WoW raid loot proven; transposition to professional Quest untested.
