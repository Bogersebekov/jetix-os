## H-M-018 — Governance arbitration / dispute resolution fees (Phase 4+)

**Hypothesis statement:** When Realm scale reaches Phase 3-4, internal disputes (member ↔ member / Clan ↔ Clan / member ↔ Clan) require dispute resolution. Jetix Tribunal mediates; fees cover process. €500-€5K per dispute. R12 concern — «Jetix profits from own community conflicts».

**Why test:** Сигнал: (a) dispute volume actually emerges at Phase 3-4 scale; (b) Tribunal independence holds (rotating member-elected); (c) fee structure perceived legitimate; (d) appeal escalation external functional.

**Minimum viable test (Phase 3-4):**
- Setup: Tribunal seats elected (rotating 1-year terms; 3-5 seats); first 5-10 disputes mediated; fees go to fund (not Jetix corp).
- Cohort: 10-20 disputes Year 1 (likely small at Phase 3 start).
- Duration: 12 months.
- Cost: €20K (Tribunal admin + legal review).

**Success metric:** Tribunal seats elected without coercion; ≥5 disputes resolved; appeal rate <20%; member NPS on Tribunal ≥+5.

**Failure metric:** Tribunal seen as captured by Jetix; disputes routed off-Realm (e.g., to traditional courts); appeal rate >50%; legitimacy crisis triggers Realm exodus.

**Confounders:** Dispute volume unpredictable; small-sample Year 1; legal precedent uncertain (Realm Tribunal vs binding arbitration unclear jurisdictionally).

**Precedent test:** AAA / JAMS commercial arbitration ($300-$1500 filing + arbitrator hourly $200-500/hr; sustained); ICC International Court of Arbitration; eBay buyer-seller resolution (high-volume, low-cost, low-stakes); Estonian e-court (full digital court precedent).

**Phase fit:** Phase 3-4 (requires Realm scale ≥10K members for dispute volume to materialize).

**Required L1 fit:** Politicians archetype (Phase 4+) most relevant; legal-experienced advisors required Phase 3.

**R12 retest:** Verify: (a) Tribunal independent (rotating member-elected; Ruslan не can override decisions); (b) fees go to fund — not Jetix corp profit; (c) free first dispute per member per year; (d) appeal escalation external (e.g., German civil court or AAA); (e) decisions public + reasoned. R12 concern persists unless (a)-(e) hold.

**Cross-pollination:** Combines with H-F-007 (Realm Tribunal — same mechanism); blocks with H-M-017 (Standards Body — if Jetix is both standard-setter AND dispute-resolver = monopoly risk); amplifies Phase 4+ legal jurisdiction choice (Estonia / Próspera / SaaS multi-juris per §12).

**Bayesian prior:** **Medium** — AAA / JAMS / eBay proven но Jetix-specific Tribunal vs traditional court legitimacy uncertain; depends on Realm scale and external recognition.
