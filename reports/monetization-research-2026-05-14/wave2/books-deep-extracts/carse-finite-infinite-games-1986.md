# Carse — *Finite and Infinite Games* (1986) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

James P. Carse — religious historian, NYU. *Finite and Infinite Games* (1986) — philosophical essay distinguishing two modes of play.

## §2 Core distinction

- **Finite game** — played to win; has defined boundaries, rules, players, winners/losers; ends.
- **Infinite game** — played to continue play; boundaries shift; rules evolve; goal = sustain game itself.

## §3 Five key concepts relevant to Jetix

### Concept 1 — Infinite game has no winners
Goal is continuation. Players join + leave; rules evolve; no «end state» where someone has «won».

**Jetix mapping:** Realm Phase 6 «Sovereign-equivalent» is not «winning» — it's continuing the game at greater scale. Path to Virtual State (§12) is infinite game framing. Constitutional R12 + fork-and-leave preserve infinite-game continuability (no one can «win» by extracting + ending game).

### Concept 2 — Finite players play within boundaries; Infinite players play with boundaries
Finite game: «How can I win within the rules?» Infinite game: «What rules let us keep playing?»

**Jetix mapping:** Charter is infinite-player approach — rules can evolve (Charter renewal annual, member-vote on substrate changes); not «fixed rules to win within». Wave 2 cataloging of hypotheses-to-test is infinite-game mode (explore + adapt, not optimize-and-end).

### Concept 3 — Theatricality vs Drama
Finite players perform theatrically (predetermined script); infinite players engage в drama (open-ended action).

**Jetix mapping:** Ritual events (H-C-016) are theatrical but Realm cooperation is dramatic — open-ended. Manifest signing (H-C-007) is dramatic commitment to ongoing engagement, not theatrical performance.

### Concept 4 — Power vs Strength
Finite players seek power (over others); infinite players seek strength (with others).

**Jetix mapping:** **Direct R12 anchor.** R12 anti-extraction prevents accumulation of power (over members); cooperation mechanisms accumulate strength (with members). Mondragón solidarity fund operationalizes strength-with vs power-over.

### Concept 5 — Garden vs Machine
Finite system is mechanical (replaceable parts; defined function); infinite system is organic (gardener-tended; emergent).

**Jetix mapping:** Foundation Part 5 LOCKED (Compound Learning + Methodology Capture) is gardener-mode — methodology evolves through use, not pre-defined. Realm Seasons (H-C-002) operate as garden cycles, not factory production cycles.
