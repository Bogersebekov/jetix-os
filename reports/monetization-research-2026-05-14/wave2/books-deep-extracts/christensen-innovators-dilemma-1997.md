# Christensen — *The Innovator's Dilemma* (1997) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

Harvard Business School. Christensen's «disruptive innovation» framework — why successful firms fail when new entrants attack from low-end + emerging markets. Disk drive industry, steel mini-mills, hydraulic excavators as case studies.

## §2 Three key concepts relevant to Jetix

### Concept 1 — Disruptive vs Sustaining Innovation
- **Sustaining** — improves existing product on dimensions current customers value (faster CPU, larger disk drive)
- **Disruptive** — initially worse on existing dimensions; better on new dimension (smaller, cheaper, simpler); creates new market

**Jetix mapping:** Jetix vs traditional consulting:
- Traditional consulting = McKinsey/BCG model (high-touch, expensive, individual-engagement)
- Jetix = cooperative substrate (lower-touch initially, much cheaper per partner, scalable network)
- Initially «worse» on bespoke depth but better on scaling + cooperation + identity

**Jetix is potentially disruptive vs traditional management consulting** per Christensen framework.

### Concept 2 — Resources/Processes/Values Framework
Why established firms fail: their resources (people, money, IP) can shift; their processes (workflows) are sticky; their **values** (definition of attractive opportunity) are very sticky. New low-margin disruptive opportunity violates established firms' values.

**Jetix mapping:** Traditional consultancies cannot pivot to cooperative substrate because their values (high-margin, exclusive engagement) preclude cooperative + low-margin entry. Jetix's R12 anti-extraction is a **values commitment** that's structural moat — traditional firms can't replicate without abandoning their own value system.

### Concept 3 — Market Creation
Disruptors don't compete in existing markets; they create new ones. PC didn't compete with mainframes; it created personal computing market.

**Jetix mapping:** Jetix doesn't compete in consulting market — creates new market (cooperative substrate for professional networks). Network State framing extends to creating new category entirely.

## §4 Failure modes (Christensen warnings)

- **Disruptor's curse** — early success makes founder team complacent; second-wave disruptor emerges
- **Premium migration** — disruptor moves up-market to chase higher margins, abandons low-end → vulnerable to next disruptor
- **Value capture vs creation** — Christensen warned that disruptors who maximize capture (Shapiro/Varian extraction) lose disruptor legitimacy

**Jetix R12 anchor protects against premium-migration drift — constitutional limit on extraction prevents up-market abandonment.**
