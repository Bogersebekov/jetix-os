# Levy — *Hackers* (1984) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

Steven Levy — journalist. *Hackers* (1984) documented MIT AI Lab + Homebrew Computer Club + early hacker culture (1959-1984). Coined «Hacker Ethic» — 6 principles.

## §2 Hacker Ethic (Levy's 6 principles)

1. **Access to computers should be unlimited and total** — hands-on imperative
2. **All information should be free**
3. **Mistrust authority — promote decentralization**
4. **Hackers should be judged by their work, not race/age/credentials**
5. **You can create art and beauty on a computer**
6. **Computers can change your life for the better**

## §4 Cooperation precedents from hacker culture

- **MIT AI Lab «Lock Hacking»** — locks symbolically rejected as access barriers; cooperation through shared resources
- **Homebrew Computer Club** — 1975-1986; 1000+ members; pre-PC era; cooperation through schematics-sharing
- **Linux kernel + free software movement** — Stallman + Raymond extension of Levy framing

**Jetix mapping:** Homebrew model = small group (10-100) of high-skill members sharing knowledge → emergent innovation. Charter Clan structure (3-10) is similar Dunbar-layer.

## §5 Failure modes

- **«Right tribe» exclusion** — early hacker culture had implicit gender + class biases; meritocracy claimed but actual inclusion limited
- **Authority creep** — Microsoft / Apple founders started as hackers; later became authority figures hackers initially opposed
- **Commercial vs hacker tension** — Linux community has periodic crises over corporate co-option (RedHat IBM acquisition)

**Jetix R12 anchors against authority-creep drift; constitutional commitment prevents «founders became extractors» pattern.**
