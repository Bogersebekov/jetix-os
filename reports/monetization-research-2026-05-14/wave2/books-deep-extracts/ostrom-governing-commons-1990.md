# Ostrom — *Governing the Commons* (1990) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

Elinor Ostrom — Indiana University political economist; Nobel Prize 2009. *Governing the Commons* refuted Hardin's «tragedy of the commons»: empirical evidence that communities CAN sustain commons without privatization or state coercion.

## §2 Ostrom's 8 Design Principles for Commons

1. **Clearly defined boundaries** — who has access; what's the resource
2. **Local rules congruent with conditions** — rules fit context, not imposed
3. **Collective-choice arrangements** — affected parties participate in rules
4. **Monitoring** — usage observable
5. **Graduated sanctions** — minor violation = light penalty; serious = heavy
6. **Conflict-resolution mechanisms** — low-cost dispute access
7. **Recognition of organizing rights** — external authorities don't undermine local
8. **Nested enterprises** — for large commons, multi-layer governance

## §4 Failure modes Ostrom flagged

- **Lacking principle 4 (monitoring)** → free-riding undetected → tragedy
- **Lacking principle 5 (graduated sanctions)** → either too lax (free-riding tolerated) or too harsh (exclusion overused, community fractures)
- **Lacking principle 8 (nested)** → at scale, single-level governance breaks down

**Jetix nested structure (Clan → Faction → Realm) is Ostrom-aligned scale architecture.**

## §5 Mondragón validates Ostrom + vice versa

Mondragón is one of Ostrom's largest case studies. 80K workers × 60 years = empirical validation of 8 principles at scale.
