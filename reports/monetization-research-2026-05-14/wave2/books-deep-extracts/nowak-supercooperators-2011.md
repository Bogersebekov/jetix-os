# Nowak & Highfield — *SuperCooperators* (2011) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

Martin Nowak — Harvard mathematical biologist; pioneered evolution-of-cooperation theory. *SuperCooperators* (2011) synthesizes Nowak's research on 5 mechanisms by which cooperation evolves.

## §2 Nowak's 5 Mechanisms of Cooperation

1. **Kin selection** (Hamilton) — cooperation among relatives
2. **Direct reciprocity** (Axelrod) — iterated games; TFT
3. **Indirect reciprocity** — reputation; A helps B; C observes; C helps A later
4. **Network reciprocity** — spatial/structural — cooperators cluster
5. **Group selection** (controversial) — group-level cooperation traits

## §3 Jetix mapping per mechanism

### Mechanism 1 — Kin selection
Jetix doesn't operate on biological kinship но Clan structure (3-10) creates pseudo-kin bonds (Dunbar friendship layer H-C-017). Real-life friendship (L4) operationalizes pseudo-kin in cooperation.

### Mechanism 2 — Direct reciprocity (Axelrod)
**Already deep-extracted in Axelrod book.** Charter Seasons (H-C-002) + rank (H-C-001) operationalize.

### Mechanism 3 — Indirect reciprocity
A helps B; **C observes** via rank visibility; C later helps A based on reputation. **Realm rank visibility (H-C-001) is direct operationalization of indirect reciprocity.**

Nowak proved indirect reciprocity is foundational to large-scale cooperation (beyond Dunbar 150). Without reputation cascade, cooperation fails at scale. **Jetix M-B layer (rank visibility) is mathematically necessary for Phase 2+ cooperation, per Nowak.**

### Mechanism 4 — Network reciprocity
Cooperators cluster → cooperation spreads. **Charter manifest filter (H-C-008) creates cooperator cluster. Federation Phase 2-3 connects multiple clusters.**

### Mechanism 5 — Group selection
Group-level traits (Realm-wide cooperation norms) compete с extractive substrates. Norm-clusters that cooperate better win at scale.

**Jetix mapping:** Realm vs traditional consulting market = group-selection competition. R12-aligned Realm outcompetes extractive substrates at recruitment + retention long-term.
