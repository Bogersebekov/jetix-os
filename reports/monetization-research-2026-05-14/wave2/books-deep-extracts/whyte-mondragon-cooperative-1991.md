# Whyte & Whyte — *Making Mondragón* (1991) — Deep Extract

## §1 Author + context

William Foote Whyte (Cornell sociologist) + Kathleen King Whyte — ethnographers of Mondragón Cooperative Corporation (MCC), Basque Country. Founded 1956 by priest José María Arizmendiarrieta + 5 engineering students; grew to 80K+ worker-owners by 1990; 96 cooperatives. *Making Mondragón* documents structure, culture, finance, education.

## §2 Five Mondragón principles relevant to Jetix

### Principle 1 — Cooperative Ownership + Member Voice
MCC: each cooperative is worker-owned; one-member-one-vote on substrate decisions (irrespective of capital contribution). General Assembly meets annually.

**Jetix mapping:** Realm Phase 2+ governance structure (per Q-PT-006); member-vote on substrate-affecting decisions (Tier 2 R7 implicit). Mondragón validates 80K-scale democratic governance is operationally workable.

### Principle 2 — Pay Ratio 6:1 (1990s; relaxed to 9:1 later)
Highest-paid worker ≤ 6× lowest-paid. Mondragón's signature anti-extraction mechanism. (Relaxed to 9:1 in some cooperatives later; still tight by capitalist standards.)

**Jetix mapping:** Direct anchor for HQ-PR-012-A (6:1 strict). Mondragón 60-year sustainability under cap proves talent doesn't all flee (some do; many stay for cultural+identity reasons — Cialdini Unity).

### Principle 3 — Caja Laboral (Internal Bank)
MCC's internal credit union (Caja Laboral Popular, 1959); finances new cooperative formation + member home loans. Captures member savings, redeploys to cooperative growth.

**Jetix mapping:** Phase 3+ Realm internal bank (capital pool from member savings → R&D fund) — parallel to Caja Laboral. HQ-RC-011-B (Realm Credits) или HQ-RC-011-C (hybrid) could implement.

### Principle 4 — Education + Continuous Learning
Mondragón University (1997+) trains worker-owners; Otalora training center; emphasis on cooperative-specific pedagogy. **«Knowledge is the substrate of cooperation»** — Arizmendiarrieta.

**Jetix mapping:** Foundation Part 3 LOCKED (Knowledge Base & Methodology Library) is Mondragón University parallel. H-A-006 (course creation) + H-A-012 (cohort mentorship) + H-O-008 (mastery class) — all align с Mondragón education pillar.

### Principle 5 — Inter-Cooperative Solidarity Fund
Profitable cooperatives transfer surplus to struggling cooperatives via solidarity fund. Reduces single-cooperative bankruptcy risk; supports network-wide resilience.

**Jetix mapping:** Cross-Clan synergy bonus pool (H-C-011) + Realm-wide infrastructure (H-F-005) operationalize Mondragón solidarity. Phase 3+ may add formal solidarity fund.

## §3 Failure modes Mondragón experienced (Jetix-relevant)

- **Fagor white goods bankruptcy 2013** — major cooperative bankruptcy; 1900 workers lost jobs. Demonstrates limits of solidarity at scale; cooperative bankruptcy is real.
- **Outsourcing temporary workers 2000s+** — pressure to use non-member temporary workers in some cooperatives; ratio 60% members / 40% non-members emerged; **member vs non-member two-tier risk** for Jetix to avoid.
- **Pay ratio relaxation** — from 6:1 to 9:1 to 12:1 in some sectors; cap drift over time pressure.
