# Shapiro & Varian — *Information Rules* (1999) — Deep Extract

## §1 Context

Berkeley economists; Hal Varian later chief economist at Google. *Information Rules* synthesizes economics of network effects, lock-in, versioning, complements — written at peak dot-com but principles persist.

## §2 Four key concepts relevant to Jetix

### Concept 1 — Network Effects
Value of network grows non-linearly with users (Metcalfe's Law: ≈ n²; later refined to n log n by Odlyzko). Tipping points: once critical mass reached, network captures market.

**Jetix mapping:** Realm value compounds с member count. H-F-005 (shared KB) demonstrates: more Clans → richer KB → more value per Clan. Tipping point Phase 1→2 transition (per Q-PT-006) is critical mass moment.

### Concept 2 — Lock-In + Switching Cost
Customers locked in by sunk investment in learning / data / relationships. Switching cost = lock-in. Standard business strategy: maximize lock-in.

**Jetix anti-pattern:** R12 fork-and-leave **minimizes** lock-in. Shapiro/Varian's strategic prescription is inverted by R12. **R12 is constitutional rejection of lock-in capitalism**. Jetix monetizes through value creation, not switching-cost extraction.

### Concept 3 — Versioning
Sell same content в multiple versions / tiers (premium, standard, basic). Captures consumer surplus across willingness-to-pay distribution.

**Jetix mapping:** H-M-006 (subscription tiers €15-50) is versioning. H-M-007 (pay-per-progression L0→L6) is versioning. H-M-008 (cert fees by tier €200-30K) is versioning.

### Concept 4 — Complements + Standards
Products with strong complements (printer + cartridges; iPhone + apps) create ecosystem lock-in. Standards battles (HD-DVD vs Blu-ray) — winner takes most.

**Jetix mapping:** Standards Body H-M-017 = standards-power thesis. **Shapiro/Varian validate why Standards Body is so lucrative — but Jetix R12 explicitly rejects standards-extraction model.** Tension catalogued in HQ-SB-008.
