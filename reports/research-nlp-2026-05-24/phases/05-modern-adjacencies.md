# Phase 5 — Modern Coaching Adjacencies

> **Phase critical question (per parent prompt):** Which Bolton patterns are FUNCTIONAL EQUIVALENTS of NLP claims that **don't carry pseudoscientific baggage**?
>
> **Phase finding:** Bolton's *People Skills* (1979) — written same year as Bandler+Grinder's *Frogs into Princes* — covers **listening + assertion + conflict-resolution** with substantially less mystique + grounded в Rogers-Gordon tradition (empirically supported). For most R12-compatible NLP rapport/communication claims, **Bolton provides the validated equivalent**. Phase 7 will preferentially IMPORT from Bolton over NLP rapport material.

---

## §5.0 Bolton's framework — five skill clusters (1979)

> «Five sets of skills: **Listening skills** — those abilities enabling one to receive and understand messages others send accurately. **Assertion skills** — abilities enabling one to maintain respect, satisfy needs, defend rights without dominating, manipulating, abusing or controlling others. **Conflict-resolution skills** — abilities to deal with the emotional turbulence that accompanies conflict. **Collaborative problem solving skills** — abilities for resolving conflicting needs that satisfies all parties. Plus implementation / core attitudes.» [src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~693-738]

This **5-skill-cluster taxonomy** is more clinically + organisationally validated than NLP's «4 pillars» (outcome / acuity / flexibility / rapport).

### §5.0.1 R12 alignment of Bolton's frame itself

Bolton's *assertion skills* definition: «satisfy needs, defend rights **without dominating, manipulating, abusing or controlling others»** [src: ~704-708].

This is essentially **R12 paired-frame avant-la-lettre** — assertion-with-respect-for-other-fork-and-leave. R12 STRICT alignment: ✅ STRONG.

## §5.1 Listening skills — Bolton's «Cluster 1» (Chapters 3-7)

### §5.1.1 Skill subclusters

**Attending skills:**
- Posture of involvement (open, slightly inclined, facing speaker)
- Appropriate body movement
- Eye contact
- Non-distracting environment

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~1393-1653]

**Following skills:**
- Door-openers (non-coercive invitations to talk)
- Minimal encouragers («mm-hm», «I see»)
- Infrequent questions
- Attentive silence

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~1653-1986]

**Reflective skills (Chapter 4):**
- **Paraphrasing** — restate speaker's content in your own words
- **Reflecting feelings** — name the emotion underneath content
- **Reflecting meanings** — combine paraphrase + feeling reflection
- **Summative reflections** — periodically summarize themes

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~2019-2489]

### §5.1.2 Comparison к NLP rapport/calibration claims

| NLP claim | Bolton equivalent | Bolton advantage |
|---|---|---|
| Calibration (read micro-behavioral cues) | Attending skills + reflecting feelings | Bolton: no pseudoscientific overclaim; just attend + ask |
| Predicate matching (VAK) | Paraphrasing (restate in their own words) | Bolton: no fictional VAK typology |
| Pacing speaker's experience | Reflecting feelings + meanings | Bolton: discloses что делаешь; not «covert pacing» |
| Building rapport via mirroring | «Posture of involvement» + attending discipline | Bolton: pro-relationship, not influence-extraction |

**Phase 7 implication**: For all R12-compatible rapport-building в Jetix Welcome-frame O-144, USE BOLTON, not NLP. The functional outcomes are equivalent (better communication); Bolton arrives там без VAK pseudoscience + без covert-mirroring abuse-mode + grounded in Rogers' empirically validated counseling tradition.

### §5.1.3 R12 + Empirical + Disposition (Bolton listening)

| Aspect | Note |
|---|---|
| Self-applied / disclosed | ✅ R12 OK throughout |
| Empirical | Rogers' active-listening + reflective-response = **strong RCT evidence** в counseling psychology (Truax+Carkhuff 1967, Lambert 2013 meta-analyses) |
| Phase 7 | **STRONG IMPORT** — primary source for Jetix Welcome-frame rapport-building |

## §5.2 Communication Spoilers — the 12 Roadblocks (Chapter 2)

Bolton catalogues 12 «communication roadblocks» (originally from Thomas Gordon):

**Judging cluster:**
1. **Criticizing** — negative evaluation
2. **Name-calling / labeling**
3. **Diagnosing** — analyzing why person feels/acts as they do
4. **Praising evaluatively** — positive judgment that frames «as good» («you're so smart!»)

**Sending solutions:**
5. **Ordering** — directing
6. **Threatening** — warning of consequence
7. **Moralizing** — «you should»
8. **Excessive/inappropriate questioning** — using questions as solutions
9. **Advising** — providing solution

**Avoiding the other's concerns:**
10. **Diverting** — pushing the topic aside
11. **Logical argument** — using facts to persuade away from feeling
12. **Reassuring** — premature consolation

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 ch 2 p ~759-1224]

### §5.2.1 R12 critique connection

These 12 roadblocks = empirically-derived list of communication patterns that **shut down** the speaker. Notable: **several overlap with NLP-derived sales techniques** that NLP-popularizers actively teach:

| NLP technique | Bolton roadblock |
|---|---|
| Sleight of Mouth «counter-example» (objection-attack) | Roadblock 11 (logical argument) — proven to shut down feeling |
| «Reframe their objection» (covert SOM) | Roadblock 4 (praising evaluatively) — frames their concern as good-to-overcome |
| «Question the criteria» (NLP-sales) | Roadblock 8 (inappropriate questioning) — uses questions as control |
| «Counter their belief» (SOM) | Roadblock 1 (criticizing) |
| «Mind-reading challenge» (meta-model attack) | Roadblock 3 (diagnosing) |

**Phase 7 implication:** Bolton's 12 roadblocks provide an **independent empirical confirmation** of why NLP's adversarial techniques (Sleight of Mouth deployed against another's beliefs) damage relationships even when «successful» at moving the position. The roadblocks list = de-facto SKIP-list of adversarial communication moves.

## §5.3 Assertion Skills — Bolton's Cluster 2 (Chapters 6-7)

### §5.3.1 Three-part assertion message

> «When you [behavior description], I feel [emotional impact], because [tangible effect].»

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 ch 6-7]

Disciplined separation of:
- **Behavior description** — specific, observable, non-judgmental
- **Feelings** — own emotional state (I-statement)
- **Tangible effects** — concrete consequences for self

Plus: **broken-record** (calm repetition of position), **fogging** (acknowledge truth in criticism without agreeing it's the whole truth), **negative assertion** (acknowledge own mistake).

### §5.3.2 R12 alignment

Bolton's assertion = **maintain own position + respect other's right to maintain theirs** = R12 paired-frame structural match. No manipulation, no escalation, no withdrawal.

| NLP «assertion» equivalent | Bolton advantage |
|---|---|
| «Frame-control» / state-elevation persistence | Bolton: just state position, no state-game |
| «Pacing-leading» к outcome | Bolton: state position, accept other's response, no «leading» |
| «Massive action» pressure (Robbins) | Bolton: assertion ≠ pressure; respects other's «no» |

### §5.3.3 R12 + Empirical + Disposition

| Aspect | Note |
|---|---|
| R12 | ✅ Structural match с paired-frame discipline |
| Empirical | Assertiveness training = RCT-validated в clinical psychology (Alberti+Emmons; Smith *When I Say No I Feel Guilty*) |
| Phase 7 | **STRONG IMPORT** — primary source for Jetix R12 paired-frame operationalization |

## §5.4 Conflict Resolution — Bolton's Cluster 3 (Chapters 8-11)

### §5.4.1 Three kinds of conflict

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~9110-9149]:
1. **Emotional conflict** — heated reactivity; needs emotion-handling before substance
2. **Conflict of needs** — actual incompatibility of interests
3. **Values conflict** — incompatible underlying beliefs

### §5.4.2 Six-step Collaborative Problem Solving

[src: bolton-people-skills-1979 p ~9377-9730]:
1. Define problem in terms of needs (not solutions)
2. Brainstorm possible solutions
3. Select solution meeting most needs
4. Plan execution
5. Implement
6. Follow up

(Compare to NLP 6-step reframing — Bolton's protocol is **inter-personal**; NLP's is **intra-personal**. Different scope, both legitimate.)

### §5.4.3 R12 + Empirical + Disposition

| Aspect | Note |
|---|---|
| R12 | ✅ Collaborative-problem-solving = R12 paired-frame extended to inter-personal |
| Empirical | Thomas Gordon's collaborative-problem-solving = wide empirical base в family + education + organizational settings |
| Phase 7 | **STRONG IMPORT** — primary source для inter-partner negotiation discipline |

## §5.5 Core Attitudes (Chapter 15) — meta-skill

> «Skills alone are insufficient. ... **Genuineness** + **Nonpossessive love (acceptance)** + **Empathy**.» [src: bolton-people-skills-1979 ch 15 p ~10116-10755]

This is Rogers' triad of «necessary and sufficient conditions for therapeutic change» (Rogers 1957) — empirically validated repeatedly. Bolton imports it directly.

**R12 alignment:** STRONG.
- **Genuineness** ≈ R12 transparency (no covert framing)
- **Nonpossessive love** ≈ R12 fork-and-leave preservation (love without attachment-binding)
- **Empathy** ≈ R12 perceptual-positions discipline + paired-frame partner-perspective

**Phase 7 implication:** Rogers triad = the **deepest R12-compatible communication foundation**. NLP rapport claims = downstream specializations; original Rogers framing is foundational.

## §5.6 Andreas — Heart of the Mind (1989) — 2nd-gen NLP

### §5.6.1 Context

Steve + Connirae Andreas = trained directly under Bandler+Grinder; subsequently became major NLP publishers (Real People Press). *Heart of the Mind* = case-study book demonstrating NLP application with self-applied / clinical-applied focus, NOT seminar-monetization.

[src: andreas-heart-of-the-mind-1989 throughout]

### §5.6.2 Notable patterns covered

Case studies include:
- Stage fright (using submodality-based protocols) — ch 1
- Spelling strategy (modeling discipline applied) — ch 2 (Ben example)
- Phobia cure protocol — multiple chapters
- Grief resolution
- Forgiveness work
- AIDS support context (p ~4758, 8545) — using NLP techniques to help patients + families cope

### §5.6.3 R12 + Empirical assessment of Andreas approach

**Genuinely more careful than Robbins:**
- Self-applied / clinical-applied; no charismatic-leader binding
- No escalating-tier seminar funnel
- Explicit acknowledgment that NLP works for some people not others
- Realistic timelines (not «cure in one session» marketing)

**Still inherits:**
- Eye accessing cues claims (Appendix I p ~239) — falsified
- Submodality claims for clinical interventions — better-validated equivalent in imagery rescripting/CBT
- Generalizations like «NLP can do X» without RCT support

**R12 status:** Andreas approach itself = MORE R12-compatible than Robbins approach. Particular techniques inherit underlying empirical-status concerns.

> **R12 finding**: The R12 compatibility of an NLP-tradition output is **strongly influenced by the practitioner's commercial model** as much as by the techniques themselves. Robbins business model creates R12 violation; Andreas clinical-publishing model preserves most R12 properties even with similar techniques.

## §5.8 Atomic Habits + Inner Game — adjacent literatures (referenced, not in corpus)

### §5.8.1 Atomic Habits (Clear 2018)

Although not in corpus, the **cue-routine-reward** habit framework (Duhigg → Clear) provides better-validated equivalent to NLP anchoring + pattern-interrupt:

| NLP claim | Atomic Habits equivalent | Validation status |
|---|---|---|
| Anchoring (resource-state) | Habit stacking (cue → existing habit → new habit) | Strong behavioral-science evidence |
| Pattern-interrupt | Environment design (remove cue) | Operant conditioning empirically robust |
| Swish pattern | Identity-based habits (cast vote for who you become) | Mixed; aligns with self-perception theory |
| Future pacing | Implementation intentions (Gollwitzer) | Strong RCT support |

**Phase 7 implication**: For self-applied behavior change (Jetix internal practice + Workshop participant practice), USE Atomic Habits framework, NOT NLP anchoring/swish. Better empirical base, fewer R12 risks.

### §5.8.2 Inner Game (Gallwey 1974)

«Self 1 vs Self 2» framework — performance arises from **quieting** internal critic (Self 1) and letting embodied competence (Self 2) act. Performance literature; particularly strong для skill-execution-under-pressure.

| NLP equivalent | Inner Game advantage |
|---|---|
| State management (Robbins Triad) | Inner Game: less prescriptive, more permissive |
| New behavior generator | Inner Game: trust embodied competence, no protocol needed |
| «Mastering your mind» | Inner Game: actually mastery via not-trying — paradoxical but better in performance contexts |

**Phase 7 implication:** For Workshop participants who need to **perform under pressure** (give Workshop pitch / first Clan invitation), Inner Game framing provides less performance-anxiety-inducing alternative to Robbins'-style «massive action».

## §5.9 Critical comparison table — Bolton vs NLP

| Dimension | Bolton (1979) | NLP (1975-2000) |
|---|---|---|
| Empirical base | Rogers + Gordon, RCT-validated | Mostly unvalidated by independent research; some claims falsified |
| Commercial model | Book + workshops (modest scale) | Seminar industry + tier-escalation + monetized certification |
| R12 alignment | Native (paired-frame structural match) | Mixed — depends on practitioner discipline |
| Listening | Reflective listening protocol | Calibration + predicate matching (VAK risks) |
| Assertion | Three-part assertion message | «Frame control» + state elevation |
| Conflict resolution | Collaborative problem-solving 6-step | 6-step reframing (intra-personal only) |
| Identity / values | Implicit; Rogers' triad | Explicit logical levels; identity-binding risk |
| Adversarial techniques | Absent | Sleight of Mouth + Milton model present |

**Conclusion:** Where NLP and Bolton both make claim X, **Bolton's version is generally preferable** for Jetix import.
