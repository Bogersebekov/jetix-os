## §1 Short-term variants (Q3-Q4 2026)

### V1 — Hackathon Sponsorship Fees

**Mechanism.** Sponsors pay для visibility + talent access + R12-aligned brand association during Tier 1-5 hackathon events.

**Pricing baseline:**
- T1 event: €1K-5K per sponsor
- T2 event: €10K-50K per sponsor
- T3 event: €50K-250K per sponsor
- T4 event: €250K-1M per sponsor
- T5 project: €1M-10M+ per sponsor (multi-year)

**Target customer.** Phase 1 Cat 16 (Corporate), Cat 8 (Investors), Cat 17 (Governments), Cat 19 (Foundations), Cat 18 (Universities).

**Pros:**
- Immediate revenue (Q3 2026 startable)
- Sustainable (recurring events)
- Tier-stacked yield (compounding)
- Brand transfer (Tier 4-5 = strategic partner pool)

**Cons:**
- Sponsor dependency
- R12 audit overhead
- Sponsor-content discipline required (avoid corporate-capture)

**R12 alignment check:** ✓ if sponsor data-extraction = NONE, R12 charter signed, fork-and-leave preserved per cohort.

**Cross-link:** Phase 3 Tier P&L; Phase 4 L8 R12 enforcement.

**Q3 2026 readiness:** NOW (T1-T2 events Q3 2026; T3 Q4 2026).

### V2 — Workshop Training / Education Fees

**Mechanism.** Cohort participants pay for Workshop substrate access (Education Layer formal curriculum).

**Pricing baseline:**
- Single intensive Workshop (1-3 days): €500-2K per participant
- Multi-week residential Workshop (Tier 4 equivalent): €5K-25K per participant (often partially scholarship-subsidised)
- Year-long Education Layer cohort: €10K-50K (corporate-sponsored common)

**Target customer.** Cat 1-7 (most learning-oriented categories); Cat 16 corporate-sponsored sub-cohorts; mid-career professionals.

**Pros:**
- Revenue + cohort filtering (paid = commitment signal)
- Methodology compounding (Workshop graduates → mentors)
- Scholarship-discipline (R12-aligned mix)

**Cons:**
- Pricing pressure (free MIT OCW alternative)
- Quality maintenance overhead
- Capacity-limited (Berlin Grundstück site)

**R12 alignment check:** ✓ if scholarship mix preserves accessibility (e.g. ≥30% scholarships per cohort); Mondragón ratio cap for cohort instructor compensation.

**Cross-link:** Education Layer 2026-05-18; Phase 3 Tier 4.

**Q3 2026 readiness:** Pilot Q4 2026; scaled Q1-Q2 2027.

## §2 Mid-term variants (Phase 2; 2026-2027)

### V6 — SaaS Platform Fees

**Mechanism.** Per-user / per-org subscription fees for Jetix substrate access (Phase 4 FPF protocol implementation).

**Pricing baseline:**
- Individual contributor: free baseline / €10-50 / month premium
- Org-tier (5-25 contributors): €500-2K / month
- Enterprise (25-200): €2K-25K / month

**Target customer.** Cat 16 (Corporate-internal cohort substrate), Cat 18 (University departments), Cat 19 (Foundation portfolios), Cat 20 (Communities operationalising).

**Pros:**
- Recurring revenue
- Substrate-network compounding (more users = more value)
- Low-marginal-cost per additional user

**Cons:**
- Platform-development overhead (R24 substrate investment)
- Support overhead
- Vendor-lock-in risk (must counter per R12 fork-and-leave)

**R12 alignment check:** Multi-mechanism enforcement:
- Always free baseline tier (accessibility)
- Data portability mandatory (fork-and-leave)
- No vendor-lock features
- Mondragón cap for tier-pricing (e.g. enterprise ≤10× individual; not 1000×)

**Cross-link:** Phase 4 L1-L8 protocol substrate; H8 trust infrastructure.

**Q3 2026 readiness:** Substrate-dev Q3-Q4 2026; pilot Q1 2027; scaled Q2-Q3 2027.
