## §1 Value 1 — Humanity Development as Primary Purpose

### §1.1 Articulation

«Проект по развитию человечества» [src: text_011 §1] = the meta-purpose. Jetix is one substrate in this meta-project; the meta-project is what unites Jetix with every potential partner.

### §1.2 Operational implications

- Every monetization variant (Phase 7) must answer «how does this serve humanity-development?»
- Every partnership accepted must contribute to humanity-development (not just Jetix)
- Direction Cards (Pillar A) explicitly tie back to humanity-development
- Resource priorities (Phase 2) weighted by humanity-development contribution

### §1.3 What it does NOT mean

- NOT universalist coercive «we know what humanity needs» (R12 alignment + IP-1 — humans decide)
- NOT requiring all partners hold same definition of «humanity development»
- NOT excluding partners whose primary motive is personal (text_011 §1 «выгода для their proj NOW» preserved)

### §1.4 IP-1 STRICT

«Develop humanity» definition = human-decided (Ruslan + cohort governance + Foundation governance). Jetix substrate does not autonomously interpret «humanity development». [src: text_010 §1 «отталкиваться от ценностей»]

## §2 Value 2 — All People = Partners (Text_011 thesis)

### §2.1 Articulation (verbatim)

«если отзумиться, то в целом эти все люди работают над проектом всего человечества, просто они этого еще не видят. И, соответственно, я, работая над своим проектом, и они, работая над своим проектом, мы находимся в одной лодке. И у нас как бы мы ебать партнеры уже. То есть, алло, дядя, блять, мы уже партнеры, мы в одной лодке.» [src: text_011 §1 verbatim]

### §2.2 Operational implications

- Outreach framing: «we are already partners» — not «can we be partners?»
- Reveal pattern: «теперь ты знаешь — поздравляю» (we acknowledge the pre-existing reality)
- No supplicant-vs-benefactor framing (mutual partner-stance)
- Cohort governance respects all-partner principle (no «founder caste»)

### §2.3 What it does NOT mean

- NOT abandoning quality discrimination (selecting collaborators is fine)
- NOT abandoning competence-recognition (Phase 1 differentiates skill)
- NOT abandoning compensation differences («деление на конкретно какой вклад ты сделал» preserved — text_011 §1)

### §2.4 IP-1 STRICT

Partner-framing is communication discipline; actual partnership-formation = mutual ack (both humans). Jetix substrate facilitates; it does not declare partnerships unilaterally.

## §11 Partner-framing reveal pattern (core operational artefact)

### §11.1 Pattern structure (verbatim derived from text_011)

```
1. ANCHOR (acknowledge their reality):
   «Ты работаешь над [their project — N задач, M ресурсов].»

2. REVEAL Jetix:
   «Я работаю над [Jetix — humanity development substrate].»

3. ZOOM OUT (meta-level reframing):
   «Zoom out → мы оба работаем над humanity-development.»

4. PARTNER DECLARATION:
   «Мы уже партнёры — просто не были знакомы.»

5. RECIPROCAL VALUE OFFER:
   «Вижу — могу помочь твоему [local project]: X, Y, Z.»

6. RECIPROCAL VALUE ASK:
   «Прошу help в моём [Jetix]: A, B, C.»

7. CONVERGE (concrete outcome):
   «Together → конкретный positive outcome.»

8. GOAL-ALIGNMENT CHECK (FPF discipline):
   «Goal-alignment с values verifiable через FPF protocol.»
```

[src: text_011 §1 verbatim derived]

### §11.2 Pre-meeting profiling required

Before outreach, brigadier-scribe surfaces:
- Their project (1-2 paragraphs description)
- Their resources offered (Phase 2 R1-R32 subset)
- Their likely asks (what they're seeking)
- X/Y/Z Jetix-can-help (3 concrete offers per category)
- A/B/C Jetix-needs (3 concrete asks per category)
- Goal-alignment expected (humanity-development overlap)
- Divergent-values risk (R12 + FPF mismatch)

### §11.3 Saturation + quality discipline

- Per Ruslan: «я каждому это запихаю» [src: text_011 §1] = saturation intent

- Quality control: 1-on-1 high-leverage outreach (Cat 12 T1) ≠ batch outreach (Cat 16 T2)

## §14 Saturation discipline (quality controls)

### §14.1 Saturation tiers per category

- **High-leverage 1-on-1** (Cat 12 Influencers): patient long-bridge cultivation; quality > volume
- **Medium-batch personalised** (Cat 7 Mentors, Cat 14 Connectors, Cat 8 Investors): personalised per target
- **Template-batch** (Cat 15 Audience, Cat 21 Open-source contributors): templated outreach with personalised touches

### §14.2 100h engagement floor (Ruslan-acked)

Per Master Picture refinement: «каждому запихаю» tempered by quality engagement metric. Cohort-quality > raw-reach. Specific: 100 hours of accumulated engagement = floor for «meaningful collaboration» claim.

### §14.3 Engagement-quality metric

- Multi-touchpoint depth (text → call → meeting → collaboration)
- F-G-R verifiable claims tracked (no inflated claims)
- Mutual-investment evidence (both parties commit substrate)

### §14.4 Disengagement discipline

- Recognise when partner-fit is wrong (early)
- Graceful exit per fork-and-leave principle
- No coercive persistence

## §16 Divergent-values handling

### §16.1 Cases of divergence

- Partner extractive (R12 violation likely) → DECLINE
- Partner non-FPF-discipline (chronic claim-inflation) → DECLINE
- Partner values orthogonal but not opposed (different domain focus) → SCOPE-LIMIT partnership
- Partner mission-conflicting (e.g. extractive AI without alignment) → DECLINE
- Partner uncertain (insufficient information) → SMALL-TEST collaboration

### §16.2 Handling protocol

1. Surface divergence transparently (not hidden)
2. Document divergence in pre-partnership artefact
3. Decide: full / scope-limited / decline / small-test
4. Audit-trail entry per FPF discipline
5. Periodic-review trigger (decision revisitable)

### §16.3 IP-1 STRICT

Divergence-handling decision = Ruslan (sole strategist) + cohort governance input. Substrate surfaces; humans decide.
