## §1 Bloom — *Taxonomy of Educational Objectives* (1956)

### §1.1 Origin + context

- 8-year committee work (1949–1956) led by Benjamin Bloom (University of Chicago), 34+ contributors across major US universities [src: bloom-taxonomy-educational-objectives-1956.pdf p 4]
- Dedicated to Ralph Tyler whose evaluation ideas seeded the work [src: bloom-taxonomy-educational-objectives-1956.pdf p 3]
- **Three domains scoped:** Cognitive (Handbook 1), Affective (Handbook 2, 1964), Psychomotor (Handbook 3, 1972) — Handbook 1 = cognitive (this corpus)
- Purpose: standardise classification of **educational goals** so curricula across institutions can be compared, evaluated, designed against a common ontology

### §1.2 The 6 hierarchical cognitive levels

| # | Level | Cognitive demand | Verbs for objectives |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1.00 | **Knowledge** | Recall facts, terminology, conventions, classifications, criteria, methodology | list / define / name / recall / identify |
| 2.00 | **Comprehension** | Translate / interpret / extrapolate the meaning | explain / paraphrase / summarise / describe |
| 3.00 | **Application** | Use abstractions in concrete situations | apply / solve / use / demonstrate |
| 4.00 | **Analysis** | Break material into parts; detect relations + organising principles | analyse / differentiate / distinguish / categorize |
| 5.00 | **Synthesis** | Combine elements + parts to form a whole; produce a plan, derived relations, abstract relations | compose / design / propose / construct / develop |
| 6.00 | **Evaluation** | Make judgments about value of material/methods using internal + external criteria | judge / critique / defend / appraise |

[src: bloom-taxonomy-educational-objectives-1956.pdf chapters 4-9 detailed]

### §1.3 Cumulative hierarchy invariant

Bloom's organising principle: **mastery at level N requires the cognitive capabilities of levels 1..N-1** ("each classification involves to some degree the behaviors found in the preceding ones"). This invariant — debated by later revisionists (Anderson + Krathwohl 2001 revised taxonomy swapping Synthesis/Evaluation order) — remains operationally useful: **don't ask Application questions until Knowledge + Comprehension are verified**.

### §1.4 Three levels of definition per category

For every taxonomy category, Bloom committee gave 3 layers:
1. **Educational objective definition** — what the educator targets (intent)
2. **Behavioral description** — what learner does observably (verbs above)
3. **Test items + examples** — how to verify it occurred (assessment)

[src: bloom-taxonomy-educational-objectives-1956.pdf p 5]

→ **This 3-layer structure = pre-cursor to backward design** (Wiggins-McTighe will formalise this 40 years later as Stage 1 / Stage 2 / Stage 3).

### §1.5 Implications for Jetix substrate

| Bloom level | Jetix substrate example mapping |
|---|---|
| Knowledge | Recall: «что такое hypothesis architecture 7-layer?» |
| Comprehension | Explain Method V2 §J meta-method in own words |

## §2 Gagné — *Conditions of Learning* (1965)

### §2.1 Origin + thesis

- Robert Gagné — US Air Force training research origin (efficient pilot training under wartime constraints); later academic codification
- Core claim: **different learning outcomes require different conditions** — there is no single «correct way to teach» — and an instructional designer must analyse outcome type FIRST, then design conditions

### §2.2 Five categories of learned capability

| # | Category | Example | Required conditions |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Verbal information** | Names, labels, propositional statements | Distinctive context, organisation, repeated retrieval |
| 2 | **Intellectual skills** | Concepts, rules, problem-solving (the bulk of academic education) | Prerequisite skills, varied examples, feedback |
| 3 | **Cognitive strategies** | Metacognition, study skills, self-regulation | Practice with novel problems, feedback |
| 4 | **Motor skills** | Physical performance | Practice, smoothing, feedback on form |
| 5 | **Attitudes** | Preferences, choices, dispositions | Modelling by valued role models, reinforcement |

[src: gagne-conditions-of-learning-1965.pdf chapters 4-8]

### §2.3 The Nine Events of Instruction

Gagné's most-cited contribution. Each event corresponds to an internal learner process:

| # | Event | Internal process |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Gaining attention** | Reception (alertness) |
| 2 | **Informing learner of objective** | Expectancy |
| 3 | **Stimulating recall of prior learning** | Retrieval to working memory |
| 4 | **Presenting the stimulus** | Selective perception |
| 5 | **Providing learning guidance** | Semantic encoding |
| 6 | **Eliciting performance** | Responding |
| 7 | **Providing feedback** | Reinforcement |
| 8 | **Assessing performance** | Retrieval cuing |
| 9 | **Enhancing retention and transfer** | Generalising / scheduling spaced practice |

[src: gagne-conditions-of-learning-1965.pdf p 308-319 + 11410-12000]

### §2.4 Conditions of learning → media decision

Gagné argued (p 12728+) that early-year learners benefit most from oral communication for events 1 + 3; later years can substitute printed/pictorial material; sound motion picture combines questioning + feedback uniquely well.

→ **Modern translation:** event-by-event media choice (text / video / live workshop / simulation) — basis of multimodal MOOC design (cross-cite Khan 2012).

### §2.5 Implications for Jetix substrate

- **Every Jetix wiki/course module should declare which Gagné events it covers** — bare facts-only documents (events 4-5 only) leave events 1, 2, 6, 7, 8, 9 ungated → low retention
- Method V2 §A-§Q natural mapping: §A welcome-frame = event 1+2 (gaining attention + objective), §F drill exercises = event 6+7 (eliciting + feedback), etc.
- **Workshop format intuition:** in-person МИМ-style Workshop = high-bandwidth coverage of events 6-8 (live performance + feedback + assessment) — async text alone fails here

## §3 Dewey — *Democracy and Education* (1916)

### §3.1 Core thesis

Education is **not preparation for life — it IS life**, sustained through participation in a community whose continuity depends on transmitting evolved meanings via experience [src: dewey-democracy-and-education-1916.pdf p 4-5].

### §3.2 Three foundational concepts

| Concept | Definition | Pedagogical implication |
|---|---|---|
| **Continuity of experience** | Every experience modifies the quality of future experience [src: p 5-6] | Curriculum must be sequenced so each step prepares the next (pre-Vygotsky ZPD intuition) |
| **Interaction** | Experience emerges from transaction between learner + environment | Learning environment must be designed (not just content) — class-as-laboratory |
| **Growth** | The aim of education is more growth, not arrival at a fixed end-state | No final exam «completes» learning; ongoing development is the goal |

### §3.3 Democracy as educational principle

- Democracy ≠ just political form; democracy = **mode of associated living, conjoint communicated experience** [src: p 4]
- Schools must be **microcosms of democratic life** — not authoritarian transmission belts
- Implication: **dialogue + co-construction** are constitutive of education, not optional add-ons

### §3.4 Critique of dualisms

Dewey systematically attacks 7 dualisms that distort educational practice:
1. Mind ↔ matter
2. Subject ↔ object
3. Knowledge ↔ action
4. Thought ↔ experience
5. Discipline ↔ interest
6. Vocational ↔ liberal
7. Method ↔ subject matter

→ **Modern translation:** «Theory vs practice» framing in adult ed is a false dualism Dewey already rejected. Method V2 §J meta-method composition is Deweyan in spirit (theory + practice transact).

### §3.5 Implications for Jetix

- Welcome-frame O-144 = Deweyan in spirit (associative living, not gatekept hierarchy)
- Cohort target O-161/162 voluntary = democratic mode of associated learning
- Workshop format = micro-democracy (peer learning, shared inquiry)
- **R12 anti-extraction** = Deweyan democratic principle applied to economic substrate (cannot extract beyond agreed share = cannot dominate via knowledge asymmetry)

## §4 Freire — *Pedagogy of the Oppressed* (1968)

### §4.1 The «banking» model — critique

Traditional education = **banking** [src: freire-pedagogy-of-oppressed-1968.pdf p 115]:
- Teacher deposits knowledge into passive student-accounts
- Reinforces oppressor/oppressed dynamic — the «knower» dominates the «known»
- Reduces critical consciousness to compliance
- Pre-suppositions: students don't know; teachers know; teachers think for students

### §4.2 Problem-posing education — alternative

- Education as **mutual inquiry** between teacher-student and student-teacher [src: p 115]
- World-mediated dialogue — both parties read «the word and the world»
- **Generative themes** — investigation of learners' actual concerns becomes curriculum [src: p 119]
- Co-investigation → conscientização (critical consciousness) → praxis (reflection + action)

### §4.3 Dialogue is epistemological — not just method

Freire (per Macedo intro): «Dialogue does not represent a somewhat false path... dialogue characterizes an **epistemological relationship**. Thus, dialogue is a way of knowing and should never be viewed as a mere tactic to involve students in a particular task» [src: p 219].

→ **Critical implication for Jetix:** Calling something «dialogical» without **content** (epistemological curiosity about an object of knowledge) = pseudo-Freirean kitsch [src: p 221-241]. Group therapy ≠ pedagogy.

### §4.4 Anti-dialogics vs dialogics — 8 characteristics

| Anti-dialogics (oppression) | Dialogics (liberation) |
|---|---|
| Conquest | Cooperation |
| Divide and rule | Unity for liberation |
| Manipulation | Organisation |
| Cultural invasion | Cultural synthesis |

[src: freire-pedagogy-of-oppressed-1968.pdf p 130]

## §5 Vygotsky — *Mind in Society* (1978)

### §5.1 Origin

Posthumous edited compilation (Cole + Scribner + others) of essays Vygotsky wrote in the late 1920s-early 1930s — published 1978 in English. Sociocultural foundation of learning theory.

### §5.2 Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)

> «The distance between the actual developmental level as determined by independent problem solving and the level of potential development as determined through problem solving under adult guidance, or in collaboration with more capable peers.»
> [src: vygotsky-mind-in-society-1978.pdf p 4180-4193]

**Three implications:**
1. **Assessment ≠ snapshot of current ability** — must include assisted performance to capture zone
2. **Teaching effectively = working in the zone** — neither what learner can already do (boring) nor what they cannot do (frustrating); the **just-beyond** layer
3. **What is in the ZPD today becomes the actual developmental level tomorrow** — sequencing principle [src: p 4224]

### §5.3 Internalization

> «An interpersonal process is transformed into an intrapersonal one. Every function in the child's cultural development appears twice: first, on the social level, and later, on the individual level... The internalization of cultural forms of behavior involves the reconstruction of psychological activity on the basis of sign operations.»
> [src: vygotsky-mind-in-society-1978.pdf p 2933-2942]

→ **Implication:** Solo study cannot fully substitute for social-learning episodes. Method V2 §A welcome + §H meta-control needs **someone external** at least once (mentor, peer cohort, Workshop) for internalization to complete.

### §5.4 Mediation

Cultural tools (language, signs, instruments, conventions) **mediate** between mind and world [src: p 82, 662, 2901]. Tools change what is possible to think — McLuhan's «medium is the message» avant la lettre.

→ **Implication for Jetix:** Notion templates / Claude Code / Method V2 wiki ARE mediating tools that change cognition. Choosing tools = choosing cognitive scaffolding (cross-cite O-128 external-system cybernetic + O-158 Notion-MVP-bypass).

### §5.5 Scaffolding (Bruner/Wood/Ross derivative, 1976)

Though «scaffolding» as a term comes from Wood-Bruner-Ross (1976) building on Vygotsky's ZPD, it operationalises ZPD as a teaching practice:
1. **Recruitment** — engage learner's interest in the task
2. **Reduction in degrees of freedom** — simplify task to manageable steps
3. **Direction maintenance** — keep learner oriented to goal
4. **Marking critical features** — highlight relevant gap between learner output and ideal
5. **Frustration control** — emotional regulation when learner stalls
6. **Demonstration** — model an idealized solution

### §5.6 Implications for Jetix

- **МИМ Workshop format** = ZPD-driven (peer-of-more-capable + mentor) — Vygotskian par excellence
- Cohort + curated community (O-145 / O-161) = ZPD via peer scaffolding
- Method V2 hypothesis-architecture 7-layer = scaffolding levels (each layer prepares the next)
- Ruslan Notes O-178 «системное мышление prerequisite then drill» = ZPD-sequenced learning

## §6 Cross-references — Bruner + Tyler (synthesized via canonical knowledge)

### §6.1 Bruner — *The Process of Education* (1960)

- **Spiral curriculum** — revisit same concept at increasing levels of abstraction across years (Bloom levels 1→6 over time)
- «Any subject can be taught effectively in some intellectually honest form to any child at any stage of development»
- → Method V2 in Jetix is **inherently spiral** — same 5 functional skills + 12 components of intellect revisited across §A-§Q at deepening levels

### §6.2 Tyler — *Basic Principles of Curriculum and Instruction* (1949)

Tyler's 4 questions (the «Tyler rationale»):
1. What educational purposes? (objectives)
2. What experiences provide them?
3. How are experiences organised effectively?
4. How do we know they're attained?

→ **Direct predecessor of Wiggins-McTighe backward design** (Stage 1 outcomes → Stage 3 assessment → Stage 2 learning plan). Bloom 1956 dedicated to Tyler [src: p 3].

## §7 Cross-book synthesis — pedagogy classics

### §7.1 Convergence diagram

```
Tyler 1949 (4 questions: objectives → experiences → organization → assessment)
   ↓
Bloom 1956 (cognitive taxonomy: 6 levels; 3-layer definition per category)
   ↓
Bruner 1960 (spiral curriculum; any subject any age intellectually honest form)
   ↓
Gagné 1965 (5 capability types × 9 instructional events; conditions vary by outcome type)
   ↓
Dewey 1916 (experience continuity + interaction + growth; democracy = associated living)
   ↓
Freire 1968 (banking critique; problem-posing; dialogue as epistemology; conscientization)
   ↓
Vygotsky 1978 (ZPD; internalization social→individual; mediation; cultural tools as scaffold)
```

(Dewey is earlier but conceptually integrative — Bloom + Gagné + Bruner build technical apparatus; Dewey + Freire + Vygotsky build social/political/developmental theory.)

### §7.2 Five orthogonal axes from pedagogy classics

| Axis | Pole A | Pole B | Source |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cognitive demand | Recall (level 1) | Evaluation/Synthesis (5-6) | Bloom |
| Outcome type | Verbal info | Cognitive strategy | Gagné |
| Source of meaning | Transmitted | Co-constructed | Dewey + Freire |
| Developmental level | Independent capable | Assisted potential | Vygotsky |
| Curriculum form | Linear sequence | Spiral revisit | Tyler ↔ Bruner |

A pedagogy that fixes the WRONG pole on any axis produces predictable failure modes.

### §7.3 Failure-mode inventory

| Failure pattern | Pole choice that produced it |
|---|---|
| Trivia-only assessment | All Bloom level 1 |
| Skills don't transfer | Gagné: verbal info treated, not cognitive strategies |
| Authoritarian classroom | Dewey: transmitted, not co-constructed |
| Cohort never grows | Vygotsky: stays at independent capable, never assisted reach |
| Course feels disconnected | Tyler/Bruner: linear-only, no spiral revisit |
