## §1 Khan — *The One World Schoolhouse* (2012)

### §1.1 Origin

Salman Khan, Bangladeshi-American hedge-fund analyst, started by tutoring cousin Nadia in math remotely via Yahoo notepad (2004); migrated to YouTube videos (2006); Khan Academy nonprofit founded 2008; book published 2012 articulating the pedagogy.

### §1.2 Core thesis — mastery learning at scale

Khan's central claim: traditional school timetable forces «move on when class moves on» regardless of mastery → swiss-cheese gaps that compound. **Mastery learning** (Bloom 1968) — don't move forward until current concept is mastered (e.g., 10 in a row correct) — works individually but failed at scale because traditional schools can't differentiate pace per student.

**Khan's argument:** software + video + individual progress tracking now make mastery-learning feasible at scale [src: khan-one-world-schoolhouse-2012.pdf p 88-92 + chapters on mastery learning + flipping].

### §1.3 The «no-frills video» discovery

Khan tested fancy production videos vs. amateur black-screen videos with voiceover. Result: amateur videos got **more engagement + retention** — viewers preferred informal, conversational, single-instructor-talking-through-problem format [src: p 88, 373].

Hypothesis: high-production-value triggers «I'm watching content» mode; amateur triggers «I'm being tutored by a friend» mode → better cognitive engagement.

### §1.4 Flipping the classroom

Khan re-popularised (term coined elsewhere) the model: **content delivery at home** (video) + **practice + discussion in class** (with teacher available for high-leverage interaction). Khan Academy → in-class «free time» for tutoring + projects [src: p 111].

This inverts the Gagné event allocation:
- Old: in-class events 1-5 (gain attention → present stimulus) at fixed pace; events 6-9 as homework with no help
- New: events 1-5 at home (video, self-pace, replay); events 6-9 in-class (with teacher + peer support)

### §1.5 Khan + Bloom — the 2-sigma anchor

Khan explicitly cites Benjamin Bloom's **2-sigma problem** (Bloom 1984):
- Average student tutored 1-on-1 with mastery learning performed **2 standard deviations above** average student in conventional instruction
- The «holy grail» of education = how to deliver 2σ effects without 1-on-1 cost
- Khan Academy = software-mediated mastery learning + teacher freed to interact = approximation of 2σ

### §1.6 R12 paired-frame ⚠️ — recruitment-dynamics + influence-ethics

«One World Schoolhouse» = **scaling pedagogy globally** narrative. Cross-cite recruitment-dynamics-expert R12 paired:

- **Use mode:** Truly accessible high-quality education = R12-aligned (mass paradigm shift O-184; anti-extraction since free)
- **Abuse mode:** «We're saving global education» can become recruitment narrative for venture-funded ed-tech that extracts data / locks-in / paywalls advanced features
- **Khan Academy specifically:** nonprofit; transparent; ≈ R12-clean. Many imitators commercial; due diligence per platform.

### §1.7 Implications for Jetix

- **Khan model directly applicable to Method V2 transformation** (Phase 7 Jetix Lens §2.A):
  - Record Method V2 §A-§Q as short Khan-style videos (3-15 min each, voiceover + simple visuals)
  - Build self-paced progression with mastery checks per §
  - Workshop time = «flipped» — content already absorbed at home; live time for high-bandwidth interaction
- **Notion templates O-158** = mastery-tracking infrastructure
- **Cohort + Workshop pairing** = approximation of Bloom 2σ tutoring

## §2 Mazur — *Peer Instruction: A User's Manual* (1997)

### §2.1 Origin

Eric Mazur — Harvard physics professor. Discovered that students who got A's on traditional exams (problem-solving) often had **fundamental conceptual misconceptions** that revealed themselves on Hestenes' Force Concept Inventory. Traditional «lecture + problem-set» wasn't fixing this. Mazur developed Peer Instruction as response.

### §2.2 The procedure — ConcepTest cycle

In a lecturing class, every 10-15 min:

1. **Brief lecture exposition** (5-7 min) — introduce concept
2. **ConcepTest posed** — conceptual multiple-choice question testing the just-presented concept [src: mazur-peer-instruction-1997.pdf p 462 + chapter 11]
3. **Think alone** (1-2 min) — students commit to an answer (clicker or card)
4. **Discuss with neighbor** (2-3 min) — «Convince your neighbor!» — students with different answers must explain reasoning
5. **Re-vote**
6. **Instructor reveals correct answer + explanation** — focusing on the conceptual move

### §2.3 Why it works

- **Retrieval + generation** (Make It Stick principles 1 + 5)
- **Articulation requirement** (Mazur: explaining to peer requires processing depth)
- **Peer scaffolding** (Vygotsky ZPD via «more capable peer» rotating per question)
- **Real-time formative assessment** (instructor sees misconceptions immediately — Hattie mindframe 2)
- **«One-third of class time for ConcepTests, two-thirds for traditional»** balance — replaces some lecture, not all [src: p 682]

### §2.4 ConcepTest design criteria

A good ConcepTest [src: mazur p 462+, chapters on ConcepTest design]:
1. Tests **a single concept** (not algebraic manipulation)
2. Has **a few common wrong answers** (distractor design exposes misconception)
3. Cannot be solved by formula plugging alone
4. Discriminates well in 1-2 min thinking time

### §2.5 Implementation barriers (per Mazur § «How much work is required»)

- Students initially resist new format (used to passive lecture) [src: p 1036]
- Instructor must change pedagogy mindset (Hattie mindframes 1, 7, 9)
- ConcepTest design is intellectually demanding (the «work» Mazur acknowledges; p 1919-1922)
- After ramp-up, less preparation than traditional lectures (instructor builds bank of ConcepTests over years)

### §2.6 Implications for Jetix

- **Workshop format ≈ Peer Instruction extended** — every Workshop module should include ConcepTest-style probes (commit → discuss → re-commit → reveal)
- Method V2 §F drill exercises = ConcepTest catalog
- Hypothesis Architecture 7-layer = ConcepTest topic (each layer tested separately)
- Async cohort (Notion + Telegram + voice updates) = «peer instruction» distributed via timeline

## §3 Cross-reference — Knowles Andragogy (1975)

### §3.1 Andragogy vs pedagogy

Malcolm Knowles popularised the distinction between teaching children (pedagogy = «pais» + «ago») and teaching adults (andragogy = «andros» + «ago»). 6 core andragogical assumptions:

| # | Adult learner assumption | Pedagogical adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Self-concept** — adults are self-directed | Co-design curriculum; learner-led pace |
| 2 | **Experience** — adults bring large prior experience | Use as resource; surface + connect, don't ignore |
| 3 | **Readiness** — adults learn what they NEED to navigate life roles | Anchor in real role-tasks (Merrill problem-centered) |
| 4 | **Orientation** — adults are problem-centered, not subject-centered | Frame around problems, not chapter-by-chapter |
| 5 | **Motivation** — adults motivated by internal pressures (job, life, identity) | Connect to learner's internal motivators |
| 6 | **Need to know** — adults want to know why they should learn it | Explain rationale before content |

### §3.2 Implications for Jetix

- Jetix audiences are **adults** primarily — andragogy applies directly
- Welcome-frame O-144 must satisfy «need to know» (assumption 6) before content
- Cohort O-145 = «use experience as resource» (assumption 2) via peer dialogue
- Method V2 problem-centered = assumption 4 directly
- O-185 question-first learning = andragogical assumption 6 operationalised

## §4 Cross-reference — MOOC research (Thrun/Norvig 2011; Coursera; edX)

### §4.1 MOOC empirical findings (synthesized from canonical literature 2012-2024)

| Finding | Source / mechanism |
|---|---|
| **Completion rates 5-15%** for free MOOCs | Lack of commitment + extrinsic motivation deficit |
| **Mastery + cohort + deadline = 40-60%+** completion (e.g., Coursera Specializations + dated cohorts) | Re-introduce commitment + peer accountability |
| **Forum participation correlates with completion** | Vygotsky internalization via discourse + Mazur peer instruction extended async |
| **Auto-graded practice critical** | Gagné event 6+7 at scale |
| **Live cohort+coach element drives premium pricing** | Workshop-format value persists vs commodity content |

### §4.2 Coursera «Learning How to Learn» case (Oakley + Sejnowski 2014→)

- Largest-ever MOOC by enrollment (millions)
- Demonstrates that **meta-skill content** (learning how to learn) has massive demand independent of any subject
- Khan-style videos + auto-graded quizzes + discussion forums
- Replays Bloom 2σ approximation at MOOC scale

### §4.3 Implications for Jetix

- Free-content alone won't sustain commitment → cohort + Workshop + deadlines + identity (cohort target O-161/162)
- Method V2 Coursera-style course = high-leverage distribution; cohort = high-margin

## §5 Cross-reference — Caplan *The Case Against Education* (2018)

### §5.1 Counter-position summary

Bryan Caplan (GMU economist): traditional education **mostly signals** (proxy for innate ability + conscientiousness + conformity) rather than transmits human capital. Empirical evidence:
- Forgetting rates → most factual content lost within years of school
- Wage premium of education concentrated at credential-line discontinuities (graduation) not gradual content accumulation
- Most jobs use little of what was taught

### §5.2 Why this matters for Jetix

Caplan's critique points at a **signalling-vs-substance** problem that Jetix faces:
- If a Jetix course/Workshop is mostly signal (a certificate that you participated), it competes with universities + bootcamps on credential terms
- If Jetix is mostly substance (verifiable skills + Method V2 mastery + R12 discipline + clan formation), it differentiates on outcome
- **Verifiable outputs > certificates** for Jetix positioning (cross-cite anti-extraction R12 + Workshop output portfolio)

→ **Anchor for Phase 7 §2.B — Strategic Plan multi-variant rollouts must produce verifiable outputs, not certificates.**

### §5.3 R12 paired-frame ⚠️

Caplan's signalling thesis itself can be R12-misused («education is mostly bullshit, so why pay for it?») as anti-investment argument. Counter: **substance-mode education** (Khan + Mazur + Workshop) = different category from signal-mode credentialism. Jetix must claim and demonstrate the substance lane.

## §6 Synthesis — innovative methods convergence

### §6.1 4 common threads across Khan + Mazur + MOOCs + andragogy

1. **Active retrieval > passive reception** (Mazur ConcepTest; Khan exercises; MOOC quizzes; andragogy problem-centered)
2. **Self-pace + mastery > forced timetable** (Khan; mastery learning Bloom 1968 echo; Coursera-spec mastery)
3. **Peer + community > solo** (Mazur peer instruction; MOOC forums; andragogy experience-as-resource; cohort cohesion)
4. **Verifiable performance > certificate** (Khan exercise streaks; Mazur conceptual exam; portfolio-driven; anti-Caplan)
