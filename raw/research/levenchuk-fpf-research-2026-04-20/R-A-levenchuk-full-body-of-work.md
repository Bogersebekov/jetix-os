## Executive Summary

Анатолий Игоревич Левенчук (born 23 January 1958, Rostov-on-Don) is Russia's foremost systems methodologist, systems engineer, and architect of a distinctive educational ecosystem that merges third-generation systems thinking with AI-augmented intellectual practice. Over more than three decades he has moved from securities-market infrastructure builder and Runet pioneer, through strategic consulting to industrial giants (Rosatom, RAO UES), to becoming the intellectual nucleus of the [Школа системного менеджмента / Мастерская инженеров-менеджеров (ШСМ/МИМ)](https://system-school.ru), the primary institution in the Russian-speaking world teaching integrated systems thinking, systems engineering, and methodology as a transdisciplinary stack.

His signature contribution is the articulation of a **third-generation systems thinking** ontology — one that supersedes both classical General Systems Theory (Bertalanffy) and the second-generation constructor-systems view (Checkland, ISO 15288), by incorporating techno-evolution, continuous-everything engineering, and scaleless (mereotopological) system descriptions grounded in physics-based ontology. Levenchuk operationalizes this through a hierarchical **интеллект-стек** (intellect stack) of 16–17 transdisciplines — from foundational logic and ontology, through methodology and systems engineering, to rhetoric and ethics — which he presents as the updated STEM standard for the 21st century.

Since approximately 2021, Levenchuk has executed a decisive pivot: positioning large language models and AI agents not as replacements for human cognition but as **exocortex** — externalized cognitive prosthetics that extend the intellect stack. His [First Principles Framework (FPF)](https://github.com/ailev/FPF), actively developed through 2025–2026 with AI-agent co-authorship (primarily Codex), is a pattern-language operating system for mixed human/AI teams doing open-ended engineering and research. This represents the current frontier of his output.

Institutionally, as of 2025–2026, ШСМ has been rebranded as **МИМ — Мастерская инженеров-менеджеров** (Workshop of Engineer-Managers), signaling a shift from school to practitioner-community model. Levenchuk holds the title of **научный руководитель** (Scientific Director / Head of Science) and Partner, with Tseren Tserenov as Managing Partner.

His work is positioned against — and in critical dialogue with — the Schedrovitsky (Щедровицкий) methodological school (SMD methodology / СМД), Austrian School economics, SEMAT/Essence (Ivar Jacobson), ISO/IEC systems engineering standards (15288, 42010, 15926), Active Inference (Friston), and open-ended evolution theory. His intellectual fingerprints are: extreme pragmatism, ontological explicitness, resistance to definitional closure ("definition as a coffin"), and insistence on writing-as-thinking (*мышление письмом*) as the primary practice of intellectual work.

## Section 1 — Биография и Intellectual Background

### 1.1 Full Biographical Sketch

Анатолий Игоревич Левенчук was born **23 January 1958** in **Rostov-on-Don (Ростов-на-Дону)**, Russia. In **1980** he graduated from the **Chemistry Faculty (химфак) of Rostov State University (РГУ)**. This scientific-technical background, unusually combined with early computing interests, shaped his cross-disciplinary orientation from the outset.

After graduation he worked at the RGU Computing Center and led a programmer group at Rostov Medical Institute. In **1987** he founded the **Rostov City Informatics Club**, conducting 13 professional retraining courses for programmers — an early sign of his enduring focus on education and methodology transmission.

**Moscow relocation (1989):** Levenchuk moved to Moscow in 1989 and managed "ИнфоМир" (InfoWorld), which built computer-based learning environments at Moscow State University's Faculty of Mechanics and Mathematics.

**Securities market phase (1990–1999):** He became a foundational figure in Russia's emerging capital markets infrastructure, founding the Association of Securities Market Participants (1990–1991), then creating the **Institute of Commercial Engineering** (1991–1996), where he served as Director. He was involved in first-ever Russian mass-share issuances (RINAKO, RELCOM, 1990), the first Russian depositary/registrar (RINAKO Depositary, April 1992), and the first commercial Internet server for securities information. In 1994 he co-founded "Московский Либертариум" (Moscow Libertarium), an online libertarian think-tank active through the 2000s, which in 1998 was rated among the "30 best Internet projects of the year" by *Internet* magazine.

From **October 1996 to August 1999** he served as project director at the Institute for Legal Economy Development, advising Russia's Federal Securities Commission (ФКЦБ). In **August 1999** he founded **TechInvestLab**, a consulting firm that became his operational base for the next decade and a half.

**IT and industrial consulting (1999–2010s):** TechInvestLab advised RAO "EES Rossii" (the national electricity company) during its structural reform, consulted Mineconomrazvitia on "E-Government," and from 2006 — after declaring a moratorium on government work — concentrated on large industrial clients: Rosatom enterprises, shipbuilding, and machine-building, focusing on strategic planning (*стратегирование*), systems engineering, lifecycle management, and semantic technologies. Key projects included:
- Initiation of the Multi-D construction management technology at NIAEEP (2009)
- Scientific consulting on ISO 15926 Reference Data Libraries for Rosenergoatom (2010–2011)
- Creation of the ontological engineering platform **.15926 Editor** (2010–)
- Russian-language terminology for ArchiMate 2.0 and Russification of the Archi editor (2012)

**INCOSE leadership:** He served as **President of the Russian INCOSE Chapter in 2010 and 2013**, Director of Research 2011–2012. He is also a member of ACM+SIGSOFT, ASEM, ACDM, and participated as committee member in Ontology Summit 2012–2013. He is listed as SEMAT supporter and served on the executive committee of the INCOSE Russian chapter in 2013.

**Teaching:** Levenchuk taught at МФТИ (Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology / Физтех), МИФИ, УрФУ, СФУ, Rosatom Corporate Academy, and the Inter-University Engineering Master's in Novosibirsk. In 2013, a controversy erupted at МФТИ when a complaint accused him of promoting "fascist ideology" by introducing libertarian and systems engineering ideas; a Duma deputy requested the Prosecutor General investigate him as a potential foreign agent. МФТИ officially defended him. He continued teaching.

**ШСМ founding (2015):** Together with **Tseren Tserenov** (Церен Церенов), Levenchuk co-founded the **Школа системного менеджмента (ШСМ)** in 2015, concluding that no existing courses taught systems thinking and systems management adequately. He has served as **научный руководитель** (Head of Science) since founding. In 2025–2026, ШСМ was rebranded **МИМ — Мастерская инженеров-менеджеров**.

Current (2026) address: МИМ office in Moscow, Taganka district. Personal website / blog: [ailev.livejournal.com](https://ailev.livejournal.com) ("Лабораторный журнал" / Laboratory Journal). GitHub: [github.com/ailev](https://github.com/ailev). He has lived in Moscow since 1987. Personal interests include anime culture and Brazilian music.

### 1.2 Intellectual Lineage

Levenchuk's systems thinking framework is explicitly a **synthesis** — he calls it "third-generation systems thinking" — that integrates and critiques prior schools:

**First-generation systems thinking (absorbed):** Ludwig von Bertalanffy's General Systems Theory (GST), cybernetics (Wiener, 1948), Koestler's holons (part-whole nesting). Key contribution: recognition of emergent properties and system-in-environment description.

**Second-generation systems thinking (absorbed and critiqued):** Peter Checkland's Soft Systems Methodology (SSM); ISO/IEC/IEEE 15288 (system lifecycle) and 42010 (architecture descriptions) as formalizations; 4D extensionalism in ISO 15926 (engineering ontology); SEMAT/Essence Kernel (Ivar Jacobson) — the alpha state machines and situational method engineering approach that Levenchuk adapted for systems engineering in his 2015 arXiv paper ["Towards a Systems Engineering Essence"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.00121).

**Third-generation (his synthesis, 2020s):** Incorporates techno-evolution, "continuous everything" (CD/CI paradigm scaled to system development), mereotopology and category theory as foundation ontology, Active Inference / Free Energy Principle (Friston) as a physics-based cognitive model, and open-ended evolution (NQD OEE — novelty-quality-diversity). This synthesis is detailed in his 2023 arXiv paper ["Toward an Ontology for Third Generation Systems Thinking"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11524).

**Russian methodological influences:** Levenchuk explicitly engages with, and situates himself against, the **СМД-методология** (Systems-Thought-Activity methodology) of Georgy Shchedrovitsky (Щедровицкий). He shares the SMD concern with mышление (thinking) as a cultural-activity phenomenon but diverges sharply in approach: where SMD uses Organizational Activity Games (ОДИ) and Hegelian-Marxist dialectics, Levenchuk grounds methodology in engineering standards, formal ontology, and pragmatic epistemology (Popper). He incorporates praxeology (Austrian School economics via von Mises) into his methodology curriculum, reflecting his 1990s libertarian orientation.

**Philosophical sources:** Charles Sanders Peirce (pragmatism, semiotics — which Levenchuk is actively formalizing in FPF's semio-architecture workstream, 2025–2026); John Searle (speech acts, institutional ontology); Karl Popper (epistemology as conjectures + refutations); Daniel Dennett ("hold your definition" anti-essentialism). The psybertron.org reviewer [Ian Glendinning](https://www.psybertron.org/archives/15807) describes Levenchuk as "the smartest person I ever met anywhere in any context" and identifies his "focus on pragmatism and practicality" as the defining intellectual characteristic.

**Positioning vs. Western systems thinking:** Levenchuk is aware of and engages with INCOSE-mainstream systems engineering; he served as INCOSE Russian Chapter President. But he consistently critiques "systems engineering standards" as insufficient when used alone — they address second-generation concerns and miss the evolutionary, continuous-delivery, and multi-agent coordination challenges of the current era. He aims at what he calls a "physics-based" systems ontology rooted in mereotopology and active inference rather than the conceptual modeling traditions dominant in Western SE.

## Section 3 — Эволюция Мышления (Decade-by-Decade)

### 3.1 2000–2010: Praxeology, Ontological Engineering, INCOSE

In the 2000s Levenchuk was primarily a practitioner-methodologist. His consulting engagements with Rosatom and the energy sector required him to operationalize systems engineering for Russian industrial contexts. During this period he:
- Deepened engagement with **ISO 15926** (the industrial ontology standard for process plants and lifecycle data) and became one of Russia's leading experts in **ontological engineering** — building the .15926 Editor tool.
- Developed **ArchiMate** competency (enterprise architecture language), producing the Russian terminology for ArchiMate 2.0.
- Engaged with **praxeology** (Mises' theory of action) as a methodological foundation — this period shows libertarian-Austrian-school influence on his emerging methodology framework, emphasizing purposeful human action and market coordination over planning-based approaches.
- Became active in international standards bodies (INCOSE, ACM, Ontology Summit), positioning himself at the intersection of Russian engineering culture and international SE community.

Writing was primarily in **Kompyuterra** columns and early blog posts. No book-length publications yet, but the conceptual groundwork was laid.

### 3.2 2010–2020: ШСМ Founding, Essence Adaptation, Textbook Writing

**2010–2015** saw Levenchuk formalize his methodology teaching. He served as INCOSE Russia President (2010, 2013) and became a SEMAT supporter. In 2013–2014 he led a joint INCOSE Russia / SEMAT project to adapt the Essence Kernel for systems engineering — resulting in the 2015 arXiv paper [*Towards a Systems Engineering Essence*](https://arxiv.org/abs/1502.00121). This work introduced the concept of **alpha state machines** (Essence's fundamental concept) into Russian systems engineering pedagogy, and Levenchuk extended the Kernel's alpha set from software-only to cover hardware and sociotechnical systems by modifying the "engineering solution" area of concern alphas.

**2015:** Co-founding of ШСМ with Tserenov. The school immediately began producing structured courses.

**2018:** First edition of *Системное мышление* published via Ridero. This initiated the annual/biannual rewrite cycle. The book crystallized Levenchuk's transdisciplinary framework: systems thinking as attention management tool, role/executor distinction, the three generations of systems approach, alpha state machines, and the creation graph.

**2019–2020:** Online course on [Coursera](https://coursera.org) ("Системное мышление"). Ridero sales exceed 10,000 copies across editions. The intellect stack transdiscipline list (then ~17 disciplines) was formalized in *Образование для образованных* (2020/2021).

### 3.3 2020–2025: AI-Augmented Intellect Pivot, Exocortex Concept

The critical turning point was the emergence of GPT-3/4 and Levenchuk's recognition that LLMs could serve as **exocortex** — not general AI, but externalized cognitive prosthetics operating alongside human intelligence through the intellect stack.

Key conceptual developments:
- **Exocortex framing:** LLMs as extensions of the intellect stack, not replacements. The human remains the intentional agent; AI handles pattern-matching, drafting, retrieval, and structured reasoning within FPF-constrained contexts.
- **Writing-as-thinking (*мышление письмом*):** Intensified emphasis on writing as the primary practice of serious intellectual work — directly compatible with LLM interaction paradigm where prompts and structured documents drive AI inference.
- **Past-participle convention:** Levenchuk developed a convention for machine-readable state descriptions using past participles (Russian: perfective participles as state predicates), making alpha state checklists interpretable by both humans and AI agents.
- **IWE (Intelligent Workflow Engine / персональное руководство):** The aisystant platform developed an AI guide that accompanies learners through residency programs, providing personalized recommendations and answering curriculum questions.

The 2023–2024 period saw intensive rewriting of all textbooks with AI assistance (for drafting, checking consistency). The 2024 two-volume *Системное мышление* explicitly incorporates third-generation ontology grounded in physics (Active Inference, free energy minimization, category theory).

### 3.4 2024–2026: FPF, МИМ Rebranding, Open-Ended Evolution

**Late 2024 / 2025:** Levenchuk began developing the **First Principles Framework (FPF)** — a pattern language for coordinating mixed human/AI teams on complex engineering and research tasks. Published to GitHub as [ailev/FPF](https://github.com/ailev/FPF).

FPF's structure:
- **Part A:** Core patterns for project alignment, bounded context maps, decision records
- **Parts B–G:** Specific reasoning routes (comparison, selection, portfolio management, open-ended search/evolution)
- **Part E:** Process for writing and reviewing patterns, AI-agent prompting
- **semio-architecture workstream:** Currently in development (10 campaign plan as of April 2026), formalizing semiotic concepts for engineering projects

By April 2026, FPF had 320 GitHub stars and 52 forks — remarkable for a purely technical specification in pattern-language format.

**2025 rebranding:** ШСМ → МИМ (Мастерская инженеров-менеджеров / Workshop of Engineer-Managers). The framing shifted from "school that teaches" to "workshop of practitioners who create." The 9th annual conference (April 2025) was titled "От школы менеджмента к клубу создателей."

**2026 focal themes** (from the 10th MIM Conference program, April 2026):
- First Principles Framework development plans
- AI agents in МИМ (IWE and personal guides)
- "Развитие для развитых" (Development for the Developed) — a seminar track for advanced practitioners
- AI agent pipelines for learning ШСМ materials
- Engineering semiotics in projects

Levenchuk's current AI-tool practice (as described in his blog, April 2026): He uses **Codex App** and **GPT-5.4 Pro** for FPF pattern writing via a 10-step quality-assurance process. He explicitly follows Theory of Constraints / Goldratt WIP limiting: one primary project at a time, resisting the urge to parallelize AI agents across multiple streams simultaneously.

## Section 4 — Major Frameworks Invented / Popularized

### 4.1 Framework Inventory

**Системное мышление (Third-Generation Systems Thinking)**
- *Definition:* A transdiscipline for attention management in complex collective projects. Provides a shared ontological vocabulary for describing systems across all levels of reality (physical, biological, social, engineering) using the same primitives. "Not a recipe but a scaffolding for thinking."
- *Primary source:* [Системное мышление 2024, Тт. 1–2](https://ridero.ru/books/sistemnoe_myshlenie_2024_tom_1/)
- *Year:* Introduced 2018; current generation since 2022–2024
- *Relation to other frameworks:* Foundation for all other ШСМ frameworks. Synthesizes GST (Gen 1) + SSM/SEMAT (Gen 2) + evolution/continuous-delivery/active-inference (Gen 3).

**Интеллект-стек (Intellect Stack)**
- *Definition:* A hierarchy of 16–17 transdisciplines (трансдисциплины) that constitute modern intellectual competence. Listed from foundational (понятизация / conceptualization, логика / logic, семантика / semantics) through methodological (онтология, рациональность, методология) to engineering (системное мышление, системная инженерия, системный менеджмент). Replaces STEM as the standard for 21st-century professional capability.
- *Primary source:* [Интеллект-стек 2023](https://ridero.ru/) (evolved from *Образование для образованных 2021*)
- *Year:* Formalized 2021; current 16-transdiscipline version 2023
- *Full transdiscipline list (2025 version):* Инженерия, Риторика, Эстетика, Исследования, Алгоритмика, Теория понятий, Математика, Собранность, Методология, Этика, Рациональность, Онтология, Физика, Семантика, Понятизация, Логика (source: [system-school.ru/stack](https://system-school.ru/stack))
- *Relation:* Structural backbone of all ШСМ curricula.

**Роль / Альфа / Граф создания (Role / Alpha / Creation Graph)**
- *Role (роль):* A functional position in a system-creation project, distinct from the executor (исполнитель / person) filling it. "A role is a hat, not a person." Multiple executors can fill a role; one executor can fill multiple roles. Critical for organizational design and responsibility assignment.
- *Alpha (альфа):* Borrowed from SEMAT/Essence Kernel (Ivar Jacobson et al.), adapted for systems engineering. An alpha is an essential element of any engineering endeavor characterized by a finite state machine (states with checklists). Levenchuk extended the original 7 Essence alphas (opportunity, stakeholders, requirements, software system, work, way of working, team) to include hardware and sociotechnical systems.
- *Creation graph (граф создания):* A directed graph of which systems create which other systems, capturing the multi-level constructor/constructee relationships. Not a simple hierarchical decomposition — it maps the full ecosystem of enabling systems, creating systems, and target systems.
- *Primary source:* Системное мышление 2024; arXiv 2015
- *Year:* Alpha state machines introduced ~2013–2015; creation graph developed through 2020–2024

**17 Трансдисциплин (17 Trans-Disciplines Hierarchy)**
- *Definition:* The original 2021 formulation listed 17 transdisciplines as: Труд (инженерия, менеджмент, предпринимательство), Системное мышление, Экономика, Методология, Риторика, Этика, Эстетика, Исследования, Объяснения, Логика, Алгоритмика, Онтология, Теория понятий, Теория информации, Семантика, Собранность, Понятизация. The current (2025) version has 16 transdisciplines after consolidation.
- *Primary source:* Образование для образованных 2021; see [MyBook "Введение в системное мышление"](https://mybook.ru/author/ceren-cerenov/vvedenie-vsistemnoe-myshlenie/read/?page=2)
- *Year:* 2021 original; revised 2023

**Мышление письмом (Writing-as-Thinking)**
- *Definition:* The practice of using writing — not just note-taking, but sustained analytical composition — as the primary cognitive tool for serious intellectual work. "Five paragraphs can take five hours — an hour per paragraph." Writing externalizes and stabilizes cognitive processes, making them reviewable and refinable. Directly compatible with LLM-assisted drafting.
- *Primary source:* Discussed throughout blog; see [YouTube video "Зачем публиковать посты"](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MnCV3sOkVVw); also Визуальное мышление (critique of visual alternatives).
- *Year:* Articulated throughout 2010s; explicit in curriculum from ~2018.

**Стратегирование (Strategizing as ongoing process)**
- *Definition:* Strategy not as a one-time plan document but as a continuous, never-ending practice of orientation and decision-making. Levenchuk emphasizes "бесконечное стратегирование" (infinite strategizing) — the process is more important than any artifact produced. Against "strategy documents" as endpoint.
- *Primary source:* Системный менеджмент 2023; former Системное стратегирование 2021 course.
- *Year:* Formalized ~2018–2021

**First Principles Framework (FPF)**
- *Definition:* An "operating system for thought for engineering, research, and mixed human/AI teams." A pattern language (300+ patterns by April 2026) organized into bounded contexts, decision records (DRR), vocabulary stabilization (UTS), and open-ended evolution (NQD OEE) patterns. Written in "neutral English" for AI-agent readability and type-safety across bounded contexts. Not a methodology or process — an architecture for reasoning coordination.
- *Primary source:* [github.com/ailev/FPF](https://github.com/ailev/FPF)
- *Year:* 2024–2026 (active development)
- *Relation:* The 2026 synthesis of all prior frameworks, now expressed as AI-readable pattern language.

**Экзокортекс (Exocortex)**
- *Definition:* External cognitive prosthetic extending the human intellect stack. In the current epoch: LLMs + structured documents (FPF, textbooks, FPF-spec loaded as context) functioning as an augmented second-brain. Humans remain intentional agents; AI agents handle structured reasoning within constrained contexts.
- *Primary source:* Blog posts 2021–2026; FPF Part E on AI-agent use.
- *Year:* 2021–present

## Section 5 — Current State (2026)

### 5.1 МИМ Curriculum Structure

As of 2026, МИМ operates on a **residency model** rather than course catalog:
- **Онлайн-руководства** (online guides): All textbook content freely accessible post-registration at aisystant.system-school.ru
- **Residencies (Р0 → Р1 → Р2):** Sequential, each ~19 weeks with weekly mentor-led "разборы"; 10–15 hours/week required
- **IWE (Intelligent Workflow Engine):** AI personal guide that tracks learner progress, answers questions, provides route recommendations — the practical implementation of the exocortex concept within the school
- **Practitioner seminars:** Paid/free topic-specific seminars (semiotics, FPF, AI agent management)
- **Annual conference:** Free, 2-day, 20 talks — the intellectual heartbeat of the community

### 5.2 Current Framework Emphasis

**Most emphasized (2025–2026):**
- FPF as the master framework integrating all prior transdisciplines
- AI-agent management and collaboration (not just using AI but managing AI as team members)
- Engineering semiotics — Levenchuk is actively developing the semio-architecture workstream of FPF (April 2026 seminar, 10-campaign plan)
- "Развитие для развитых" — advanced practitioner track for МИМ masters and reformers
- Open-ended evolution (NQD OEE) theory as foundation for FPF's portfolio/frontier patterns

**De-emphasized / background:**
- Individual course certificates and formal credentials — МИМ is explicitly moving away from school-like certification toward community-of-practice model
- Structured strategy documents — replaced by infinite strategizing practice
- Single-discipline ISO standard compliance as sufficient — subsumed into richer integrated framework

**Current intellectual investment (as declared in blog, April 2026):**
1. Completing NQD OEE patterns in FPF (frontier/palette/portfolio taxonomy)
2. Semiotics architecture in FPF (10 campaign plan)
3. Second- and third-principles frameworks (SPF/TPF) — domain and enterprise-specific models built on FPF meta-model
4. Methodology for human/AI pattern-writing quality control

### 5.3 AI Integration Stance

Levenchuk's AI stance is notably **precise and non-evangelical**:

- LLMs are **exocortex**, not general intelligence — they extend the intellect stack but don't replace it
- FPF provides the **architecture for AI-readable reasoning** — bounded contexts, stable vocabulary (UTS), decision records (DRR) make AI outputs reviewable and non-drifting
- AI agents are **team members with specific roles** — they need onboarding to the FPF context (loaded as system prompt / document context), not just instructions
- The **past-participle convention** for state descriptions (e.g., "requirements identified," "architecture selected") makes alpha-state checklists machine-parseable
- **Quality control challenge:** Levenchuk extensively documents the failure mode of AI agents in Codex — historical drift (inserting "guardrails" and "red-entry" notes instead of making clean corrections), fanout proliferation, and inability to distinguish content edits from process artifacts. He actively develops counter-patterns in FPF Part E.
- **WIP discipline with AI:** He explicitly applies Goldratt's TOC to AI-agent use — one primary project active, avoiding parallel-agent overload, achieving faster throughput by less multitasking. Cited as "unfair competitive advantage" others won't adopt because it's counterintuitive.
- **Models used:** Primarily Codex App and GPT-5.4 Pro for FPF development (as of April 2026).

## Section 6 — Critical Reception and Criticisms

### 6.1 Reception in Russian Methodological Community

Levenchuk occupies a distinctive and somewhat controversial position relative to the **SMD-methodology (СМД) tradition** of Georgy Shchedrovitsky (Щедровицкий). He engages with SMD concepts (thought-activity methodology, organizational activity games) but rejects the dialectical-Marxist epistemology in favor of Popperian falsificationism and engineering pragmatism. His 2021 blog post ["Системная методология как трансдисциплина"](https://ailev.livejournal.com/1559209.html) explicitly addresses this positioning.

Petr Shchedrovitsky (Пётр Щедровицкий, son of Georgy) acknowledged Levenchuk's school as a positive example of systems engineering education in the Russian context (cited in [mtsepkov.org notes on P. Shchedrovitsky lecture, 2020](https://mtsepkov.org/)).

**On LeanZone.ru:** A practitioner debate thread notes Levenchuk's classification of system dynamics as cybernetics (because it uses feedback) — this is contested by Lean/systems dynamics practitioners who argue for a distinct lineage. Source: [leanzone.ru](https://www.leanzone.ru/index.php?option=com_kunena&view=topic&catid=9&id=13813).

**МФТИ 2013 controversy:** As noted in Section 1.1, Levenchuk faced a formal complaint and Duma inquiry at МФТИ for allegedly promoting "fascist ideology." This reflects tension between his libertarian-systems engineering approach and Soviet-era engineering education norms. МФТИ defended him.

**General reception:** Within the Russian IT/engineering community Levenchuk is widely respected — cited on Habr (Habrahabr), discussed in book reviews, and his Ridero books rank as bestsellers. His writing style is characteristically dense (he acknowledges it himself: "I write primarily for myself") and his courses have a reputation for being intellectually demanding.

### 6.2 International Reception

The most detailed English-language engagement is by **Ian Glendinning** at [Psybertron.org](https://www.psybertron.org/archives/15807), who describes Levenchuk and Agroskin as "the smartest people I ever met anywhere in any context" and provides a substantive review of *Systems Thinking 2020* (the English translation). Glendinning notes the connection between Levenchuk's approach and Active Inference (Friston), pragmatism (Peirce, Dennett), and the "definition as a coffin" anti-essentialism.

Levenchuk's arXiv papers are indexed and cited in English-language AI/CS literature. The 2023 ["Toward an Ontology for Third Generation Systems Thinking"](https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11524) has been indexed at [emergentmind.com](https://www.emergentmind.com/papers/2310.11524). His 2015 paper on Systems Engineering Essence has been referenced in subsequent SE methodology literature.

FPF's GitHub presence (320 stars, 52 forks as of April 2026) suggests an international audience, though its technical English is highly specialized.

**Active Inference Institute:** МИМ mentor **Ivan Metelkin** (co-founder of Active Inference Institute) represents an interface between Levenchuk's framework and the Fristonian active inference international community.

### 6.3 Criticisms

**Inaccessibility:** Levenchuk himself acknowledges that his blog ("Лабораторный журнал") is "written primarily for myself" and "low on readability." The written textbooks require significant prerequisite knowledge and are not casual reads — the school explicitly states prerequisites (Р0 before Р1, etc.).

**Ontological overload:** Critics from a pragmatic engineering background sometimes find the ontological foundation (mereotopology, category theory, 4D extensionalism) overly abstract for practical project work. Levenchuk's counter is that without proper ontology, AI systems and large teams produce semantic drift — exactly the problem FPF addresses.

**Ecosystem dependency:** The curriculum assumes familiarity with a specific vocabulary (alpha, граф создания, интеллект-стек) that requires full investment in the ШСМ/МИМ educational path. Cross-pollination with Western SE education is limited by language and by the distinctiveness of the framework.

**Annual rewrite pace:** The frequent complete rewrites of textbooks (annually for some titles) create moving-target challenges for practitioners who need stable reference material. This is acknowledged but framed as a feature — the curriculum tracks the SoTA.
