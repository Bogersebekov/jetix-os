I need a deep synthesis research on **product management methodologies as they apply to AI-native teams in 2026** — teams where LLM agents, AI-augmented engineering, and Compounding Engineering change the discovery-build-ship cadence. ## Background

"AI-native team" for this research = a team (1-50 people, including hybrid human+AI) where:
- Compound Engineering or equivalent: Plan → Work → Review → Compound as the core loop
- Significant (>30%) of individual-contributor output is AI-augmented
- The team ships multiple times per day; discovery often happens in parallel with build
- The solo-founder → small-team → holding-company trajectory is a specific shape of interest

### D4. Top 10 product traps to avoid

In AI-native teams, with citations:

- Treating AI as "faster developer" instead of first-class discovery partner
- MVP fetishism when full prototype takes hours
- User interviews at Scale-like cadence when AI can synthesize patterns faster (but this is itself a trap — AI-synthesized patterns hallucinate)
- Over-reliance on eval suites that diverge from real user value
- "Vibe revenue" (Greg Isenberg) — shipping AI features without validated outcome
- OKR cargo cult
- RICE theater (inflating confidence scores)
- Category creation before product-market fit
- Premature productization (Domain 4 trap)
- Solo founder building PM function when it's not the binding constraint
