### Section 2 — Holon theory (Koestler + extensions)

2.1. **Arthur Koestler's holon concept** (1967, "The Ghost in the Machine"):
- Definition: holon = whole-and-part simultaneously
- "Holarchy" — recursive hierarchy of holons
- Janus-faced character (looking down at parts; looking up at wholes)
- Self-assertive vs integrative tendencies

2.2. **Ken Wilber's extension:**
- Four-quadrant holons (interior-individual, exterior-individual,
  interior-collective, exterior-collective)
- Holarchic levels of consciousness / matter / culture
- "Tetra-arising" of all four quadrants together

2.3. **Other holon theorists:**
- Mella's hierarchy of holons
- Jantsch on self-organizing systems as holons
- Maturana / Varela autopoiesis as holon-related

2.4. **Holon vs system vs whole** — distinctions:
- Holons emphasize part-whole simultaneity
- Systems emphasize boundaries + relations
- Wholes emphasize unity
