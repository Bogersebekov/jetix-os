## §2 Counter-cases inventory (≥3 mandatory; breadth-NOT-selection)

### §2.1 Counter-case 1 — Crisis innovation (Manhattan Project, Mulberry harbours)

**Context:** WWII conditions; existential threat; develop-first imperative.

**Manhattan Project (1942-1945):**
- Atomic weapons development under wartime urgency
- Safety bounds (radiation exposure, contamination, ethical oversight) compromised or suspended
- Operational mode: «develop-first; safety as constraint, not gate»
- Famous Oppenheimer quote (Trinity test 1945): «Now I am become Death, the destroyer of worlds» — safety realisation *after* development

**Mulberry harbours (D-Day 1944):**
- Floating concrete harbours для Normandy invasion
- Designed + built in <6 months; testing minimal; structural failure tolerated
- Develop-first acceptable because failure modes < failure-to-develop (invasion necessity)

**Pattern violation:** Under existential threat, develop-first may dominate safety-first. Time-criticality outweighs safety bound.

**Bounding condition:** Safety→Develop applies when (a) time available to bound safety + (b) failure-to-develop cost < safety-failure cost. Under crisis: condition (b) reversed.

**F-grade impact:** F3 (universal) → F2 (bounded). Pattern remains canonical *within* non-crisis context.

[src: Rhodes «The Making of the Atomic Bomb» 1986 + Pakenham «Pakenham's Mulberry» 1958]

### §2.2 Counter-case 2 — Exploration / greenfield discovery

**Context:** Early-stage research where safety bounds are themselves unknown.

**Examples:**
- Early antibiotic discovery (Fleming 1928) — penicillin safety bounds unknown until human trials
- Early space exploration (Mercury / Gemini programs 1961-1966) — astronaut safety bounds calibrated *through* missions
- Early internet protocol design (Cerf+Kahn 1974) — security model added 20+ years later (TLS 1995)
- Early CRISPR research (2012-2018) — ethical bounds emerging as technology develops

**Pattern violation:** Safety bounds can only be *defined* after some development creates the safety knowledge space. Pure safety-first = paralysis (cannot bound unknown).

**Bounding condition:** Safety→Develop applies when prior knowledge sufficient to bound safety ex ante. Pure greenfield = bootstrap problem (develop minimally to learn safety; iterate).

**F-grade impact:** F2 (well-corroborated) for established domains; F3 for greenfield domains.

**Cross-discipline nuance:** This aligns с Knight (Phase 4): under true Knightian uncertainty, safety classification ex ante is impossible — develop with consent to bear uncertainty is the operational mode.

[src: Fleming 1928 Nobel lecture + Cerf+Kahn 1974 RFC 793 lineage + Doudna 2017 CRISPR retrospective]

### §2.3 Counter-case 3 — Embedded competitive race (AGI labs 2022-2026)

**Context:** Multi-agent competitive equilibrium where unilateral safety-first investment loses race.

**Examples:**
- OpenAI / Anthropic / Google DeepMind AGI race (2022-2026)
- Safety framework not yet established at frontier
- Each lab faces «if I don't develop, competitor does» dynamic
- Coordination via voluntary commitments (Asilomar 2017; Bletchley 2023; Seoul 2024) limited

**Pattern violation:** Under multi-agent race, individual safety-first dominated by competitor develop-first. Nash equilibrium ≠ Pareto-optimal.

**Bounding condition:** Safety→Develop applies when individual decision-maker can bound safety without competitive penalty. Competitive race requires *coordinated* safety bound (e.g. regulation, voluntary moratoria) — institutional, not individual.

**F-grade impact:** F2 (well-corroborated) for single-agent contexts; F3 for multi-agent competitive contexts.

**R12 bridge:** Multi-agent race with safety race-to-bottom = institutional extraction (society bears AGI safety cost while labs extract value). R12 anti-extraction implies coordinated regulation needed.

[src: Bostrom «Superintelligence» 2014 + Asilomar AI Principles 2017 + Bletchley Declaration 2023 + Seoul Declaration 2024]

### §2.4 Counter-case 4 (bonus) — Liberty Ships (1942-1945)

**Context:** WWII mass production; built-in-quality discipline relaxed.

**Operational:** ~2710 Liberty ships built 1941-1945; defect rate high; some failures (notably welded-hull cracking — SS Schenectady 1943); throughput prioritised.

**Pattern violation:** Toyota Jidoka principle (Phase 3) violated. Throughput > quality temporary.

**Outcome:** Mission success (war effort) at cost of post-war refit cycles. Validates that under crisis, develop-first acceptable IF post-crisis remediation feasible.

[src: Sawyer+Mitchell «The Liberty Ships» 1985 + USS Liberty Ship retrospective]

### §2.5 Counter-cases summary

| Counter-case | Domain | Bounding condition | F-grade adjustment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crisis innovation | Wartime; existential threat | Time + relative-cost reversed | F3 universal → F2 bounded |
| Greenfield exploration | Early-stage research | Safety knowledge bootstrap problem | F2 established / F3 greenfield |
| Competitive race | Multi-agent equilibrium | Individual-vs-coordinated safety | F2 single / F3 multi-agent |
| Liberty Ships (WWII) | Mass-production crisis | Throughput > quality temporary | F3 (manufacturing context only) |

**Net effect:** Safety→Develop pattern is **F2 within bounded context**. Universal claim → F3.

**This is the K-5 correct positioning:** «Safety→Develop is a well-corroborated ordering invariant under specifiable conditions, not a universal law.»

## §3 Adjacent disciplines bridging (≥3 mandatory)

### §3.1 NASA safety-first ethos

**Sources:**
- NASA System Safety Handbook NPR 8715.3 (current)
- NASA System Safety Handbook NASA-STD-8709 series
- Columbia Accident Investigation Board Report (CAIB 2003)
- Challenger Rogers Commission Report (1986)

**Pattern parallel:** «Design for safety; verify against safety requirements; halt development if safety not demonstrated.»

**Verbatim (CAIB 2003 vol. 1 §6.5):**
> «The Board found that NASA's organizational culture had as much to do with this accident as the foam… The Shuttle Program failed to integrate safety as a top-level criterion in mission decisions.»

**Cross-corroboration F-grade boost:** Aviation / aerospace independent corroboration of Safety→Develop. Operational mechanism (Mishap Investigation Board + Safety Review Process) ≈ SRE blameless postmortem + Jidoka Andon.

**Bridge insight:** Institutional safety primacy = institutional design problem (Columbia / Challenger both = institutional failure, not technical).

[src: CAIB 2003 vol. 1 §6.5 + Rogers Commission 1986]

### §3.2 Nuclear plant safety hierarchies (defense-in-depth)

**Sources:**
- IAEA Safety Standards Series (IAEA NS-R-1; current ISO 19443)
- NRC Reactor Safety Study WASH-1400 (Rasmussen 1975)
- INSAG (International Nuclear Safety Advisory Group) reports

**Pattern parallel:** «Defense-in-depth» — multiple independent safety layers; no single point of failure; safety primacy embedded в design philosophy.

**Verbatim (INSAG-12, «Basic Safety Principles for Nuclear Power Plants» 1999):**
> «The principle of defence in depth is implemented primarily through the combination of a number of consecutive and independent levels of protection that would have to fail before harmful effects could be caused to people or to the environment.»

**Cross-corroboration:** Nuclear discipline = ZERO TOLERANCE для catastrophic failure modes. Safety→Develop is *constitutional* — no nuclear plant operates without primary safety case demonstration.

**Counter-case 1 (crisis) parallel:** Chernobyl (1986) and Fukushima (2011) demonstrate institutional safety-discipline failure under different stressors. Reinforces «institutional embedding» as critical (cf. NASA §3.1).

[src: INSAG-12 1999 + WASH-1400 1975 + Chernobyl IAEA report 1986 + Fukushima IAEA report 2015]

### §3.3 Aviation accident discipline (NTSB / ICAO)

**Sources:**
- NTSB Aircraft Accident Report series
- ICAO Annex 13 (Aircraft Accident and Incident Investigation)
- Reason «Human Error» (1990) — Swiss Cheese model

**Pattern parallel:** «Accident investigation discipline» — every accident analysed root-cause; safety recommendations binding; develop (new aircraft / new procedures) only after safety verification.

**Verbatim (Reason 1990, Swiss Cheese model):**
> «Defences, barriers, and safeguards may be penetrated by an accident trajectory… higher-order organisational factors create the conditions in which active failures occur.»

**Cross-corroboration F-grade boost:** Aviation = 5th independent operational corroboration. Cross-discipline pattern stable.

**HRO bridge:** Reason's organisational factors → Weick+Sutcliffe HRO research.

[src: Reason 1990 + NTSB report archive + ICAO Annex 13]

### §3.4 HRO (High-Reliability Organisations) — Weick + Sutcliffe

**Sources:**
- Weick K.E. & Sutcliffe K.M. «Managing the Unexpected» (Jossey-Bass 1st ed.) 2001; 2nd ed. 2007; 3rd ed. 2015
- Roberts K.H. «Some Characteristics of One Type of High Reliability Organization» (Organization Science 1990)
- Schulman P.R. «The Negotiated Order of Organizational Reliability» (Administration & Society 1993)

**Pattern parallel:** «Mindful organising» — preoccupation with failure; reluctance to simplify; sensitivity to operations; commitment to resilience; deference to expertise.

**Verbatim (Weick+Sutcliffe 2007 ch. 1):**
> «High-reliability organizations sustain reliable performance in the face of constant change. They do so by anticipating the unexpected and by improvising responses when the unexpected occurs.»

**Five HRO principles:**
1. **Preoccupation with failure** — track weak signals
2. **Reluctance to simplify** — preserve nuance in safety models
3. **Sensitivity to operations** — frontline awareness
4. **Commitment to resilience** — recovery capacity
5. **Deference to expertise** — authority flows to competence, not rank

**Cross-corroboration:** HRO = synthesis of multiple operational disciplines (nuclear / aviation / aircraft carriers / medical). Institutional Safety→Develop = institutional design pattern.

**K-5 implication:** Constitutional R13 candidate has HRO-research backing (institutional design field).

[src: Weick+Sutcliffe 2007 + Roberts 1990 + Schulman 1993]
