## §6 Pattern 6 — Sponsor-driven vs participant-driven dynamics

**Observation:** Hackathons cluster into two attractor states:
- **Sponsor-driven** (corporate hackathons; recruitment + IP + brand) — examples: MLH, ETHGlobal, Anthropic Build Days.
- **Participant-driven** (community-led; learning + portfolio + connections) — examples: g0v.tw, ODS DataFest, Hugging Face community.

Hybrid models (e.g. Kaggle, Devpost) sit between but tend to drift toward sponsor-driven over time (revenue gravity).

**Anti-extraction trade-off:** sponsor-driven ↔ higher extraction risk; participant-driven ↔ lower funding capacity.

**Cross-phase cite:** Phase 1 §7.3 governance + Phase 4 §3 corporate.

**Jetix implication:** Goal = hybrid с participant-driven gravity preserved through R12 contractual + QF mechanism. Differentiator: most platforms drift sponsor; Jetix programmatically prevents drift.

## §12 Pattern 12 — First-event success template (5 elements)

**Observation:** Successful first hackathons across platforms (MLH first event, Lablab.ai first event, Encode first event) share 5 elements:
1. **Tight cohort** (20-50 participants; quality > quantity).
2. **Theme specificity** (vague theme = mediocre output; specific theme = great output).
3. **Sponsor-mentor-participant triangle** (all three present, balanced).
4. **Day-rhythm or weekend** (NOT week+ for first event — coordination complexity).
5. **Demo + judging artefact** (something visible to outside world).

**Cross-phase cite:** Phase 5 entire (incorporates all 5 elements); Phase 1 cross-precedent.

**Jetix implication:** Event 1 Phase 5 blueprint = template-compliant. Q3 2026 Berlin event has all 5 elements; success template predicts viability.
