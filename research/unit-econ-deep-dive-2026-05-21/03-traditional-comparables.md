# DR-26 Phase 3 — Traditional Industry Comparables

> **Цель Phase 3.** Зафиксировать traditional take rate benchmarks (marketplaces / SaaS / consulting / cooperatives / education) для positioning Jetix против industry-cited ranges.

---

## §1 Marketplaces

### §1.1 Uber

**Take rate:** 25% базовая (driver gets 75% of fare); реально варьируется 20-30% per region per ride-type [src: Uber S-1 IPO filing 2019; Uber quarterly investor reports]

**Mechanics:** booking fee + service fee + per-ride percentage; surge pricing increases ride economics
**Driver classification:** independent contractors (regulatory contestation ongoing 2020-2024)
**Lesson для Jetix:** ~25% take rate is industry-acceptable for marketplace if value-add (matching + payments + dispute resolution) clear

### §1.2 Lyft

**Take rate:** ~20-25% effectively (similar to Uber band) [src: Lyft S-1 IPO 2019; investor relations 2024]

**Mechanics:** similar to Uber; somewhat lower platform fee historically
**Lesson:** convergence к ~25% indicates marketplace equilibrium where supply (drivers) and demand (riders) balance

### §1.3 Etsy

**Take rate:** **6.5% transaction fee** + 0.20 USD listing fee + 3% payment processing = **~10% effective** [src: Etsy seller fees policy 2024; Etsy 10-K 2023]

**Mechanics:** sellers pay; lower than Uber because Etsy не provides physical fulfillment (handicraft sellers ship themselves)
**Lesson для Jetix:** ~6-10% feasible если platform value-add minimal (matching + payments only)

### §1.4 eBay

**Take rate:** **10-13.25%** Final Value Fee depending on category + payment processing 2.9% + 0.30 USD per transaction [src: eBay seller fees 2024; eBay 10-K 2023]

**Mechanics:** seller pays; varies by category (electronics typically 13.25%, others 10-12%)
**Lesson для Jetix:** category-variable take rate established pattern

### §1.5 Airbnb

**Take rate:** **3% host fee + 14% guest fee = ~17% effective combined** [src: Airbnb help center; Airbnb 10-K 2023]

**Mechanics:** dual-sided fee split; lower host fee reduces friction для supply
**Lesson для Jetix:** dual-sided extraction is established pattern; not applicable to single-sided cohort model

### §1.6 Marketplace summary

| Platform | Take rate | Distinctive |
|---|---|---|
| Uber / Lyft | 20-30% | High platform value-add (physical service matching) |
| Airbnb | 17% combined | Dual-sided split |
| eBay | 10-13% | Category-variable |
| Etsy | ~10% | Low platform value-add |
| **Range** | **6.5% - 30%** | **Median ~15-20%** |

**Industry median:** ~15-20% for matching-only marketplaces; ~25-30% для marketplaces с heavy platform value-add (physical service matching).

## §2 SaaS

SaaS take rate ≠ marketplace take rate; SaaS pricing is **subscription-based revenue** rather than percentage extraction. Comparison is awkward but useful for cohort-subscription analogue.

### §2.1 Stripe (payments infrastructure)

**Take rate:** **2.9% + $0.30 per transaction** (standard card processing) [src: Stripe pricing public; Stripe ATR 2024]

**Margin:** thin per-transaction; volume-driven; gross margin ~40-50% за вычетом interchange [src: Stripe investor disclosures; industry analyst reports]
**Lesson для Jetix:** processing infrastructure ≠ comparable; не applicable как take rate analogue

### §2.2 Twilio (communications infrastructure)

**Take rate:** per-message / per-call usage; не percentage of customer revenue
**Comparable:** не applicable как marketplace take rate

### §2.3 Notion / Linear / etc. (SaaS subscription)

**Pricing model:** $5-50 per user per month (typical SaaS)
**Margin:** SaaS gross margin ~70-85% [src: SaaS Capital benchmarking reports 2023-2024]
**Lesson для Jetix:** если Jetix sells «membership subscription» as Workshop access fee, SaaS-style pricing applicable; institutional take rate ≠ direct comparable

### §2.4 SaaS summary

SaaS не provides direct take rate comparable; relevant only if Jetix monetization shifts к pure subscription model (currently mixed: workshop + consulting + advisory; per Distribution Plan §5 partial spec).

## §3 Consulting / Professional Services

### §3.1 McKinsey / BCG / Bain (top-tier strategy consulting)

**Revenue model:** project billing + retainer; markup 30-40% over consultant fully-loaded cost [src: industry analyst data; partner economics literature; «McKinsey Way» Rasiel 1999; «House of Lies» 2005]

**Partner economics:** 50%+ of revenue retained для partnership equity + bonus pool; ~30-40% direct consultant compensation; rest = operations + L&D + technology [src: industry analyst data]

**Lesson для Jetix:** consulting industry retention rate (50%+) significantly higher than marketplace; reflects high partner-level cost of business development + IP development

### §3.2 Boutique consulting

**Revenue model:** project billing $200-500+ per hour; markup 50-100% over consultant cost
**Partner economics:** smaller fixed cost = higher direct profit margin
**Lesson для Jetix:** boutique-scale consulting (~5-15 person team) can sustain higher take rate

### §3.3 Freelance platforms (Upwork / Toptal)

**Take rate:**
- **Upwork:** 10-20% sliding scale based on freelancer-client lifetime spend [src: Upwork fee policy 2023-2024]
- **Toptal:** ~30-50% markup на freelancer rate [src: industry estimates; Toptal pricing pages historically opaque]

**Lesson для Jetix:** freelance platforms 10-30% range; closer к Jetix model if «individual cohort member completes work»

### §3.4 Consulting summary

| Model | Take rate / retention | Distinctive |
|---|---|---|
| Top-tier strategy (McKinsey / BCG / Bain) | 50-60% retention | High partner economics |
| Boutique | 30-40% markup; 30-50% retention | Small team; lean |
| Upwork (freelance marketplace) | 10-20% | Platform extraction sliding scale |
| Toptal | 30-50% markup | High extraction; vetting premium |

## §4 Cooperatives (traditional, non-Mondragón)

### §4.1 REI (Recreational Equipment Inc.) — US consumer cooperative

**Members:** ~24M+ paying members; one-time $20 lifetime membership [src: REI annual reports; REI cooperative structure pages]

**Member dividend:** ~10% of member's annual spend returned as dividend (member rewards) [src: REI member rewards policy 2023-2024]

**Effective «take rate»:** REI operates retail at standard margins; ~10% of member spend returned via dividends; rest funds operations + member services + cooperative reinvestment

**Lesson для Jetix:** ~10% direct return to members + remainder retained для operations is established cooperative pattern.

### §4.2 Ocean Spray (agricultural cooperative)

**Members:** 700+ cranberry farmer-owners [src: Ocean Spray about page; agricultural coop literature]

**Pricing:** member farmers receive proportional share of cooperative sales revenue based on cranberry contribution; reserves retained для marketing + R&D + capital

**«Take rate»:** institutional retention ~15-25% (industry estimates; non-public exact figures); rest distributed to member farmers proportionally

**Lesson для Jetix:** agricultural coop typical retention 15-25% for institutional operations + marketing + R&D = comparable к Mondragón pattern.

### §4.3 Credit unions (US)

**Model:** member-deposit institutional; profit returns as lower loan rates + higher deposit rates rather than dividends
**Comparable take rate:** не applicable как direct take rate; depository model different

### §4.4 Cooperatives summary

| Cooperative | Effective extraction | Distribution |
|---|---|---|
| REI | ~10% direct dividend; rest institutional | Retail dividend |
| Ocean Spray | 15-25% institutional | Member share of sales |
| Mondragón (per Phase 1) | 60% institutional / 40% member direct | Multi-layer |
| Credit unions | non-applicable | Banking-rate dynamics |

**Cooperative pattern range:** ~10-25% institutional retention is typical; matches Mondragón institutional layer (10% inter-coop + ~50% retention = ~60% institutional; balance to members) but cooperatives vary в institutional retention size.

## §5 Education / Knowledge Platforms

### §5.1 Coursera

**Take rate:** **50% revenue share** typically (instructor / institution gets 50%); higher for specific contracts [src: Coursera partner agreements; «Course of Their Own» reports; Coursera 10-K]

**Mechanics:** course creators receive ~50% of paid enrollment revenue; Coursera retains 50% for platform + marketing + technology
**Lesson для Jetix:** education-platform take rate **substantially higher** than marketplace; reflects content investment + marketing + branding leverage

### §5.2 Udemy

**Take rate:** **37-63%** depending on referral source [src: Udemy instructor fee structure 2023-2024]
- Instructor's own promotion: 63% instructor / 37% Udemy
- Udemy organic marketing: 37% instructor / 63% Udemy
- Udemy promotional: 25% instructor / 75% Udemy

**Lesson для Jetix:** education-platform take rate varies sharply with marketing attribution; sweet spot ~37-50% extraction

### §5.3 Maven (cohort-based courses platform)

**Take rate:** **10% platform fee** for course creators [src: Maven creator pages; Wes Kao / Gagan Biyani public statements 2021-2024]

**Mechanics:** Maven focuses на cohort-based courses (CBCs); lower fee than traditional education platforms; creator owns audience/email list
**Lesson для Jetix:** cohort-based education platform 10% take rate = excellent comparable; Maven model close to Jetix Workshop model

### §5.4 Teachable / Thinkific (SaaS-style course platforms)

**Take rate:** monthly platform fee + 0-5% transaction fee depending on plan tier [src: Teachable pricing 2024; Thinkific pricing 2024]

**Mechanics:** SaaS subscription model with creator paying platform fee; creator keeps majority of course revenue
**Lesson для Jetix:** SaaS-style ~5% transaction take rate viable если Jetix monetises via subscription too

### §5.5 Education summary

| Platform | Take rate | Distinctive |
|---|---|---|
| Coursera | ~50% | High-touch institutional partnership |
| Udemy | 37-63% sliding | Marketing attribution-based |
| Maven | 10% | Cohort-based courses; creator-owned audience ⭐⭐ |
| Teachable / Thinkific | ~5% transaction (+ SaaS fee) | Pure infrastructure model |

**Education platform range:** 5-63%; **cohort-based subset (Maven) = 10%** — closest comparable to Jetix Workshop model.
