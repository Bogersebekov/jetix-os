# Phase 3 — Senge + Sterman organisational systems thinking

> Peter Senge (MIT) + John Sterman (MIT System Dynamics Group) — operationalised
> systems thinking for organisations. Senge popularised; Sterman formalised.
> Both descendants of Jay Forrester's MIT system dynamics tradition.

## §1 Peter Senge — «The Fifth Discipline» (1990, rev. 2006)

### §1.1 Verbatim canonical statements (4 anchors)

1. *«Systems thinking is the fifth discipline. It is the discipline that integrates the other four, fusing them into a coherent body of theory and practice.»* [src: Senge 1990, ch. 1]
2. *«Many of the problems facing executives appear as no-win dilemmas because they have grown up thinking the way that the executives thought when those problems were created.»* [src: Senge 1990, ch. 4 §«Laws of the Fifth Discipline»]
3. *«Learning organizations are organizations where people continually expand their capacity to create the results they truly desire, where new and expansive patterns of thinking are nurtured, where collective aspiration is set free, and where people are continually learning how to see the whole together.»* [src: Senge 1990, Introduction]
4. *«Personal mastery is the discipline of continually clarifying and deepening our personal vision, of focusing our energies, of developing patience, and of seeing reality objectively.»* [src: Senge 1990, ch. 9]

### §1.3 11 «Laws of the Fifth Discipline» (Senge 1990, ch. 4)

1. Today's problems come from yesterday's «solutions»
2. The harder you push, the harder the system pushes back
3. Behaviour grows better before it grows worse
4. The easy way out usually leads back in
5. The cure can be worse than the disease
6. Faster is slower
7. Cause and effect are not closely related in time and space
8. Small changes can produce big results — but the areas of highest leverage are often the least obvious
9. You can have your cake and eat it too — but not at once
10. Dividing an elephant in half does not produce two small elephants
11. There is no blame

**Direct cross-link к text_012 §2.9 «sense of measure»:** Laws #1, #2, #4, #5, #6 are sense-of-measure failure modes (over-intervention; under-intervention; speed mistaken for progress).

## §2 John Sterman — «Business Dynamics» (2000)

### §2.1 Verbatim canonical statements (4 anchors)

1. *«All decisions are based on models of the world, and all models are wrong.»* [src: Sterman 2000, ch. 1; echoes G.E.P. Box]
2. *«Systems thinking requires you to broaden your boundary, see longer time horizons, and consider how today's decisions create tomorrow's problems.»* [src: ibid., ch. 1]
3. *«Policy resistance is the tendency for interventions to be defeated by the response of the system to the intervention itself.»* [src: ibid., ch. 1 + ch. 5]
4. *«Stock-flow distinction is the foundation of all dynamics.»* [src: ibid., ch. 6]

### §2.2 Core system-dynamics archetypes

Sterman catalogues + simulates 10+ system archetypes; subset most relevant to K-6:

| Archetype | Pattern | K-6 cross-link |
|---|---|---|
| Fixes that Backfire | Short-term fix creates long-term problem | text_012 §2.9 sense of measure failure mode |
| Limits to Growth | R-loop dominates until constraint binds, then B-loop dominates | text_013 §2.14 «improvement drive» + sense-of-measure ceiling |
| Shifting the Burden | Symptomatic fix substitutes for root cause; root-cause capability atrophies | text_013 §2.11 (self-knowledge) + text_014 §2.30 exokortex DEPENDENCE RISK |
| Tragedy of Commons | Individual rationality → collective ruin (R12 anti-extraction context) | Pillar C Tier 2 rule 12 directly addresses |
| Drifting Goals | Standards lowered to match performance; slow erosion of ambition | text_013 §2.14 «improvement drive» anti-drift |
| Growth and Underinvestment | Demand outstrips capacity; investment lags; quality degrades | text_014 §2.22-24 (3-stage throughput bottleneck) |

### §2.3 Stock-flow primitives

Direct cross-link к text_014 §2.22-24 (3-stage throughput) and §2.24 «memory dependency»:

- **Stocks** (state variables) = system memory ↔ text_014 §2.24 explicitly
- **Flows** (rate variables) = transformation processes ↔ text_012 §2.4 «вход → процесс → выход»
- **Auxiliaries** (computed) = method-of-processing decisions ↔ text_012 §2.3
- **Constants / Parameters** = values + foundation ↔ text_012 §2.7

### §2.4 Policy resistance

Sterman's central operational insight: «interventions are defeated by the response of the system itself». Cross-link к text_013 §2.18 step 5 «появляются новые questions / hypotheses» — recursive lifecycle acknowledges policy resistance as the *trigger* for re-planning (steps 5-6).

Direct cross-link к Pillar C Tier 2 rule 7 («agents do NOT negotiate contradictions autonomously without human gate») — policy resistance must be surfaced to Ruslan-acked decision, not auto-negotiated.

- **Critique 1 (Cynefin Snowden):** Sterman methodology presupposes knowable causation; in complex domain dynamic modeling may project spurious confidence.
