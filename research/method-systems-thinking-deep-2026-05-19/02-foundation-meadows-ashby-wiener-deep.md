## §1 Donella Meadows — «Thinking in Systems» (2008, posthumous)

### §1.1 Verbatim canonical statements (5 anchors)

1. *«A system is an interconnected set of elements that is coherently organized in a way that achieves something. ... A system must consist of three kinds of things: elements, interconnections, and a function or purpose.»* [src: Meadows 2008, ch. 1 §«So, What Is a System?»]
2. *«Feedback loops can cause systems to behave in ways that defy our linear, cause-and-effect intuition.»* [src: ibid., ch. 2]
3. *«Leverage points are places within a complex system where a small shift in one thing can produce big changes in everything.»* [src: Meadows 1999 «Leverage Points: Places to Intervene in a System», pre-publication of book]
4. *«The least obvious part of the system, its function or purpose, is often the most crucial determinant of the system's behavior.»* [src: Meadows 2008, ch. 1]
5. *«Don't fall into the trap of bounding the system too narrowly. The proper boundary depends on the question you are asking.»* [src: ibid., ch. 5 «System Traps»]

### §1.2 Core concepts surface

- **Stocks and flows** — quantity (stock) + rate of change (flow). Stock = system memory; flow = process. Direct cross-link к text_014 §2.24 «memory dependency in output use».
- **Feedback loops** — balancing (B-loop, goal-seeking) vs reinforcing (R-loop, growth/decline). Direct cross-link к text_012 §2.13 «task → consume → understand → plan → train → goal».
- **12 leverage points** (Meadows 1999, ordered weak→strong): (12) constants/parameters, (11) buffer sizes, (10) stock-flow structure, (9) delays, (8) balancing feedback strength, (7) reinforcing feedback gain, (6) information flows, (5) rules of system, (4) self-organisation, (3) goals, (2) paradigm, (1) power to transcend paradigms.
- **System traps** — eight archetypes (policy resistance / tragedy of commons / drift to low performance / escalation / success-to-successful / shifting burden to intervenor / rule beating / seeking the wrong goal).
- **Iceberg model** (popularised by Meadows pedagogically) — events → patterns → structures → mental models.

## §2 W. Ross Ashby — «Introduction to Cybernetics» (1956)

### §2.1 Verbatim canonical statements (4 anchors)

1. *«Only variety can absorb variety.»* (Law of Requisite Variety, often quoted as «the variety of the controller must be at least as great as the variety of the system being controlled») [src: Ashby 1956, ch. 11 §11/7]
2. *«Cybernetics deals with all forms of behaviour in so far as they are regular, or determinate, or reproducible.»* [src: ibid., ch. 1 §1/3]
3. *«The Black Box ... is opaque to direct observation; only its inputs and outputs are accessible.»* [src: ibid., ch. 6]
4. *«[The homeostat is] a machine that, when disturbed, returns to a state of stability.»* [src: Ashby 1948 «Design for a Brain» + 1956 ch. 12 §12/14]

### §2.2 Core concepts surface

- **Variety** = count of distinguishable states a system can be in. Quantitative complexity metric.
- **Law of Requisite Variety** — regulator must match disturbances. Foundational principle for control theory + management cybernetics (Beer VSM directly built on this).
- **Homeostat** — Ashby's 1948 mechanical device demonstrating self-stabilisation through random reconfiguration. Adjacent to autopoiesis (Maturana-Varela, post-Ashby).
- **Black Box** — operational epistemology: characterise system by I/O without internal model.
- **Ultrastability** — system property where if instability detected, system re-organises until new stable configuration. Pre-cursor к bandwagon evolutionary algorithms.

## §3 Norbert Wiener — «Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine» (1948)

### §3.1 Verbatim canonical statements (4 anchors)

1. *«We have decided to call the entire field of control and communication theory, whether in the machine or in the animal, by the name Cybernetics, which we form from the Greek κυβερνήτης or steersman.»* [src: Wiener 1948, Introduction]
2. *«The amount of information in a system is a measure of its degree of organization, so the entropy of a system is a measure of its degree of disorganization.»* [src: ibid., ch. 1 «Newtonian and Bergsonian Time»]
3. *«Information is information, not matter or energy. No materialism which does not admit this can survive at the present day.»* [src: Wiener 1948, ch. 5]
4. *«Feedback is a method of controlling a system by reinserting into it the results of its past performance.»* [src: ibid., ch. 4 §«Feedback and Oscillation»]

### §3.2 Core concepts surface

- **Steersman metaphor** (κυβερνήτης) — control as continual correction toward goal. Direct cross-link к text_012 §2.8 «direction».
- **Information theory adjacency** — Wiener parallel к Shannon 1948 «Mathematical Theory of Communication». Direct cross-link к text_012 §2.2 «ALL = information».
- **Entropy** — disorder measure; information = negentropy (negative entropy). Direct cross-link к text_013 §2.14 «improvement drive» as anti-entropy.
- **Feedback** — past performance reinserted. Direct cross-link к text_013 §2.13 «active selection» + text_014 §2.25 reconnaissance.
- **Time-series + prediction** — Wiener filter (signal extraction from noise).
