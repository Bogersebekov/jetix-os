## §1 Ludwig von Bertalanffy — «General Systems Theory» (1968)

### §1.1 Canonical claims

1. *«General System Theory ... is a logico-mathematical field whose task is the formulation and derivation of those general principles which are applicable to "systems" in general.»* [src: Bertalanffy 1968, ch. 2]
2. *«[Open systems are] characterized by exchange of materials with surrounding environment, and may attain a time-independent state called steady state.»* [src: ibid., ch. 6]
3. *«Equifinality: in an open system the same final state may be reached from different initial conditions and in different ways.»* [src: ibid., ch. 3]
4. *«Isomorphism of laws in different fields ... arises as a consequence of the fact that the systems considered are systems.»* [src: ibid., ch. 2]

### §1.2 Core

- **Open vs closed systems** — open = matter/energy/info exchange with environment (biological, social); closed = isolated (textbook physics idealisation).
- **Equifinality** — multiple paths to same end-state in open systems; refutes Laplacian determinism.
- **Isomorphism** — same formal principles govern thermodynamic, biological, social systems → justification for general systems theory.
- **Hierarchical levels** — atoms → molecules → cells → organisms → populations → societies → planets — each level has own laws + isomorphic with adjacent levels.

### §1.3 Jetix mapping

- text_012 §2.1 «ALL = systems» = direct Bertalanffy claim
- text_013 §2.16-17 (gradient + parts) = hierarchical-levels application
- text_014 §2.19 (3-categories info: own / interaction / rules-of-others) = open-system exchange
- text_013 §2.18 step 8 «развивается в хорошем ключе» = equifinality (multiple paths)
- Cross-link к O-46 «Society-as-Code» (Tier A) — society = open-system level

### §1.4 Critique

- **Critique 1 (Hofstadter):** Bertalanffy's isomorphism claim conflates formal similarity with substantive identity; strange-loops show some isomorphisms are only at certain abstraction levels.
- **Critique 2 (R1 internal):** Bertalanffy's «equifinality» is descriptive, not prescriptive — does NOT authorise autonomous Jetix self-finalisation (IP-1 Tier 2 rule 9 preserved).

## §2 John Boyd — OODA loop (1976+ papers; biography Coram 2002)

### §2.1 Canonical statements

1. *«He who can handle the quickest rate of change survives.»* [src: Boyd's «Patterns of Conflict» briefing, 1976-1990 series]
2. *«Time is the dominant parameter. The pilot who goes through the OODA cycle in the shortest time prevails.»* [src: ibid.]
3. *«Without our [orientation], we are dead in the water. ... Orientation shapes the way we interact with the environment.»* [src: Boyd 1996 «Conceptual Spiral»]
4. *«The destruction and creation of mental models is the key intellectual process.»* (Destruction-Creation, Boyd 1976 essay) [src: Boyd 1976 «Destruction and Creation»]

## §3 Gregory Bateson — «Steps to an Ecology of Mind» (1972) + «Mind and Nature» (1979)

### §3.1 Canonical statements

1. *«The pattern which connects.»* (Bateson's central metaphysical claim) [src: Bateson 1979 «Mind and Nature», ch. 1]
2. *«Information is a difference which makes a difference.»* [src: Bateson 1972 «Steps to an Ecology of Mind», pp. 459]
3. *«The map is not the territory.»* (Bateson-popularised Korzybski) [src: Bateson 1972 + Korzybski 1933]
4. *«Double-bind: a context where every choice is a wrong choice.»* [src: Bateson et al. 1956 «Toward a Theory of Schizophrenia»]

### §3.2 Core

- **Pattern-which-connects** — meta-pattern uniting biological/mental/social systems. Direct cross-link к Bertalanffy isomorphism + к text_012 §2.1.
- **Information as difference** — definitional refinement over Shannon: not all entropy reduction = information; only *consequential* differences. Direct cross-link к text_013 §2.12 influence-discrimination.
- **Double-bind** — paradoxical communication contexts; cross-link к sense-of-measure failure modes.
- **Mind-as-systems** — mind is not in the head; mind is system-extending (cross-link к Phase 5.5 extended mind thesis Clark+Chalmers).

### §3.3 Jetix mapping

- text_013 §2.11 self-knowledge = mind-as-system thesis applied к Jetix
- text_013 §2.12 influence-discrimination = «difference which makes a difference»
- text_014 §2.30 exokortex = direct ancestor of Clark+Chalmers extended-mind (Phase 5.5)
- text_012 §2.7 values/foundation = «pattern which connects» Jetix substrate

### §3.4 Critique

- **Critique 1 (analytical philosophy):** Bateson's «pattern which connects» is metaphysically vague; resists formalisation.
- **Critique 2 (R1):** «Mind in extended system» = ancestor claim к Jetix=Exokortex (Phase 5.5); must respect IP-1 — exokortex = substrate, NOT autonomous mind.

## §4 Douglas Hofstadter — «Gödel, Escher, Bach» (1979) + «I Am a Strange Loop» (2007)

### §4.1 Canonical statements

1. *«A strange loop is a paradoxical level-crossing feedback loop.»* [src: Hofstadter 2007 «I Am a Strange Loop», ch. 8]
2. *«Self-reference is at the heart of the «I».»* [src: ibid., ch. 22]
3. *«Tangled hierarchies are systems where what is going on at one level can influence what is going on at supposedly higher (or lower) levels.»* [src: Hofstadter 1979 GEB, ch. 20]
4. *«Reductionism may be inadequate to explain the «soul».»* [src: ibid., dialogue with Achilles]

### §4.2 Core

- **Strange loop** — self-referential level-crossing structure (Bach's «Endlessly Rising Canon», Escher's «Drawing Hands», Gödel's incompleteness).
- **Tangled hierarchies** — levels of system can feed back to each other across «levels» that ostensibly should not.
- **Consciousness-as-strange-loop** — «I» = persistent self-referential pattern across substrate.

### §4.3 Jetix mapping

- text_013 §2.11 «system self-knowledge» = strange-loop instantiation in K-6 method
- text_013 §2.18 (recursive lifecycle) = strange loop (method describes itself + method describes plan to improve itself + applies plan + revises method = level-crossing self-reference)
- text_014 §2.29 (Jetix=Exokortex) + text_014 §2.27 (PROCESS не круг) = process-strange-loop (continuous self-reference; not repetition)
- Direct cross-link к Phase 5.5 §3 (exokortex-self-loop application)

### §4.4 Critique

- **Critique 1 (Dennett):** strange-loop framing is suggestive but lacks predictive power; Hofstadter himself acknowledges this in 2007 afterword.
- **Critique 2 (R1):** strange-loop applied к Jetix = pattern (recursive self-reference) — autonomous self-modifying instance = IP-1 violation (Tier 2 rule 9). Distinction preserved throughout.
