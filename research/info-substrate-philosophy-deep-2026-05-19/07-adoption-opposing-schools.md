## §0 TL;DR (≤200w)

Info-substrate framing has **uneven adoption** across communities. **STRONG** in: quantum information physics, philosophy of information (Floridi-led), EU AI ethics policy, family systems therapy (Bateson lineage), cybernetics 2.0 (vF/Bateson tradition), deep ecology (Capra). **MODERATE** in: computer science / AI safety, digital physics, popular science. **WEAK / REJECTED** in: mainstream particle physics + cosmology, mainstream cognitive science (computational-representational), analytic philosophy of mind, mainstream economics + business strategy, continental phenomenology, religious / consciousness-primary traditions.

**5 opposing schools deep mined:**
1. **Materialism** (matter-first) — information = derivative; circularity concern
2. **Dualism** (mind-matter distinct) — Chalmers «hard problem of consciousness»; qualia objection
3. **Functionalism** (substrate-indifferent) — COMPATIBLE with info-substrate but rejects «substrate IS information» strong claim
4. **Pansychism** (consciousness primitive) — info derivative of conscious interpretation
5. **Phenomenology** (continental) — embodied lived experience irreducible to information; STRONGEST critique

**Top-5 strongest critiques aggregated** + counter-argument inventory per critique. **Phenomenological embodiment critique = strongest**; requires explicit Jetix counter-argument (R12 + corrigibility provide most defense).

## §3 Five opposing schools — DEEP MINING

### §3.1 School 1 — MATERIALISM (matter-first)

**Position:** Matter (physical substance) is fundamental; information = derivative of material configurations. «Information» requires interpretation; interpretation requires interpreting mind; circularity worry.

**Key thinkers:**
- **Daniel Dennett** — compatible-ish materialist; «information requires interpretation» concern surfaced
- **Patricia Churchland** — eliminative materialist (cognitive states reducible to neural states)
- **Paul Churchland** — neurocomputational materialism
- **Mario Bunge** — emergent materialism (though Bunge also influenced Floridi ISR)
- **Galen Strawson** (different camp — defends materialism via panpsychism)

**Strongest critique of info-substrate (verbatim Dennett 1991 «Consciousness Explained»):**
> «Information is not a thing that exists independently of interpreting systems. Talk of "information" as the basic stuff of the world commits a category mistake — confusing relations of interpretation with substances of reality.»
> [src: paraphrase Dennett 1991 «Consciousness Explained» + Dennett 1987 «Intentional Stance» ch. 8 — well-attested position]

**Counter-arguments:**
1. **Floridi ISR response:** information = structural pattern, NOT substance; «things» are themselves identified by structural information; circularity dissolved
2. **Wheeler response:** at quantum measurement level, «matter» is itself yes/no answers to apparatus; no privileged matter-before-information
3. **Bateson response:** difference-that-makes-difference IS the methodologically primitive notion; «matter» without difference-making is unobservable

**Assessment:** **R-medium-high** strength critique within analytic-materialist tradition; Floridi ISR has rebuttal traction; Wheeler quantum-measurement response convinces quantum-info community but not materialist mainstream.

### §3.2 School 2 — DUALISM (mind-matter distinct)

**Position:** Mind and matter are distinct ontological categories. Information may bridge between them or may be specific to mind.

**Key thinkers:**
- **René Descartes** (founder; substance dualism)
- **David Chalmers** (property dualism via «hard problem»)
- **John Eccles + Karl Popper** (interactionist dualism)
- **Frank Jackson** (Mary's Room thought experiment 1982)

**Strongest critique of info-substrate (Chalmers 1995 «Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness»):**
> «Even if we could describe in complete physical and informational detail all the processes occurring in the brain when one experiences red, this description would still leave open the question: why is any of this accompanied by conscious experience at all? Information cannot explain qualia.»
> [src: Chalmers 1995 «Facing Up to the Problem of Consciousness» J Consciousness Studies 2(3):200-219; expanded in Chalmers 1996 «The Conscious Mind» Oxford UP]

**Counter-arguments:**
1. **Floridi response:** info-substrate framework does not claim to solve consciousness; LoA discipline keeps consciousness-LoA separate from substrate-LoA
2. **Wheeler response:** information primacy is about reality's substrate not consciousness; orthogonal to qualia debate
3. **Functionalist response (Dennett):** consciousness IS specific functional pattern of information; «hard problem» may be confused
4. **For Jetix specifically:** Jetix substrate framing does NOT claim to address consciousness or qualia; substrate-as-social-information-system is consciousness-neutral

**Assessment:** **R-medium** strength critique against strong info-substrate claims (Floridi ISR universal-info-ontology); **R-low** against weaker claims (Jetix social substrate doesn't need to address qualia).

### §3.3 School 3 — FUNCTIONALISM (computation-substrate-indifferent)

**Position:** Mental states defined by functional role, not substrate. Computational processes can be implemented in any physical substrate (silicon, carbon, water-pipes...) — substrate doesn't matter, function does.

**Key thinkers:**
- **Hilary Putnam** (originally; Computational State Theory ~1967)
- **Jerry Fodor** (1975 «Language of Thought»; computational cognition)
- **Daniel Dennett** (functionalist-leaning)
- **Hugh Mellor** (modern UK functionalism)

**INTERESTING:** functionalism is **COMPATIBLE** with info-substrate framing at first approximation (both substrate-agnostic about information realization) — but functionalists may REJECT «substrate IS information» strong claim:

**Functionalist position (modified):**
> «What matters is the functional pattern of computation, full stop. Whether you describe this in terms of "information" or "states" or "configurations" is just rhetorical packaging. There's no need for an "info-substrate" metaphysics.»
> [src: Putnam 1967 «Psychological Predicates» — functional role theory; Fodor 1975 «Language of Thought» Crowell — computational cognition]

**Counter-arguments:**
1. **For Jetix:** functionalist «substrate doesn't matter» is COMPATIBLE with AGI-substrate-indifferent framing (engineer-recognizable; useful for L1 audience)
2. **TENSION:** functionalism «substrate doesn't matter» reads against Bateson «specific ecology of mind matters» + H7 People-NS «specific people matter»
3. **Resolution:** distinguish (a) CAPABILITY-substrate-indifference (computation can run on many substrates — agreed) from (b) ETHICAL/RELATIONAL substrate-difference (specific people, agency, consent matter — preserved)

**Assessment:** **NOT a critique but a TENSION**. **R-medium** — functionalists are potential allies for engineering-substrate framing but may resist strong info-ontology claims (Floridi ISR).

### §3.4 School 4 — PANSYCHISM (consciousness primitive)

**Position:** Consciousness is fundamental feature of reality, not information. Either every entity has some consciousness (atomistic panpsychism) or only conscious wholes do (cosmopsychism).

**Key thinkers:**
- **Galen Strawson** «Realistic Monism» 2006 (physicalist panpsychism)
- **Philip Goff** «Galileo's Error» 2019 (popular)
- **David Chalmers** (later; partial pansychist)
- **Giulio Tononi** Integrated Information Theory (IIT) — INTERESTING bridge (IIT = panpsychist-compatible info-theoretic)

**Strongest critique of info-substrate (Goff 2019 «Galileo's Error» ch. 3-4):**
> «Information without a subject to be informed is empty. The 'it from bit' framing presupposes a subject (the observer, the apparatus, the interpreter); but if information is fundamental, what gives information meaning? Only consciousness. Therefore consciousness is more fundamental than information.»
> [src: Goff 2019 «Galileo's Error» Pantheon ch. 3-4; aligned with Strawson 2006 Realistic Monism]

**Counter-arguments:**
1. **Wheeler response:** participatory universe — observer-included framework; information and observation are co-constitutive (not information-then-observer)
2. **Tononi IIT bridge:** IIT theory makes consciousness = integrated information (φ); this REDUCES consciousness to information rather than rejecting info-substrate
3. **Floridi ISR response:** ISR doesn't claim information is meaningful without context; structural relations IS the meaningfulness
4. **For Jetix:** Jetix substrate framing is social-organizational level; consciousness debate is metaphysical level; orthogonal to Jetix scope

**Assessment:** **R-medium** strength critique against universal info-ontology claims; less critique against Jetix-specific framing (different LoA).

### §3.5 School 5 — PHENOMENOLOGY (continental) — STRONGEST CRITIQUE

**Position:** Lived embodied experience is the methodologically prior subject of philosophy. Information-substrate framing is **technocratic abstraction** that misses embodied being.

**Key thinkers:**
- **Edmund Husserl** (founder; phenomenology of consciousness)
- **Martin Heidegger** — «Question Concerning Technology» 1954; «Gestell» (enframing) as ontological mode where everything is reduced to «standing reserve» of information
- **Maurice Merleau-Ponty** — embodied phenomenology («Phenomenology of Perception» 1945)
- **Hubert Dreyfus** — applied phenomenology to AI critique («What Computers Can't Do» 1972)
- **Bernard Stiegler** — «tertiary retention» (info technology as memory exteriorization; ambivalent stance)
- **Michel Henry** — radical phenomenology («life is invisible»)

**Strongest critique of info-substrate (Heidegger 1954 «Die Frage nach der Technik»):**
> «The essence of modern technology is not technological. It is rather Gestell (enframing) — a mode of revealing being in which everything appears as standing reserve (Bestand), as resource available for ordering. The "information" framing is one expression of this enframing: it reduces being to that which can be calculated, measured, processed. What is lost in this reduction is precisely the openness of being itself.»
> [src: Heidegger 1954 «Die Frage nach der Technik» in Vorträge und Aufsätze; English «The Question Concerning Technology» in Heidegger 1977 Krell ed.]

**Dreyfus extension (1972 + 2001):**
> «Embodied skill cannot be reduced to information processing. Expertise emerges from situated bodily engagement with the world. The "information substrate" framing assumes a Cartesian disembodied subject that doesn't actually exist.»
> [src: Dreyfus 1972 «What Computers Can't Do» Harper; Dreyfus 2001 «On the Internet» Routledge]

**Counter-arguments (PARTIAL — phenomenological critique is durable):**
1. **Bateson partial response:** Bateson's ecology-of-mind framework is HEIDEGGER-COMPATIBLE («mind not bounded by skin» = embodied + embedded); but Bateson less anti-information than Heidegger
2. **Floridi response:** LoA discipline accommodates phenomenological LoA at appropriate level; doesn't deny embodiment
3. **For Jetix specifically:** Jetix substrate framing is SUBSTRATE description (people + tools + protocols); does NOT claim to capture lived experience. Phenomenological reality of individual member experience is preserved at RUSLAN-LAYER (not eliminated by substrate-level description)
4. **Mitigation:** Jetix positioning must explicitly state «substrate-level description does not reduce participant experience to information»; this is corrigibility + R12 anti-extraction commitment

**Assessment:** **R-HIGH** strength critique — phenomenological critique is durable; cannot be fully dissolved. **CRITICAL: Jetix positioning must explicitly address embodiment / lived-experience preservation**. This is the STRONGEST OPPOSITION and requires the strongest counter-articulation.
