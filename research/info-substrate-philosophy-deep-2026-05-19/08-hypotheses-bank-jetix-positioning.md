## §7 Three Jetix positioning options — TRADEOFFS SURFACED PARALLEL

### §7.1 Option A — Commit lineage

**Description:** Jetix explicitly cites Wheeler / Wolfram / Floridi / Bateson info-substrate lineage в pitch decks + Vision narrative + investor materials. «Jetix substrate philosophy stands in 70+ year tradition from Shannon 1948 through Wheeler/Bateson/Wolfram/Floridi.»

**Pros:**
- Academic depth signaling
- L3 investor differentiation (institutional thesis fit)
- EU AI Act regulatory tailwind visibility (HLEG-AI lineage)
- Counter-argument inventory ready (Phase 6)
- High-prestige citation chain (quantum info physics + PoI + cybernetics 2.0)

**Cons:**
- Alienates L1 engineers (philosophical jargon)
- Commits к specific school with opponents (phenomenology / materialism / mainstream physics)
- «Tall poppy» risk — academic claims invite academic scrutiny
- Specific positioning bound (harder to pivot if opposition gains)

### §7.2 Option B — Discreet

**Description:** Substrate framing used internally в Foundation + design docs but NOT cited externally in pitches / Vision narrative / investor materials. «Jetix is an AI consulting business with effective methodology.»

**Pros:**
- Avoids opposing-school flak entirely
- Broader audience accessibility
- L1 engineer-friendly (no philosophy required)
- Easier to pivot positioning without abandoning citations
- Lower «pretentious» risk

**Cons:**
- Misses L3 academic-depth differentiator
- Regulatory tailwind less visible (EU AI Act alignment claim weaker without HLEG citation)
- Loses high-prestige citation chain leverage
- Foundation methodology origin obscured (harder to defend «why this approach»)

### §7.3 Option C — Adapt (multi-frame)

**Description:** Info-substrate cited as ONE of several frames (alongside biological / ecological / network-state / pragmatist) depending on audience. «Jetix operates at the intersection of information theory, ecology, and network governance.»

**Pros:**
- Resilient against single-school critique (no single attack-surface)
- Audience-flexibility (L1 / L2 / L3 / academic / policy / philosopher each get suitable frame)
- Game-theoretically robust (multiple defensible positions)
- Bateson-aligned methodologically («pattern of patterns» framing)

**Cons:**
- Positioning dilution risk («who is Jetix really?»)
- Higher cognitive load for new audience contact (must select frame)
- Requires discipline to maintain coherence across frames
- Harder to communicate в short pitches (need elevator pitch per frame)
