## §0 TL;DR (≤200 слов)

70-летняя линия мыслителей о intelligence-as-tool: **Bush (1945) — Memex** → **Engelbart (1962) — H-LAM/T augmentation** → **Nelson (1960) — Xanadu hypertext** → **Kay (1968+) — Dynabook / personal dynamic media** → **Clark+Chalmers (1998) — Extended Mind** → **Victor (2012+) — Dynamicland** → **Karpathy (2025) — LLM Wiki**. Параллельная ветка: **Cybernetics** — Wiener / Ashby (Law of Requisite Variety) / **Beer (VSM + Project Cybersyn 1971)** → **Friston (FEP / Active Inference, 1990s+)**.

**Общий тезис:** intelligence ≠ что-то «внутри головы», а **coupled system** — человек + artifacts + language + methodology (Engelbart H-LAM/T = literal antecedent FPF: «trained human + artifacts + language + methodology»). Extended Mind (Clark/Chalmers) формализует это эпистемологически.

**Адаптивный успех:** Кай (Smalltalk → GUI), Engelbart (mouse, hypertext, video conferencing), Karpathy (LLM Wiki adopted в нашем wiki/ substrate). **Адаптивная неудача:** Xanadu — 30 лет over-design + scope creep; Cybersyn — оборван 1973 coup, не technical failure.

**Adjacency к Jetix:** FPF substrate напрямую наследует Engelbart H-LAM/T (наша «trained human + artifacts + language + methodology»). Karpathy LLM Wiki pattern уже в нашем `wiki/` substrate. VSM (Beer) уже в Foundation Part 4. **Extended Mind = эпистемологическая legitimization** того, что FPF делает.

## §1 Key works / movements / people

### §1.1 Foundational lineage (intelligence amplification)

**Vannevar Bush — "As We May Think" (1945)** — Atlantic Monthly, July 1945. Memex = "library of collective knowledge stored in a piece of furniture" using microfilm + electromechanical controls. Predicted hypertext, PCs, Internet, WWW, Wikipedia. **Engelbart read Bush shortly after publication, leading directly to NLS.** [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/As_We_May_Think; https://www.w3.org/History/1945/vbush/vbush.shtml, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Douglas Engelbart — "Augmenting Human Intellect: A Conceptual Framework" (1962)** — AFOSR-3233 report. Defines **H-LAM/T system: trained human being together with his artifacts, language, and methodology**. Mother of All Demos (1968): mouse, hyperlink, real-time text editing, screen sharing, video conferencing — all in one 90-minute presentation. Doug Engelbart Institute (https://dougengelbart.org/) preserves the legacy. [src: https://dougengelbart.org/content/view/138/; https://archive.org/details/1962-engelbart-AHI-framework, retrieved 2026-05-17]

> Verbatim (Engelbart 1962): "By 'augmenting human intellect' we mean increasing the capability of a man to approach a complex problem situation, to gain comprehension to suit his particular needs, and to derive solutions to problems."

**Ted Nelson — Project Xanadu (1960+)** — coined "hypertext" 1965. Vision: global hypertext + bidirectional links + micropayments + automatic broken-link prevention + universal copyright resolution. **Failure pattern:** over-scoped; first attempt 1960, "working deliverable" OpenXanadu only 2014. [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_Xanadu; https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/wr9dH2GjztvCz6pYX/the-lessons-of-xanadu, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Alan Kay — Dynabook (1968+), Smalltalk (1972+)** — Xerox PARC. Vision: "personal dynamic medium" — computer as **metamedium** that incorporates all other media. Influenced by Bruner + Piaget + Papert. Smalltalk pioneered OO + GUI. Turing Award 2003. Active through 2024+; Viewpoints Research Institute and FONC project. [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynabook; https://amturing.acm.org/award_winners/kay_3972189.cfm, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Bret Victor — Dynamicland (2017+) / "Inventing on Principle" (2012)** — ex-Apple iPad designer. Dynamicland mission: "incubate a humane dynamic medium, whose full power is accessible to all people" via spatial computing (Realtalk system, paper-based interaction in physical space). Berkeley, CA. Active 2024; MIT Media Lab events. Principle: "creators, authors, readers and users all need immediate feedback". [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bret_Victor; https://dynamicland.org, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Andrej Karpathy — LLM Wiki (April 2026)** — Github Gist `442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f`. Pattern: persistent markdown wiki maintained by LLM, growing compounded knowledge across sessions. Karpathy himself runs >100 articles / 400,000 words. **Direct адаптация в Jetix wiki/ substrate** (см. CLAUDE.md "Wiki Architecture v2"). Eureka Labs (founded July 2024) — broader AI education vision. [src: https://gist.github.com/karpathy/442a6bf555914893e9891c11519de94f; https://github.com/skyllwt/OmegaWiki, retrieved 2026-05-17]

### §1.2 Extended Mind / situated cognition

**Andy Clark + David Chalmers — "The Extended Mind" (1998)** — Analysis 58(1): 7-19. Thesis: mind extends into physical environment when external objects are coupled into cognitive process. "Otto and Inga" thought experiment: Otto with Alzheimer's notebook is functionally equivalent to Inga's biological memory. **Active externalism.** "The mind and the environment act as a coupled system that can be seen as a complete cognitive system of its own." [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extended_mind_thesis; https://www.alice.id.tue.nl/references/clark-chalmers-1998.pdf, retrieved 2026-05-17]

> Verbatim (Clark/Chalmers 1998 abstract): "cognitive processes ain't all in the head."

**Andy Clark — "Supersizing the Mind" (2008)** + "The Experience Machine" (2023). Cognitive science philosopher continuing the thread. Current at University of Sussex.

**Edwin Hutchins — "Cognition in the Wild" (1995)** — distributed cognition + cognitive ethnography. Studied ship navigation как distributed cognitive system. Pre-Extended Mind formal thesis.

### §1.3 Cybernetics tradition

**Norbert Wiener — "Cybernetics" (1948)** — founded cybernetics as field. Feedback + control + communication in animals + machines. MIT.

**W. Ross Ashby — "An Introduction to Cybernetics" (1956)**. **Law of Requisite Variety**: "only variety can absorb variety" — regulator must be ≥ as complex as regulated system. Applies across engineering / biology / organizations / AI. [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Variety_(cybernetics), retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Stafford Beer — Viable System Model + Project Cybersyn** — VSM = 5 interconnected subsystems for self-regulation. **Project Cybersyn (Chile, 1971-1973)** with Fernando Flores under Allende: telex-based real-time economic management + Operations Room + Cybernet software (predates Internet conceptually). Killed by 1973 coup. Beer continued in Canada, Mexico, Uruguay (URUCIB 1986-88), Colombia (1990s-2000s). [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stafford_Beer; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_Cybersyn, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Heinz von Foerster — second-order cybernetics**. "Observer is part of the system." Influence on autopoiesis (Maturana/Varela).

**Gordon Pask — Conversation Theory** — interactive cognitive system; pre-internet groupware experiments.

### §1.4 Active Inference / Free Energy Principle

**Karl Friston — Free Energy Principle (FEP) + Active Inference (2006+)** — Professor of Imaging Neuroscience, UCL. FEP: biological systems minimize free energy (surprise / prediction error). Active Inference = brain as Bayesian inference engine that acts to reduce prediction error. 2024: Cybernetics Society talk (April 2024) on sentience; National Science Review interview (May 2024); Dissenter podcast #1000. [src: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_energy_principle; https://academic.oup.com/nsr/article/11/5/nwae025/7571549, retrieved 2026-05-17]

**Beren Millidge** — Apple ML / Verses; Active Inference popularizer; blog posts on FEP for engineers.

**Conor Heaney** — UK philosopher of mind, FEP/AI alignment intersection.

**Verses AI** — startup commercializing Active Inference (Bayesian agents for IoT/control).

## §4 Failure cases / lessons learned

**Xanadu — 30 years of over-design.** Lesson: every additional feature added to scope before working implementation extends timeline non-linearly. **Direct lesson for FPF:** ship usable subset (FPF B.3 F-G-R + A.2.8 Commitment + A.2.9 SpeechAct) rather than full spec. WWW's «winning» pattern (Tim Berners-Lee 1989) was **explicit narrower scope** vs Xanadu.

**Cybersyn — political fragility.** Lesson: cybernetic governance dependent on political continuity. **Direct lesson for Jetix:** distributed substrate must survive single-actor failures (Ruslan/Balaji-equivalent loss). Per Pillar C R12 + Corrigibility: foundation must not be locked to one person.

**Engelbart's underuse — institutional resistance.** NLS (1968) was technically ahead but Xerox PARC ate the credit (mouse/GUI commercialized by Apple, not by Engelbart). Lesson: **institutional substrate matters at least as much as invention quality**. Engelbart founded Doug Engelbart Institute but it remained niche.

**Dynamicland — slow adoption.** Bret Victor's Dynamicland (Berkeley, 2017+) has captivated visual-thinking-engineers community but has not produced mass-adopted product. Lesson: «laboratory beats product» often = mission staying laboratory.

**Karpathy LLM Wiki rapid spread (April 2026 → now).** Lesson: **simple pattern + LLM substrate + GitHub Gist** = viral adoption mechanism. Karpathy's authority + minimal viable spec + zero infrastructure = month-long spread. Possible distribution model for FPF.

**Active Inference critique:** FEP universality (Wikipedia + critique papers) — some argue it's «universal but unfalsifiable» — explains too much. Lesson for FPF: too-broad theoretical scope risks unfalsifiability (refutation conditions matter).

### §8.1 Что это

Это **обзор кто думал про intelligence как extended tool, не «штука внутри головы»** — 70-летняя линия мыслителей. Начинается **1945** с Vannevar Bush «As We May Think» (Atlantic Monthly, июль 1945, где он описал **Memex** — «гипертекстовая мебель» из microfilm + electromechanical controls, предсказал Internet за 50 лет).

**Main lineage:**
1. **1945** — **Vannevar Bush** «Memex» = «furniture-sized library of collective knowledge»
