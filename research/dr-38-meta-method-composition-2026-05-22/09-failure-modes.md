## §2 Category A — Per-component degradation (5 modes)

### §2.1 A1 — Perfectionism trap (C1 без C13)

**Pattern**: Radical elaboration (C1) without speed-bound (C13) → infinite-deferral, never-shipping.

**Examples**:
- The PhD student who refuses to defend the thesis because «one more chapter needs rework»
- The startup that won't launch because «product not ready»
- The book draft в 17th revision

**Cross-reference**: TOGAF zombie pattern (Phase 7 §2.2) — phase-perfectionism replaces value.

**Mitigation**: C1 + C13 (radical-but-fast) + Method V2 cycle deadline-discipline + C7 leverage (perfectionism on low-leverage tasks = especially-wasteful).

**Indicator**: Cycles starting but not closing; SG predicates never met; «working on it» as default-status.

### §2.2 A2 — Rabbit-hole (C2 без portfolio-balance)

**Pattern**: Depth (C2) without breadth/Ashby variety-check → exhaustive engagement в one area, neglected portfolio variety.

**Examples**:
- Researcher 5 years on one paper while field passes them
- Investor 95% of portfolio в one sector
- Entrepreneur obsessing one product while market shifts

**Cross-reference**: Munger latticework warning (Phase 2 §7) — single-model thinking + Ashby variety law (Phase 3 §4).

**Mitigation**: C7 leverage (portfolio-level) + C9 mechanism literacy (system-wide visibility) + cycle cadence (forces portfolio re-evaluation).

**Indicator**: Time-in-one-area exceeds time-in-other-areas by >3x without explicit decision; cycle outputs all from same area.

### §2.3 A3 — Quality theatre / perfectionism stall (C3 misapplied)

**Pattern**: C3 «качество» becomes ceremony-of-quality without actual quality; or, alternatively, becomes blocker for shipping anything.

**Examples**:
- Software CI checks that take 2 hours but don't actually catch bugs
- Code review process that gates everything but rubber-stamps
- «Quality» rituals that have become decoupled from outcome

**Cross-reference**: Institutional failure modes (Phase 7 §10.2) — «ceremony theatre».

**Mitigation**: C6 honesty (is the quality-process actually catching defects?) + measurement (Phase 7 success condition 5) + objective fitness criteria.

**Indicator**: Defect-rate not improved by quality-process; cycle time not justified by quality-gain.

### §2.4 A4 — Burnout from sustained C11 без recovery

**Pattern**: 1000% голодный without recovery discipline → physiological / cognitive / motivational collapse.

**Examples**:
- Marathon training without rest week
- Startup founder 18 months without vacation, then crashes
- Surgeon ER rotation without sleep, performance degrades and patients suffer

**Cross-reference**: Sports science + Csikszentmihalyi flow research — sustained high-intensity requires recovery cycles.

**Mitigation**: Recovery-discipline as explicit complementary practice (NOT in Ruslan's 16 components explicitly; Phase 10 will surface as gap).

**Indicator**: Sleep degradation; mood deterioration; decision-quality decline; physical symptoms; «I just need to push through» framing.

### §2.5 A5 — Speed kills quality (C13 без C3)

**Pattern**: Accelerated execution without quality-discipline → fast crap; throughput at expense of fitness.

**Examples**:
- Daily-output discipline without quality-check
- Fast features that introduce bugs
- Quick decisions that miss material considerations

**Cross-reference**: Toyota Production System «stop the line» discipline; quality embedded in speed.

**Mitigation**: C13 + C3 paired (the elusive «fast and good») + cycle retros that catch quality regression.

**Indicator**: Defects in completed work; rework rate increasing; «we'll fix it later» as default.

## §3 Category B — Cross-component conflict (3 modes)

### §3.1 B1 — Focus conflict between C4 / C7 / C8

**Pattern**: C4 (focus on important) + C7 (leverage) + C8 (system development) can conflict — what's «important right now» may differ from «highest leverage» may differ from «system-development needed».

**Example**: Today the urgent client deliverable (C4 important right now) takes precedence over the system-process improvement (C8 system development) that would compound to 10x productivity. Both are correct in their own frame.

**Cross-reference**: Cynefin domain switching (Phase 3 §6) — different domains imply different priorities.

**Mitigation**: Cycle pattern (Method V2 §M) — alternating focus between operational (C4) and developmental (C8); explicit time-allocation per priority-type.

**Indicator**: «Always firefighting» symptom; system never gets developed; OR «always developing system», nothing actually delivered.

### §3.2 B2 — Honesty-depth conflict (C6 × C9)

**Pattern**: C6 honesty might surface uncomfortable truth (e.g., «we don't actually understand this system»), while C9 mechanism literacy work might be incomplete. Honesty about incomplete understanding can become paralysis vs. action.

**Example**: Investor honest about not-fully-understanding a technology → either pass on opportunity (high cost if wrong) or invest anyway (violating C6 honesty about uncertainty).

**Cross-reference**: Polanyi tacit dimension (Phase 2 §3) — honest about tacit incomprehension; Aristotle phronesis (Phase 2 §8) — situated judgement under uncertainty.

**Mitigation**: Calibrated confidence framing — F-G-R grading allows «honest acknowledgement that R is low but action proceeds». Don't conflate «honesty» with «certainty».

**Indicator**: Either «paralysed by honest uncertainty» pattern OR «forcing decisions despite honest uncertainty» pattern.

### §3.3 B3 — State-procedural decoupling (C11-C14 vs C1-C10)

**Pattern**: State-cluster (C11-C14) can drift from procedural-cluster (C1-C10). E.g., one can be 1000% голодный (C11) but not actually doing C1-C5 procedural discipline. Or vice versa — going through procedural motions without state-engagement.

**Example**: Doctor exhausted (low C11) but still executing standard protocol (procedural intact) — risk of routine-without-engagement. OR Entrepreneur on fire (high C11) but skipping discipline — passion-without-craft.

**Cross-reference**: Csikszentmihalyi flow conditions (Phase 2 §4.3) — state + skill must match.

**Mitigation**: Cycle retro explicit state-check; Method V2 §6 «reverse engineering» check on state-procedural alignment.

**Indicator**: «I worked hard but didn't accomplish much» (high state, low procedure) OR «I followed all the steps but feel hollow» (low state, high procedure).

## §4 Category C — Compositional pathologies (3 modes)

### §4.1 C1-pathology — Component-overlap (Unix Rule 1 violation)

**Pattern**: Some of the 16 components overlap functionally (per Phase 4 §2.3 Unix discipline: each component should do one thing well). Overlap = redundancy + ambiguity about which component to invoke.

**Audit**: Phase 8 examination shows actual overlaps:
- C1 + C2 + C3 (all «depth & quality cluster») — significant overlap
- C6 + C12 (honesty foundational + honesty state) — explicit reinforcement, not pure overlap
- C8 + C14 (development + developed-state) — overlap (process vs result)
- C15 + C16 (capture + honesty-foundation) — paired, not overlapping

**Mitigation**: The 8-cluster-lead reduction (C1, C4, C6, C7, C8, C9, C11, C15) is the de-overlapped form. Use 16-form for richness, 8-form for clarity.

**Indicator**: Different practitioners disagree on which component an example illustrates; pedagogical confusion.

### §4.2 C2-pathology — Hidden coupling (distributed monolith analog)

**Pattern**: Components apparently-independent but actually coupled — changing one forces changing others; «independent» discipline becomes deceptive.

**Example**: C5 «отсечение неважного» appears independent of C9 «изучение системы» — but actually, deciding what's «non-essential» requires C9 mechanism literacy. They're coupled.

**Cross-reference**: DDD anti-pattern «Big Ball of Mud» (Phase 4 §4) + Microservices anti-pattern «distributed monolith» (Phase 4 §6).

**Mitigation**: Explicit dependency-mapping (Phase 8 «integration hooks» surfaced some); cycle retro to detect coupling.

**Indicator**: «I tried to apply C5 alone but didn't work» — coupling forces multi-component invocation.

### §4.3 C3-pathology — Frankenstein-necrotic (composition without integration)

**Pattern**: Per Phase 5 §3.3, three composition outcomes: living / dead-functional / necrotic-incoherent. If integration discipline fails (despite 5/5 satisfaction baseline), composition can degrade toward necrotic.

**Examples**:
- Methods that fight each other (e.g., C5 cut-non-essential and C8 system-development might conflict if «non-essential» elimination cuts the development substrate)
- Components applied as ritual without coherent purpose
- Composition treated as checklist not as living-method

**Cross-reference**: Phase 5 §3.3 outcomes taxonomy; Shelley's monster as anti-pattern.

**Mitigation**: C6 honesty + cycle retro + Latour love-your-monsters discipline — nurture the composition, don't abandon.

**Indicator**: Composition feels mechanical / dead; cycle outputs don't compound; team / cohort can't activate it.

## §5 Category D — Institutional / scaling (2 modes)

### §5.1 D1 — Universalisation risk (H-batch-10-06 «any task» reading)

**Pattern**: H-batch-10-06 «sufficient method-arsenal + meta-level → solve any task» universalised → over-claim that the meta-method works for any task.

**Falsifiability test**: Per Phase 0 §3 acceptance + Phase 2 v2 refinement. The «any task» reading IS refutable. Tasks where it fails:

| Task type | Why meta-method may fail |
|---|---|
| Tasks requiring tacit / non-articulated mastery (Polanyi) | Cannot acquire by deliberate composition; only via long apprenticeship |
| Tasks in unfamiliar paradigm (Kuhn) | Method-arsenal incommensurable; needs paradigm shift first |
| Tasks beyond variety bound (Ashby) | Regulator variety insufficient regardless of method-count |
| Tasks at scale exceeding individual capacity | Organisational composition required, not personal |
| Tasks где state cannot be sustained (e.g., extreme fatigue / illness) | State-cluster (C11-C14) compromised |
| Tasks где integration time exceeds task deadline | Composition assembly slower than direct execution |

**Cross-reference**: All Phase 2-7 H-06 refinement chain (v2 through v6).

**Mitigation**: Defensible-domain framing — «substrate-compatible composition envelope» (per Phase 4 v4) + epistemic humility («не открытие, мой личный метод» framing preserved).

**Indicator**: Practitioner claims «any task» without bounds-acknowledgement = red flag.

### §5.2 D2 — Cohort transfer failure (Conway's Law violation)

**Pattern**: Per Conway's Law (Phase 4 §10), composition reflects substrate. If cohort practitioners lack the substrate (LLM stack / Foundation parts / cycle discipline / R12 culture), meta-method will fail-to-transfer.

**Example**: A cohort member trying to adopt the 16-component meta-method without the Foundation v1.0 substrate — will produce ceremony-without-substance (Phase 7 §10.2 failure mode 1).

**Mitigation**: Phase 10 recommendations may surface «substrate adoption» as precondition for meta-method transfer; not «teach the components in isolation».

**Indicator**: Cohort members adopt vocabulary («meta-method», «1000% голодный») without behavior change; ceremony adopted без discipline.

## §6 Category E — Epistemic (2 modes)

### §6.1 E1 — Paradigm rigidity (Kuhn anomaly accumulation)

**Pattern**: Per Phase 6 §7, the meta-method operates within a paradigm. If paradigm becomes outdated (AI commoditisation per DR-34 / regulatory shift / market structure change), the meta-method's effectiveness degrades. Without paradigm-update discipline, accumulating anomalies undermine system.

**Example**: A meta-method tuned for 2024 LLM-substrate may need overhaul по 2027 substrate (different cost / capability / deployment patterns). C8 «развитие системы» must include paradigm-level update, not just component-level refinement.

**Cross-reference**: Kuhn paradigm shifts (Phase 6 §7) + Karpathy LLM-stack evolution (Phase 4 §9).

**Mitigation**: Explicit paradigm-monitoring discipline; Foundation Part 4 Strategic Direction (Pillar A) + DR-34 AI commoditisation thesis ongoing.

**Indicator**: Anomalies accumulating without integration; cycle retros surfacing «this doesn't fit the model» repeatedly; substrate-shift signals.

### §6.2 E2 — Reflexive blindspot (Schedrovitsky meta-position not maintained)

**Pattern**: Per Phase 6 §2, ММК reflexive-position discipline is foundational. If methodologist forgets to step outside their own method to examine it, blind-spots accumulate. The meta-method ABOUT methods can itself lose meta-perspective.

**Example**: Ruslan applies the 16-component meta-method continuously but never steps outside it to ask «is this composition still correct?» → drift, ossification, paradigm-lock-in.

**Cross-reference**: Schedrovitsky reflexive position (Phase 6 §2) + Hofstadter strange-loop (Phase 2 §9) — strange-loops require continuous self-reflection.

**Mitigation**: Annual / quarterly meta-cycle that explicitly re-examines the meta-method itself; Method V2 §6 «reverse engineering» applied к the meta-method recursively.

**Indicator**: Cycle retros never surface meta-method critique; «the method itself» becomes unquestionable.

## §8 Recovery discipline — the missing 17th component candidate

Phase 9 surfaces the most-significant gap in Ruslan's 16-component articulation: **recovery / restoration discipline**.

### §8.1 The case

Across failure modes:
- A4 Burnout — sustained C11 without recovery → physiological collapse
- B3 State-procedure decoupling — without recovery, state degrades
- A3 Quality theatre — without recovery, observation degrades
- A5 Speed crap — without recovery, judgement degrades

Sports science / sleep research / meditation traditions / vacation-discipline studies all converge: **sustained high-performance requires structured recovery.** This is not in audio_719's 16 components explicitly.

### §8.2 Possible C17 candidate

**C17 «восстановление»** (recovery) — sustained-deliberate restoration discipline; treating recovery as performance-enhancing investment, not as «time off».

| Aspect | Description |
|---|---|
| Voice | Not in audio_719; emergent from Phase 9 analysis |
| Operational definition | Structured sleep + nutrition + meditation / contemplation + relationships + leisure as performance-enabling investment |
| Examples | Athlete's nap discipline; researcher's «walking thinking» time; Bezos's «cardboard box hours»; Buffett's reading time |
| Cross-tradition | Stoic ataraxia + Buddhist meditation + sports science + Csikszentmihalyi (flow requires recovery) |

### §8.3 R1 surface: should C17 be added?

DR-38 surfaces but does NOT prescribe. Recovery as 17th component is a candidate. Alternatives:
- C17 explicit addition
- Recovery integrated as «invisible» complement to C11 hunger (hunger sustains via recovery)
- Recovery moved to Foundation institutional substrate (Part 9 Owner Interaction Scaffold could host)

Decision = Ruslan-ack pending. Phase 10 will surface as recommendation #1.

## §9 Distribution / Who-discipline gap (Zachman Where + Who)

Per Phase 7 §3.2 Zachman matrix mapping, Ruslan's 16 components cluster around What / How / When / Why but UNDERWEIGHT *Where* (distribution / geography / context) and *Who* (responsibility / roles / relationships).

### §9.1 The gap

| Zachman dimension | Ruslan articulation strength |
|---|---|
| What (inventory) | ✓✓ (C9 «изучение системы») |
| How (process) | ✓✓ (C1-C5, C7) |
| Where (distribution) | (gap) |
| Who (responsibility / roles) | (gap; partially via Foundation Part 4) |
| When (timing) | ✓ (C13 «ускоренный») |
| Why (motivation) | ✓✓ (C6 + C16) |

### §9.2 Implications

For solo personal-method, Where + Who gaps are less critical (Ruslan handles both implicitly). For cohort transfer or institutional scaling, Where + Who become structural. Foundation v1.0 partially fills (Part 4 Role Taxonomy = Who; Part 10 External Touchpoints = Where) — but at the meta-method level, not explicit.

### §9.3 R1 surface

Phase 10 recommendation will surface: explicit Where + Who articulation if scaling to cohort/institutional. NOT required for solo personal use.
