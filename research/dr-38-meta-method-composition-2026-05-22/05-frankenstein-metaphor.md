## §2 Shelley original — close reading of the 1818 text

### §2.1 Plot recap as composition narrative

Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein, or The Modern Prometheus" (1818) is structured as a frame-narrative — Captain Walton's letters to his sister contain Victor Frankenstein's autobiographical account, which itself contains the creature's first-person testimony. The composition operation appears at multiple levels [src: D1 Shelley 1818].

**Victor's composition operation (Volume I, ch. 3-4):**
- Education in alchemy (Cornelius Agrippa, Paracelsus, Albertus Magnus) + modern chemistry (Krempe, Waldman at Ingolstadt)
- Two-year obsessive labour: «I collected bones from charnel-houses; and disturbed, with profane fingers, the tremendous secrets of the human frame. ...The dissecting room and the slaughter-house furnished many of my materials» [D1 Shelley 1818, vol. 1 ch. 4]
- Composition method: gathering parts from heterogeneous sources, animating через «principles of life» (galvanism implied) [D1 vol. 1 ch. 4]
- The moment of creation: «the dull yellow eye of the creature opened; it breathed hard, and a convulsive motion agitated its limbs»

**Critical text moment (vol. 1 ch. 5):**

> «How can I describe my emotions at this catastrophe, or how delineate the wretch whom with such infinite pains and care I had endeavoured to form? His limbs were in proportion, and I had selected his features as beautiful. **Beautiful! — Great God! ...the beauty of the dream vanished, and breathless horror and disgust filled my heart.**»

[src: D1 Shelley M. — *Frankenstein, or The Modern Prometheus* 1818, vol. 1 ch. 5]

### §2.2 What Shelley actually depicted

**Critical scholarly point**: Shelley did NOT depict composition-as-monstrous. She depicted **abandonment** as the monstrous act. The creature is articulate, intelligent, sensitive, and morally lucid. He learns language (Paradise Lost, Plutarch's Lives, Sorrows of Werther [D1 vol. 2 ch. 7]), articulates his suffering, and asks for justice and companionship. He becomes destructive *because* Victor abandons him, refuses to make a companion, hunts him.

The composition itself is *not* what Shelley critiques. Anne Mellor (1988) argues the novel's moral centre is Victor's **failure to nurture / take responsibility / accept emergent obligation** — not the original act of composition [src: D2 Mellor 1988].

This re-reading is crucial для DR-38. **If «Frankenstein» = composition-fails-ethically, the failure is abandonment, not composition.** Ruslan's choice of metaphor может signal precisely this: composition is fine; the question is whether one nurtures + integrates + takes responsibility for what emerges. This is consistent with C8 «развитие системы» (continuous nurturing) and C6 «честность» (honest about emergent behaviour).

### §2.3 «Modern Prometheus» — the subtitle

Shelley's subtitle invokes Prometheus, the Titan who composed humans from clay + stole fire (technology, knowledge) from the gods to give to mortals. He was punished by Zeus eternally — but Prometheus is a *positive* figure in most Western tradition: bringer of civilisation, friend of humanity.

The Prometheus framing suggests: **transgression via composition is necessary for human advancement.** The punishment is the eternal cost; the gift is the irreversible benefit. Shelley does not condemn Prometheus; she explores the human inability to bear Prometheus-cost gracefully.

**F3 insight:** This Prometheus-frame is *positive-with-cost*, not «cautionary». Anyone composing something new bears Prometheus-cost. Ruslan's meta-method composition = a Promethean act. The question is whether Ruslan + Jetix Foundation can bear the cost gracefully (= ethical-integration + nurturing + responsibility).

[src: D1 Shelley M. — "Frankenstein, or The Modern Prometheus" 1818, full text; D2 Mellor A.K. — "Mary Shelley: Her Life, Her Fiction, Her Monsters" 1988 Routledge]

### §2.4 The creature's articulation — surprising eloquence

Volume 2 chapters 3-9 contain the creature's first-person account. He is rational, lyrical, deeply moral. His central grievance:

> «Cursed, cursed creator! Why did I live? Why, in that instant, did I not extinguish the spark of existence which you had so wantonly bestowed? I knew not; despair had not yet taken possession of me; my feelings were those of rage and revenge. I could with pleasure have destroyed the cottage and its inhabitants, and have glutted myself with their shrieks and misery. ...But this was a luxury of sensation that could only be enjoyed by being possessed of a heart capable of feeling.»

[D1 Shelley vol. 2 ch. 8]

The creature is more morally articulate than Victor. **The «monster» in the novel is more rational than its creator.** This is Shelley's most profound move: the composed being is not the moral failure; the creator's *abandonment of moral responsibility* is.

## §3 Composition-as-monster — modern interpretation

### §3.1 The «composed-from-parts» reading

Popular contemporary usage treats «Frankenstein's monster» as = «composed-from-incompatible-parts thing that doesn't cohere». This is the reading that worries about Ruslan's «солянка» — does the method-composition hang together?

Three subclaims this reading makes:

1. **Emergent un-belonging** — A composed being doesn't belong anywhere (gravedigger's parts + slaughterhouse + galvanism = no community)
2. **Hidden incoherence** — Composed-from-parts may look-coherent от outside but be internally fractured
3. **Uncontrolled emergence** — Behaviour не predictable от sum-of-parts; surprises follow

For Ruslan's meta-method, these become:

1. **Un-belonging**: Does the 16-component composition fit any community of practice? Or does it sit between Polya-mathematicians, Senge-system-thinkers, Levenchuk-methodologists, Ericsson-practice-researchers — belonging-with-none?
2. **Hidden incoherence**: Are the 16 components actually mutually consistent? Or does C5 «отсечение неважного» conflict with C9 «досконально изучение»? (Phase 8 audit needed.)
3. **Uncontrolled emergence**: What if applying the meta-method produces emergent behaviour Ruslan didn't intend?

### §3.2 Counter-reading: the body-as-composition

But every human is also «composed-from-parts». Hearts, lungs, kidneys evolved from disparate ancestral lineages; the eukaryotic cell is a composition of bacterial endosymbionts (mitochondria); the human brain composes reptile + mammalian + cortical layers. Biology IS composition-from-disparate-parts.

The difference between «monster» and «living organism» is not composition per se but **integration discipline + life-supportive feedback**.

**F3 insight:** This makes the «Frankenstein» metaphor more nuanced than its popular use suggests. Composition-from-parts is biology's normal mode. The question isn't *whether to compose* but *how to integrate*.

Cross-link к Phase 4 software composition: distinguishing «pattern language» (Alexander, living-composition) from «big ball of mud» (DDD anti-pattern, dead-composition) requires explicit integration discipline. Same applies к Ruslan's meta-method.

### §3.3 Three composition outcomes

| Outcome | Software analog | Meta-method analog |
|---|---|---|
| **Living-composition** | Unix kernel, well-architected SOA | C8 + C6 + integration discipline = living meta-method |
| **Dead-but-functional** | Legacy monolith, big ball of mud | Method-collection without integration = «солянка» (food stew — functional but not living) |
| **Necrotic-incoherent** | Frankenstein-system, distributed monolith | Methods that fight each other — produces emergent incoherence |

**F3 insight:** Ruslan's «солянка» metaphor is actually MORE accurate than «Frankenstein» — солянка is functional-not-living (food-stew, eaten then gone), whereas Frankenstein attempts living-composition and may achieve or fail to. The question Ruslan's voice raises (claim 10): is the composition *living* (developing, learning, integrated) or *dead* (functional-collection, not coherent system)?

Ruslan's choice of *both* metaphors (claim 8 солянка + claim 10 Frankenstein) signals he's holding the question open. **R12 paired-frame discipline preserved:** Phase 5 surfaces both readings; does NOT prescribe which.

## §4 Latour — «We have never been modern» — Frankenstein revalidation (1993)

### §4.1 The hybrid thesis

Bruno Latour's "We Have Never Been Modern" (1993) advances: modernity's central conceit was that nature + society are distinct realms, hybrids (technoscience artefacts that combine both) are anomalies to be purified. But hybrids proliferate; modernity has *never* successfully purified them. We are surrounded by Frankensteinian compositions: GMOs, climate models, particle accelerators, the internet [src: D3 Latour 1993].

Latour's later essay «Love Your Monsters» (2011) makes the Frankenstein point explicit: Victor's sin was *not creating the monster* but **abandoning it**. Modern responsibility = love-your-monsters, take care of what you create, nurture the hybrids [src: Latour B. 2011 *Love Your Monsters: Why We Must Care for Our Technologies As We Do Our Children* in *Breakthrough Journal* 2].

### §4.2 Mapping to Ruslan's meta-method

| Latour concept | Ruslan substrate |
|---|---|
| Hybrid | The meta-method itself — hybrid of philosophy, systems, software, personal-discipline |
| Failed purification | Trying to claim «one true method» = doomed purification attempt |
| Love-your-monsters | C8 «развитие системы» = caring for what's been composed |
| Responsibility | C6 «полная честность» = explicit responsibility for emergent behaviour |

**F3 insight:** Latour's «love your monsters» is the **directly applicable ethical principle** для Ruslan's composition. Don't claim purity (don't pretend the meta-method is anything other than a Frankensteinian hybrid); don't abandon (continue cycle work / iteration / responsibility); take care of what's been created. C8 + C6 = the substrate for Latourian responsibility-ethics.

[src: D3 Latour B. — "We Have Never Been Modern" 1993 Harvard University Press, trans. Catherine Porter; Latour B. — "Love Your Monsters" 2011 *Breakthrough Journal* 2]

### §4.3 Implication

Latour vindicates Ruslan's choice of «Frankenstein» metaphor: it signals *embrace of hybridity* + *acknowledgement of responsibility*. This is more honest than claiming «I invented THE method». Audio_719 claim 3 («это даже не открытие нихуя») = Latourian humility.

## §6 Heffernan + Mellor — scholarly interpretive tradition

### §6.1 Mellor's contextual reading (1988)

Anne Mellor's "Mary Shelley: Her Life, Her Fiction, Her Monsters" (1988) argues Shelley wrote Frankenstein at age 18-19 after losing a child + during Romantic-period sublime engagement + critique of male-Romantic ambition (Byron / Percy Shelley) [src: D2 Mellor 1988]. The novel's themes:

- Critique of unbridled scientific ambition (Victor's monomania)
- Critique of male reproductive envy / appropriation
- Critique of abandonment + failure of relational responsibility
- Anti-Romantic depiction of solitude / un-belonging

For DR-38: Mellor's reading reinforces «abandonment, not composition» as the moral centre. Ruslan's meta-method composition is not Mellor-condemned; abandonment of the composed system would be.

### §6.2 Heffernan «Bringing Frankenstein Back to Life» (2003)

Teresa Heffernan's article in *Mosaic* (2003) [src: D4 Heffernan 2003] re-reads Frankenstein for contemporary biotech / AI-era: «what does it mean to bring Frankenstein back to life» = what hybrids are we creating now, and do we take responsibility?

Heffernan's central question: are we «pre-Frankenstein» Victor (innocent / ambitious) or «post-Frankenstein» Victor (after-abandonment-tragedy, with hindsight)? Modern composers of biotech / AI hybrids have post-Frankenstein knowledge. We *know* what abandonment costs.

**F3 insight:** Ruslan's voice (audio_719) signals post-Frankenstein knowledge — he names the monster, holds it as cautionary, signals responsibility (C8 «развитие системы» = anti-abandonment commitment). Cross-link к Method V2 §J 6-step procedure as anti-abandonment discipline.

[src: D4 Heffernan T. — "Bringing Frankenstein Back to Life" *Mosaic* 36(2) 2003]

## §7 Žižek — monster-composition adjacency (2002)

### §7.1 The «desert of the real» analogy

Slavoj Žižek's "Welcome to the Desert of the Real" (2002) discusses post-9/11 cultural composition: hybrid artefacts (terrorist + state + media + financial systems combining into Frankensteinian totality) [src: D6 Žižek 2002].

Žižek's broader theoretical move (drawing on Lacan): the «monster» is *what does not fit any symbolic order* — the real that ruptures category. Frankenstein's monster = real-that-doesn't-fit.

### §7.2 Mapping

For Ruslan's meta-method composition: the «солянка / Frankenstein» = a hybrid that **doesn't fit any disciplinary category** — neither pure-philosophy (Polya) nor pure-systems (Beer VSM) nor pure-software (Unix) nor pure-pedagogical-tradition (Schedrovitsky). It's something that ruptures these categories by combining them.

Žižek's read: the rupture is a *feature*, not a bug. New compositions create new symbolic-orders. The monster-discomfort is the birth-pain of new category.

**F3 insight:** Ruslan's hesitation about «Frankenstein» framing (audio_719 claim 10 — naming the metaphor with mild self-deprecation) IS the Žižek-symptom: holding open the rupture, not prematurely closing into a new pure category. This is *epistemically rigorous*. Phase 6 method-engineering will explore whether Russian / Soviet methodological tradition has a vocabulary for this.

[src: D6 Žižek S. — "Welcome to the Desert of the Real!" 2002 Verso]

## §8 The солянка counter-frame — Russian-positive metaphor

### §8.1 Linguistic context

«Солянка» = Russian soup combining many ingredients (meat / olives / pickles / lemon / spices). Etymology suggests «salty» (from соль = salt) — the salt-cured + pickled mixture. Cultural connotation: hearty, satisfying, generous, *not-monstrous*.

When Ruslan says «собрал такую ебаную солянку из методов» (audio_719 claim 8), the Russian-cultural reading is: «I composed a rich, satisfying mix» — positive-tone, generous-tone, *not* negative-cautionary.

### §8.2 Why both metaphors matter

Phase 1 §3.2 noted: Ruslan uses *both* солянка (claim 8, positive) and Frankenstein (claim 10, ambivalent). This is *not* indecisiveness — it's holding two valid readings:

| Frame | Reading | Useful for... |
|---|---|---|
| Солянка | Rich, satisfying composition; food/nourishment metaphor | Internal motivation; permission to compose; positive substrate |
| Frankenstein | Hybrid-with-responsibility; cautionary aware | External pitch; honest framing; Latour-ethics |

Both true; both useful; surface-context determines which to deploy.
