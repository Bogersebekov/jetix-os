## §2 Polya — heuristic composition (1945)

### §2.1 The four-phase heuristic

George Polya's "How to Solve It" (1945) names a 4-step heuristic for mathematical problem-solving [src: A1 Polya 1945]:

1. **Understanding the problem** — restate it; identify unknown / data / condition; draw a figure
2. **Devising a plan** — recall analogous problems; specialise / generalise; work backward; use auxiliary elements
3. **Carrying out the plan** — verify each step
4. **Looking back** — examine the solution; check the result; can you use it elsewhere?

This is the canonical Western articulation of *method-as-decomposable-procedure*. Polya argues that mastery emerges not from memorising solutions but from internalising heuristic moves — «if you can't solve a problem, there is an easier problem you can solve: find it» [src: A1 Polya 1945, *How to Solve It*, p. 114].

### §2.2 Mapping to Ruslan's components

| Polya phase | Ruslan component (Phase 1) | Mapping note |
|---|---|---|
| 1 Understanding | C9 «досконально изучение системы» + C10 «выявление узких горлышек» | Both = understanding-the-problem; Goldratt TOC analogue |
| 2 Devising | C4 «фокус на важном» + C5 «отсечение неважного» + C7 «использование рычагов» | Method-choice + leverage = devising-a-plan |
| 3 Carrying out | C1 «радикальная проработка» + C2 «глубина» + C3 «качество» + C13 «ускоренный» | Execution-quality + speed |
| 4 Looking back | C8 «развитие системы» + C6 «честность с собой» | Retrospective adjustment requires honesty |

**Key insight (F3):** Polya's 4 phases ARE compositional — sequential procedure with feedback («look back»). Ruslan's 16 components ARE NOT a sequence — they're a *state-machine* + *operating discipline* simultaneously held. This = first divergence: Polya = procedural composition; Ruslan = compositional discipline that includes state + procedure + motivation.

[src: A1 Polya G. — "How to Solve It" 1945; A2 Polya G. — "Mathematics and Plausible Reasoning" Vol I+II 1954]

### §2.3 Polya's plausible reasoning — induction + analogy + specialisation

In "Mathematics and Plausible Reasoning" (1954), Polya extends к 4 patterns of plausible reasoning [src: A2 Polya 1954]:

- **Inductive reasoning** — generalising from verified cases
- **Analogy** — using structural similarity
- **Specialisation** — testing on simpler cases
- **Variation** — perturbing the problem to see what's invariant

Cross-link к Ruslan C9 (досконально изучение): «исследование самой себя как системы» = inductive + analogy moves. Cross-link к C8 (развитие системы): variation-experimentation = Cycle 19/22/23 retrospectives [src: H1 METHOD-LIFE-DEVELOPMENT-V2 §J].

### §2.4 Polya's contribution to DR-38 thesis

H-batch-10-06 («достаточный method-arsenal + meta-level → solve any task») has direct Polya precedent: Polya's program («find the right pattern in your repertoire») assumes a *repertoire-of-heuristics* and a *meta-level capacity to recognise which heuristic fits*. This = H-batch-10-06 in 1945 vocabulary. **Difference:** Polya restricted to mathematical problem-solving (bounded domain); Ruslan generalises to «any task / change any system» — Phase 9 will discuss this universalisation risk.

## §3 Polanyi — tacit knowing + personal coefficient (1958, 1966)

### §3.1 The tacit dimension thesis

Michael Polanyi's "Personal Knowledge" (1958) advances: **all knowledge has a tacit coefficient that cannot be fully articulated** [src: A3 Polanyi 1958]. His famous formulation: «we can know more than we can tell» [src: A4 Polanyi 1966, *The Tacit Dimension*, p. 4].

Polanyi's structural insight: knowing has *from* and *to* aspects — we attend *from* the proximal (the tacit clues, the felt subsidiary awareness) *to* the distal (the explicit target). Composition of a method involves both:

- **Explicit components** = articulated rules (e.g., «if X then Y»)
- **Tacit substrate** = unspoken integration of which rule applies, when, how forcefully

### §3.2 Mapping to Ruslan's components

| Polanyi concept | Ruslan component | Mapping |
|---|---|---|
| Personal coefficient | C6 «полная честность с собой» | Cannot have valid personal knowledge without honest tacit calibration |
| Tacit clues | C9 + C10 mechanism-literacy | The «feel» for bottlenecks predates explicit articulation |
| Indwelling | C11 «1000% голодный» + C14 «развитый» | Method becomes part of one's being — not external rule but internalised stance |
| From-to structure | C7 leverage + C4 focus | Attending *from* method *to* outcome (not vice versa) |

**Key insight (F3):** Ruslan's claim «это просто мой личный метод» (audio_719 claim 3) = Polanyi's *personal coefficient* thesis exactly. The meta-method is not universalisable as algorithmic procedure; it's *indwelt* — it requires personal coefficient (C6 honesty + C11 hunger + C14 maturity). This is why Ruslan emphasises «не открытие нихуя» — discoveries are explicit; what he has is tacit-integrated method.

[src: A3 Polanyi M. — "Personal Knowledge" 1958 University of Chicago Press; A4 Polanyi M. — "The Tacit Dimension" 1966 Doubleday]

### §3.3 Polanyi vs Polya — explicit-procedural vs tacit-integrated

Polya = procedure-explicit. Polanyi = tacit-integration-as-precondition. **Ruslan's meta-method requires both:** procedural (C1-C10) AND tacit/state (C11-C14, C15-C16). This = the first major insight of DR-38: **Ruslan's composition combines two traditions Western thought has often separated.**

### §3.4 Implication for H-batch-10-06

If composition requires tacit coefficient (Polanyi), then «sufficient method-arsenal» is necessary but NOT sufficient — also required is tacit-integration capacity (= what Ruslan calls «мета-уровень»). This makes H-batch-10-06 weaker than universalisable claim («any task») but stronger than «just have many methods» (which Polya alone might suggest).

## §4 Csikszentmihalyi — domain-field-person triad + flow (1990, 1996)

### §4.1 The systems model of creativity

In "Creativity" (1996), Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi proposes a three-element model: creativity emerges from interaction between **domain** (codified body of knowledge), **field** (gatekeepers / community of practitioners), and **person** (individual with internalised domain) [src: A5 Csikszentmihalyi 1996].

Composition / mastery requires:
1. **Domain absorption** — learn what's already there
2. **Field engagement** — situate within community of practitioners
3. **Personal synthesis** — bring novel combination

### §4.2 Mapping to Ruslan's components

| Csikszentmihalyi element | Ruslan substrate |
|---|---|
| Domain | The «куча маленьких методов» (audio_719 claim 2) — the method-arsenal IS the domain Ruslan composed FROM |
| Field | Cohort feedback substrate (per H1 METHOD-LIFE-DEVELOPMENT-V2 §6 reverse engineering + cohort cadence) + Phase 11 Strategic Plan cohort-engagement |
| Person | Ruslan's personal coefficient (C6 + C11 + C14 + C16); the «мой личный метод» framing |

### §4.3 Flow as compositional state-marker

In "Flow" (1990), Csikszentmihalyi names 8 conditions for flow state [src: A6 Csikszentmihalyi 1990]:

1. Clear goals
2. Immediate feedback
3. Challenge-skill balance
4. Action-awareness merger
5. Concentration on task
6. Sense of control
7. Loss of self-consciousness
8. Time distortion

Cross-link к Ruslan C13 «ускоренный» + C14 «развитый» + C11 «1000% голодный» = compositional state precursors to flow. **The Ruslan state-cluster (C11-C14) is best interpreted as the precursor-conditions for sustainable flow-engagement.**

[src: A5 Csikszentmihalyi M. — "Creativity" 1996 HarperCollins; A6 Csikszentmihalyi M. — "Flow" 1990 Harper & Row]

### §4.4 Implication

Csikszentmihalyi shows: **state matters as much as procedure.** A method-composition cannot ignore state. Ruslan's 4 state-components (C11-C14) are not afterthoughts but structurally necessary for the procedural components (C1-C10) to execute. This is consistent with Polanyi (tacit indwelling) and refines Polya (procedural alone is insufficient).

## §5 Ericsson — deliberate practice + mental representations (2016, 2018)

### §5.1 Deliberate practice thesis

K. Anders Ericsson's research synthesis в "Peak" (2016) and "Cambridge Handbook of Expertise" (2018) advances: expert performance emerges not from raw experience but from **deliberate practice** — structured effortful activity with feedback aimed at specific aspects of performance [src: A7 Ericsson 2018; A8 Ericsson + Pool 2016].

The 5 features of deliberate practice [src: A8 Ericsson "Peak" 2016 ch. 4]:
1. Well-defined goals
2. Focused attention
3. Immediate feedback
4. Frequent stretch beyond comfort
5. Build / refine mental representations

### §5.2 Mental representations — the chunking mechanism

Ericsson's central technical contribution: experts differ not in raw cognitive capacity but in **mental representations** — pre-compiled chunks that compress domain content. A chess master sees not 32 pieces but ~7 patterns. A musician hears not 50 notes but ~5 phrases. Expertise = quality of mental representations + speed of access [src: A8 Ericsson + Pool 2016 p. 60-80].

### §5.3 Mapping to Ruslan's components

| Ericsson concept | Ruslan component |
|---|---|
| Deliberate practice | C1 «радикальная проработка» + C2 «глубина» — *exactly* Ericsson's «effortful focused engagement» |
| Mental representations | C9 «досконально изучение системы» — system-mechanism literacy = high-quality MR |
| Feedback | C6 «полная честность с собой» = self-feedback discipline necessary for MR refinement |
| Stretch | C5 «отсечение неважного» enables focus on stretch boundary |
| Frequency / volume | C11 «1000% голодный» = motivational substrate for sustained volume |

### §5.4 Implication for H-batch-10-06

Ericsson research suggests: «sufficient method-arsenal» is *necessary but slow-to-acquire* — typical expertise emerges over ~10,000 hours of deliberate practice (Gladwell's popularisation of Ericsson's data) [src: A8]. **This refutes the «instant any-task solvability» reading of H-batch-10-06.** A more defensible reading: meta-method enables *efficient task-acquisition* (faster MR-building) without claiming immediate任 task-solvability.

[src: A7 Ericsson K.A. ed. — "Cambridge Handbook of Expertise and Expert Performance" 2nd ed. 2018 Cambridge University Press; A8 Ericsson K.A. + Pool R. — "Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise" 2016 Houghton Mifflin Harcourt]

## §6 Newell + Simon — production systems + bounded rationality (1972, 1969)

### §6.1 Production systems as composition substrate

Allen Newell & Herbert Simon's "Human Problem Solving" (1972) proposes the *production system* as cognitive architecture: a knowledge base of «if-condition-then-action» rules + a working memory + a conflict-resolution mechanism [src: A9 Newell+Simon 1972 Prentice-Hall].

Composition в this framework = selecting from the rule-base, sequencing applications, resolving conflicts when multiple rules fire.

### §6.2 Bounded rationality

Simon's "The Sciences of the Artificial" (1969) advances **bounded rationality**: cognition is computationally limited; satisficing (good-enough-given-constraints) replaces optimisation [src: A10 Simon 1969 MIT Press].

### §6.3 Mapping

| Simon/Newell concept | Ruslan component |
|---|---|
| Production rules | C9-C10 mechanism literacy = explicit «if-X-then-Y» knowledge |
| Working memory | C4 «фокус» = limited attention budget per Simon |
| Conflict resolution | C7 «leverage» + meta-method = choose-which-method procedure |
| Satisficing | C3 «качество» + R12 anti-extraction → not maximising at the wrong margin |

### §6.4 Implication

Simon predicts: even with infinite method-arsenal, computational bounds force selection. Therefore the meta-method's *primary value* is not having-many-methods but *fast-selection-from-many-methods*. This refines H-batch-10-06: the constraint is selection speed × selection accuracy, not arsenal size alone.

[src: A9 Newell A. + Simon H. — "Human Problem Solving" 1972 Prentice-Hall; A10 Simon H. — "The Sciences of the Artificial" 1969 MIT Press]

## §7 Munger — latticework of mental models (2005)

### §7.1 The latticework thesis

Charlie Munger's "Poor Charlie's Almanack" (2005) advances: durable competence requires not specialised expertise but a **latticework of ~100 mental models** drawn from multiple disciplines (physics, biology, psychology, economics, statistics, history) [src: A11 Munger 2005 ed. Peter Kaufman].

Munger's mechanism: cross-domain models illuminate each other. «To the man with a hammer, every problem looks like a nail» — defence is to carry many hammers. The composition operation = selecting which model(s) fit, combining their lenses, watching for invariants across them.

### §7.2 Mapping

This is the closest Western analogue to Ruslan's meta-method. Compare:

| Munger | Ruslan |
|---|---|
| ~100 mental models | «куча маленьких методов» (claim 2) |
| Multidisciplinary | SD1-SD5 (language / Claude / team / psycho / info-processing) + Ruslan's own meta-method |
| Lattice = cross-model integration | Composition into «солянка / Frankenstein» (claim 8-10) |
| «Invert, always invert» | C5 «отсечение неважного» — focus by elimination |
| Circle of competence | C7 «leverage» applied within capability boundary |
| «Always make it simple» | C2-C3 quality + leverage discipline |

**Key F3 insight:** Ruslan's audio_719 meta-method articulation = Munger latticework localised к Ruslan's life (Berlin entrepreneur, 2026, AI-native, Russian). Munger's framework gives Western institutional vocabulary for what Ruslan describes phenomenologically.

### §7.3 Munger differs from Ruslan in two important ways

1. **State component absent in Munger:** Munger talks little about hunger / honesty / state-discipline. Ruslan adds C6 + C11-C14 as *prerequisites*. This makes Ruslan's framing more complete.
2. **Personal-method humility:** Munger framed latticework as «what worked for me, recommend to you». Ruslan goes further: «это не открытие, это просто мой личный метод» — *non-universalisable* by explicit framing. This is closer to Polanyi's personal-coefficient thesis.

[src: A11 Munger C. — "Poor Charlie's Almanack: The Wit and Wisdom of Charles T. Munger" 2005 ed. Peter Kaufman, Donning Co.]

## §8 Aristotle — phronesis (practical wisdom) ~340 BC

### §8.1 The phronesis concept

Aristotle's "Nicomachean Ethics" Book VI distinguishes 5 intellectual virtues: episteme (scientific knowledge), techne (craft), phronesis (practical wisdom), nous (intuitive understanding), sophia (philosophic wisdom) [src: A12 Aristotle ~340 BC NE VI.1140a-b].

**Phronesis** = the capacity to deliberate well about what is good and useful for oneself in particular concrete circumstances; *not* universal rule-application but situated-judgement [src: A12 NE VI.5].

### §8.2 Mapping

| Phronesis element | Ruslan substrate |
|---|---|
| Situated judgement | Meta-method as «method-for-choosing-methods» — selection is *situated*, not universal |
| Deliberation | The 6-step §J procedure (METHOD-LIFE-DEVELOPMENT-V2 §J lines 371-380) [src: H1] |
| Concerned with the variable | Real-world tasks where rules can't pre-specify (vs episteme which deals with the invariable) |
| Embedded with virtue | C6 honesty + C16 honesty-foundation = virtue-substrate for phronesis |

**Key F3 insight:** Ruslan's meta-method is best classified philosophically as a **phronesis-discipline**, not as episteme (scientific theory) or techne (craft skill alone). Phronesis is the right category because (a) it's situated (Ruslan-localised), (b) it integrates virtue (honesty), (c) it requires deliberation (meta-level method-choice), (d) it's about the variable (life / business / Jetix). This classification will return в Phase 6 (method-engineering) and Phase 8 (Jetix integration).

[src: A12 Aristotle — "Nicomachean Ethics" Book VI ~340 BC, Bekker numbering 1138b-1145a]

## §9 Hofstadter — strange loops + recursive composition (1979)

### §9.1 The strange loop thesis

Douglas Hofstadter's "Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid" (1979) advances the **strange loop** as fundamental cognitive structure: a hierarchical system that, when traversed, returns to its starting level despite seeming to ascend [src: G2 Hofstadter 1979].

### §9.2 Mapping

Ruslan's meta-method is a strange loop: the meta-method *is itself one of the methods in the arsenal* (audio_719 claim 8: «потом мой же вот этот метод... его же тоже туда добавил»). The composition includes itself.

| Hofstadter | Ruslan |
|---|---|
| Self-reference | Meta-method includes itself in arsenal |
| Tangled hierarchy | Components classify components (C8 «развитие системы» applies к the meta-method itself) |
| Emergent property from recursive substrate | Phronesis emerges from recursive method-application |

**Key F3 insight:** This is structural — *not just a Hofstadter analogy*. It means **the meta-method cannot be evaluated outside its own substrate.** Any external benchmark (e.g., compare to TOGAF — Phase 7) measures only the *explicit components*, not the *self-referential coherence*. The latter is what makes Ruslan's composition «Frankenstein-but-alive», not «kit-of-parts».

[src: G2 Hofstadter D. — "Gödel, Escher, Bach: An Eternal Golden Braid" 1979 Basic Books]

## §10 Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development + external scaffolding (1978)

### §10.1 ZPD thesis

Lev Vygotsky's "Mind in Society" (1978, posthumous compilation of 1920s-30s work) advances the **Zone of Proximal Development**: the range of tasks an individual can perform with assistance but not yet alone; learning occurs *в этой zone* via external scaffolding (other person, tool, sign) [src: G1 Vygotsky 1978].

### §10.2 Mapping

| Vygotsky | Ruslan |
|---|---|
| ZPD | The «развитый» state C14 = at-the-edge-of-current-capability, with external support |
| External scaffolding | LLM stack (SD2 «методы построения Клода»), cohort feedback, Method V2 cycle retrospectives |
| Internalisation | Component-by-component absorption — what was external (Polya / Senge / Levenchuk) becomes internalised method |
| Mediation | Method-as-mediator between Ruslan and task |

**Key F3 insight:** Ruslan's meta-method is not Stone-age «figure-it-out-alone» but Vygotskian «figure-it-out-WITH-external-substrate». This connects к Method V2 §J «external system» (the cycle substrate as external scaffold) + к O-128 cybernetic external system pattern (per DR-40 active research) [src: AUDIO-721-INSIGHTS-REPORT §3 O-128].

[src: G1 Vygotsky L. — "Mind in Society: The Development of Higher Psychological Processes" 1978 Harvard University Press, ed. Cole et al.]

## §11 Kahneman + Taleb — adjacencies (2011, 2012)

### §11.1 Kahneman System 1 / System 2

Daniel Kahneman's "Thinking, Fast and Slow" (2011) names two systems: System 1 (fast / intuitive / automatic) and System 2 (slow / deliberate / effortful) [src: G3 Kahneman 2011].

Mapping: Ruslan's default-method vs meta-method quadrant (Method V2 §5 «default vs choosing» line 350-400) [src: H1] = System 1 vs System 2 differentiation. Default = System 1 inherited routines. Meta-method = System 2 deliberate method-choice. Mastery = ratio of System 2 to System 1 in method-application decisions.

### §11.2 Taleb antifragility

Nassim Taleb's "Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder" (2012) advances: systems can be fragile (broken by volatility), robust (unaffected), or **antifragile** (improved by volatility) [src: G4 Taleb 2012].

Mapping: Ruslan's method-portfolio = antifragile via diversification. C8 «развитие системы» = explicit antifragility-discipline (system improves through volatility / mistake / cycle). Cross-link к Method V2 §M «cycle pattern» = antifragile-by-design [src: H1 §M].

[src: G3 Kahneman D. — "Thinking, Fast and Slow" 2011 Farrar, Straus and Giroux; G4 Taleb N. — "Antifragile: Things That Gain from Disorder" 2012 Random House]

## §13 The «not-discovery» framing — epistemological humility benefit

Ruslan's audio_719 claim 3 («это даже не открытие нихуя») has structural literature support:

- Polanyi: tacit dimensions of personal knowledge cannot be claimed as «discoveries» without losing their personal coefficient
- Csikszentmihalyi: creativity = novel-combination, not invention-from-nothing; Ruslan's combination of existing methods IS the «creativity»
- Munger: «I never invented anything; I just put things together» (paraphrase, *Poor Charlie's Almanack* 2005)
- Aristotle: phronesis is *practical wisdom* not *theoretical discovery*; it cannot be discovered because it's not propositional

**F3 insight:** The humility framing is *philosophically rigorous*, not modest-by-courtesy. It aligns with how Western tradition has long understood personal-method-composition.
